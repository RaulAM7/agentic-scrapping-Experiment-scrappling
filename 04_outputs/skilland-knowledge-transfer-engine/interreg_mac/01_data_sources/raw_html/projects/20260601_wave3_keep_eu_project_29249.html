MAking Sustainable Tourism possible

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Les défis communs abordés par le projet sont : (i) surmonter les différences et unir les PME d'hébergement méditerranéennes autour de l'objectif commun de remodeler le tourisme de manière plus durable ; (ii) les stimuler et les soutenir dans la mise en œuvre de systèmes de gestion durables, à travers le développement de solutions facilement applicables. L'objectif général est de faciliter et de soutenir les PME d'hébergement méditerranéennes dans la mise en œuvre d'un système de gestion durable, qui est actuellement un facteur critique pour le succès et la compétitivité à long terme. Le changement attendu est de faciliter la transition vers un tourisme durable par ces PME, en leur permettant de mettre en place un système de gestion durable et de préparer le terrain pour obtenir la certification ISO 21401:2018. À cette fin, le projet prévoit le développement de deux résultats : un protocole de durabilité et un outil d'auto-évaluation. Ce dernier permettra à ces PME de vérifier leur degré actuel de durabilité ; sur la base des résultats obtenus, le protocole indiquera les activités nécessaires pour se conformer à la norme. L'approche consistera à réaliser une étude basée sur l'ISO 21401:2018 et l'identification des barrières et des besoins des PME d'hébergement en termes de gestion durable. Compte tenu des différentes réalités socio-économiques et des étapes de développement touristique dans les cinq pays partenaires, la coopération transnationale est un facteur crucial pour garantir la réalisation de haute qualité des objectifs ainsi que la diffusion des résultats. Le projet représente une nouveauté par rapport aux pratiques actuelles dans le secteur de l'hébergement ; il propose une étude visant à définir des solutions qui équilibrent les spécificités de toutes les dimensions de la durabilité. Jusqu'à présent, la plupart des professionnels et universitaires du tourisme ont proposé la durabilité des installations d'hébergement en se basant presque exclusivement sur la dimension environnementale, entraînant un retard dans le plein développement d'une culture organisationnelle durable.

Description (EN):
The common challenges addressed by the project are: (i) overcoming differences and uniting Mediterranean SMEs accommodation around the common objective of reshaping tourism in more sustainable ways; (ii) stimulating and supporting them in the implementation of sustainable management systems, through the development of easily applicable solutions. The overall objective is to facilitate and support Mediterranean SMEs accommodation in implementing a sustainable management system, which is currently a critical factor for the success and competitiveness in the long term. The expected change is to facilitate the transition towards sustainable tourism by such SMEs, enabling them to implement a sustainable management system and to pave the way for achieving the ISO 21401:2018 certification. To this end, the project foresees the development of two outputs: sustainability protocol and the self-assessment tool. The latter will enable these SMEs to check their current degree of sustainability; based on the results obtained, the protocol will indicate the activities needed to comply with the standard. The approach will be to conduct a study based on ISO 21401:2018 and the identification of the barriers and needs of SMEs accomodation in terms of sustainable management. Given the different socio-economic realities and stages of tourism development in the five partner countries, transnational cooperation is a crucial factor in ensuring the high-quality achievement of the objectives as well as the dissemination of the results. The project represents a novelty compared to current practices in the accommodation sector; it proposes a study aimed at defining solutions that balance the specificities of all dimensions of sustainability. To date, most tourism professionals and scholars have proposed the sustainability of accommodation facilities based almost exclusively on the environmental dimension, causing a delay in the full development of a sustainable organisational culture.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The MAST project has made significant strides since its inception, demonstrating a robust commitment to advancing sustainable tourism practices in the Mediterranean region. The project's initial phase has been marked by noteworthy achievements that have set a solid foundation for future endeavours. From the outset, the consortium, led by UNIURB, has shown exemplary coordination and management. The successful online kick-off meeting in March 2024 aligned all partners, establishing a clear vision for the project's objectives. This was further reinforced by the Steering Committee meeting held in Barcelona in May 2024, which provided a valuable platform for in-depth discussions and strategic planning. A cornerstone of the project's early success has been the establishment of efficient communication and management structures. Implementing the Basecamp platform for internal project management, along with launching a WordPress website and active social media channels, has facilitated seamless collaboration amongst partners and effective engagement with external stakeholders. The research component of the project has made impressive headway. A comprehensive methodology for investigating sustainability in the accommodation sector has been meticulously designed, encompassing scoping studies, quantitative analyses, and qualitative research. To date, 26 qualitative interviews have been conducted across partner countries, providing rich insights into the current landscape of sustainable practices in the Mediterranean tourism industry. A thorough literature review analysing approximately 45 relevant studies has also been undertaken, laying the groundwork for a robust theoretical framework. In parallel with these research efforts, significant progress has been made in developing practical tools. The consortium has begun work on a Market Search Report and initiated the planning phase for a sustainability self-assessment tool. These endeavours have been informed by carefully studying existing sustainability labels and schemes in the tourism industry, ensuring that the project's outputs will be innovative and relevant to industry needs. Stakeholder engagement has been a priority from the outset. The project has been represented at key events such as the Sustainable Tourism Community kick-off meeting in Rome and various EU MED meetings. These platforms have provided valuable opportunities for networking, knowledge sharing, and raising awareness about the MAST project's objectives and potential benefits. Quality assurance has not been overlooked, with a comprehensive MAST Quality Assurance plan developed to guide all project activities. This attention to quality underpins the project's commitment to delivering high-calibre outputs that will have a lasting impact on the sector. The collaborative nature of the MAST project has proven to be one of its greatest strengths. By bringing together diverse expertise from across the Mediterranean region, the consortium has developed a nuanced and comprehensive approach to sustainability in tourism. This multi-stakeholder, cross-border cooperation has facilitated valuable knowledge exchange on sustainability practices and challenges specific to different contexts within the Mediterranean. In conclusion, the MAST project has made remarkable progress in its initial phase. The foundations laid through meticulous research, strategic planning, and stakeholder engagement have positioned the project to significantly contribute to sustainable tourism practices in the Mediterranean region. As the project moves forward, it is well-equipped to develop innovative tools and strategies to drive the transition towards more sustainable accommodation practices, ultimately contributing to Mediterranean tourism's long-term environmental, social, and economic sustainability. The MAST project has made substantial progress in helping small and medium-sized accommodation providers across Europe adopt sustainable practices. By combining expertise from partners in Italy, Spain, Greece, Slovenia, and Bosnia and Herzegovina, the team has delivered valuable insights, tools, and outreach activities designed to address real-world challenges faced by the hospitality sector. A major milestone is the completion of the Consultation Report on SME Needs and Barriers to Sustainability (D2.1), led by the University of Urbino. This report synthesises data from over 211 accommodation providers and 22 interviews, identifying common challenges in environmental, social, and economic sustainability. Partners such as CENFIM in Spain and Sarajevo Susret Kultura in Bosnia contributed regional survey data, while 3WIN in Greece shared examples of successful sustainability practices from local hotels. The collaborative review process ensured the final report offers practical, actionable recommendations tailored to SMEs. Building on this research, the project is developing tools to support sustainable transitions. For example, 3WIN is creating an online self-assessment tool to help accommodation providers evaluate their sustainability performance. Market research and external expertise have informed its design, which aligns with the ISO 21401 standard. A beta version is expected by mid-2025. Meanwhile, the University of Primorska in Slovenia has produced guidelines for a Sustainability Protocol, translating research findings into clear steps for SMEs to follow. Alongside these practical tools, the project has prioritised stakeholder engagement. Events like the Info Day in Sarajevo brought together over 150 participants to discuss sustainability challenges and solutions. 3WIN also showcased Greek hotel success stories at this event, inspiring peer learning. Upcoming activities include a Steering Committee Meeting in Greece in 2025, designed to further engage stakeholders. Communication efforts—such as social media campaigns, website updates, and a promotional video—have raised awareness of the project’s goals and early achievements. Underpinning these efforts is strong coordination and quality assurance. The University of Urbino, as lead partner, has ensured smooth collaboration between work packages, including the transition from research (WP1) to tool development (WP3). Regular meetings, such as the Steering Committee in Sarajevo, have kept partners aligned. CENFIM’s rigorous quality checks, including surveys and progress reviews, have maintained high standards across deliverables. The MAST project highlights the power of cross-border cooperation. Partners have pooled expertise in sustainability standards, data analysis, and tool development, while regional diversity—from Spanish hotels to Bosnian guesthouses—ensures solutions are adaptable to different contexts. Early milestones, such as the accelerated completion of the Market Search Report, demonstrate the efficiency of this collaborative approach. Looking ahead, the team will focus on finalising the self-assessment tool and rolling out training webinars to empower SMEs. By combining research, innovation, and partnership, MAST is paving the way for a more sustainable future in Mediterranean tourism. In the first 18 months of implementation, the MAST project has successfully transitioned from foundational research to the creation of tangible outputs designed to support sustainability in the tourism sector. The main achievements of this reporting period centre on the development of the project’s core tools, underpinned by a robust transnational cooperation that has been vital to their quality and relevance. The primary achievement has been the finalisation of the MAST Sustainability Protocol for SMEs in accommodation (D2.4.1). Led by UNIURB, this protocol serves as a practical guide for small and medium-sized hotels, translating the complex requirements of the ISO 21401:2018 standard into accessible, actionable steps. The protocol is grounded in a comprehensive comparative analysis (D2.3.1) that cross-referenced the standard's requirements with the real-world needs and barriers previously identified among accommodation providers across the partner countries. Complementing the protocol, the partnership has developed the first FETA version of the AI-based self-assessment tool. This digital application, led by the technical experts at 3WIN, will allow accommodation providers to evaluate their sustainability performance and identify areas for improvement. The beta version is now ready for the crucial phase of user-based assessment, which will begin in the next reporting period. The added value of the transnational cooperation has been clearly demonstrated in this phase. The project successfully combined UNIURB's academic rigour in developing the protocol with 3WIN's technical expertise in software development. This synergy was enriched by continuous feedback from all partners, ensuring the outputs are applicable across diverse national contexts in Spain, Slovenia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Greece. The 3rd Steering Committee Meeting and Stakeholder Info Day in Crete, involving over 40 local stakeholders, was a key moment for this collaboration, allowing for direct exchange and validation of the project's direction. Furthermore, effective project-wide dissemination, led by SMOC, has enhanced visibility through the production of two newsletters and a promotional video, actively shared by all partners.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The project will contribute significantly to the areas identified by the EU Green Deal, with specific reference to: raising the ambition of the EU's climate targets for 2030 and 2050; securing the supply of clean, affordable and secure energy; mobilising industry for a clean, circular economy; resource-efficient construction and renovation; zero pollution target for a toxic-free environment; preserving and restoring ecosystems and biodiversity. Through the study conducted based on ISO 21401:2018, it will be possible to define strategic solutions with a high sustainable content, whose combination with the challenges and needs of SMEs accommodation will ensure an effective transition towards sustainable tourism. This study will lead to the development of a sustainability protocol and self-assessment tool that can facilitate and support such SMEs in implementing a sustainable management system. The self-assessment tool will enable such SMEs to verify their current degree of maturity in terms of sustainability; based on the result obtained, the protocol will provide them with indications on the activities they will need to implement in order to comply with the standard. These activities certainly include those for reduction of emissions into the air; energy efficiency; implementation of circular economy models through reduction, reuse or recycling of solid waste; minimisation of the impact of architecture on the landscape; management of storage, use and disposal of hazardous substances; ensuring water quality and safety; minimisation of water consumption; conservation of natural areas, flora and fauna. These activities, together with those of a social and economic nature, will provide an opportunity for SMEs to think and act in a sustainable way. In this framework, it is therefore evident the contribution the project makes by fostering the development of a sustainable and inclusive growth strategy, perfectly in line with the objectives of the EU Green Deal.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 Through transnational cooperation between the project partners, it will be possible to define a common strategy for the development of sustainable solutions for tourism sector, thus addressing the socio-economic differences between the consortium members. By doing so, the project will contribute to the area of Integration beyond borders identified by the Territorial Agenda 2030, favoring the development of a common strategy that enhances cross-border cooperation and promotes sustainable development. The strategy for sustainable tourism defined jointly by the consortium will then be adapted to the national context of each one. Thus, it will firstly be possible to respond to the current global social and environmental challenges and to increase the competitiveness of partner countries, taking into account the peculiarities and needs of each territory and encouraging collaboration between local public institutions and SMEs accommodation. This will rebalance the different socio-economic assets of the partner countries, laying the foundations for a more balanced growth. In this sense, the project will contribute to the priority named by the Territorial Agenda as A Balanced Europe. The declination of strategic solutions in the national context will also allow it to contribute to the Functional Regions area. Indeed, the project will foster the local and regional development of each partner country through sustainable strategies capable of reducing convergences and inequalities between places, as well as through the active involvement and constant dialogue between stakeholders involved. The Healthy Environment area will also benefit from such project as it includes actions strongly aimed at promoting and respecting the areas cultural and environmental heritage. The project will also contribute to the Circular Economy area as activities will be designed and shared to support SMEs in implementing economic models oriented towards waste reduction, reuse and recycling.
