Green and Resilient European Excellence Network for Smart MED SMEs

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Le contexte socio-économique et politique a radicalement changé, mettant en évidence la nécessité de repenser les priorités de fabrication et l'organisation de l'approvisionnement.Les concepts de résilience, d'économie d'énergie, d'économie circulaire, de pénurie de matières premières et de dépendance deviennent urgents. L'industrie manufacturière peut contribuer à la "croissance verte", en favorisant une industrie neutre sur le plan climatique et circulaire.Les PMEs, avec leur rôle crucial dans les économies, le développement régional, manquent souvent de capacité financière pour investir dans la R&I, ce qui entraîne une faible participation aux activités de collaboration. Ainsi, l'objectif est d'intégrer et d'améliorer les méthodologies développées dans les projets Interreg EuroMED GREENOMED et FinMED en s'appuyant sur la Communauté GREEN GROWTH ainsi que de développer, tester et rendre transférable la méthodologie GREENSMARTMED et la boîte à outils associée.Cette méthodologie et ces outils GREENSMARTMED visent à renforcer la coopération transrégionale entre les parties prenantes de 4helix dans les 5 régions concernées (Italie, Espagne, France, Grèce et Bulgarie) afin de créer une communauté européenne capable d'apporter des innovations pour une fabrication verte et résiliente, de contribuer à la mise en œuvre de RIS3 et d'aider les PME à transformer les défis de la durabilité en opportunités.Le test de la méthodologie GREENSMARTMED et de la boîte à outils correspondante dans des régions européennes présentant différents niveaux de développement économique aidera le partenariat à développer une méthodologie facilement transférable.Sur la base de la RIS3, les secteurs cibles sont les machines, le textile, les plastiques, l'agroalimentaire et la mobilité et les batteries. L'accent sera mis sur les solutions, le transfert de technologie concernant les matériaux et produits chimiques durables, l'optimisation de l'énergie et des ressources et la réduction des émissions

Description (EN):
The socio-economic and political context has dramatically changed in the last two years, highlighting the need to rethink manufacturing priorities and supply chain organization. Due to the problematic geopolitical situation and uncertainty in health conditions, the concepts of resilience, energy saving, circular economy, raw materials scarcity and dependency are becoming increasingly relevant and urgent. Manufacturing can significantly contribute to “green growth”, fostering a climate-neutral, circular and clean industry. In this situation, SMEs, with their crucial role in economies, regional development, and competitiveness, often lack the organizational structure and financial capability to invest in R&I, leading to a scarce participation in collaboration activities. The objective of GREENSMARTMED is to integrate and upgrade the methodologies developed in the Interreg EuroMED GREENOMED and FinMED projects and, leveraging the GREEN GROWTH Community, to develop, test, and make transferable the new integrated methodology and related toolkit. These GREENSMARTMED methodology and toolkit aim at enhancing transregional cooperation among the 4helix stakeholders in the 5 Regions involved (in Italy, Spain, France, Greece and Bulgaria) to create a European community able to uptake innovations for a green and resilient manufacturing, contributing to RIS3 implementation, and helping SMEs to turn sustainability challenges into opportunities and establish sustainable business development opportunities. Testing GREENSMARTMED methodology and toolkit in Regions with various economic development levels will allow the development of a widely transferable methodology in Europe. Based on RIS3, the target sectors are machinery, textile, plastics, agri-food, and mobility&batteries with a focus on solutions, technology transfer, and business practices addressing sustainable materials/chemicals, energy&resources optimization, and emission reduction.

Actual Achievements (EN):
After an in-depth of the methodologies developed in GREENOMED, finMED projects and in the GREEN GROWTH community, we developed the GREENSMARTMED methodology that, starting from the development of regional networks, has the aim of enhancing transregional cooperation among 4helix stakeholders in the 5 Regions involved (in Italy, Spain, France, Greece and Bulgaria) to create a European community bringing innovation for a green and resilient manufacturing and contributing to RIS3 implementation. The methodology, that is part of the result 1 “GREENSMARTMED methodology and toolkit”, resulted in the following 8 steps: - Regional ecosystem mapping - Identification of key topics for R&I focusing on RIS3 - Establishment of regional core groups - Motivating ideas and consolidation of regional core groups - Involvement of other regions - Ideas’ development and evaluation - Assessment of SMEs capabilities - Identification of Financial Instruments - Regional/Transregional cooperation initiative design This methodology has been pre-validated in the 5 regions separately through internal meeting with the associate partners involved in the project and other regional stakeholders, in which the methodology has been presented and discussed to assess its robustness and applicability. In this activity all the partners and associate partners have collaborated in the definition and validation of the methodology, as foreseen in output 1.1. Furthermore, a regional ecosystem mapping tool has been developed with the objective of identifying opportunities for integration and needs for upgrading, given the current novel socio-economic and political context. Each Region has filled out this Regional ecosystem mapping, with of initial list of the main stakeholders to be involved in the project activities, according to their typology: - industrial stakeholders: they will provide bottom-up priorities and contents for establishing regional core groups. - policy makers: they will be the main target of activity 3.2 in WP3 (Policy strategies and Policy makers engagement). In fact, they should direct their green innovation funding policies considering the needs emerged in their Regions through the collective efforts of regional stakeholders. In addition, within activity 3.2 it is foreseen that partners will be responsible for carrying out at least two transfer actions towards Regional/national/local authorities. - Intermediaries and Business Support Organisations: this kind of stakeholders, and Clusters in particular, will implement the methodology developed. In addition, they should allow the systematic identification and exploitation of synergies with other MED regions which complement or reinforce capabilities of regional actors in the design of Regional/Transregional cooperation initiatives. They will be the recipients of the training activities related to the methodology and its toolkit as well. In addition, the business support organizations belonging to the partnership, supported by the research and educational partners, will be responsible to transfer results to other business support organizations external to the Partnership and to Regional, National and/or local Authorities. - HEIs and research centers: they will be the stakeholder group which will enable the technological support to Industrial stakeholders, which should be orchestrated by Business Support Organizations. As for Industrial Stakeholders, they will benefit from the Regional core groups that will emerge, and from its potential trans-regional cooperation. Furthermore, since the methodology includes also a set of tools to support the implementation of each steps, an in-depth analysis of tools developed in GREENOMED, finMED projects and in the GREEN GROWTH community has been carried out and a first list of tools have been defined. The list of tools will be finalized and validated in Period 2. With the definition of the tool we can completely achieve the Result 1 “GREENSMARTMED methodology and toolkit”, that with will be tested in the different Regions in the other period of the projects. One of the most significant achievements of the GREENSMARTMED project so far has been the development of the GREENSMARTMED pre-validated methodology and toolkit, a practical resource designed to support green manufacturing. The methodology, developed in Period 1 (A1.1) by the consortium under the coordination of UNIBG and pre-validated in the 5 Regions involved, represents a set of steps and activities to design and set up transregional collaboration activities. The toolkit, developed in Period 2 by AFIL (A1.2) with the collaboration of all the partners, offers tools to implement the methodology. It includes a range of documents to assist stakeholders, such as templates for ecosystem mapping and stakeholder engagement, guidelines for forming regional core groups, revised tools for assessing SME capabilities, and financial instrument analysis. Additional resources, such as training materials, presentations, manuals, and creative workshop formats, ensure that SMEs and intermediaries can easily adopt and apply its practices. Validated by partners, the toolkit has proven adaptable to various regional and industrial contexts, making it a key component in fostering green innovation across the Mediterranean. Always related to WP1 activities, an assessment and testing plan has been developed by CERTH, aiming at defining a common format and process for the testing activities that will be realized in the five Regions participating in the GREENSMARTMED project, in order to identify strategic regional and trans-regional collaboration in the area of green manufacturing. The assessment plan, tools and format has been defined in terms of evaluation criteria, indicators, and responsibilities. A common time and work plan, governance and communication plan has been also defined for piloting in different Regions to enable transregional cooperation. Both in period 1 and 2, data for the calculation of the carbon footprint has been gathered by all the partners under the coordination of TEXFOR (A3.4). Strategic meetings have played a pivotal role in ensuring the project’s results. In February 2024, a key meeting in Bergamo brought all partners together to finalize the methodology and toolkit, setting the stage for real-world testing in the next phase. The meeting in Lyon focused on scaling the project’s outcomes and planning for the toolkit’s transferability to regions beyond the partnership. All the activities have been realized coherently with the planning of the project main strategy for Communication and RAS, to ensure the overall coherence of the project with its main final objectives, as well as with its role in the framework of the industrial transition focus area of the ISE Mission. In this sense all the communication activities have been realized under the coordination of CUEIM, who is responsible of the creation and management of a Communication Committee: working at the final release of the project website and making all the improvements and adjustments as requested by the JS, and ensuring its regular management and update; regularly publishing and updating the project social media accounts with posts about news and events related to the project activities; planning and releasing the Project Communication Strategy; working on communication material, among which an updated version of the project poster as well as a project summary and a flyer, made available (both on Basecamp and on the website Media Kit) to other partners for their proper use, to ensure the overall coherence of the project communication activities. In the framework of activities foreseen in the Communication and RAS, project partners participated in trainings and seminars organized by the Programme, like the ISEC Hub and Capacity Building webinar (02/10/24) and the ISE Community of Practice Training (14/11/24), that proved to be useful to better align the project strategy to the ISE Mission one, as well as in several events, such as the Forum Euro-MED Italia (Florence, 21/11/24) and the MED Talks – 2nd Edition (online, 16/12/24), that allowed It to better focus on the potential impact of the project results both at National and Mediterranean levels. The project results have also been disseminated in conferences and workshops, such as in the International Conference on “Advances in Production Management Systems” (APMS) held in September 2024 in Chemnitz (Germany). With these achievements as a foundation, the GREENSMARTMED project is ready to move forward with the next steps. The upcoming phases will focus on testing the toolkit in real-world scenarios, further refining its usability, and expanding the collaboration networks across regions. By building on the progress made so far, the project is poised to deliver even greater impact, paving the way for sustainable innovation and regional resilience. During Period 3 the following activities have been carried out: WP2: the activities of implementing and testing the methodology and the tools developed in WP1 were carried out in the 5 Regions involved in the project. Working groups were created in all the Regions by the partners engaging Small and Medium Enterprises, Large companies, Business Support Organizations (BSOs), Higher Education Institutions (HEIs), Local, Regional, and National Public Authorities. Meetings and discussions on the relevant Research&Innovation priorities related to green manufacturing were organized and will continue in the next periods. CERTH as Work Package Leader constantly provided guidelines regarding the specific activities to ensure smooth execution and diligently monitored their progress through online and in-person progress meetings. Hereafter the sectorial focus on the Regions: Italy: focus on polymers, textile, batteries, and machinery sectors Spain: focus on the textile sector, with the establishment of the Textile-Forestry Regional Core Group Greece: focus on the agri-food sector, with CERTH’s core group focusing on wine-making and RDF’s core group focusing on dairy France: focus on polymers sector Bulgaria: focus on the machinery and agrifood sector Furthermore, testing and assessment plan and tool has been elaborated by POL and presented during Thessaloniki meeting to prepare activity 2.2. WP3 – Activity 3.3: under the lead of CUEIM, all the partners carried out several communication activities to promote the GREENSMARTMED project and contribute to its sustainability objectives. In particular, the partners collaborated with TCP and IDP governance projects, participating in: Peer-reviewing, mentoring, and twinning sessions, ISE CoP Gathering (Sarajevo, April 2025), ISE Mission Communication Network activities. As follow up of the ISE CoP Gathering, the consortium started planning some initiatives to involve “Birzeit University”, the taker assigned to the project under the “twinning and Mentoring programme”, led by the ISE Governance Projects. Regarding Communication and RAS Strategy Implementation, under the leadership of CUEIM, the consortium carried out the following activities: - Annual Communication Report (2024): Finalized and submitted in February 2025. - Communication Committee Meetings: Held formal meetings (30 Jan, 27 May 2025) and several internal sessions to coordinate events and participation in ISE CoP and project meetings. - Partner Coordination: Conducted bilateral meetings between CUEIM and AFIL, UNIBG, CERTH, EURECAT, and TEXFOR to define messaging and outreach strategies. - Website and Social Media: Maintained weekly posts on Facebook and LinkedIn, updated the website, and ensured content promotion for regional core groups. - Communication Materials: Updated project media kit (summary, flyer, posters, roll-up) and submitted the final project video (17 Jan 2025). Furthermore, the Consortium Meeting was held in Thessaloniki, where the project consortium discussed about the progress of the project’s activities, and participated in the capacity building transnational cooperation event “The Green Innovation in the Agrifood sector” organized by CERTH where some partners brought their stakeholders. WP3 – Activity 3.4: under the leadership of TEXFOR, the consortium contributed to the project’s environmental sustainability goals by completing the Carbon Footprint assessment and contributing to the deployment of the Carbon Footprint report. Furthermore, 3 Carbon Footprint sessions (April 2025, May 2025, and June 2025) were carried out, engaging in discussions on reducing environmental impact.

(FR), FinMED (Financed by Interreg MED Programme 01/02/2018 – 31/02/2022). Fostering economic growth and development, while ensuring that natural assets continue to provide the resources and environmental services on which prosperity relies, is no more a choice but an imperative for economic and social future wellbeing of people. Although green transition will not take off without financing and assure the financing of innovation in green sectors has become a common concern for all MED countries and regions. To answer this challenge, a pool of regions, clusters, business support organisations, knowledge providers and development agencies joined in the finMED project, whose aim is to boost the financing of innovation in green growth sectors through improved delivery of policies and strategies and the introduction of innovative cluster services.
  • The project delivers concrete outputs and results in three fields:
  • 1- Public policies delivery, by developing and adopting new concepts, principle and practices to finance green growth strategies;
  • 2- Innovation and business environment, by improving interaction and collaboration (at regional and transnational level) among involved stakeholders and bridging the gap among enterprises and financers;
  • 3- Green sectors capacities, by stimulating and actively supporting their adaptability to the most impellent financing challenges. finMED also develops a proper specific position on the future of green sectors innovation financing, supporting the policy debate for 2021-2028 programming period in MED and EU area

It stands out in Europe for its great potential for utilizing textile waste: they have a long tradition of factories using recycled textile materials in their production. At a time when decisive measures are being defined to curb the impact of the climate crisis and advance the circular economy, and after an extensive process of dialogue and consensus, the Pact for Circular Fashion is born. It is a voluntary pact between institutions and companies from the different links of the textile value chain in Catalonia that will facilitate the incorporation of circularity measures, the promotion of collaborative projects and the improvement of the sector’s competitiveness. An open and transversal sectoral initiative. A group of driving agents from the entire textile value chain have been working alongside the Generalitat de Catalunya (Catalan Government) and other organizations to define the bases of the Pact. The Pact was born with the desire to add and coordinate the efforts of all the textile value chain agents and respond jointly and effectively to the sector’s challenges, establishing common objectives and generating instruments that make possible the sector’s transformation towards a circular model. This sectoral initiative is one of the circular economy pilot actions chosen by the European Union within the Interreg Europe CircE project for its innovative nature and its potential for replicability in other territories.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The European Green Deal presents a roadmap for making the EU’s economy sustainable by turning climate and environmental challenges into opportunities across all policy areas and making the transition just and inclusive for all. It aims to boost the efficient use of resources by moving to a clean, circular economy and stop climate change, revert biodiversity loss and cut pollution; it outlines investments needed and financing tools available, and explains how to ensure a just and inclusive transition. The European Green Deal covers all sectors of the economy, notably transport, energy, agriculture, buildings, and industries such as steel, cement, ICT, textiles and chemicals.
    • GREENSMARTMED will contribute to the economy sustainable transition described focusing on machinery, textile, plastics, agrifood and mobility&batteries sectors, enhancing transregional cooperation with quadruple helix stakeholders by:
    • - bringing innovation in green manufacturing in the MED Area;
    • - facilitating the implementation of regional RIS3 with a sustainable value chain perspective;
    • - adopting a systemic approach to emphasize experimentation and working group activities to support SMEs to turn sustainability challenges into competitiveness in the framework of the designed innovative ecosystem;
    • - activating capacity building and training with pro-active re-skilling and upskilling of project target groups to reap the benefits of the ecological transition.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The GREENSMARTMED project will address the needs highlighted by the Territorial Agenda 2030 Strategy to respond to the increasing pressure concerning sustainable development and climate change in European value chains. Indeed, the project focuses on green and smart manufacturing and aims at enhancing innovations to setup circular and sustainable European value chains, favoring transregional cooperation as mean to provide responses to global societal challenges and improving working, living and business conditions in all places, as well as strengthening socio-economic prosperity, innovation capacity, positions in global value chains and global competitiveness across Europe. Furthermore, the target sectors (machinery, textile, plastics, agri-food, mobility&batteries) are those that should contribute the most to sustainability of the manufacturing industry in Europe (see section C2.2).
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion The GREENSMARTMED project will deal with some main priorities of EUSALP. Specifically, the overall objective of the GREENSMARTMED project overlaps with Objective 1 and, in particular, with Pillar 1, namely ‘Developing innovation and research capacity and transfer into practice’ with the tested and transferred GREENSMARTMED methodology, and Pillar 2, namely ‘Improving and developing support for enterprises’ with the structured GREENSMARTMED toolkit. Furthermore, Action Group 1 (‘to develop an effective research and innovation ecosystem’) and Action Group 2 (‘to increase the economic potential of strategic sectors’) will be addressed, as they include setting up of transnational frameworks for the identification of existing innovation resources, potential and obstacles, and setting up of trans-border networks for the coordination of innovation policy investment opportunities, as well as funding support instruments of business support centers, chambers of commerce, clusters, public administrations and financing institutions. Besides this, the GREENSMARTMED project will address Pillar 3, namely ‘building further on the position of the Alpine Region as world-class in terms of energy efficiency and sustainable production of renewable energy’ of Objective 3, since the focus of the methodology is on green and smart manufacturing with the aim of enhancing innovations to setup circular and sustainable European value chains, favoring transregional cooperation and so on.
    • Other The GREENSMARTMED project will contribute to the NextGenerationEU strategy which aims at making Europe greener, more digital and more resilient after the pandemic. In particular, GREENSMARTMED will contribute to stimulating a fair climate transition and fighting climate change. In fact, the outputs of the GREENSMARTMED project include solutions to make green innovation affordable and accessible even for SMEs and to foster the creation of European value chains for green innovations. In turn, this will produce new clean and renewable technologies to power up Europe and make it less dependent from key and scarce raw materials currently produced outside Europe. Besides the direct connection with the Italian recovery plan, through the involvement of UNIBG, GREENSMARTMED will create a network of partners from multiple countries that will allow to create synergies among the ways in which different countries decided to leverage the NextGenerationEU programme.
