Med Diet identities - from territorial networking to cluster organization

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The Mediterranean is considered a climate change hotspot: the region is warming 20% faster than the global average with severe consequences in the environment, increased by an unbalanced use of territorial resources characterised by urban congestion and depopulation/desertification of rural areas. The tourism sector is a vital industry for the region, although it affects these conditions on urban/coastal areas, often neglecting rural potential. Counteracting this dualism needs to implement an "industry of the territory" focused on rural areas. The Mediterranean Diet (MD) – to which MedDietGo! (MDG) is dedicated - represents a social, economic and territorial model of extraordinary interest from this point of view: healthy lifestyle model recognised by UNESCO as Intangible World Heritage, it is, also, a model of resilience for landscape, production, traditions, crafts and rites which constitutes an opportunity for thinking of a more balanced tourism destinations. Starting from MD.net achievements - Med Diet Declaration, MD.net platform and Euro-Cluster - MDG connects old values of the Mediterranean Diet with new and innovative tools to increase stakeholders' awareness about the potential of a MD holistic approach to make Mediterranean inner regions more competitive in the tourism global offer. Especially important, for rural contexts, will be the MDG support to technical and business networks and the help to micro and small entrepreneurs to match research bodies geared towards innovation for green and blue growth. MDG transfers the main MD.net results preserving the MD traditions through technological innovation and networking to the aim of: - enlarging the adherence to MD values, both in MD.net communities and new partners’ territories, - consolidating the Med Diet Euro-Cluster network to support rural economies and cope with the global market, - promoting the MD territories (and products) as destinations of niche international tourism.

Description (FR):
La Méditerranée est un point chaud du changement climatique : la région se réchauffe 20 % plus vite que la moyenne mondiale, avec de graves conséquences sur l'environnement, aggravées par une utilisation déséquilibrée des ressources territoriales, caractérisée par la congestion urbaine et le dépeuplement/désertification des zones rurales. Le secteur du tourisme est une industrie vitale pour la région, bien qu'il affecte ces conditions sur les zones urbaines/côtières, négligeant souvent le potentiel rural. Pour le contrecarrer, il faut mettre en œuvre une "industrie du territoire" axée sur les zones rurales. Le régime méditerranéen (MD) - auquel MedDietGo! (MDG) - représente un modèle social, économique et territorial d'un intérêt extraordinaire de ce point de vue : modèle de vie sain reconnu par l'UNESCO comme patrimoine mondial immatériel, c'est aussi un modèle de résilience pour le paysage, la production, les traditions et l'artisanat qui constitue une opportunité des destinations touristiques plus équilibrées. En partant des réalisations de MD.net - la Déclaration du Régime Méditerranéenne, la plateforme MD.net et l'Euro-Cluster - l'OMD relie les anciennes valeurs du RM avec des outils nouveaux et innovants afin de sensibiliser au potentiel d'une approche holistique du MD. Pour les contextes ruraux, le soutien de l'OMD aux réseaux techniques et commerciaux et l'aide apportée aux micro et petits entrepreneurs pour qu'ils correspondent aux organismes de recherche orientés vers l'innovation pour la croissance verte et bleue seront particulièrement importants. Med Diet Go! transfère les principaux résultats de MD.net en préservant les traditions du MD par l'innovation technologique et la mise en réseau, pour - élargir l'adhésion aux valeurs du MD, - consolider le réseau de l'Euro-Cluster Med Diet, - promouvoir les territoires (et les produits) du MD en tant que destinations de niche du tourisme international.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the period covered by the initial PPR, the project was launched effectively. Most of the activities were successfully started in this "preparatory phase" and most of the deliverables were completed, respecting the project work plan. The project coordination fulfilled its objectives and the work plan set up for the reporting period. Regarding the management tasks implemented, it includes the familiarisation with the final project application form, the implementation of the administration and contractual tasks in relation to project start, with a Kick-off Meeting in Seville (Spain), coinciding with the Launching Seminar. The first 2 months of the project were devoted to the contractualisation process (Grant Agreement negotiation signature) between the INTERREG EURO-MED Secretariat and the Lead Partner organisation (CCSEV) and also the signature of the Partnership Agreement with all the partners. In March 2024, the Kick-off Meeting was celebrated in Seville (Spain) together with a Launching Seminar, on 12th and 13th of March 2024. Meanwhile, the Launching Seminar gathered several stakeholders, including business organisations, SMEs from the interested sectors (tourism, agri-food, health, etc.) and public organisation, to which the project was presented and the objectives and approaches disussed. Furthermore, concerning the WP1: Raise awareness of the MDG strategy (MD Branding) in partner regions, all partners gathered data in a methodological manner. This collaborative effort aims to build a comprehensive database or catalogue of best practices, specifically focusing on the "MD circuit" strategy and common policies to increase tourism opportunities (A 1.1 Highlighting the "MD circuit" strategy and common Policy). This database is intended to identify models, indicators and criteria enhancing tourism quality based on a wide range of best practices and successful strategies selected through a comparative analysis of previous cooperation projects, thereby enhancing the MD.net existing knowledge map and digital tools. By compiling these insights, the partners are creating a robust framework that not only highlights effective approaches but also fosters the sharing of knowledge across different regions. In addition to this foundation work, all partners have initiated the investigation of promoting success stories that can effectively ilustrate the impact of the MDG strategy. By showcasting these success stories, the aim is to engage and enlist the stakeholders and policymakers, thereby increasing the visibility and understanding of the MDG initiatives. The promotion of these success stories will be an integral part of the activities within WP3 (A 1.2 Promoting success stories), where efforts will be made to disseminate these narratives widely, highlight the tangible benefits realised in the MDG local communities through the implementation of the MDG participatory approaches, transfer this experience into measures and recommendations for mainstreaming pathways. Additionally, with regard to WP2: Promote the capitalisation of MD.net high-quality results, coinciding with the Launching Seminar in Seville on the 12th-13th of March 2024, the partnership together contributed to the successful implementation of a Living Lab making use of the MD.net past experience, with exchange and discussion with regional stakeholders. Moreover, all partners have shown a strong commitment to identifying potential stakeholders within their respective regions. This transnational, proactive approach is essential for ensuring that the outcomes of the MD.net initiative resonate with local needs and contexts. As part of this, the partners have been working on the preparation of the first drafts for key activities, specifically A 2.1: Drawing up tourism strategies having impact in rural economies (living-labs) and A 2.2: Tourism sectors modernisation (one-stop-shops) for the Second Steering Committee Meeting on the 19th of June 2024, held online. During this meeting, partners reviewed the progress made and refined these strategies, ensuring they are well-aligned with the overaching goals of the project. During this period, the internal communication has been through emails, phone calls, videoconferences every 2 months and the Steering Committee Meetings. External Communications has also started through the partners' website and social networks, and the project website through the INTERREG EURO-MED Platform. The partnership was also present in a Thematic Community Event in Rome (Italy), the 9th and 10th of April 2024, where MedDiet Go was presented to the rest of the projects' representatives, information from other projects in the Community was received and found possible connections among some projects' contents and relations to be cultivated during the project work. - The Chamber of Commerce of Seville, as lead partner, organised the kick-off meeting in Seville (Spain) on 12-13 March 2024. The two-day event included the Partnership and Steering Committee meeting, as well as the MedDiet Go launch seminar, which was integrated into the EcologicaKOK gastronomy event, focusing on sustainable and organic gastronomy, with the participation of all relevant stakeholders in the region. The event was held in facilities shared by the Seville Chamber of Commerce and in the Municipality of Seville. The project was announced to local stakeholders in the morning, and the detailed presentation of the project took place in the afternoon during the "Mediterranean Diet and Sustainable Tourism" session, which was entirely dedicated to MedDiet Go and the exchange with local and international stakeholders. - The Chamber was also present in the Event of the Thematic Community of the Sustainable Tourism Mission (C4T) held in Rome (Italy), the 9-10 April 2024, where MedDiet Go was presented to the rest of the projects’ representatives, information from other projects in the Community was received and found possible connections among some projects’ contents and relations to be cultivated during the project work. In particular, projects of particular interest for MDGs’ objectives were identified at that time: Libeccio for digital tools, Tourism for the methodologies of construction of niche tourism routes and Med Giahs for its connection with FAO strategies that also characterise our project. - Moreover, the Seville Chamber of Commerce actively participated in and organised the 3rd Steering Committee meeting in Sofia (Bulgaria) on 12 November 2024, coinciding with the visit to the Food Exhibition that took place in Sofia that day. - Signing of the Grant Agreement with the Managing Authority of the Interreg Euro-MED Programme and the signature of the Partnership Agreement with all the partners. - During this period, the internal communication has been through emails, phone calls, videoconferences every 2 months and the Steering Committee meetings. - Preparation, organisation and management of the online meetings to coordinate the project activities among all partners during the first year of implementation. There have been four official meetings of the Consortium so far. - External communication was also carried out via the partners' websites and social networks, and the project website via the INTERREG EURO-MED platform. - Preparation of the first partner report by all the Consortium partners. - All partners have shown a strong commitment to identifying potential stakeholders within their respective regions. During the partnership meetings, many reflections were devoted to the issue of identification and selection of local actors, also in view of the organisation of the Living Labs. The analysis of previous projects and good practices (WP1) served as a knowledge base for the improvement of methodologies and tools. This transnational, proactive approach is essential for ensuring that the outcomes of the MD.net initiative resonate with local needs and contexts. - The Seville Chamber of Commerce has also participated in three online bilateral meetings with governance projects. The MedDiet Go project was also presented at the International Startup Congress 2024, held in Seville on 10 December. These are the main specific objectives that have been achieved, along with the key outputs delivered. - The development of the MedDiet Go Platform. This semester, the platform has been finalised to include information about the International Eurocluster, its areas of work, and content about the Mediterranean diet. The platform is practically finished, and the final version will be ready in the last semester to make it more engaging and visually appealing for users. - The implementation of Living Labs and One-Stop Shops in the seven Mediterranean countries involved in the project. The design of the Living Labs and One-Stop Shops was completed in the previous semester. This semester has been devoted entirely to implementing the Living Labs and One-Stop Shops in the seven regions involved in the project. Each partner has set up Living Labs in their regions, involving SMEs, local and regional authorities, business support organisations and public and private associations, to promote the Mediterranean diet and its values, and to address current topics and issues in their specific area that relate to the objectives pursued by MedDiet Go. The OSS have also been implemented in the Mediterranean regions involved in the project (Portugal, Spain, Italy, Slovenia, Greece and Bulgaria). Their main aim is to support local businesses in promoting their products abroad and connecting them with businesses in other regions. - Organising field visits to partner regions. On this occasion, field visits and project-related activities, such as Living Labs and awareness-raising events, were held in the Alandroal region of Portugal. The Consortium seized the opportunity to attend the River Festival in March 2025 and participate in the kick-off workshop and field visits in Portugal. The partners visited local Portuguese producers involved in regenerative agriculture and the promotion of Mediterranean values. This event was held in person, providing an opportunity for the Steering Committee to meet face-to-face. - Participation in thematic projects The Seville Chamber of Commerce has participated in various online events to present the MedDiet Go project, and it is planned that all the partners will continue to participate in this kind of event as part of Work Package 3 (WP3). This will foster possible synergies with other projects focusing on similar themes, such as sustainability, tourism, cultural heritage, the Mediterranean diet and sustainable agriculture.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal Moving towards a more healthy and sustainable EU food system, is a cornerstone of the European Green Deal. The MD is a model of a sustainable food system, as it acts to get healthy, affordable food, protects the environment, preserves biodiversity and landscape. To make this model possible, it is necessary that the economic return is fair to those who are working in the sector. The intersection between Agriculture and Tourism is a fair strategy, coping with desertification and depopulation of rural areas. MDG deals with the EU Green Deal strategy boosting the development of EU organic farming areas with the aim to achieve 25% of total farmland under organic farming by 2030. Organic farming is an environmentally-friendly practice that can have a strong impact on the international tourism market as it intersects the MD production and health/well-being tourism. The food labelling promoted by the MDG (Med Diet Declaration Brand developing a labelling framework that covers nutrition, territory, landscape and social aspects) will empower the MD promotion strategy towards consumers who want to choose healthy, environmental-friendly and sustainable tourism and diet. Knowledge transfer will be essential: MDG will share research and innovation findings from former works related to food, natural resources, agriculture, territorial cultural heritage that will be instrumental in assisting the partners’ local communities in understanding the opportunities of the transition.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 As stated in the Territorial Agenda, MDG underlines the importance of, and supports integrated actions for, strengthening the territorial dimension of sectoral policies in rural contexts. Thus, MDG helps achieve Sustainable Development Goals; its strategy is based on fostering the common understanding that a sustainable and resilient growth needs to be place-based: the MD is a fair model for it. Furthermore, MDG follows the path of the TA in the applied methodology: cooperation and coordination between different/although similar places, involving the different levels of territorial key players, integrating policy sectors and involving local communities are the main purposes of the project, to address complex issues and utilise diverse potential. MDG will investigate the practices developed under the Territorial Agenda priorities. The report ‘Implementing the Territorial Agenda 2030: Examples for a territorial approach in policy design and delivery‘ highlights 52 previous or existing projects, initiatives and instruments that pursue a territorial approach. These actions, increasing recognition of the importance of place-based policies, can mirror and give hints to the MDG partnership showing how the territorial dimension can be actively addressed. Nevertheless, the transfer of similar actions carried out by the 13 MD.net living labs can be an optimal example of the need of vertical and horizontal coordination to design and deliver integrated development strategies at local levels. In line with the TA, these different methodological approaches will drive innovative solutions to common challenges and unlock new potentials in the partners’ territories.
    • Other EU strategy for sustainable tourism: Tourism is a cross-cutting economic activity with a wide-ranging impact on the environment and climate and on the EU regions’ economic growth, employment, social and sustainable development. According to the European Parliament resolution of 25 March 2021 on establishing an EU strategy for sustainable tourism (2020/2038(INI)), in 2018 around 67 % of EU tourist accommodation establishments were in urban and coastal areas, while 33 % of them were in rural areas, because of their remoteness, lack of transport and tourism services. The COVID-19 pandemic strongly affected the tourism industry while introducing a new trend in international tourism, that started to choose rural destinations in response to pandemic danger.
    • Also in MDG rural regions tourism can help to promote a balanced territorial pattern, with a positive impact on their less developed economies; furthermore, the focus on MD values can contribute to the conservation of biodiversity, social welfare, and landscape. MDG promotes a tourism chain encompassing cultural, traditional MD values which can represent the main industry ecosystems for these territories. It is a complex strategy since it needs to make up the four closely linked key issues - mobility, accommodation, experience, intermediation. The success of this “industry of the territory” lies in the degree of improvement of these vectors. MDG activities, both through the local level Living Labs with One-stop-shops and the international Med Diet Euro Cluster, help upgrade tourism performance. The connection with experts and R&I organisations encompasses a great diversity of experiences and expertise boosting innovation in services and professionals. Further MDG contribution is in supporting micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (MSMEs) of the tourism sector, whose activities generate employment and resilience in rural economies.
