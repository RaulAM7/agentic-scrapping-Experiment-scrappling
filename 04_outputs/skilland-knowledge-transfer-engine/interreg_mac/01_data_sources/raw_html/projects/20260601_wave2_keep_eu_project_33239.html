Connecting LOcal proximity networks Sustaining participatory govERnance

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 3 - Governance Projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 173. Enhancing institutional capacity of public authorities and stakeholders to implement territorial cooperation projects and initiatives in a cross-border, transnational, maritime and inter-regional context ~ SUMMARY: The need to reinforce social serivices and social rights in the MED area is urgent and particularly relevant in rural/remote/peripheral areas, increasingly suffering from depopulation and aging, often lacking of basic services’ provision or where the access to them is most difficult, leaving the population more exposed against both systemic shocks and ordinary life-cycle risks (ILO, 2022). CLOSER aims to promote joint actions in the Mediterranean for the promotion of participatory governance at local and regional level, by: a) the creation of territorial “proximity networks” in Italy, Spain, Greece, Palestine, Jordan and Lebanon engaging local social actors – alongside with the public authorities - as relevant stakeholders that often fill the gap in the existing public service’ provision; and b) their capacity building in becoming facilitators of participatory processes of local governance for the co-design and delivery of social services aimed at improving the wellbeing of the population. The trained local networks will jointly develop a strategical model of participatory governance that will be piloted in each of the 6 participating regions, for the provision of co-design bottom-up social services with a higher level of respondence to real needs of the involved contexts, thanks to the participation of all concerned actors and a consequent closeness of the delivered services to the individuals to whom they are directed. Participatory governance facilitates cooperation between stakeholders, transparency of institutional processes, direct engagement of the local population with a consequent higher co-responsibility and ownership of the decision-processes and sense of belonging, more effective and inclusive services’ delivery and improved territorial cohesion. The final goal is to connect and build the capacity of these networks in jointly designing solutions to provide social services, planned and delivered through participatory models of governance. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Governance, partnership, Institutional cooperation and cooperation networks
