Transformative co-creation innovation labs for the Blue Sustainable Economy

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
BLUE ECOSYSTEM expérimentera des méthodologies innovantes centrées sur le soutien des processus de création de territoires via la production de connaissances dans l'économie bleue durable, en s'appuyant sur les expériences existantes réalisées par précédents projets MED ( MISTRAL, BLUE BIO MED et B-BLUE). Le partenariat solide et synergique, dirigé par Region Emilia-Romagna, garantira des résultats efficaces et des tentatives concrètes vers une économie bleue plus durable et résiliente dans la région méditerranéenne. L'approche du cadre conceptuel partagé avec le projet Dialogue4Innovation garantit un impact plus large sur le dialogue institutionnel et la gouvernance des politiques d'innovation. Le projet concevra, testera et validera: 1.un laboratoire d'innovation de co-création transformative (TRAIN labs) dans chaque région pilote : Île Baléares, Émilie-Romagne, Sud Paca-Occitanie, Comté de Zadar, Alentejo, Attique, Albanie, en cohérence avec les plans d'action régionaux sur l'économie bleue. 2.un programme BLUE ECOMATCH: un processus transnational d'innovation ouverte pour relever les défis complexes de la région MED et pour identifier les opportunités transnationales afin de fournir plus rapidement des solutions. 3.un livre sur la transformation de l'économie bleue durable: les principaux résultats et les meilleures pratiques pour l'apprentissage mutuel de l'innovation transformative.L'utilisation de la méthodologie TRAIN Labs, soutenue par des initiatives d'apprentissage mutuel telles que BLUE ECOMATCH, permettra un changement d'état d'esprit des parties prenantes de la quintuple hélice et des acteurs clés des économies bleues, en faveur d’une utilisation plus large de l'innovation, générant enfin l'adoption de modèles commerciaux plus durables dans le secteur privé, le développement de nouvelles compétences sur les marchés des secteurs bleus et contribuant à la suppression des barrières institutionnelles et culturelles qui entravent l'innovation avancée des économies bleues.

Description (EN):
BLUE ECOSYSTEM will test innovative methodologies focused on supporting territory-making processes through knowledge production in the blue sustainable economy, building on existing experiences achieved thanks to previous MED projects ( MISTRAL, Blue BIo MED and B-BLUE). The strong and complementary partnership, led by Emilia-Romagna Region, will assure effective results and concrete attempts towards a more sustainable and resilient blue economy in the Mediterranean area. Shared conceptual framework approach with Dialogue4Innovation project guarantees a wider impact on institutional dialogue and innovation policy governance. BLUE ECOSYSTEM will design, test and validate: - a Transformative co-creation innovation labs (TRAIN labs) in each pilot region: Balearic Island, Emilia-Romagna, Sud Paca-Occitanie, Zadar County, Alentejo, Attica, Albania, in coherence with regional action plans on blue economy. - a BLUE ECOMATCH program: a MED transnational open innovation process to address MED complex challenges and match, connect and identify transnational opportunities for the solution acceleration; - a Transformative Book for Blue sustainable economy, sharing the overall project approach, the main outcomes and best practices for the mutual learning about Transformative innovation. A wide use of TRAIN Lab methodology, supported by initiatives of mutual learning such as the BLUE ECOMATCH program, will make possible a change in the mindset of 5 helix stakeholders and key-players of the Blue economies, towards a wider use of co-designed innovation and cutting edge technologies, lastly generating the adoption of more sustainable business models in the private sector, the development of new skills in the blue sectors markets and contributing to the removal institutional and cultural barriers that hamper advanced innovation in blue economies.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The first six months have been dedicated to start-up activities, complying with JS requests, revising the AF, providing the undersigning SC and PA, setting up the internal operational structure of the project teams (identifying Project, Financial and Communication managers within all PP organisations) and participating to relevant events such the SC eMeeting held on line the 28th of March, setting -up the SC of the project, the KoM hosted by PP7 CMIB in Barcellona, along with the Innovation Camp organized within Dialogue4Innovation project the 30th May 2024, and arranging several emeetings to jointly develop the first project activities. In more details, AT1.1 has been launched and completed as planned, collecting info from all PPs to enable analysis and mutual learning about knowledge, practises and case studies to co-desing and co-create models related to Research and Innovation with a 5le Helix approach. In parallel, AT1.2 a preliminary collection of documents and strategies has been launched to idendify challenges and opportunities that in coming months will drive to draft regional action plans in the areas of the project to develop a Blue transformative challange matrix that is palnned to be completed in the following semester. The animation of the 5le helix stakeholders has been also launched (AT1.3) and a stakeholders engagement strategy has been developed, linking to AT3.3 to share knowledge, raise awareness and engaging all key stakeholders working in Blue sectors. The project website, along with LinkedIn have been created, as well as poster and articles have been developed and all PPs have shared info using also their institutional communication channels. Blue Ecosystem has started networking and coordinating activities with Governance project as mentioned above and in addition to it, the project has been presented in UfM event (stakeholders conference, 19-20 February 2024), Ocean Decade Conference (Barcelona, 10-12 April 2024), at the MedPAN event on 19 April 2024 (Regional Experience Sharing Workshop), WestMED / Our Ocean Conference (Athens, April 2024), the Sustainable Economy Mission on 28 and 29 May 2024, in the launch of the Community of Practice for projects/Med Innovation Day on 18 and 19 June 2024 in Marseille, WestMED Steering Committee (Online, 26 June), UfM Regional Platform on Sustainable Blue Economy (Online, 27 June). Blue Ecosystem team has achieved the 1st relevant result: an interesting methodology built-up by co-desing efforts and contributions provided by the each partner area, to set-up transformative, co-creative innovation labs. The Transformative co-creation innovation labs (BlueTRAIN labs) co-design methodology will be available and downloadable from the project website. During the 1st year of implementation, partners have actively involved quintuple helix stakeholders (industry, academia, civil society, governments and environment) to analyse territorial needs, collecting significant data to detect complex issues, defining territorial missions, exchanging and sharing knowledge within and outside the project, to plan the TRAIN labs. To reach this result, a specific stakeholders’ engagement strategy and different kind of communication activities have strongly supported the tecnical activities implementation by using effective means of communication, widespreading local/regional co-creative workshops results, to inform and reaise awareness towards all those stakeholders committed in Blue Economy sectors, widening opportunities of synergies and collaborations beyond the duration of each activity implemented so far. A preliminary analysis of co-design and co-creation models in the framework of RRI, exploring practices and case studies at project level area has been carried out thanks to the support and contributions provided by all project partners. Similarly, common design principles for codesign and co-creation activities have been identified and mutual learning process has been carried out to understand capacities and competences to design the transformative innovation labs. In addition to that, challenges and opportunities in the Blue Economy sectors have feed a priority matrix, collecting also the definition of the Blue Transformation challenges at the Mediterranean level, together with a 1st round of co-creative workshops implementation, to co-design transformative portfolio of missions, that will feed the work of the TRAIN Labs, equipping the Mediterranean Blue Economy to strategically address future challenges. Many meeting have been arranged online, reducing carbon emissions, and two in person partner meetings have been hosted one in Barcelona (28-30 May 2024 -Spain) alligned with the Innovation camp organized within the Governance project "Dialogue 4 Innovation) and later on, one in Sines (15-16 October 2024 -Portugal). Furthermore, Blue Ecosystem has been on the stage, involved in several relevant events such as the CPMR Intermediterranean Commission General Assembly (3 July 2024, online), CPMR Intermediterranean Commission Task Force on Cultural and Maritime Sustainable Tourism (20 November 2024, Almeria, Spain), Forum Mondial de la Mer (13 September 2024, Bizerte, Tunisia), European MSP Week (22-24 October 2024, Marseille, France), Sun&Blue Congress (20-22 November 2024, Almeria, Spain), WESTMED Technical group on sustainable tourism (21 November 2024, Almeria, Spain) Interreg Community of Practice Innovation (14 November 2024, online). Participation in the Euro-Med Italy Forum in Florence on 21st November 2024, dedicated to partners, regional and national authorities, programme authorities, and various stakeholders involved in the Euro-MED world. Similarly, the project has been also linked with Euro-MED organizations and other MED initiatives, especially with Dialogue4Innovation project, Blue Misson Med for the ISEC Hub, PREP4BLUE, WESTMED and UfM’s works. Networking sessions have been also arranged with the partners of the Blue Mission Med and EU Mission: restore our Ocean and Waters projects on 6 November 2024 (during the Ecomondo Fair- Rimini Italy, as well as participating to the "Shaping Sustainable Innovation in the Mediterranean" event in Marseille. Next steps foreseen will be the design and launch of calls, to start implementing accelleration processes and next destination to reach will be Albania with new initiatives that will be announced very soon! In the semester concerned, all PPs have actively participated in the meetings arranged online, to guarantee a smooth and well-integrated development and implementation of the actions foreseen. Similarly, several meetings have been arranged, with specific focus on Communication (24 January, 8 April, and 30 May) which is a relevant part of the project, supporting dissemination of technical activities, events' participation and networking. A PP meeting has taken place in person in Durres (8th-9th April 2025), hosted by PP8 and involving PPS' Associated partners, with the participation of JS. In terms of technical activities, specific efforts have been devoted in the development of the "calls for solutions" after an intense work that the partnership has put in place, identifying specific territorial challenges in the driving strategic blue sectors selected in the previous semester. A deep analysis has driven to shape the elaboration and publication of calls, supported by territorial meetings whereas key stakeholders of the quintuple helix have been involved and whose contributions have been collected. These activities have enriched numbers and typology of stakeholders involved (AT1.3) and have launched the basis for the acceleration process within the TRAIN LABS together with the selection of solutions that are foreseen in period 4, 5 and 6. The partnership has agreed to have a PP meeting in Lanarca (Cyprus) the 8th and 9th of July, to better monitor and exchange information about the outcomes of the publications of the calls. The activities implemented so far, have enabled to gain important elements to access step by step the outcomes, also in relation to S3 (AT3.1, AT3.2) carrying on also the networking via the "Blue Task Force" presented at Durres meeting, and involving Interreg Euro-MED Innovative Sustainable Economy Mission, the Horizon Europe BlueMissionMed project, with the support of the IMC (AT3.3, AT3.4). Blue Ecosystem has been presented in several events: 18th February 2025 "INFORMAL GROUP ON THE BLUE DIMENSION OF S3" that has driven to deliver a Policy paper for an impactful S3 (involving RER and CPRM), 3rd Community of Practice (COP) of the ISE Mission in Sarajevo (April 2025), and the Mediterranean Day of the United Nations Ocean Conference (UNOC) held in Nice (June 2025) involving PMM-TVT, CPRM and HCMR, AZA4ICE project during the Circle the Med Forum in Athens (June 4, 2025) and during the European Bioremediation Forum in Chania, Greece (June 15–19, 2025), held under the auspices of the EU Mission “Restore our Ocean and Waters” and DG RTD OCEAN, involving HCMR. Also, for the period concerned, data about the carbon footprint have been duly collected (by CPRM) and made available to all PPs.

As foreseen by the Green Deal, the project promotes economic growth by creating new businesses and job opportunities, with a specific focus in the Blue economy and with a strong approach towards sustainability, keeping, as minimum requirements for elaboration of transformative innovation solutions the saving materials’ cost, the improving of security of supply, while at the same time, the reduction of environmental pressures and impacts. The transition to a sustainable blue economy, envisaged by the EU Green Deal also by the communication for “new approach for a sustainable blue economy in the EU” (COM/2021/240 final) is factually promoted by BLUE ECOSYSTEM by providing methodologies and approaches that are able to support more competitive and factual investments in innovative technologies and solutions, that will create new green jobs and businesses in the blue economy. The project, in fact, assumes as central the concept that all blue economy sectors and emergences, affected by the same project, should innovate to reduce their environmental and climate impact, providing a factual answers to tackle the climate and biodiversity crises and to increase the healthiness of seas and a sustainable use of their resources, to create alternatives to fossil fuels and traditional food production.

Through the support offered for the development of transformative innovation solutions, the BluEcosystem project, in fact, contributes factually to the: A) SDG 7 on energy by paving the way for innovative solution allowing the exploitation of alternative energy sources; B) SDG 8 on economic growth, by setting framework condition for economic actors to develop new business and jobs; C) SDG9 on industry, innovation and infrastructure, by paving the way for new methodology and approaches for the establishment of an proper innovation context; D) SDG11 on sustainable cities and communities and specifically to its first application within the transformative Agendas for urban city that are under implementation in several city of the EuroMED area; SDG 12 on sustainable consumption and production, by supporting transformative innovation solutions able to reduce the impact of production and involve consumers on strategic chooses; E) SDG 13 on climate change, by promoting low impact solutions and mitigation measures; F) SDG 14 on oceans, being focussed on blue economies sector for the increase of their sustainability.
