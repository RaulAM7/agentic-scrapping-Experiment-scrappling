MEDiterranean COllaborative LOgistics for the URban Space

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Climate change is the foremost worldwide challenge. Some of the most pressing factors include congestion, which costs the European economy €100 billion per year- 1% of its GDP, air pollution, affecting people’s health, and transport emissions (freight counts 25% of total urban transport emissions). The EU Green Deal targets to reduce emissions in cities by 55% by 2030 and be climate-neutral by 2050 cannot be met only by vehicles decarbonisation goals. MED COLOURS aims at upscaling to a new generation of urban logistics and planning enabling the transition to decarbonised and smart cities. By developing new Sustainable Urban Logistics Plans (SULPs), MED Cities will plan resilient, sustainable, integrated, and collaborative innovation-driven solutions for the Functional Urban Areas (FUAs). This would help reducing the negative impacts of freight and logistics activities. MED COLOURS’ joint territorial ambition is to enable urban freight distribution in MED-FUAs from a low carbon environmental perspective and, at the same time, give cities a boost in sustainable development and human well-being. MED COLOURS will jointly validate 1) strategy for continuous cross-monitoring of SULPs 2) New Resilient SULPs and 3) solutions for innovative and sustainable urban logistics. This would foster the more effective implementation of existing sustainable policies and improve access to funds for climate-resilient living areas. MED COLOURS supports the digital and environmental transition in six MED cities (Livorno, Cesena, Thessaloniki, Koper, Lisbon, and Lyon) and their related FUAs . Transition is built upon a quadruple helix approach supported by the liaison with other European Projects in the same field. MED COLOURS will adopt a new planning approach introducing new collaborative concepts and disruptive/enabling technologies. The project outcomes will go beyond existing practices through long-term transition scenarios to achieve the targets set in the EU green Deal in MED Area.

Actual Achievements (EN):
Overall, the implementation of MED COLOURS activities proceed according to schedule. After a slow start due to the articulated contracting phase, activities kicked off smoothly with a Virtual Kick-off meeting held online on March 20th 2024. During Period 1 activities concentrated particularly on Activity 1.1 "Legacy, capitalisation and adaptation of background knowledge/experiences to SULPs/needs of COLOURS urban areas", Activity 3.1 "Coordination with MED TCP and other EU Projects for building synergies", Activity 3.2 "Coordination with IDP and other Initiatives for capacity development", Activity 3.3 "Monitoring of COLOURS impacts". In the context of A.1.1 project partners produced D.1.1.1 "COLOURS SULPs benchmark". The MED COLOURS project has made significant progress across multiple work packages, achieving key objectives and delivering impactful outputs that contribute to sustainable urban logistics in MED COLOURS cities (Livorno, Cesena, Thessaloniki, Koper, Lisbon, and Lyon). In Period 1, in the context of WP1, Activity A1.1 produced an initial benchmark study comparing urban logistics policies and needs of MED COLOURS cities with best practices derived from major EU projects and initiatives. This research established a foundational understanding of the current context and successful practices, which informed the development of a common framework for sustainable and resilient urban logistics planning. By Period 2, the framework design entered its core phase under A1.2, laying the groundwork for a harmonized approach among partner cities towards joint methodologies and tools for sustainable resilient urban logistics planning. Activity A2.1 launched during Period 2, led by ZLC with support from ITL, focused on feasibility studies for the pilot actions. Collaborative efforts between pilot cities and knowledge partners evaluated the financial, technical, social, and policy feasibility of the planned pilot activities to be implemented in Periods 3 and 4 (A2.2). This phase ensured that pilot actions are well-prepared, actionable, and tailored to local contexts while maintaining alignment with overarching project goals. In the context of WP3, the MED COLOURS consortium set the basis for a strong engagement with Green Living Areas Mission and other EU-funded projects. The consortium established meaningful collaborations with the Green Living Areas (GLA) Mission and its governance projects, fostering synergies and knowledge exchange. Representatives from GLA were invited to the MED COLOURS Bologna meeting (June 5-6, 2024), facilitating direct interaction and collaboration. MED COLOURS partners actively participated in key GLA Mission events, including in-presence events such as the kick-off meeting in Turin (April 2024) and the Green Mobility thematic working group (June 2024). External visibility was further strengthened by presenting MED COLOURS at international events such as the 8th Danube Region Transport Days 2024 and the Urban Logistics Innovation Day 2024 in Brussels. Further, the consortium mapped and initiated collaboration with relevant projects to enhance the project’s impact and scalability. Regular engagement on platforms such as Basecamp ensured continuous dialogue and updates with other EU projects, fostering a dynamic exchange of ideas and best practices. The cooperative nature of MED COLOURS has been instrumental in knowldge sharing and cross-city learning. Collaboration among partner cities has fostered the adaptation of best practices to diverse local context. Close cooperation with knowledge partners like ZLC, ITL, Umaribor, MemEx, CERTH, and Lisboa E-Nova has facilitated the informed design of pilot actions and will be increasingly valuable throught the project implementation. Indeed, active participation in EU-level initiatives has positioned MED COLOURS as a key contributor to sustainable urban logistics discussions. In short, MED COLOURS has made significant strides in benchmarking, framework development, feasibility assessments, and stakeholder engagement. The project’s achievements reflect a shared commitment to fostering sustainable and resilient urban logistics across MED COLOURS cities, creating a model for replicability and long-term impact. Overall, the implementation of MED COLOURS activities proceeds according to schedule. The engagement levels of the project partners is high and a collaborative approach is fostered by the LP throught the regular monthly meetings and the WP coordination meetings. The MED COLOURS Consortium Meeting & Steering Committee held in Lyon on the 15th and 16th May 2025 marked a significant mid-term milestone, renewing PPs energies and commitment to the project. Significant progress has been made in the development of joint methodologies and tools for sustainable resilient urban logistics planning, thanks to the leadership of PP3 CERTH and the other knowledge partners of the consortium. Following the fine-tuninig of the feasibility study and preparatory activities, pilot activities have been clearly defined (and pilot factsheets produced for each city) and works underway. Under the leadership of PP6 MemEx, a monitoring plan has been developed and implemented to ensure a smooth implementation of the solutions and the collection of data, organizing also a risk analysis and two rounds of peer-review workshops which each city and knowledge partners. Finally, the MED COLOURS Consortium has been very active in creating synergies with other projects and promoting institutional dialogue. The Consortium has joined the ALICE Liason Programme, which provides support to project dissemination through ALICE communication channels and grant free access for MED COLOURS partners to ​the ALICE Knowledge Platform. In the context of WP3, channels of communication and exchanges have been opened with other urban logistics projects and stakeholders on innovative solutions, enabling technologies, future trends in urban logistics.

It aims to develop novel last-mile delivery solutions combining green automated vehicles and shared space utilisation models. Leading European cities, research experts and knowledge partners, solution providers for shared, connected, and automated operations, and industry leaders in logistics services kicked off the project in September 2022.
  • Due to the e-commerce and the crisis of the last years, including covid, Europe urgently needs to identify and scale-up novel last-mile transportation solutions. The last mile deliveries is the portion of the transport that produces the highest emissions, and more demand causes this.
  • URBANE aims to tackle the negative trends associated with the pressure on last-mile deliveries. Its ambitions are in line with the objectives of the European Green Deal, the Paris Agreement, and the Green City Accord, towards the fast adoption of zero-emission urban logistics solutions by 2030, achieving improved efficiency of road transport and logistics systems.
  • Considering the timeframe of the URBANE project (started in September 2022, and lasting for 42 months) the synergies with MED COLOURS are enormous. First of all 2 partners (ITL and CERTH) are WP Leaders in Urbane and can ease the communications among the two projects. On the other side, URBANE can be used as guidance from the technological point of view, while MED COLOURS can have the role to amplify the results and apply similar concepts in other areas, working especially on the institutional and governance level.
