Dialogue4Tourism. Institutional dialogue on Sustainable Tourism and Governance in the Euro-Med area

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Le manque de coopération entre les autorités publiques et les décideurs, le manque de dialogue multisectoriel entre les parties prenantes du tourisme en matière de durabilité, la saisonnalité, le changement climatique, le faible niveau de digitalisation, sont quelques-uns des principaux défis auxquels le secteur doit faire face. Ces défis peuvent être résolus, qu’en utilisant une approche intersectorielle, et qu’en créant des structures de dialogue innovantes et durables entre les décideurs. L’objectif global du projet vise à créer un schéma de collaboration entre les parties prenantes du tourisme de la zone et au-delà (autorités politiques, universités, secteur privé…). La multiplication des interdépendances entre les domaines d’action permet de créer des synergies et d’adopter une approche plus systémique pour définir et relever les défis. L’objectif est de renforcer la durabilité des politiques touristiques en améliorant le niveau de coordination et la capacité institutionnelle des autorités publiques, et en atteignant les organismes, les programmes, les stratégies et initiatives de plusieurs niveaux. Le projet de dialogue institutionnel travaillera main dans la main avec le projet de communauté thématique de la mission 4, en participant au travail visant à guider les projets thématiques dans la conception de stratégies pouvant produire des recommandations politiques transférables. D’une part GOV4MED co-construira, avec l’ensemble de la communauté du tourisme durable, des résultats, des stratégies et des politiques publiques transférables, et, d’autre part il garantira que ces résultats atteignent d’importantes parties prenantes et institutions décisionnelles afin qu’ils soient intégrés dans les politiques régionales, nationales et internationales. L’ambition est de diffuser la vision unique du Programme, un tourisme équitable et responsable pour tous, dans plus de territoires, dans plus d’organes décisionnels, en façonnant l’avenir du tourisme dans la zone.

Description (EN):
Tourism urges to overcome a great range of challenges, including lack of solid cooperation among Euro-MED public authorities and policy makers, lack of multidisciplinary and multi sectoral dialogue of tourism related stakeholders for sustainability, overtourism, seasonality, climate change, crises like Covid-19, low level of digitalization, etc. To effectively address these challenges a shift of paradigm in governance -towards increased collaboration among levels, sectors and territories- is required. The pathway to tourism transformation requires increased capacities, institutional dialogue, improved and more effective collaboration and coordination mechanisms. Policy-makers are at the heart of the transition. The overall objective of Dialogue4Tourism is to increase the coordination level and institutional capacity of public authorities, multi-level bodies, programmes, strategies and initiatives in the Euro-MED, aiming to transform tourism into greener, smarter and more resilient, embracing the fourfold approach of the mission: circularity of tourism services, environmental neutrality, sustainable ecosystem services, and cultural and natural preservation of resources. To achieve its goals, Dialogue4Tourism brings together 10 PPs and 25 APs and collaborates closely with Community4Tourism Project, the JS and the other Programme Missions. Dialogue4Tourism establishes cooperation and advocacy mechanisms: Driving Tourism Transition Panel and Hub, Network of Sustainable Tourism Observatories deriving from BESTMED, the cross-synergies alliance and the Tourism Leaders Summit. Sustainable Tourism Policy Labs (STPLs) will be organised. A robust advocacy strategy will be deployed, to bring Mediterranean Tourism in the top of the policy-leaders agendas. Contribution to high-level and high profile events is ensured. Dialogue4Tourism is expected to contribute to Mission#4 4 SOs, Green Deal, UfM Greener Med Agenda & Ministerial Declaration on Sustainable Blue Economy, Mediterranean Strategy for Sustainable Development etc.

Actual Achievements (FR):
We have devoted this first period to create a global action strategy and project procedures for a multilevel and paralel coordination in different axes: internal of the partnership, coordination with the JS, coordination in the framework of the Sustainable Tourism Mission together with C4T Project, coordination with the PAs and coordination with external actors to ensure transferability and mainstreaming in the whole future process, as is the case of UfM and the contacts initiated with Unit 3 of DG Mare. We believe that it is important from the beginning to articulate a permanent structure of institutional dialogue and collaboration within different governance levels and different territories, to build up collaboration mechanisms and models, synergies and to build capacity to policy-makers to serve this goal, alongside advocacy activities. In addition, in order to take further steps in this period, the internal Statutes and Rules of Procedure of the project have been signed by all partners immediately after the signature of the Subsidy Contract and the corresponding Partnership Agreement. We think that the most feasible way of acting was to spend the necessary time at the beginning of the project to develop the appropriate mechanisms such as the foreseen documents in the Application Form already delivered: MoU with C4T project, the work plan updated with the template provided by the JS, the first document made for the coordination of internal communication processes in common with C4T Project, the calendar of common actions of the Mission, the internal meetings of the project, the meetings together with C4T for coordination of the Mission and the meetings with the JS; D4T has provided with contributions and feedback in all meetings participating and have studied the best and most feasible way to carry out the actions taking into account the objectives, target groups and outputs to be achieved in the project, the suggestions made by the JS and the permanent open dialogue with C4T Project and external actors. In this sense, we reached the Southern Mediterranean stakeholders through the participation and presentation of the project in a first meeting with this policy level in the 4th UfM Working Group on Environment and Climate Action and the presentation of all Missions of Interreg Euro-MED to this 4th Working Group that took place in Sitges in 05-23. Also, in the collaboration with external actors, we must also highlight the participation of the project partners in the event "Towards a more sustainable tourism in the Mediterranean - 3 Interreg programmes joining forces" in Florence 05-23 where our partners participated in all sessions and did the wrap up of one of the pannels. Concerning this, we have begun to lay the groundwork to achieve the Output 1.1. "Collaborative networks reinforcing the dialogue on sustainable tourism" and to establish the basis for other deliverables as the "Cross Network Alliance for governance in sustainable tourism and related policy recommendations". The organization of the KoM in Athens, in which all partners participated (1 and 2-03-23) and in which the first steps for the establishment of the internal operating strategy were taken, was very useful for the internal coordination of the partnership in an effective way.
