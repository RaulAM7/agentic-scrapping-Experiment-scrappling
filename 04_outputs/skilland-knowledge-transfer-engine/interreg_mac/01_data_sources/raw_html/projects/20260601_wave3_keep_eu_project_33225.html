Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 021. SME business development and internationalisation, including productive investments ~ SUMMARY: Three years ago in a rural village coffeeshop, a group of young people expressed despair over the lack of job opportunities & contemplated emigration. This scene reflects a common rural challenge: sustaining businesses & creating jobs remains difficult. MSMEs particularly struggle in industrialized markets due to their small scale, remoteness, limited innovation & weak identity. This economic fragility not only threatens local livelihoods but also contributes to depopulation & environmental degradation exacerbated by unsustainable agricultural practices. Recognizing this shared plight across the EuroMed region, we successfully completed the MedBEESinessHubs project to support local communities in Cyprus, Egypt, Italy, Lebanon & Palestine using the honeybee as a catalyst for prosperity. Our established partnership, now extended to Jordan, aims to leverage Bee Economy for rural revitalization, community empowerment & proactive environmental stewardship, using differentiation techniques & enhancing the potential offered by Apitourism. We envision returning to that same coffeeshop, witnessing the smiles of those we've assisted, while embracing the broader Bee Economy Concept. This vision encapsulated by the PLAN-BEE acronym responds to the urgent signal of declining bee populations worldwide—this is the only Plan B for our planet. Under ‘beeTRAINED’ we use mixed reality tools to attract young people & women into the beekeeping world, existing beekeepers are upskilled with sustainable beekeeping practices, bee-entrepreneurs are offered customised professional services. ‘beeDIFFERENT’ emphasises differentiation: the MED Queen Bee branding highlights female bee entrepreneurs, smart beehives encourage innovation & promote authentic, flora-based honey. Under ‘beeCONNECTED’ 6 regional offices are established to support new & existing HoneyBeeRoutes developed under MedBEESinessHubs, expanding Apitourism to even higher levels on the global stage. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Agriculture and fisheries and forestry, SME and entrepreneurship, Tourism

(EN), R-ITINERA Enhancing Ecotourism ITINeraries in Network to increase the competitiveness of SMEs (Italy-France Maritime Interreg Program) (EN) | The R-ItinERA project focused on enhancing ecosustainable tourism itineraries across 5 regions of the Mediterranean area: Sardinia, Tuscany, Liguria (in Italy); Var and Corsica (in France). Initially designed for cruise tourists, these itineraries aim to boost SME competitiveness and contribute to sustainable development.
  • The methodology used for developing the ecotourism itineraries is based on the following key-factors:
  • a) Stakeholder Engagement: The process began by engaging relevant stakeholders, including local authorities, tourism boards, environmental organizations, and SMEs.
  • b) Mapping and Assessment: The project team conducted a comprehensive mapping exercise to identify ecotourism resources (e.g., trails, viewpoints, historical sites). Sites were ranked based on their suitability for inclusion in itineraries.
  • c) Route Design and Optimization: Using Geographic Information Systems (GIS), the team designed ecotourism routes connecting key sites. Routes were optimized for sustainability, minimizing environmental impact (e.g., avoiding fragile ecosystems).
  • d) Thematic Itineraries: The team developed thematic itineraries based on specific interests (e.g., bird-watching, historical landmarks, culinary experiences). Local communities were involved in shaping thematic content.
  • e) Business Network Integration: SMEs (e.g., local guides, restaurants, accommodations) were engaged to participate in the itineraries. Businesses received training on sustainable practices and storytelling.
  • f) Digital Platform and Promotion: A digital platform (website or app) was developed to showcase the itineraries.
  • Led by PP5 CSPI (R-ITINERA parner), PPs will upscale the methodology within WP5 for developing the PLAN-BEE HoneyBeeRoutes. These routes aim to extend the network of regional honeybee eco routes, coaching SMEs in sustainable practices. (EN), BE-HIVE Smart beehives and camera traps for bees and wasps (CYENS research centre funding) (EN) | "Smart Beehive Monitoring System" https://superworld.cyens.org.cy/project16.html): The Be-Hive project aimed to develop a multi-modal sensing system, which uses multiple sensors to collect a range of different types of data, employing various AI-based techniques to analyze and observe the bee colony’s health in real-time. The proposed system constitutes a low-cost, DIY solution, which allows beekeepers to understand the well-being of their bee colonies, be notified about the current internal and external threats to the colony in real-time, and reduce human labour by facilitating remote observations and analytic. LP CCCI and PP1 FILAGRO contacted the developers of the smart beehive system in Cyprus and confirmed the potential of this technology to record pollen types entering the hive. This serves as an authenticity signal of the flora/plants, enabling the identification of the pollen sources. The smart beehives will be part of the PLAN-BEE pilot stage and study visit in Cyprus, where project partners (PPs) and their beneficiaries will explore the potential for developing flora-specific honey. This initiative aims to support beekeepers in their efforts to achieve added value through product differentiation, promoting long-term economic sustainability within the Bee Economy concept. (EN)

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other The Bee Economy concept proposed to be enhanced within PLAN-BEE builds on the foundation established by the MedBEESinessHubs project, which in turn leveraged several Interreg projects. This vertical synergy demonstrates the commitment of partners to achieving sustainable economic growth centered around the honeybee, particularly benefiting deprived rural populations, including women and youth.
    • The bee economy concept for rural development is grounded in three key horizontal principles:
    • a) Compliance with the "do no significant harm" principle: By focusing on the honeybee and biodiversity, the project ensures that economic activities do not adversely impact the environment.
    • b) Promotion of sustainable development: A bee-driven economy thrives only when local ecosystems are well-preserved, making environmental sustainability essential.
    • c) Promotion of gender equality: Supporting part-time women beekeepers helps improve family incomes and aligns with the bee economy concept, promoting gender equality.
    • The project aligns with and complements several major strategies and policies, including: (1) The European Green Deal; (2) The United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (Agenda 2030); (3) The Union for the Mediterranean's '2030 Greener Med Agenda'; (4) The Mediterranean Strategy for Sustainable Development (UN Environment Programme/Mediterranean Action Plan).
    • By integrating economic and environmental sustainability, the project proposes a new model for local economic development where these elements are interdependent. This approach reduces the need for additional governmental intervention by making environmental sustainability a fundamental aspect of economic activities, thereby supporting the implementation of the aforementioned policies and strategies effectively.
