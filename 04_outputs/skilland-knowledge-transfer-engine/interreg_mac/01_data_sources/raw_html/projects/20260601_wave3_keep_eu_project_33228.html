Mediterranean Pathway for Circular Textiles

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 075. Support to environmentally-friendly production processes and resource efficiency in SMEs ~ SUMMARY: The textile & fashion industry are notorious for its significant environmental footprint, ranking among the top sectors for negative life cycle impacts, water & land use, raw material consumption & greenhouse gas emissions. This environmental toll is felt throughout the value chain, but the design phase is especially crucial as it sets the stage for all subsequent impacts. MATRIX aims to revolutionize the fashion design process in the Mediterranean textile industry by shifting from linear to circular production. It tackles the joint challenges of environmental sustainability faced by Mediterranean fashion SMEs. By leveraging cutting-edge innovations & technologies, MATRIX will foster a new, circular Mediterranean value chain. The expected change is transformative: we will equip the fashion industry, particularly SMEs, with the tools, knowledge & awareness needed to adopt circular practices & meet the EU's high standards on circularity & emerging market trends. Our main output, the Circular Fashion Design Toolbox, will provide innovative solutions focused on 3 strategic areas: resources (water & energy), materials & digitalization. This toolbox will serve as the cornerstone of a comprehensive training program, empowering 200 SMEs & stakeholders to implement these solutions effectively. Furthermore, 90 selected SMEs will pilot these solutions, generating tangible improvements in circularity through actions such as footprint measurement, sustainability plans, new circular product prototypes using AI & digital technologies or obtaining relevant ecolabel certifications. The project will also feature study visits, peer-review exchanges, & matchmaking event connecting circular solution providers with the fashion industry. These activities will foster collaboration & knowledge sharing, culminating in the Mediterranean Pact for Circularity—a new value chain that brings together policymakers, tech centers, civil society & the industry. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Green technologies, Knowledge and technology transfer, SME and entrepreneurship

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other MATRIX broadest framework is the UN’s 2030 Agenda. It is directly related to SDGs 12, 15 & 14. SDG 12 aims to “ensure sustainable consumption & production patterns”. MATRIX develops the Mediterranean fashion industry, bringing producers & consumers closer & thus reducing transport emissions. At the same time, it spreads knowledge & create capacity on selecting materials with low environmental impact & facilitating its future circularity (e. g. design for disassembly), which directly contribute to SDGs 15 (“promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems”) & 14 (“conserve & sustainably use the oceans, seas & marine resources for sustainable development”), as eco-design guarantees the reduction of microplastics & toxic pollutants release to waters & oceans.
    • At EU level, the project’s main framework is the Green Deal. More specifically, it contributes to the EU Circular Economy Action Plan, as it reduces the EUs consumption footprint by reinforcing proximity value chain & increase its circular material use rate by creating capacity. Secondly, it facilitates the implementation of the EU Strategy for Sustainable Textiles & the Eco-design Regulation (including the digital product passport), both applying to products placed in the EU market even if produced in third countries. Also at EU level, the New Agenda for the Mediterranean will benefit by project results, as one of its cooperation areas is the green transition, with the goal of “taking advantage of the potential of a low-carbon future, protect the region's natural resources & generate green growth”. At Mediterranean level, MATRIX finds synergies with the Med. Strategy for Sustainable Development (UNEP/MAP), whose Obj 5 is to facilitate the transition towards a green & blue economy through, among others, training & capacity building. Also with the UfM Ministerial Declaration on Environment & Climate Action, which sets the commitment of accelerating transition towards sustainable, climate-neutral & green economies
