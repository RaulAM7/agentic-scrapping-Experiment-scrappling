Holistic EnvironmenTAl and Social Adaptation Framework for Extreme heat

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Les villes deviennent souvent beaucoup plus chaudes pendant les vagues de chaleur en raison des surfaces en béton, du manque d'espaces verts et de la conception urbaine. Certaines communautés sont particulièrement à risque, notamment dans les quartiers défavorisés où les habitants font déjà face à des défis sanitaires et sociaux. Pour y faire face, le projet HEATSAFE agit en Espagne, en Italie, en Albanie, en Bulgarie et en Grèce. Il aide les villes à élaborer des plans clairs pour se préparer aux vagues de chaleur et y répondre efficacement. Le projet soutient les autorités locales dans la prise de décisions éclairées grâce à des outils pratiques, tout en impliquant les habitants afin que les solutions soient adaptées aux besoins spécifiques de chaque territoire. Dans chaque zone d’intervention, HEATSAFE teste des actions concrètes telles que la création de zones fraîches accessibles au public, l’amélioration des réponses d’urgence et des campagnes de sensibilisation. Les résultats seront largement diffusés pour que d’autres villes de la Méditerranée puissent reproduire ces méthodes. En combinant science, urbanisme, santé publique et participation citoyenne, HEATSAFE vise à construire des villes plus sûres, plus saines et mieux préparées au changement climatique, pour tous.

Description (EN):
Cities often become much hotter during heatwaves because of concrete surfaces, lack of green spaces, and city design. Some communities are especially at risk, particularly in poorer neighborhoods where people may already face health and social challenges. To tackle this, HEATSAFE is taking action in Spain, Italy, Albania, Bulgaria, and Greece. The project is helping cities create clear plans to prepare for and respond to heatwaves. It supports local governments in making smart decisions using practical tools, while also involving local people to make sure solutions match the needs of each area. In each location, HEATSAFE is testing real-life improvements like cool areas for public use, better emergency responses, and education campaigns to raise awareness. The results will be shared widely so that other cities across the Mediterranean can use the same methods. By combining science, urban planning, public health, and community input, HEATSAFE aims to build safer, healthier, and more climate-ready cities for everyone.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The EU Green Deal aims to overcome climate change and environmental degradation. The EU is setting key steps for managing climate risks to protect people, and manifests the concerns on last’s year record temperatures. It calls for action from all levels of government, to improve governance and tools for climate risk owners.
    • All these lines of actions are aligned with HEATSAFE objectives: to provide a methodology and clear guidelines so governments can improve the management of risks associated with temperature increase and heatwaves. HEATSAFE will also contribute by the development of new governance multi-level structures so every actor is aligned in the process, so an effective Green Deal implementation is achieved. Moreover, green infrastructure solutions and energy efficiency measures, through buildings renovation to create climate shelters will be promoted and tested in 6 pilots with the ambition of serving as guide for other territories across the Mediterranean area.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The Territorial Agenda promotes territorial cohesion in Europe, motivated by the rise in heatwaves. It prioritizes transitioning to resilient cities and regions, supporting nature-based solutions and new crisis management tools for increased safety. Additionally, it emphasizes respecting natural limits and enhancing resilience against climate change.
    • HEATSAFE will work on this direction: developing a methodology so local authorities can elaborate specific planning to act in the face of high temperatures, testing pilot actions to mitigate their effects, monitoring and validating the approach, and increasing institutional capacity to improve the prevention and management of these risks. Project results will be disseminated and transferred to key actors so the project can directly contribute to the achievement of the strategy objectives and the bottom-up approach related to the multi-level governance approach.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The project aligns with the EU Strategy for the Adriatic and Ionian Region (EUSAIR), particularly under Pillar 3 – Environmental Quality. It supports Western Balkan integration by fostering regional cooperation and socioeconomic resilience through joint mitigation efforts to tackle climate change impacts. It also supports sustainable tourism by ensuring destinations remain safe and appealing despite climate challenges. This integrated approach enhances regional stability, economic growth, and environmental sustainability, supporting EUSAIR’s overall objectives. The presence of relevant partners from Athens (Uoa, AAK) and Lecce (CMCC), areas included in the strategy, ensure the contribution of project results in the development and implementation of the EUSAIR, especially in Pilar 3. The project results will be transferred to EUSAIR responsible bodies through target actions and a dedicated webinar will be developed for the Adriatic and Ionian Region.
    • Other The European Climate Law writes into law the goal set out in the European Green Deal for Europe’s economy and society to become climate-neutral by 2050. In its Article 5, it addresses adaptation to climate change, ensures continued progress in enhancing adaptive capacity by EU institutions and Member States, and makes it mandatory to implement national adaptation strategies and plans. The Local Action Plans developed during the project and the joint methodology produced so other municipalities can develop them, will contribute to reach the objectives of this law. In particular, HEATSAFE project will enhance the adaptative capacity of local authorities and produce guidelines for the deployment of plans and measures to adapt to heatwaves.
    • Other The European Commission adopted its new EU strategy on adaptation to climate change on 24 February 2021. The objective of this strategy focuses on moving towards a climate-neutral, climate-adapted and climate-resilient Europe by 2050 by promoting smarter, faster and, above all, more systemic adaptive capacity. It explicitly mentions heatwaves, defining them as Europe's deadliest natural disaster. It also mentions the urgency of accelerating the implementation of physical adaptive solutions, such as the creation of more green spaces, to reduce the impacts of heatwaves. The Strategy also projects the need for a deeper understanding of climate change-related health risks, including extreme heat-related deaths. Therefore, the contribution of HEATSAFE to the goals and purpose of this strategy is obvious, as it is completely aligned with its goals of adapting our territories to climate change effects. HEATSAFE will accelerate the implementation of solutions to reduce the impacts of heatwaves
    • Other The European Climate Pact is a movement of people united around a common cause, taking steps in their own worlds to build a more sustainable Europe. Individuals and organisations can take part of the Pact and contribute to climate action. HEATSAFE will engage local communities on taking climate action by framing projects events as group climate activities of the Pact and promote the practical guides of the initiative among the population. This will be a way to raise awareness of the EU Climate Pact initiative but also, the Pact website will mean a dissemination channel to communicate and disseminate the results of the project to other entities.
    • Other HEATSAFE aligns with the principles of the New European Bauhaus by promoting sustainable, inclusive, and resilient urban environments in response to rising heatwaves. The project enhances the quality of public spaces through bioclimatic interventions and nature-based solutions, contributing to a more climate-resilient built environment. It fosters inclusion by actively involving local communities and vulnerable groups in the co-design of climate shelters and adaptation strategies. While rooted in climate action, HEATSAFE also values the social and spatial quality of interventions, thus embracing the NEB vision of building spaces that are not only green and functional, but also accessible and enriching for all.
