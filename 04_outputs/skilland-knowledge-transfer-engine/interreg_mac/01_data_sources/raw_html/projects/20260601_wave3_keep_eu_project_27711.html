C4Nature

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Med regions share common challenges related to global change impacts: environmental degradation, biodiversity loss & lack of appropriate governance mechanisms. C4Nature aims to achieve coordination -among different levels- of knowledge, best practices & policies to approach the challenges of biodiversity protection and climate change mitigation/adaptation. It enhances the coordination between multi-level stakeholders such as public authorities, sectoral agencies, schools, academic & research centers, NGOs, etc. in the Med area. Concretely, C4Nature will establish a Community of Practice and deliver an Adaptive Resource recipient, a Methodology for assessing the potential of available community "resources", a set of training programmes, a portfolio of services embedded in the Community Amplification Room including a Mentorship programme addressed to specific target groups. In the Mediterranean, the current fragmentation of institutional, financial, and political arenas, as well as a lack of coordinated support, will be overcome by identifying opportunities that benefit all parties, boosting common interests and tackling current inertia towards participation in collaborative environments. Transnational cooperation is a key enabler for capitalising lessons learned from pilot cases and successful policy implementation results. The real novelties of C4Nature are: i) the "tailor- made approach" that applies to all planned activities and ii) the availability of an initial "core community" which allows a smooth identification and engagement of the target groups. According to the coupled philosophy of "resource valorization", C4Nature always exploits, reuses, reshapes the available knowledge, the existing practices and infrastructure (produced by Euro-MED thematic and strategic projects) to avoid the duplication of efforts and to amplify the results.

Description (FR):
Les régions Méd. partagent des défis communs liés aux impacts du changement global: dégradation de l'environnement, perte de biodiversité & manque de mécanismes de gouvernance appropriés. C4Nature vise à assurer la coordination -entre différents niveaux- de connaissance, meilleures pratiques et politiques pour aborder les défis de la protection de la biodiversité & de l'atténuation du changement climatique. Il améliore la coordination entre des multi-niveaux acteurs dans la Méd, tels que autorités publiques, agences sectorielles, écoles, centres de recherche, ONG, etc. Concrètement, C4Nature établira une Communauté de Pratique et fournira un récipient de Ressource Adaptative, une Méthodologie pour évaluer le potentiel des «ressources» disponibles de la Communauté, un ensemble de programmes de formation, un portefeuille de services intégrés dans la Salle de la Communauté d'Amplification, y compris un programme de Mentorat adressé à des groupes cibles spécifiques. En Méd, la fragmentation institutionnelle, financière et politique, ainsi que le manque de soutien coordonné, seront surmontés en identifiant les opportunités pour le profit de toutes parties, en renforçant les intérêts communs et en s'attaquant à l'inertie actuelle vis-à-vis de la participation dans des environnements collaboratifs. La coopération transnationale est un catalyseur clé pour capitaliser les enseignements tirés par les cas pilotes et les résultats réussis de la mise en œuvre des politiques. La vraie nouveauté du projet. est: i) "l'approche sur mesure" qui s'applique à toutes les activités prévues, et ii) la disponibilité d'une «communauté de base» initiale qui permet l'identification et l'engagement des groupes cibles. Selon la philosophie couplée de «valorisation de ressources», C4Nature exploite, réutilise, remodèle la connaissance disponible, les pratiques & les infrastructures existantes (produits par les projets Euro-MED) évitant la duplication des efforts & amplifiant les résultats.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the first year of activities, the partners spend a large part of the first 6 months for coordinating the technical team (WPs responsible partners) for finalizing the design of the overall approach and building the roadmaps for the three WPs (SHARING/REUSE, TRANSFER/MAINSTREAM, COORDINATION), while effort was put on communication activities aiming to give visibility to the project and the Natural Heritage Mission as a whole. At the end of the year an expert communication manager, for boosting the social media channels. Expecially after the project meeting in Barcelona that took place at the end of the first semester, the project launched some of its activities, such as the community building set-up, the stakeholders mapping, the resources’ collection, the first awareness campaign, the design of the Academy and the forming of the amplification room and its respective Working Groups. The basis of the cooperation with the Dialogue4Nature (D4N) project was also set, as well as relationships with the other Interreg Euro-MED Missions and other Initiatives and institutions (e.g. UfM, EC, etc). As for WP1 – SHARING/REUSE, development of the ‘REUSE Roadmap’, overviewing the WP1 activities, as well as the foreseen synergies with the D4N project (Activity 1.1 REUSE roadmap and WP coordination). In this context, many meetings with the project’s partners were held, as well as with the D4N partners coordinating D4N’s relevant activities. All partners participated in the preparation and implementation of the two community building events and one community building activity (Activity 1.2 Community of practice set-up), i.e., the KoM of Community4Nature (22 March 2023) with the participation of Associated Partners (APs), the hybrid event held in Barcelona (9 June 2023), co-organised with D4N, and engaging the Mission’s APs, and the community building session during the first Institutional Dialogue event (D4N activity) in Piran (Slovenia). All partners, duly coordinated, contributed in the development and updating of the excel tool aiming to map the Mission’s stakeholders (Activity 1.3 Mapping of stakeholders and potential target groups). One of the first activities to be activated has been the methodology for resources collection and the draft Adaptive Resources Recipient (Activity 1.4). All partners contributed by adding resources to the Adaptive Resources Recipient. Moreover, a great effort has been done by the communication team in designing and implementing the awareness campaign on coastal erosion (Activity 1.9 “SOLVE the CHALLENGE” campaigns). In WP2, leaded by the WP responsible, all partners contributed to the development of the ‘TRANSFER roadmap’ (Activity 2.1 TRANSFER roadmap and WP coordination). As for the Interreg Euro-MED Academy activities (Activity 2.2.) during the second period the partnership focused on the design of the first summer school in presence. A discussions for the design of the services of the Amplification Room (Activity 2.3 Creation of the C4Nature Community Amplification Room) has been initiated, while all partners actively contributed to the launching of the thematic Working Groups (WGs) and the elaboration of the relevant factsheets (Activity 2.5 Transfer by clustering through WGs for strategic liaising, exchanges and advice panels). In addition, partners took part in Activity 2.6 (Mainstreaming support to D4N), where contribution to the policy related objectives was offered to D4N, mainly on the organisation of the first Institutional Dialogue event on 22 & 23 November 2023, in Slovenia. Finally, some partners are actively involved in the Mission4Nature joint communication team that meets every week for planning and implementing the planned communication activities (Activity 2.7 Communication and dissemination). In WP3, all partners contributed to the overall project’s planning.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • TerritorialAgenda2030 -
    • EuropeanGreenDeal European Green Deal priorities aims to protect biodiversity and ecosystems, reduce pollution, promote circular economy and ensure sustainability of the blue economy. Indicatively, the EU Biodiversity Strategy 2030 acknowledges nature restoration as a key contribution to both climate change mitigation and adaptation and the Forest Strategy proposes measures for strengthening forest protection and restoration. Moreover, one of the European Green Deal Actions is dedicated to the Environment and Oceans. This Action includes priorities aiming to protect biodiversity and ecosystems, reduce air, water and soil pollution and move towards a circular economy, improve waste management and ensure the sustainability of the blue economy and fisheries sectors. C4Nature through its multi-discipline partnership and collaboration with TPs (A1.2) covers all the thematic areas and objectives of the above Action and Strategies. The achievement of these priorities requires action in multiple layers, engaging a cross-sectoral partnering, augmenting knowledge and upscaling, as well as mainstreaming of pioneering ideas (A2.4). C4Nature will target locations and regions (A2.5) that are biodiversity hotspots, face pollution challenges and are characterized by intense economic activity, focusing also in the coastal and maritime areas. The project will aim to take these activities one step further by promoting and systematizing transferring and mainstreaming of the results aiming to address the environmental objectives of Green Deal on a larger scale producing real impact in the Med region (A3.3). In addition, C4Nature will seek for a collaboration with CINEA (European Climate, Infrastructure and Environment Executive Agency) which is the European Green Deal Executive Agency, mainly on the ‘Climate and Environment’ & ‘Maritime’ Programmes.
    • Other The EU Strategy on Adaptation to Climate Change (an EU Green Deal Climate Action) was built based on the impacts of climate change that earth is facing, such as extreme weather including heat waves and droughts. Due to climate change impact, environmental degradation is faced and also the health and the well-being of people all around the world is affected.
    • This new EU Strategy identifies ecosystem-based approaches as a cross cutting priority. Its principal objectives are to make adaptation smarter, swifter and more systemic, and to step up international cooperation on adaptation to climate change.
    • C4Nature will be aligned with the EU Strategy on Adaptation to Climate Change and will work towards the Strategy’s vision by improving knowledge on climate impacts by promoting nature-based solutions for adaptation,, by enhancing climate risk assessment and adaptation actions and streamlining these into the CLIMATE ADAPT platform (A1.4, A 1.6, A 2.7), and by helping to strengthen climate resilience in the whole Mediterranean area and beyond (A 3.3).
    • Other The EU Integrated Maritime Policy (IMP) is a holistic approach to all sea- related EU policies, which seeks to provide a more coherent and coordinated approach to marine and maritime issues, also considering climate change. The Marine Strategy Framework Directive (MSFD) is the IMP’s environmental pillar, which seeks to protect the marine ecosystem and biodiversity upon which our health and marine-related economic and social activities depend. It establishes a common framework requiring Member States to take the necessary measures to achieve and maintain Good Environmental Status of the EU's coastal and marine waters.
    • C4Nature will take into consideration MSFD’s 11 Qualitative descriptors for determining good environmental status (e.g., that biological diversity and sea-floor integrity is maintained, human-induced eutrophication is minimized, etc.) when evaluating proposed solutions (A1.5), ensuring the resources presented all contribute to the IMP goals..

    • Other The EU Floods Directive (FD), adopted in 2007, aims to assess and manage all kind of floods (river, lakes, flash floods, urban floods, coastal floods, including storm surges and tsunamis) in a coherent way across the EU and integrates the consideration of climate change impacts directly in its implementation. In order to reduce and manage the risks that floods pose to human health, the environment, cultural heritage and economic activity, the Directive requires Member States to identify the river basins and associated coastal areas at risk of flooding, then draw up flood risk maps and establish flood risk management plans focused on prevention, protection and preparedness.
    • C4Nature will focus flood risk reduction solutions aligned with the EU Floods Directive (A1.4) and capitalize the implementation related challenges diagnosed in the the 1st Floods Directive Implementation Report and the 2nd Preliminary Flood Risk Assessments Report, integration lessons learned into specific working groups (A 2. 5). and policy making processes (A 2.6)
    • Other The Mediterranean Action Plan (MAP) of the United Nations Environment Programme was established in 1975 by Mediterranean governments as the first regional action plan under the UNEP Regional Seas Programme with clear objectives to foster regional collaboration against marine pollution and promote integrated planning and sustainable use of marine resources. At the heart of MAP lies the Convention for the Protection of the Mediterranean Sea - the so called Barcelona Convention . Over time the Convention was complemented by seven (7) Protocols addressing different aspects of marine and coastal environment and resource protection and management.
