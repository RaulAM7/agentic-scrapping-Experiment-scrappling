Supporting Adaptation to Mediterranean Sea Level Rise

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Climate change is evident in many aspects, among them there is the progressive increase of the average sea level, which is affecting the rare extremes too, both on their frequency and intensity. The assessment of risks deriving from sea level variations requires a robust quantification of all the elements bringing the sea level to become a hazard thus, for an effective adaptation to future sea conditions, it is mandatory to have reliable knowledge on scenarios for sea level trends. This is the challenge addressed by this project with the objective of improving the assessment of risks deriving from the Mediterranean sea level variations, through a robust quantification of its hazardous source. Mediterranean coastal impacts are deduced from global sea level trends or climate simulation each affected by significant uncertainties, furthermore, there is an open debate, in the scientific community, on them. As a consequence, there is an urgent need to quantify the likelihood of available scientific information on the expected XXI century sea level trends, in the Mediterranean sub-basins, with an estimate of the uncertainties. This project outputs are expected to contribute in meeting this need, defining best practices on sea level trends usage, in the risk assessment processes, according to the sensitivity of impacts to the hazard information likelihood. Best practices, supported by examples of applications and summary datasets, will help stakeholders to flow the scientific information into the risk assessment logic. Benefits are expected to reach a wide spectra of stakeholders from the economic and safety to the coastal ecosystem preservation sectors. The project transnational partnership brings the inter-regional portrait of the challenge together with the interdisciplinarity required to understand how anthropic solutions and better preservation of the coastal ecosystems have to be involved in resilience and adaptation actions to the progressive sea level rise.

Description (FR):
Le changement climatique a des conséquences directes, dont l’augmentation du niveau moyen de la mer, qui entraîne l’apparition d’événements climatiques extrêmes, tant en termes de fréquence que d’intensité. L’évaluation des risques découlant des variations du niveau de la mer nécessite une quantification des éléments qui font que le niveau de la mer devient un danger pour une adaptation efficace aux conséquences futures de la montée des eaux. Il est indispensable de disposer de connaissances fiables sur les scénarios des évolutions du niveau de la mer. L’objectif de MedSeaRise est d’améliorer l’évaluation des risques découlant des variations du niveau de la mer, grâce à une quantification robuste des risques de montée des eaux. Les impacts côtiers méditerranéens sont évalués à partir des tendances globales de variation du niveau de la mer ou de simulation de conséquences du changement climatique. Il est urgent de quantifier la probabilité des informations sur les tendances attendues du niveau de la mer au XXIe siècle, avec une estimation la part d’incertitudes. MedSeaRise entend répondre à ce besoin, en définissant des pratiques exemplaires sur l’utilisation des données sur le niveau de la mer, dans les processus d’évaluation des risques, en fonction de la sensibilité des impacts et de la probabilité des scénarios. Des études de cas aideront les intervenants à intégrer l’information scientifique dans une logique d’évaluation des risques. Les résultats de MedSeaRise s’adresseront à un large éventail d’intervenants, des secteurs de l’économie et de la sécurité jusqu’aux secteurs de la préservation des écosystèmes côtiers en passant par les pouvoirs publics. MedSeaRise est un partenariat transnational qui repose sur une approche interdisciplinaire pour comprendre comment les solutions anthropiques et une meilleure préservation des écosystèmes côtiers peuvent contribuer aux actions de résilience et d’adaptation face à l’élévation progressive du niveau de la mer.

Regarding the implementation of the Work Packages, namely WP1, a summary is presented bellow: -Activity 1.1. PP2 started the survey of scientific paper on sea level projection for the Mediterranean area. The survey is in progress in strict collaboration with PP6. This contributes to the deliverable D.1.1.1. Numerical data of future scenarios have been collected from CMIP6 and MEDCORDEX sets of simulations. Besides sea level, air temperature and precipitation are available for at least three scenarios, namely RCP2.6, RCP4.5 and RCP8.5. For sea level, in addition the RCP7.0 is available. Time extension of the scenarios spans from the historical section, which is covering the second half of XX century, and the projections, that go to the end of the XXI century. This contributes to the deliverable D.1.1.2. -Activity 1.2. PP2 defined the methodology for stakeholder engagement and information harvesting. The implementation of the methodology started and the survey of potential stakeholders completed. Future climate scenarios information, in graphical format, have been prepared and let available to support the interactions with stakeholders. The stakeholder survey and engagement will be done during the next period. This contributes to the deliverables D.1.2.1, D1.2.2 and D.1.2.3. -Activity 1.3. The deliverable D.1.3.1 “Definition of project contribution to Amplification Strategy” that was issued at the end of this project period, is finalized and uploaded on Basecamp. MedSeaRise showcase a great example of combination of organizations with different expertise to achieve the project goals. The scientific results of the 1st Semester have been collaboratively prepared by the scientific PPs (PP2, PP5) and were thoroughly presented & explained to the partnership to be easily transferred to potential local and international stakeholders. This excellent collaboration during the 1st semester can be also seen in the responses of the 1st quality check, highlighting the value of cooperation for the participating organizations. This feedback mechanism helped identify areas for improvement in collaboration. Additionally, ANATOLIKI prepared the first progress report and facilitated the consolidation of individual reports from all partners, ensuring smooth communication with the Joint Secretariat on administrative, financial, and reporting matters. MedSeaRise maintained an active presence in communication and dissemination efforts. MedSeaRise also actively participated in key international events. At the 3rd International Conference on Adriatic Biodiversity Protection (ADRIBIOPRO 2024) in Kotor, Montenegro, a dedicated workshop on sea level rise was organized, bringing together 44 experts to discuss climate change scenarios, marine indicators, river discharge impacts, and ecosystem responses. These discussions informed the project’s methodological developments. Additionally, a project meeting at the Institute of Marine Biology focused on stakeholder engagement, case studies, and effective communication of scientific findings. MedSeaRise also contributed to an Interreg Euro-MED webinar on the EU Nature Restoration Law and participated in the Natural Heritage Mission’s Joint Communication Meeting, enhancing collaboration and project visibility. Across work packages, partners advanced research and stakeholder engagement. WP1 activities included risk analysis, scientific data collection, and stakeholder awareness efforts. PP2 led the development of impact indexes for risk assessments and supported case study selection. MedSeaRise partners strengthened ties with governance projects like Community4Nature, while the Euro-MED Academy was identified as a potential platform for peer-reviewed project outputs. Case study development progressed with refined methodologies for integrating stakeholder input. Efforts were made to address technical challenges, including the collection of additional sea level data for risk assessments. Meetings with organizations such as Cerema and CASA explored stormwater management solutions, and stakeholder engagement was intensified through targeted invitations and discussions. Partners actively contributed to institutional dialogues and capacity-building efforts. PP4 and PP6 participated in the second Mission for Natural Heritage Institutional Dialogue in Málaga, showcasing MedSeaRise’s role in sustainable adaptation strategies. An online meeting in December between PP2, PP4, and PP6 focused on risk analyses, statistical indexes, and methodologies for assessing sea level rise impacts. The team also generated tide-level datasets using the TPXO10v2a model to support future stakeholder meetings. Throughout this period, MedSeaRise remained committed to advancing scientific understanding, stakeholder engagement, and practical applications for climate adaptation. Finally the deliverables D.1.1.1 and D.1.3.1. were finalized and released. ANATOLIKI played a central role in project coordination, preparing the Carbon Footprint Report with input from all partners and designing a Quality Evaluation Form for this semester, whose feedback highlighted some areas for improvement. The dissemination of the project was managed on a rotating basis by different partners, with the Lead Partner overseeing overall publication. The project’s visibility grew steadily across platforms, reaching substantial audiences (LinkedIn: 189 followers, 49 posts, 92 connections, 12,115 post impressions, and 2,173 members reached; Instagram: 49 posts, 100 followers, 39 accounts reached; X: 14 followers). In early July, the project published its third newsletter on the project's website and was disseminated by each partners to their stakeholders. MedSeaRise was represented by two representatives from the Lead Partner in the Natural Heritage Mission's Community of Practice meeting, held on July 2–3 at the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA). During the European Maritime Day workshops, the project was presented to the participants, drawing strong interest from attendees. MedSeaRise also contributed actively to the communication-focused sessions of the #SolveTheChallenge 2025 NHM 's campaign. The preparation for this meeting was organised by the LP and PP2. On April 1st and 2nd, a project meeting was held in Malta with the participation of all partners. The main focus of the meeting was the discussion of each partner's case study and the activities related to their WP1 commitments. PP2 successfully coordinated WP1 activities, ensuring that all partners met their commitments and deadlines. They monitored progress, guided the preparation of deliverables, and kept the Lead Partner informed. PP2 organized scientific data and future sea level rise scenarios, consolidated stakeholder-driven impact assessments, and summarized case studies prepared by each partner. PP2 has actively contributed to the Community4Nature Amplification Room, supporting the wider dissemination of project results. PP3, leading Work Package 3, initiated the organization of upcoming activities in order to validate the Medsearise methodology. PP4 undertook several dissemination actions, including the organization of Info Days, and also supported other partners in WP1 and WP2, while finalizing the deliverables of WP2. Additionally, PP4 and PP5 contributed to dissemination efforts through presentations of the project’s expected results. All partners successfully completed their case studies during this semester, fully achieving their objectives, finalizing the respective deliverables within each Work Package, and establishing the basis for the subsequent project activities.

Relevant linked projects:

  • INTERREG IT-HR AdriaClim project (EN), INTERREG IT-HR AdriaClim project (FR) | The analyses conducted on sea level rise scenarios in the Adriatic area by the INTERREG IT-HR AdriaClim project (https://www.italy-croatia.eu/web/adriaclim), are going to be extended in the whole Mediterranean. The AdriaClim dataset, on future sea level rise scenarios, are considered the starting point on which to carry on the survey on the available data this Euro-MED project has to consider.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal In the frame of the European Green Deal, it is foreseen to achieve climate neutrality by 2050, that means to give a great contribution in mitigating the present trend of climate evolution. Anyway, meanwhile the effect of mitigation actions will become effective, it is essential to support the adaptation to stress the changing climate will continue to produce on anthropic activities and on biodiversity. In fact, the Green Deal underlines the need to strength our efforts on climate-proofing, resilience building, prevention and preparedness. So, climate adaptation will continue to influence public and private investments, then it will be important to ensure all actors composing the EU society, thus those the Mediterranean too, namely investors, insurers, businesses, cities and citizens are able to access data and to develop instruments to integrate climate change into their risk management practices. According to this specific points of the European Green Deal, it is clear that the methodology this project will develop to support the adaptation to sea level rise is going to contribute to the strategy.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion According to the mission of European Union Strategy for the Adriatic and Ionian Region, this project will contribute to the strategy thanks to the partnership that includes partners operating in that Mediterranean area. This is a clear manifestation of the consolidation of cooperation among the countries of the Adriatic and Ionian region foreseen in the strategy. Furthermore, the essential interaction among all the partners, to conduct the project activities, promotes the sharing of the experience acquired thanks the strategy implementation with the rest of the Mediterranean partners. Since this specific EU strategy promotes sustainable economic and social prosperity in the region, while at the same time preserving the environment and ensuring healthy, balanced marine and coastal ecosystems, the project results will support the strategy in defining and implementing and climate change adaptation, risk prevention and resilience, through ecosystem-based approaches. Finally, it is worth to remember that EU Strategy for the Adriatic and Ionian Region is also expected to contribute to the EU integration of the potential candidate countries in the region This project, thanks to the Euro-MED inclusion of the IPA countries among the partners, has allowed to create a partnership in which a IPA country, overlooking the Adriatic Sea, is an effective and relevant partner, that is going to collaborate in the supporting one of the most important EU missions, working integrated with EU partners.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin This project tackles a challenge that is shared with the WestMED initiative, namely the consequences of the progressive sea level rise due the future trends of climate. In fact, the initiative clearly states that the Climate Change greatly affects the western Mediterranean, and the rise in sea level is a major threat to coastal ecosystems and economies. So the outputs of the project directly contribute into the strategy, both because of the complete overlapping of the project objectives with those foreseen in the WestMED initiative and because more than half of the project partners belong to countries that are part of the geographical area on which the strategy is operating. Since the mission of the strategy is pursued through coordination and cooperation among the countries in the area, this project practically increases the cooperation, that is also coordinated by way of the project work plan. Finally, if we look at the main results expected for the WestMED, it is easy to recognize that the project is going to match some of the actions promoted by the initiative, such as new approaches in facing the climate change hazards, besides producing examples generated also with the participation of stakeholder.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The Territorial Agenda 2030 provides an action-oriented framework to foster territorial cohesion in Europe also promoting responses to the increasing pressure concerning sustainable development and climate change. This project foresees a methodology meant to increase the efficacy and improve the efficiency of the adaptation and the resilience to sea level rise, due to climate change in the Mediterranean area, so it is clearly inserted into the frame defined by the Agenda 2030. Furthermore, the application of the Territorial Agenda 2030 is strongly based on the significant benefits that are being achieved through the strategic use of existing resources. The resources let available by the Euro-MED program, through this project, are an example of that. In fact, the Agenda explicitly refers to synergies with the EU Recovery Plan and the EU macro-regional and sea basin strategies. Last but not least, among the six actions that have been launched to put in practice the Territorial Agenda 2030, there is the Climate change adaptation and resilience through landscape transition, which is focused on the rural areas, but shares with this project almost all the four thematic priorities for a landscape transition approach. It is worth to recall that rural areas, that are close or including the Mediterranean coats, are directly or indirectly affected by sea level rise. Keeping in mind all this aspects, this Euro-MED project gives a not negligible contribution to the Territorial Agenda 2030
