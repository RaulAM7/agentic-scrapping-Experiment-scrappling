Digital Drought Risk Management enabling the drought mitigation and adaptation strategies for the restoration of the ecosystem equilibrium in Mediterranean European Countries

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The Mediterranean region is considered one of the regions of the globe with the highest socioeconomic exposure to droughts that is likely to be exacerbated in the future (Gu et al., 2020). The recent Sixth Assessment Report from the Working Group I of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC-AR6) defines drought as a period with below-average moisture conditions, which results in negative impacts on various components of natural systems and socio-economic sectors. Four macro-categories of droughts have been defined by experts (e.g., Wilhite and Glantz (1985) and Mishra and Singh (2010)), however there is not a universal definition of drought nor droughts cannot, due to their multifaceted aspects, be measured or assessed based on a single variable or through a unique index (Lloyd-Hughes, 2014). In this context, not only drought severity matters, as drier-than-normal conditions are often characterized through their spatial and temporal attributes: extent, frequency, duration, and timing. It is therefore of crucial importance to adopt standardized indices that quantify a drought and flag its severity, using threshold-based classifications, according to the level of deviation from normal conditions (Crausbay et al., 2020). Germ of Life project aims at developing, test and uptake a “drought risk management preventive approach” based on jointly developed and adapted solutions built on commonly defined data, monitoring sources and indices that will support drought risk prediction for the territorial monitoring systems and timely warning of drought conditions. The cross-border dimension of the project allows stakeholders to collaborate in the vulnerability assessment of the territories based on a shared taxonomy and comparable data-driven risk assessment as well as sharing evidence of innovative Nature-based Solutions and technological solutions targeting drought risk mitigation challenges.

Description (FR):
La région méditerranéenne est considérée comme l'une des régions du globe les plus exposées aux sécheresses sur le plan socio-économique, une situation qui risque de s'aggraver à l'avenir (Gu et al., 2020). Le récent Sixième rapport d'évaluation du Groupe de travail I du Groupe d'experts intergouvernemental sur l'évolution du climat (IPCC-AR6) définit la sécheresse comme une période où les conditions d'humidité sont inférieures à la moyenne, ce qui entraîne des effets négatifs sur diverses composantes des systèmes naturels et des secteurs socio-économiques. Quatre macro-catégories de sécheresses ont été définies par des experts (par exemple, Wilhite et Glantz (1985) et Mishra et Singh (2010)), cependant il n'y a pas de définition universelle de la sécheresse et les sécheresses ne peuvent pas, en raison de leurs aspects multiformes, être mesurées ou évaluées sur la base d'une seule variable ou à travers un indice unique (Lloyd-Hughes, 2014). Le projet Germ of Life vise à développer, tester et adopter une "approche préventive de la gestion du risque de sécheresse" basée sur des solutions développées et adaptées conjointement, fondées sur des données, des sources de surveillance et des indices définis en commun, qui soutiendront la prévision du risque de sécheresse pour les systèmes de surveillance territoriaux et l'alerte rapide des conditions de sécheresse. La dimension transfrontalière du projet permet aux parties prenantes de collaborer à l'évaluation de la vulnérabilité des territoires sur la base d'une taxonomie partagée et d'une évaluation des risques fondée sur des données comparables, ainsi que de partager des preuves de solutions innovantes fondées sur la nature et de solutions technologiques visant à atténuer les risques de sécheresse.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the first reporting period, the Germ of Life project achieved significant progress, successfully advancing the planned activities and deliverables according to schedule. The strong collaboration and seamless communication between all project partners, coordinated by UPatras as the Lead Partner, played a crucial role in meeting the project’s objectives. Through a well-managed pre-contracting phase and a detailed work plan, the consortium ensured that the project started on time and without delays, setting a solid foundation for the next phases. A major accomplishment was the successful kick-off meeting held in Seville, which brought together all partners and enabled them to develop a detailed roadmap for the project. This meeting facilitated the establishment of key governance structures, such as the Steering Committee and Technical Committee, which were activated early in the project to ensure effective coordination and decision-making. These committees have been instrumental in guiding the project’s development, contributing to smooth project management and communication. In WP1, from a technical standpoint, CMCC led the development of a comprehensive drought indicator dataset, which forms the foundation for future drought risk assessments. This dataset is critical to understanding the severity of drought impacts across the pilot areas and will support the development of new indicators in the coming months. This work is central to the project’s goals of enhancing drought resilience and provides a solid framework for future technical advancements. One of the key accomplishments under WP1 was the progress made towards the Collaborative Vulnerability Assessment Tool (VAT), which is designed to assess drought vulnerabilities in real time. This tool is central to the project’s goal of enhancing the resilience of local ecosystems and communities affected by drought. During this period, Dotsoft and other partners collaborated closely to define the functional specifications and data requirements for VAT. The tool will integrate data from multiple sources, including pilot site sensors, to provide a comprehensive vulnerability assessment for each region. In addition, Dotsoft developed a questionnaire to gather information from stakeholders, focusing on the social, environmental, and economic dimensions of drought. The feedback from this questionnaire will inform the design and functionality of VAT, ensuring that it meets the needs of diverse end-users, including public authorities, researchers, and SMEs. In terms of WP2 and pilot testing, UPatras collaborated closely with the Region of Western Greece (RWG) to identify and plan the Greek pilot area. Together, they outlined the necessary specifications for the equipment to be installed, ensuring that it aligns with regional needs. Similarly, EDIA in Portugal and LAMORO in Italy have made substantial progress in defining their pilot areas, which are key to implementing the project’s drought mitigation strategies. These pilot areas will soon be equipped with innovative technology to monitor and respond to drought risks in real time. The communication strategy for the project, led by Ubitel, was another major focus during this period. Ubitel spearheaded the development of promotional materials, launched the project’s website, and set up social media accounts to raise awareness of the Germ of Life project. These platforms are essential for engaging stakeholders, sharing project results, and ensuring wide dissemination of the project’s outputs. The communication team also actively engaged local and regional authorities, helping to position the Germ of Life project as a key initiative in addressing drought risks. Cross-border collaboration between partners has been one of the project's greatest strengths, fostering knowledge exchange and mutual support. For instance, the feedback provided by Germ of Life partners to the Joint Secretariat (JS) and governance projects under the Natural Heritage Mission was essential in aligning the project with broader European sustainability goals. This feedback also helped create synergies with other Missions, facilitating knowledge sharing and identifying complementary actions that can further the goals of the Germ of Life project and contribute to common targets. These cross-project collaborations are key in ensuring that the lessons learned from Germ of Life are integrated into a wider European framework for addressing climate and environmental challenges. In line with the project’s commitment to sustainability, Germ of Life partners took proactive steps to monitor and reduce the project’s environmental footprint. Partners have kept detailed records of the CO2 emmissions generated by project activities, which will be integrated into the Programme’s dedicated online tool. In the second reporting period of the Germ of Life consortium's activities, significant progress was made across various work packages, demonstrating the collaborative efforts of all participating partners. The consortium held its second project meeting and stakeholders meeting in Marseille on September 16-17, 2024, an event crucial for reviewing ongoing activities and strategically planning future steps. This meeting fostered valuable discussions and ensured alignment among partners. Throughout this period, the Germ of Life consortium maintained consistent communication through regular monthly progress meetings, conducted online to facilitate participation from all partners. These sessions allowed for a collective evaluation of the project's advancements and the timely identification of any areas requiring attention. The consortium actively engaged in technical and administrative meetings, ensuring effective coordination and collaboration. Within Work Package 1 (WP1), focused on data collection and analysis, partners made substantial contributions. Specifically: All pilot partners provided comprehensive data for their pilot area, including meteorological information, photographs, maps, and other relevant data crucial for the study. CMCC continued the activity A1.1 by providing a comprehensive review of algorithms available from existing drought observatories and initiatives. In collaboration with project partners and references for the PTA, CMCC delivered the description of algorithms and algorithm in the internal D.1.1.2 which was made available to all partners in Basecamp. Algorithms and codes included in the D.1.1.2 deliverable contributed to the design of the Germ Of Life solutions in A.1.3 and the development of the pipeline for the data processing and calculation of drought n indicators and the implementation of the platform in A.1.4. The consortium initiated the development of the "Database of drought risk projects D.1.2.1," identifying key projects and stakeholders. This remains an ongoing collaborative effort, with all partners contributing to the continuous updating of the database. Partners actively participated in online workshops organized by the WP coordinator, contributing to activities related to the development and co-design of the Germ of Life project platforms. These workshops addressed requirements, technical aspects of climate data, environmental factors, common indicators, platform flows, and crucial factors for identifying vulnerability to drought. Partners provided input on the most important indicators for their respective pilot areas, considering social, economic, infrastructure, governance, environmental, and agricultural practices, while taking into account the habitat and socio-economic environment. Human resources with relevant expertise participated in workshops dedicated to the co-design of the Innovative Procurement Platform and the Drought Forecasting Tool, contributing to discussions on platform flows, user requirements, drought management, scale of analysis, and indicator selection. Partners also provided relevant maps and presentations on their pilot areas. Within Work Package 2 (WP2), partners collaborated on activities A2.1 and A2.4: The pilot partners began the process of acquiring their equipment for their pilot sites. Notably, the Region of Western Greece (RWG) oversaw the public procurement process for the acquisition of equipment needed for the meteorological station in the Greek pilot area, supervising the installation and initial data collection and parameterization.Furthermore, EDIA ensured the development and monitoring of the entire public procurement process for the acquisition of all the equipment needed to implement the meteorological station in the portuguese pilot area. A partner responsible for calculating the carbon footprint (PP10 - INFO) received necessary data from other consortium members to facilitate this analysis. Consortium representatives also participated in the Natural Heritage Mission activities, including working group meetings, webinars, and the 2nd Institutional Dialogue event in Malaga, Spain, from November 27th to 29th, 2024. This participation aimed to establish new collaborations and strengthen the network for the Germ of Life project.
