MEDiterranean Youth for Creative Impact

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 2 - Youth-oriented projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 152. Measures to promote equal opportunities and active participation in society ~ SUMMARY: The MED-Y.C.I project addresses transnational challenges in the Mediterranean region, targeting educational disparities, digital gap, and high youth unemployment. With a significant youth demographic facing low civic engagement, and informal employment, particularly among young women, the project unites France, Spain, Turkey, Tunisia, and Egypt to co-develop an innovative training program for youth Civil Society Organizations (CSOs). MED-Y.C.I aims to foster digital, creative, and analytical skills crucial for new job markets and active civic participation. By pooling expertise from multidisciplinary experts and artists across five countries, the project develops new educational practices that promote lifelong learning and regional solidarity. The initiative integrates international exchanges, cross-border and regional actions led by youth, rethinking engagement as a cross-cutting skill for better employability and empowerment. In this frame, MED-Y-CI aims to train 200 youths aged 18-30 from diverse backgrounds through an original joint scheme training within an analytical, a creative and a practical part. Additionally, video facilities, known as MED-Y.C.I Studios, are established in each partner country to serve as hubs for ongoing skill development and innovation. Managed by the youth, they impact at least 500 additional youngsters. Thanks to this infrastructure, transnational WebTV shows (Medtlaks) and creative engaged events are produced by trained youngsters and shared in international events, to advocate with regional authorities. Moreover, the project plan to involve 50 young entrepreneurs showcasing their initiatives for positive changes, highlighting different mediterranean approaches. In addition, to ensure the replicability and sustainability of the project’s methodologies, a MED-Y.C.I Handbook and E-learning Platform are created. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Education and training

Relevant linked projects:

  • CLUSTER under the ENI CBC Mediterranean Sea Basin (ENI CBC Med) Programme (EN) | 1. Targeted labour market oriented training courses and traineeship scheme
  • One of the main outputs of the CLUSTER programme is the targeted labor market oriented training courses and traineeship scheme. This output is exploited and delivered with appropriate changes in the Work Package 3 of MED-Y.C.I joint schemes training, but in an artistic and interactive manner and a blended online and face-to-face approach. This joint scheme training is also aimed at equipping the youth with important skills that are needed in the evolving labour market while also training them to act to bring about positive changes in the Mediterranean region.
  • 2. E-learning tools and e-platform
  • The MED-Y.C.I project exploits this output in the Work Package 5, specifically Output 5.4 which creates a MED-Y.C.I e-learning platform, that will be available in all 5 languages to disseminate the content produced during the project.
  • 3. Compilation of identified efficient practices
  • The MED-Y.C.I project exploits this but with a different twist, naming it the MED-Y.C.I handbook compiling all the information and content from the trainees, trainers, associated organization and other youngsters. The aim of this handbook is to encapsulate the Med-Y-Ci process in order to facilitate its replicability and also disseminate the best practices of the project to other stakeholders, so that other future projects will be able to leverage the knowledge and practices. (EN), Young Civil Society Leadership Programme under Med Dialogue for Rights and Equality II (EN) | 1. Recruitment of 25 young Mediterranean leaders
  • The MED-Y.C.I project exploits this output as it calls for the participation of Mediterranean youth between 18 to 30 years old, publishing a call for candidature as done by the Young Civil Society Leadership Programme. The candidature in the context of the MED-Y.C.I project will be limited to the 5 partner countries and will select 200 participants.
  • 2. Community Building Bootcamp
  • The MED-Y.C.I project exploits and builds on this output which is a face-to-face gathering of three days that focuses on jointly formulating the actions and ideas, with the guidance of project team, mentors and experts. This output corresponds with the MED-Y.C.I training sessions implemented across the 5 countries, facilitated by 30 experts on Mediterranean issues and artists. It exploits the approach of facilitating interaction between experts and participants to facilitate discussion and knowledge-sharing.
  • 3. Participation at high level policy dialogues within the Euro-Med space.
  • This output is exploited by the MED-Y.C.I project in the frame of transnational events with regional authorities. The MED-Y.C.I trained youth will be able to participate in significant Euromediterranean exchanges under the Mediterranean Youth Council (MYC) banner to contribute constructively to regional discourse and advocate for positive change in the Mediterranean region. (EN), Art for Clim’Act under Fondation Allianz (EN) | 1. Local co-creation lab in each organization together with local artists, artistic structures, local communities
  • The MED-Y.C.I project exploits the co-creation lab strategy to co-design the pedagogical framework for the MED-Y.C.I training, involving not only the Mediterranean youth but also the experts. This approach is an inspiration from the approach of the Art for Clim’Act which involved local artists and experts.
  • 2. Simultaneous creative actions on an international and local scale
  • The MED-Y.C.I project exploits this output of implementing simultaneous creative actions in the local context. This will be adopted and adapted in the implementation of the 25 simultaneous local creative engaged actions by the youth in the Mediterranean. (EN)

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other The implementation of MED-Y.C.I will contribute to the New European Agenda for the Mediterranean under the policy area of Human development, good governance and the rule of law.The policy area of human development sets out 2 significant spheres, with one being ‘Empowered Youth’. The project supports the mainstreaming of youth in national policies via specific activities set out in Output 5.1 of transnational WebTv shows to promote cross-border dialogue and Output 5.2 of involving the youths in transnational events with regional authorities. All the prior activities culminate in these activities in order to promote mainstreaming of youths in national and regional policies.
    • The UN SDG 4 is also prioritized as the project highly promotes lifelong learning opportunities for the youth involved, especially via WP3 pedagogical activities which offer transnational face to face co-creative lab, online workshops and peer to peer sessions allowing youth participants to gain competences via engagement with peers from other countries, like collaborative problem-solving or creative thinking. Effective communication, critical thinking as well as synthesis skills are promoted via participation in WP4 cross regional youth dialogues and WP5 webtv shows (Medtalks). These activities provide immense learning opportunities for the youth participant.
    • The project is based on the main principles of the European Skills Agenda, as it strives to guarantee social equity by ensuring that young people from different territories and social backgrounds have equal access to training and lifelong learning. Indeed, the selection criteria for participants must give equal opportunity to young people from different social backgrounds to take part. In addition, the program, thanks to its blended approach integrating online methodologies, aims to strengthen resilience, particularly with the growing need for not only online but also innovative approaches, especially in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic.
