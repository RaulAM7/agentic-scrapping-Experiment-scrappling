Developing community based innovative business models for the revival of the internal areas in the Mediterranean

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
In the last decades, the internal territories of the Mediterranean have been affected by steady depopulation processes that have led people to move to urban and coastal areas with a higher concentration of economic activities. Low economic dynamism, scarce public services, lack of job opportunities, poor digitalization and accessibility are the main challenges that make the internal areas more vulnerable. REVIVE fits into this context, by building on participatory and capacity building approaches to reinforce the collaboration among quadruple helix stakeholders, strengthen the cohesion of communities and co-design innovative and integrated solutions to common challenges. Through a transnational cooperation and coordination project partners will develop and test collaborative economic models through the creation of Innovative Community Cooperatives with the aim of developing interconnected products and services for the supply chains of existing economic sectors. In parallel, the project will co-design a set of digital services and test the application of advanced technologies through the creation of Mediterranean Digital Innovation Hubs. The MDIHs will then be integrated in the REVIVE transnational cluster of Innovative Community Cooperatives that will jointly provide concrete solutions for creating new business opportunities, encourage people to live and invest in these areas. In it's final phase the project will provide a transferring framework that will set the basis for up-scaling of results, strengthen the cohesion and promote the transition towards competitive, innovative and resilient ecosystems of the internal territories of the transnational cooperation area.

Description (FR):
Au cours des dernières décennies, les territoires internes de la Méditerranée ont été affectés par un processus continu de dépeuplement, qui a conduit les habitants à se déplacer vers des zones urbaines et côtières avec une plus grande concentration d'activités économiques. Les principaux défis qui rendent ces zones plus vulnérables sont un faible dynamisme économique, des services publics médiocres, le manque d'opportunités d'emploi, une numérisation et une accessibilité insuffisantes. REVIVE s'inséré dans ce contexte grâce à des approches participatives et de renforcement des capacités, pour améliorer la collaboration entre les partenaires soutenir la cohésion communautaire et concevoir ensemble des solutions aux défis communs qui soient innovantes et intégrées. Grace à un projet transnational de coopération et de coordination, les partenaires développeront et testeront des modèles économiques collaboratifs, qui prévoient la création de coopératives communautaires innovantes, dans le but de développer des produits et services interconnectés pour les chaînes d'approvisionnement des secteurs économiques existants. En parallèle, le projet créera un ensemble de services numériques et testera l'application de technologies avancées, à travers la création de pôles méditerranéens d'innovation numérique. Les MDIH seront ensuite intégrés dans le cluster transnational REVIVE des coopératives communautaires innovantes, qui fourniront conjointement des solutions concrètes, pour créer des nouvelles opportunités d'affaires, encourager les populations à continuer vivre et à investir dans ces zones. Le projet fournira, dans sa phase finale, un cadre de transfert qui posera les bases de l’expansion des résultats, renforcera la cohésion et favorisera la transition des territoires internes inclus dans l'espace de coopération transnationale, vers des écosystèmes compétitifs, innovants et résilients.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The first phase of the project, was focused on defining a methodological framework for developing community based collaborative economy business models and enabling innovation processes in our pilot areas. The work started with a deep analysis of the pilot areas to understand the current situation, the challenges and problems they face and to identify the opportunities for collaboration and innovation. Based on a template provided by IPTPO (activity leader) a deep analysis of the pilot areas was done with information on the existing policies at the national/ regional level and on the characteristics of the pilot areas. A detailed mapping and description of the policies on rural development, tourism, creative industries, innovation (RIS), social and employment and any other policies that could be consider relevant (sustainable growth, climate change, etc.) in addressing the challenges of our pilot areas, have been identified and analysed. Then a detailed analysis of each pilot areas was conducted based on the following: 1) demography characteristics, 2) climatic information, 3) natural conditions (e.g. forests, plains, landscape), 4) cultural heritage, 5) infrastructure and mobility, 6) internet infrastructure and digital services, 7) economic analysis and collaboration and connections of various entities), 8) SWOT analysis of the pilot areas. All this detailed information has been gathered in a final deliverable D1.1.1 that includes all the analysis of the pilot areas as well as a summary of the analysis with mind maps that provide an overview of existing strategies and policies at the national, regional and local levels divided into pilot areas and pertains to one of the following fields: rural development and agriculture, tourism, creative industry, innovation, society and employment, and others. Also, a brief overview of the characteristic economic activities for each area as well as the number of business entities and employees in the primary, secondary, and tertiary sectors. As a result this analysis, it provides insight into the common main features of the internal and external environment of the project's pilot areas. It is a document analysis that will help the pilot areas to develop a comprehensive action plans to implement an innovative business models in their areas and also work together based on the common challenges that they face. In parallel, the activity of exploiting the lessons learnt by past and ongoing projects and best practises was also done by all partners aiming to raise awareness about innovative and collaborative business models, measures and projects, sharing the good practices implemented in different countries, strengthening peer-to-peer learning and innovative development. Following the instructions of e-Zavod based on a template, all partners collected best practices for developing innovative business models based on three main drivers, that is innovation process, digitalization and technology and collaborative economy and also based on economic sectors, such as agriculture, production and trade of local products, tourism and culture, energy and infrastructures and mixed sectors. Deliverable 1.2.1 was drafted and shared with the partners. One of our main goals during the first period of the project was the engagement of the stakeholders from the beginning of the project. The process started with the identification of the relevant stakeholders and the organization of regional workshops in each pilot area to discuss the challenges, necessities and possible solutions that can be addressed in the pilot areas in the fields of agriculture, food production, tourism and cultural and creative industry. The results of the regional workshops, the analysis of the pilot areas and best practises and the framework for testing were detailed discussed during the meeting in Leros, Greece beginning of July before starting with the local pilot action plans process. We brough all the stakeholder's from the beginning together to exchange on their experiences, to get inspired from each other and to set the ground for future collaboration for the implementation of the pilot actions. The output O1.1 REVIVE Framework for pilot testing has been also reached since the final business model framework has been drafted that constitutes an operational instrument to favour innovation processes by promoting emergent community based on collaborative economy business models and uptake of advanced technologies. The model is unique for each area and it includes all the steps that guide pilot areas in defining a business model suitable for their area. The social media (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram) and the project website have been set up. Until project website was ready, dissemination was done through the social media. We presented the pilot areas, participation to events and the organization of regional workshops. In total we have 20 posts. Also, our poster and banner have been designed. The focus of the second semester of the project was on the establishment of the innovative community cooperatives in the 7 pilot areas as well as defining the potential digital services and advanced technologies to be implemented to the pilot areas in the next two periods. Partners, following the REVIVE business model framework developed on the first period as well as the set up of the stakeholder groups, established their innovation community cooperatives and drafted the relevant action plans that will be implemented in period 3 and 4. More specifically, at the beginning of the semester, the D2.1.1 Engagement and participatory process action plan (ANCI Toscana) was prepared to provide to the partners guidelines and a step by step process on how to engage and facilitate the participation of the local stakeholder groups, in different steps of co-design and implementation of the action plans in the 7 project pilot areas, leading to the establishment of the Innovative Community Cooperatives and the Digital Innovation Hubs and their subsequent merging into the transnational cluster of REVIVE communities. Also, in the D2.1.1, the REVIVE Pilot action plan template was included as well as the stakeholder list template and engagement calendar template that was filled by all partners with their activities (meetings, workshops, etc). All partners identified their local stakeholders and organize different meetings, workshops and events with the main aim to engage the local stakeholders and to work together to define the activities and draft the action plans. As a result, each pilot area has drafted its action plan (D2.2.1 - 7 Action Plans) that includes a brief description of the initial engagement process, a description of who is joining (or founding the innovation community cooperative), the economic sectors involved, the collaborative business model description, the activities, the monitoring and evaluation of the action plan as well as the economic sustainability and plans for the future. Another important activity for the second period was the setting up of the MED Digital innovation hubs (MDIHs) and digital laboratories in the pilot areas. Under the guidance of MCC, all partners have identified the DIHubs in their areas and already had the first communication with them as to establish cooperation for the capacity building activities (trainings in digital technologies). Also, all partners identified the initiatives related to technology applied to the sectors of the pilot areas. Based on discussions with the local stakeholders on the digital technologies and immersive experiences the partners pilot areas have identified the technologies that will implemented in their pilot areas and would bring improvements. This activities are also included in the action plans. For the Data Integration the data platform will be based on FIWARE (NADIA platform from AnySolution) that enables the collection and analysis of data from heterogeneous sources and formats and also allows for an easy visualisation and interpretation of data through dashboards. This activity will be starting in the next semester. The project website and social media have been updated on a regular basis. In this period we had more than 40 posts in our social media. The followers are as follow: Facebook 73, LinkedIn: 75 and Instagram: 36. All partners have contributed with news articles of their activities. Also, the project communication plan has been approved. The 2nd project meeting and study visit was organized at the beginning of the period in Leros, Greece (10-11 July 2024) with the participation of local stakeholders. Τhe results of the regional workshops, the analysis of the pilot areas and best practises and the framework for testing were detailed discussed. The stakeholders had the opportunity to meet and exchange on their experiences, to get inspired from each other and to set the ground for future collaboration for the implementation of the pilot actions. The 3rd project meeting was organized in Maribor, Slovenia (13-14/11/2024). The main aim of the meeting was the discussion of the next steps for the pilot areas action plans for the set up of the innovation community cooperatives and the implementation of the immersive experiences. Study visits were also organized for exchange of experience and inspiration for the partners. The focus of the 3rd semester was on the implementation of the Action Plans of the 7 pilot areas focusing on the ICC and the development of the immersive experiences: ANETEL: engaging stakeholders with several meetings and especially with the core group for the implementation of the action plan (setting up the ICC and identify the branding and communication for rural Larnaca). The immersive experience to be developed was agreed which is the creation of an interactive AI Avatar that will welcome visitors and respond in real time to questions about the pilot area highlighting also existing immersive experiences of rural Larnaca. CNR-ANCI: drafted the Charter of Values and analyse potential legal structures of the Food Community. They drafted the Statute and Articles of the Association (signed by 12 members) and registered officially the Pistoia Mountain Food Community. They design a logo and visual identity, develop an official website for the Food Community, create promotional materials and defined a targeted communication strategy. They customized training sessions on digital marketing and immersive technologies and started the process for the immersive experiences (360 VR Videos). IPTPO: engage stakeholders with relevant meetings and discussions for creating a digital solution. They further encourage local participation by organising the event ‘Study visit Istria’, bring together project partners and their stakeholders. The 1st digital workshop was organised aiming to involve stakeholders in the co-creation process of the b2b platform. Further, they collaborated with EDIH Adria and drafted the Digitalisation Action Plan. IPTPO started to design their digital solution. MCC-AETIB: develop and assess the feasibility of the ideas contained in the Local Action. AETIB did benchmarking research on existing B2B platforms in the Balearic Islands used by hotel chains to purchase local foods and decided to support an already existing platform that promotes the commercialisation of local products among the tourist sectors. Also, they have assessed funding possibilities as well as the willingness of producers and tourist businesses in the pilot area to join the platform. They had several meetings (16) with stakeholders to ensure coordination, long-term sustainability, and adoption of the solution. Further, they advanced in the organization of the digital laboratories, by defining 4 training sessions and organized the 1st international course “Digital Innovation Hubs: an introduction” that took place on 2nd of July. SWG-RRD: started promoting honey production, local products and natural and cultural heritage of the pilot area. Trainings and workshops were organized ion cooperation, LAGs, leadership, advantages and disadvantages of cooperatives. Also, they started to test the application of drones in agriculture by mapping and prescription map creation to showcase the potential use in agriculture activities. Also, they decided to develop a prototype for monitoring the behavior of the bees in the beehive. e-Zavod: started the implementation of the Local Action Plan for the area of Slovenske gorice through a participatory co-design process involving local stakeholders. They started the process for the establishment of the Slovenske Gorice Experience Cooperative (SGX Coop), a community-led platform aiming to develop sustainable and experiential tourism, promote digital innovation, and strengthen local entrepreneurship. Also, they launched a Digital Innovation Hub and operationalised a digital lab in Slovenske gorice. Five tailored training modules were designed to be implemented through national workshops. READ SA.: supported the continuation of the Aegean Cuisine network that enters a new era of digital communication, strengthening its brand and boosting the gastronomic and cultural profile of the Dodecanese. The project website and social media have been updated on a regular basis. In this period, we had more than 30 posts in our social media. The followers are as follow: Facebook 101, LinkedIn: 113 and Instagram: 53. All partners have contributed with news articles of their activities. Two transnational exchanges were organized. The first in Porec, Istria (18-19/2/2025) and the second in Appennino Pistoiese (Tuscany) on 19-21/3/2025. The transnational exchanges were an opportunity for partners and their stakeholders to meet, exchange and see best practises from the pilot areas. The 4th project meeting was organized in Skopjie (25-26 /6/2025) discussing the setup of the innovation community cooperatives and the implementation of immersive experiences. Study visits was also organized for exchange of experience and inspiration for the partners. Last, we participated to all the activities of the ISE Mission. We are participating to the mentoring programme where REVIVE is a giver to AgroID Social Cooperative Enterprise: focused on empowering small-scale farmers.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The project delivers innovative solutions for the implementation of collaborative business models, emphasising the uptake of sustainable development pathways addressing directly the EUSAIR 3rd and 4th pillars: - Environmental quality - by ensuring sustainable economic models that contribute to the protection of biodiversity and valorisation of ecosystem services; - Sustainable tourism - by providing means to develop new diversified tourism offer (tourism is one of the sector with highest development potential in project targeted areas), also by implementing circular economy practices, and by enhancing sustainable and responsible tourism capacities. The partnership and territories of the AIR involved in REVIVE (Croatia, Greece, Slovenia, North Macedonia, and through SWG RRD all Western Balkan countries) have the capacity to connect the results of the project to those developed in the framework of the EUSAIR, contributing directly to the fulfillment of these objectives
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion With its actions REVIVE will provide concrete solutions for the social, economic and environmental revival of the types of territories included in the Alpine macroregional space.
    • The project will contribute directly to: - the 1st Thematic Policy Area "Economic Growth and Innovation" and the work done by the EUSALP Action Group 2 ("To increase the economic potential of strategic sectors") by providing replicable solutions to improve the economic and social environment in the Alpine macroregion; - 3rd thematic policy area Environment and energy by providing innovative tools to protect natural resources and their economic use and to maintain biodiversity and ecosystem services
    • EuropeanGreenDeal Through its actions REVIVE contributes in tackling climate and environmental-related challenges set by the European Green Deal. First of all, the project will directly contribute to preserving and restoring ecosystems and biodiversity through the development of economic models which aims to respond to the territorial challenges among which the abandonment of territories. Depopulation is one of the first causes of impoverishment and loss of biodiversity. Further, the testing digital innovation solutions activities will contribute to create a toxic-free environment, through the set-up of indicators to monitor and measure the relation between products/services and environment variables. Finally, REVIVE adopts a participatory approach, involving local communities and enhancing the transnational cooperation in working towards a more sustainable future, in initiatives that seek to combine economic competitiveness, societal pull and technology push.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 REVIVE addresses the need to boost innovation capacities through the implementation of collaborative business models enhancing the economic tissue bridging the gap between MED territories. It will do so through transnational cooperation and coordination of stakeholders with the aim to accelerate the smart and green transitions of the Mediterranean inland areas. Through its actions the project will contribute to both pillars of the Territorial Agenda 2030: 1) Just Europe - by strengthening innovation capacity, competitiveness of the Mediterranean internal areas, by strengthening territorial cohesion across borders, by encouraging all governance levels to unleash the unique potential of territories through integrated and cooperative approaches; 2) Green Europe - by strengthening innovation capacities and empowering local and regional communities to protect, rehabilitate and utilise their environments, landscapes, material and immaterial cultural assets
    • Other The rural areas are the main target territory of REVIVE. The project will contribute directly to the EU Rural Action Plan by delivering actions and solutions for stronger, more connected, resilient and prosperous rural areas and communities. The project will contribute by: 1) enabling the communities to take active part in economic and social development of their territories, also by applying place-based and integrated approaches; 2) providing innovative services and making the most of digital tools to encourage innovation pathways; 3) connecting rural areas by strengthening cross-border and transnational cooperation;4) by diversifying economic activities to new sectors with positive effects on employment and making an attractive environment for companies
    • Other EU SME Strategy for a sustainable and digital Europe. The project will contribute directly to one of the key actions of the strategy, the creation and establishment of Digital Innovation Hubs. The REVIVE network of Mediterranean DIHs will support companies to improve business/production processes, products, or services using digital technologies, to test new technologies, access financing advice and market intelligence and benefit from transnational networking opportunities.
    • Other New European Bauhaus. REVIVE incorporates in its methodological approach the values of the New European Bauhaus Initiative: 1) sustainability, from climate goals to circularity, zero pollution, and biodiversity; 2) aesthetics, quality of experience and style beyond functionality; 3) inclusion, from valuing diversity to securing accessibility and affordability
    • The New European Bauhaus brings citizens, experts, businesses, and institutions together to reimagine sustainable living in Europe and beyond, such as REVIVE brings the same categories together to reimagine their areas. In addition to creating a platform for experimentation and connection, REVIVE supports positive change also by providing access to EU funding for beautiful, sustainable, and inclusive projects in the pilot areas. REVIVE coherently with NEB, intends leveraging our green and digital challenges to transform our lives for the better and intend to address complex societal problems together through co-creation.
    • Other EU Adaptation Strategy. By delivering solutions to fight depopulation and the consequent abandonment of territories, the shortage of jobs, the poor digitalization knowledge, difficulty in the conservation of cultural and environmental heritage , REVIVE will contribute to adaptation to climate change. It will do so thanks to the improved maintenance of the territory, and by introducing new economic activities based on economic and social sustainability
