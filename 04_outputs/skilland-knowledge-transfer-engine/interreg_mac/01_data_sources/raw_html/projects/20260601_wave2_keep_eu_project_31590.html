Green Participatory Budgeting for Local Environmental Action and sustainable Future

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
The Green B-LEAF project addresses the urgent challenge of climate resilience and sustainability in rural Mediterranean communities. These areas, facing the dual pressures of environmental degradation and limited resources, require innovative solutions to adapt to climate change and enhance their sustainability. The project's overall objective is to empower these communities through participatory budgeting, providing them with the tools and knowledge to drive climate-resilient and sustainable transformations. By equipping local authorities with hyper-local climate data and fostering community-driven green projects, Green B-LEAF aims to create lasting, impactful change. The expected change involves transforming rural communities into proactive agents of climate resilience. This will be achieved through the development and implementation of Green Participatory Budgeting (Green PB) models tailored to local needs. The main outputs include comprehensive data mapping reports, identified climate vulnerabilities, Green PB methodologies, and a toolkit for climate-focused budget analysis. These outputs will benefit local authorities, community members, and stakeholders, providing them with the necessary resources to plan, finance, and execute effective climate adaptation strategies. Our approach leverages a cross-border, transnational, and inter-regional collaboration to maximize impact. By fostering knowledge exchange and collaborative design among Mediterranean communities, the project ensures that best practices and innovative solutions are shared and adapted across the region. This approach not only enhances the effectiveness of local interventions but also builds a resilient network of communities capable of supporting each other in the face of climate challenges. The participatory nature of the project ensures that solutions are community-driven, fostering a sense of ownership and commitment. The Green B-LEAF project will build synergy with other initiatives such as the Cities of Tomorrow and the NEBA project, which support integrating New European Bauhaus values into regional planning. By aligning with these projects, The overall objective of the Green B-LEAF project is to enhance climate resilience and sustainability in rural Mediterranean communities. By equipping local authorities and residents with the necessary tools and knowledge, the project seeks to foster data-driven planning and budgeting decisions that are responsive to hyper-local climate data. This participatory approach will enable residents to propose and implement green projects, thereby actively contributing to their community's climate adaptation and sustainability efforts. The expected change from this project is a significant improvement in the capacity of these communities to address climate vulnerabilities, resulting in more sustainable and resilient local economies. The main outputs of the Green B-LEAF project include: - Green B-LEAF Participatory Budgeting Methodology and Policy Tool: A comprehensive methodology tailored for rural municipalities to integrate participatory budgeting with climate adaptation strategies. - Green Budget Tagging (GBT) Toolkit: A tool designed to help communities assess and manage the environmental impact of their budgetary decisions. - Online Manual and Toolkit for Green PB Replication: Resources to guide other communities in adopting and implementing the Green PB model. - Training and Capacity-Building Workshops: Programs aimed at enhancing the skills and knowledge of local authorities, decision-makers, and stakeholders in participatory budgeting and climate resilience. - Establishment of a Transnational Network and Community of Practice: Platforms for continuous knowledge exchange and collaboration among project partners and external stakeholders. - Real-World Pilot Projects: Implementation and validation of Green PB models in participating communities to refine and demonstrate their effectiveness. The beneficiaries of these outputs include local authorities, community leaders, policymakers, and residents of the participating rural communities. By engaging these stakeholders in the project's activities, the Green B-LEAF project ensures that the tools and methodologies developed are practical, effective, and widely adopted. What sets Green B-LEAF apart is its focus on rural areas, where participatory budgeting is less common, and its integration of NEB (New European Bauhaus) principles, emphasizing beauty, sustainability, and togetherness. The project's innovative use of Green Budget Tagging (GBT) and simulation of climate scenarios ensures that interventions are both effective and future-proof. By involving residents in every step, from data collection to project implementation, Green B-LEAF not only addresses immediate climate challenges but also builds long-term resilience and sustainability through community empowerment and engagement.

Expected Achievements (FR):
Equip rural communities and local authorities with comprehensive, hyper-local climate data and participatory budgeting tools to foster data-driven planning and budgeting decisions that align with the specific needs of rural communities. This objective aims to enhance their capacity for effective planning and financing for climate change adaptation and energy transition, while complying with the New European Bauhaus (NEB) principles of sustainability, inclusiveness, and aesthetic quality. Enhance the capacity of public authorities in rural communities for effective planning and financing of climate change adaptation and energy transition initiatives. This will be achieved through a holistic and transdisciplinary approach to addressing green living areas issues, integrating sustainability, resilience, and aesthetic principles. Furthermore, developing a framework to enable residents of rural communities to propose and implement green projects that address their specific climate challenges, fostering more more sustainable living areas by developing, piloting, and validating Green Participatory Budgeting (PB) models that incorporate community-driven decision-making processes. To enhance the skills and knowledge of local authorities, decision-makers, policy experts, and community stakeholders in rural communities. The focus is on providing practical training and resources that enable these groups to effectively implement Green Participatory Budgeting (Green PB) and climate adaptation strategies. By the end of the project, these participants will be equipped to integrate green budgeting practices into their local governance, fostering sustainable development and climate resilience. To foster transnational collaboration and knowledge exchange among rural communities, enhancing the collective impact of Green PB initiatives and promoting sustainable development across the Mediterranean region. This will be achieved by establishing robust networks and communities of practice, integrating with thematic communities, and monitoring the project’s carbon footprint to ensure environmentally responsible implementation.

By empowering local communities to participate in decision-making and implement green projects, Green B-LEAF will help to protect and restore ecosystems, improve water and air quality, and reduce the vulnerability of these communities to the impacts of climate change.
