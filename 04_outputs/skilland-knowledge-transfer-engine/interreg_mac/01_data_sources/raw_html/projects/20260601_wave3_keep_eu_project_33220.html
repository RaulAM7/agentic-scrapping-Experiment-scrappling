Community-based Approach for Resilient Mediterranean Health

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 160. Measures to improve the accessibility, effectiveness and resilience of healthcare systems (excluding infrastructure) ~ SUMMARY: CAREMED addresses the challenge of improving access to healthcare by promoting a shift from institutional to community-based care, focusing on non-communicable diseases (NCDs). In the Med region NCDs account for 80% of healthcare utilization and expenditures. They are an increasingly critical public health and development need, exacerbated in case of pandemics and concerning an especially large, disadvantaged and vulnerable social group. Currently, NCDs are mainly treated at hospital level and are the most appropriate to shift to community-level, freeing also hospital capacity for more secondary/tertiary healthcare activities. Building on project partners/associates experiences and within a structured cooperation framework (WP3), CAREMED pilots innovative proactive healthcare models (WP4-5) and digital HIMS solutions (WP6) that transform NCD management from hospital-centric to community-focused. This not only enhances patients’ health outcomes but also generates cost savings, impacting on long-term sustainability of NCD care and on the resilience and crisis-preparedness of overall health systems (project legacy). By implementing the Active Patient Care Model (APCM) and the Chronic Care Model (CCM) in 30 PHCs (684 patients) in Tunisia, Lebanon, Turkey, Italy and Spain, CAREMED emphasizes prevention, health education and patient engagement, resulting in reduced hospitalizations and prevented complications. The project targets 10 groups (TGs), including national/international stakeholders, healthcare professionals, pharmacists and patients, with key outputs of capacity building, personalized care plans, peer-to-peer focus groups, collaborative healthcare networks, improved HIMS, community awareness and screening days, improved health infrastructure and equipment. CAREMED implementation contributes to build a sustainable, resilient healthcare framework, improving health outcomes, reducing costs, and strengthening community health in the Mediterranean Region. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Health and social services

Relevant linked projects:

  • Essaha Aziza - Programme to Support the Health Sector in Tunisia (ENI/2016/39819),financed by the European Union in partnership with the Ministry of Health (EN) | The “Essaha Aziza” programme, based on a previous EU support programme to the Ministry of Health and the recommendations of the Societal Dialogue, aims to expand universal coverage of essential front-line care to all citizens to achieve the strategic objectives of the national health policy. In particular, ESSAHA Aziza aims at improving the performance of the primary healthcare system across 13 governorates in Tunisia, through the provision of specialized medical equipmentand enhancement of HIMS systems. In particular, the pilot use of the digitalized medical dossier in both district hospitals and PHCs has been implemented in the Nabeul district.
  • In line with the National Strategical Plan for Digital Tunisia 2020, CAREMED builds upon the ESSAHA AZIZA's implementation approach and further scale it up by piloting the informatized medical dossier in other 10 PHCs (O.6.1) and by providing capacity building for health personnel on NCDs Surveillance, Monitoring and Reporting (O.6.1). The informatized medical dossier is exploited by CAREMED as it is property of the Tunisian Ministry of Health, who is partner in the project and which, through PP3 and its IT Centre, ensure the access to the software. (EN), HEARTS: Technical package for Cardiovascular Disease Management in Primary Health Care, financed by WHO (EN) | The WHO HEART technical package presents a strategic framework for enhancing cardiovascular health within countries. Comprising six modules and an implementation guide, it is tailored to assist Ministries of Health in fortifying cardiovascular disease management within primary healthcare settings. These modules, offering practical, step-by-step guidance, are accompanied by a comprehensive technical document that elucidates the rationale and structure behind this integrated approach to addressing non-communicable diseases. Currently the WHO HEARTs is implemented in Tunisia and Jordan.
  • CAREMED will leverage the outcomes of the WHO HEARTS Technical package for CVDs to facilitate the implementation of APCM and CCM both for the capacity building of healthcare staff (O.4.1, O.5.1) and NCDs patients (O.4.1, O.4.3) as well as for the development of tailored-made Individual Care Plan (O.5.2) in designated PHCs. The results will then be scaled up to other PHCs in Tunisia and additional Mediterranean countries, including Turkey and Lebanon. By collaborating with WHO country office (Tunisia) and with WHO EMRO, CAREMED will be able to access the outputs and scale them up. Furthermore, in A.2.5.1, CAREMED will take into account other WHO technical packages focused on NCDs prevention: MPOWER for tobacco control, ACTIVE for physical activity promotion, SHAKE for salt consumption reduction, and REPLACE for diet trans fats elimination. (EN), CURE: Enhancing the healthcare and protection provision of 16 Social Development Centers across Lebanon (ISOSEP – TF-MADAD/2020T04.253), financed by AICS (EN) | Financed by AICS in collaboration with the Ministry of Social Affairs (MOSA) in 16 Social Development Centres (SDCs) in Lebanon, the project focuses on strengthening the provision of health services to local communities (especially women and children) through community health and social screening to tackle NCDs (in particular, CVDs). Moreover, the project aims at mapping the secondary health service providing in Lebanon to establish an effective referral system between primary and secondary line.
  • The CAREMED proposal is in alignment with the CURE project, given that healthcare services in Lebanon are collaboratively overseen by the Ministry of Social Affairs (MoSA) and the Ministry of Public Health (MoPH), respectively managing SDCs a. CAREMED seeks to extend the impact of the CURE project by launching awareness campaigns and organizing screening events for NCDs, such as Cardiovascular Diseases (CVDs), at selected PHCs beyond the scope of the CURE project's coverage area (Activity 2.5.2). Furthermore, the service mapping developed in the CURE project will be leveraged in Output 5.3: indeed, the Individual Care Plans identified the patients needs for referring tospecialized services that can be identified in the service mapping. the Individual Care Plans will identify patients' needs for referral to specialized services, which can be identified through the service mapping. CAREMED will utilize these outputs effectively, benefiting from PP1's role as the Lead Partner applicant of the CURE project, and by involving MoSA, local, and international NGOs in LTCs 1 to 6 activities (WP3). (EN), Patient Active Programme of the Balearic Island, funded by Gerencia de Attestation Primaria de Mallorca (EN) | The Active Patient Programme implemented in the Balearic Islands is an integral component of the Plan for the Care of People with Chronic Illnesses 2016-2021 and the Social Strategy for Health Promotion of the Balearic Islands, under the Ministry of Health. Simultaneously, it aligns with the Strategy for Addressing Chronic Illness and the Health Promotion Strategy of the Ministry of Health, Social Services, and Equality. This program aims to enhance the health and well-being of patients with NCDs and their caregivers by fostering peer support, providing guidance, and offering training mechanisms to empower them to manage their care more independently and effectively.
  • In terms of output, the CAREMED project will utilize the training program developed by the Active Patient Programme in the Balearic Island, customizing it for healthcare professionals and NCDs patients in the targeted Partner Countries (PPs) – Tunisia, Lebanon, Turkey, and Italy - through the trasnational cooperation of TTC2. Additionally, CAREMED will expand the impact by providing capacity-building to healthcare personnel in the PPs (Output 4.1), selecting and training Active Patient Trainees (Output 4.1), and facilitating peer-to-peer focus groups with NCDs patients (Output 4.3) within these countries. These outputs will be effectively leveraged through the coordination of PP2-GAPM, the lead implementer of the Active Patient Programme in the Balearic Islands. (EN), Strengthening of the healthcare system and the strategic and operational capacities of public sector stakeholders, funded by Italian Agency of Development cooperation – AICS (EN) | Funded by AICS, the project aimed to offer technical support to the Ministry of Health in creating management tools related to the decentralization of healthcare services. This was achieved through knowledge exchange workshops aimed at capitalizing on best practices with Italian local health entities. Additionally, the project undertook the renovation and equipping of four Primary Healthcare Centers (PHCs) in the Kesserine region. It also conducted capacity-building sessions for 200 healthcare professionals in the delivery of primary healthcare services, focusing on maternal and newborn health as well as Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs). Furthermore, the project organized six awareness campaigns on NCDs, involving 1000 citizens. CSG is the Lead Partner of the project.
  • As part of its output, the CAREMED project will expand upon the achievements of this initiative by targeting two different regions in Tunisia (Beja and Kebili) for implementation. In these areas, CAREMED will conduct awareness campaigns for citizens on NCDs prevention and early diagnosis (Activity 2.5.1), provide capacity-building sessions for healthcare staff on NCDs community-based care models (Output 4.1, 5.1, 6.1), and rehabilitate and equip two PHCs in Tunisia, Lebanon, and Turkey (Output 4.2). Moreover, the activity aimed at sharing and capitalizing on best practices between Italian and Tunisian health stakeholders will be expanded through Technical Transfer and Capacity-building activities, also involving Lebanon, Turkey, and Spain (WP3). CAREMED will have access to the project's results through LP-CSG, the lead implementer of the project, and thanks to PP3, which benefited from the project. (EN), JADECARE: Joint Action on implementation of digitally enabled integrated person-centred care (HaDEA -951442), co-funded by the European Union (EN) | ADECARE leverages four successful practices from prior EU-funded projects and initiatives, namely:
  • - The Basque Health Strategy in Aging and Chronicity.
  • - Catalonia’s Prevention and Chronic Care Program.
  • - The OptiMedis model in Germany.
  • - The digitally enabled care pathways from the Norrbotten region in Sweden.

Furthermore, it seeks to enhance health outcomes for patients by advancing chronic disease management and embracing digital health technologies such as telehealth and electronic records in primary and integrated care settings. The project entails transferring and adapting these practices to other participating regions, promoting collaboration and knowledge sharing.
  • CAREMED facilitates the transnational discussions on Health Information management System and Survailance (TTC4) and develops the capcity building and related materials for healthcare staff in Output 6.2, drawing insights from the best practices identified by the JADECARE project and the guidelines it has developed. CAREMED will exploit the outputs by inviting JADECARE's partners, namely the Regional Health Agency (ARS) for the Tuscany region, to participate in both LTC4 (IT) and TTC4. (EN), TSI Digital Skills: Digital Skills To Increase Quality And Resilience Of The Health System In Italy (ProMIS through the TSI – Technical Support Instrument 2021), funded by the European Union (EN) | The TSI Digital Skills project presents a comprehensive strategy aimed at enhancing the digital proficiency of healthcare personnel in Primary Healthcare Centers (PHCs) across Italy. It encompasses various components, including mapping existing national and regional initiatives to assess and expand digital best practices, identifying synergies with initiatives in other sectors, and reviewing international best practices for potential implementation in selected regions or hospitals. The project underscores the importance of ongoing engagement with stakeholders such as health departments, local health units, scientific societies, universities, professional associations, and civil society. Additionally, capacity development efforts, including workshops and seminars, will prioritize cultural change and change management for both healthcare workers and authorities.
  • CAREMED project will leverage the outcomes of the TSI Digital Skills initiative in Italy to contribute to both national (LTC 4) and transnational exchanges (TTC 4) on Health Information Management Systems (HIMS) and Surveillance. This contribution aims to facilitate scaling up these initiatives in all Partner Countries (PPs) (Output 3.1, 3.2, 3.4). Furthermore, these results will support capacity-building activities for healthcare staff in targeted PHCs under Output 6.1. Healthcare personnel at designated PHCs have received training on NCDs prevention, diagnosis, and treatment, with a specific emphasis on chronic kidney diseases and chronic respiratory diseases (e.g., pulmonary cancer; chronic obstructive pulmonary disease). Additionally, staff capacity-building efforts have included a comprehensive workshop on NCD Screening and Referral Training.
  • The CAREMED project will leverage the training program developed for NCDs Screening and Referral Training by integrating it into the capacity-building initiatives conducted for healthcare personnel at targeted PHCs (Output 5.1), facilitated by the support of LTCs 3 (in Lebanon) and TTC3 for adaptation to other Partner Countries. Furthermore, CAREMED will expand the impact of the project by conducting capcity building for healthcare staff addressing two additional NCDs in Lebanon - diabetes and cardiovascular disease - in both WP4 and 5. Moreover, capacity-building efforts will target PHCs under the Ministry of Health in Lebanon, alongside those operated by CARITAS LEBANON. This dual approach aims to reinforce the overall healthcare system. The outputs of these efforts will be effectively exploited, thanks to the involvement of A002-CARITAS Lebanon, the lead implementer of the project in Lebanon, who will actively participate in LTC3 and TTC3. (EN), Chronic Care Model for the implementation of Proactive Healthcare at the local level, implemented in Tuscany region (IT) since the adoption of the Regional Health Plan 2008-2010 (EN) | Implemented since 2009 in the Tuscany Region (Italy), and currently underway in the Azienda Sanitaria Locale- ASL Tuscany Sud, the project aims to cultivate a "proactive healthcare" approach. This model integrates traditional "wait-and-see" medicine, typically geared towards acute illnesses, with a focus on addressing the health needs of NCDs patients before the onset or exacerbation of their conditions. It manages illnesses in a manner that slows their progression, ensuring patients receive tailored interventions based on their risk levels. At the local level, the Chronic Care Model (CCM) is the reference model recommended by the Regional Health Plan for implementing this innovative healthcare approach. The CCM involves the development of ICP-Individual Care Plans for NCDs patients, which highlight their health and social needs and establish a close monitoring system to prevent complications and ensure proper management through community-level service provision.
  • Regarding outputs, the CAREMED project will draw upon the training program developed for health/social staff by the CCM in the Tuscany region. This program will be customized for healthcare professionals and NCDs patients in the targeted Partner Countries (PPs) – Tunisia, Lebanon, Turkey, and Spain – through transnational cooperation facilitated by TTC3. Additionally, CAREMED will extend its impact by providing capacity-building to healthcare personnel in the PPs (Output 5.1) and developing Individual Care Plans (Output 5.2) for NCDs patients to enhance health outcome monitoring and prevent complications. During the proposal phase, CAREMED has already initiated the exchange of practices with both ASL Toscana Sud and Regional Health Agency (ARS), facilitated by the networking of LP-CSG. These outputs will be effectively leveraged through collaboration between LP-CSG and the above-mentioned public health entities, involving them in both LTC3 (IT) and TCC3. (EN)
