Capturing and Storing Atmospheric CO2 for Improvement of Soil Quality

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Deux grands défis actuels sont : comment réduire le CO2 dans l'atmosphère et comment préserver la qualité du sol. Trop de carbone dans l'air provoque l'effet de serre et trop peu de carbone dans le sol réduit sa qualité. Le projet préparera les bases scientifiques pour réduire le CO2 dans l'air et le stocker dans le sol en utilisant des techniques de culture du carbone, un sujet innovant dans l'UE ; sa politique ayant émergé en 2021 il est donc urgent de définir rapidement des normes, des procédures de surveillance et des modèles socio-économiques pour l’avenir. Le projet CARBON 4 SOIL QUALITY préparera une boîte à outils pour la culture du carbone comprenant : une liste de valeurs organiques du sol ; une méthodologie pour analyser le carbone organique et le suivi de la qualité du sol ; un guide des techniques de culture du carbone ; des modèles commerciaux d'agriculture du carbone ; des recommandations pour le crédit carbone agricole et la certification environnementale. Un plan d'action sera préparé au niveau transnational pour planifier l’essai envisageant au moins 5 sites dans différents pays, dont au moins un pays IAP et un nouveau territoire Interreg Euro-MED (Bulgarie, Estrémadure). Une coopération transnationale est nécessaire pour étudier différentes zones climatiques méditerranéennes (subtropicales, océaniques, semi-arides, arides), différentes conditions d'érosion et différents types de sols. Le suivi du projet testera les techniques de culture du carbone pour augmenter la matière organique du sol ce qui améliorera les éléments nutritifs des plantes, augmentera le stockage de l'eau et fournira un habitat aux organismes pour améliorer la biodiversité du sol. La culture du carbone est considérée comme l'une des actions climatiques du Green Deal européen et les politiques européennes concernées seront développées entre 2023 et 2030. Ce projet, s'il est approuvé, deviendra un exemple phare de la réduction du CO2 de l’Euro-MED et un « showcase » pour l'UE.

Description (EN):
Two of the biggest challenges of human civilization are how to reduce CO2 in the atmosphere and how to preserve the soil quality. Too much carbon in the air causes greenhouse effect, and too little carbon in the soil reduces soil quality. Proposed project will prepare scientific foundations for reducing CO2 in the air and storing it into the soil, using SUSTAINABLE SOIL MANAGEMENT, called "CARBON FARMING". Since carbon farming is innovative topic in EU (policy emerged in 2021), there is an urgent need to quickly define standards, monitoring procedures and socio-economic models to test carbon farming in future. CARBON 4 SOIL QUALITY project will prepare "Toolbox for carbon farming" consisting of (i) Catalogue of soil organic reference values, (ii) Methodology for organic carbon analysis and soil quality monitoring (iii), Guidelines for carbon farming techniques, (iv) carbon farming business models, (v) Recommendations on agriculture carbon credit schemes and environmental certification systems. Action plan will be prepared on the transnational level to properly plan testing phase. It will consider at least 5 testing sites in different countries, including at least one IPA country and one new Interreg Euro-MED territory. Transnational cooperation is needed for studying different Mediterranean climate zones (sub-tropical, oceanic, semi-arid, arid), different erosion conditions and different types of soil. Project follow-up will test carbon farming techniques to support farmers with practical knowledge, how to increase soil organic matter. This will improve plant nutrients, increase soil water storage and provide good habitat for soil living organisms, which enhances soil biodiversity. Carbon farming is considered as one of the European Green Deal climate actions and related EU policies will be developed in the 2023-2030 period. If approved, this project could become SUSTIAINABLE SOIL MANAGEMENT lighthouse example of Euro-MED territory and showcase for the EU.

Actual Achievements (EN):
Management Activities: In the first reporting period, all project partners familiarized themselves with the project application form. A kick-off meeting was organized and attended by all partners, where the project framework, management, communication, and reporting activities were presented. The project timeline, deliverables, and roles and responsibilities of the partners were outlined. Several online meetings were held to discuss WP1 activities and management and communication tasks. Communication Activities: The communication strategy was prepared, and participation in communication manager training was completed. A presentation outlining communication responsibilities for partners was developed. Social media channels (LinkedIn and Facebook) were managed, and partners were coordinated for post publishing. A press release about the kick-off meeting was prepared, and the official project website was launched. All partners posted the project poster and mentioned the project on their official websites. WP1 Activities: Activity 1.1: Soil organic matter reference values for different soil types were defined through the incorporation of relevant documents and online meetings to achieve deliverable D1.1.1: Catalogue of Soil Organic Matter Reference Values. Activity 1.2: Organic carbon analysis standards and monitoring procedures were adapted through the use of provided documents and online meetings to achive deliverable D1.2.1: Methodology for Organic Carbon Analysis and Soil Quality Monitoring. Activity 1.4: Carbon farming socio-economic models were defined, and a plan for secondary research on the socio-economic impacts of carbon farming, including sustainability aspects, was prepared. Implementation of WP3: Activity 3.1: Participation in the Carbon Footprint Coordinators Group was completed, and the Carbon Footprint Monitoring and Assessment Tool was reviewed. A presentation with an action plan for all project partners was prepared. Management -Organised and implemented the kick-off meeting in Bled, Slovenia. -Organised and implemented the project meeting in Bar, Montenegro. -Initiated collaboration with other Euro-MED projects, fostering synergies and exploring opportunities for joint activities. Communication -Produced and published the official project video. -Completed and regularly updated the project website with relevant news, documents, and event announcements. -Established and maintained active social media channels (LinkedIn and Facebook), sharing project content and promoting visibility. -Participated in events organised by the Natural Heritage Mission, enhancing capitalisation and knowledge exchange. -Developed and implemented the project’s communication strategy, including key messages, target audiences, and dissemination tools. Implementation of WP1 Activity 1.1 – Definition of Soil Organic Matter (SOM) Reference Values We started with the development of the Catalogue of Soil Organic Matter Reference Values by reviewing proposed templates and methodologies. Input data were gathered, reviewed, and aligned with regional datasets to ensure consistency across the Euro-MED area. The deliverable was finalised based on shared contributions and joint coordination. Activity 1.2 – Adaptation of Organic Carbon Analysis Standards We began implementing this activity by reviewing the proposed methodology and ensuring its compatibility with regional soil monitoring systems. The draft document was reviewed and finalised in collaboration with expert inputs. A common approach for organic carbon analysis and quality monitoring was established. Activity 1.4 – Definition of Carbon Farming Socio-Economic Models We launched the activity by drafting the main concept and outlining the structure of the socio-economic models. A preliminary document was prepared, defining four model types (within and outside the agri-food chain, farm level, and public sector). Reporting templates were developed to collect structured input from all partners. Work continues on refining the models with a sustainability focus. Implementation of WP2 Activity 2.1 – Strategic Framework for Soil Quality Improvement We started the development of a strategic approach to improve soil quality in Mediterranean climates. A draft protocol and methodology were prepared, including a PEST analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological) to guide contributions from all regions involved. Initial discussions focused on aligning soil management practices with regional policy contexts. Activity 2.2 – Development of Training Materials for Carbon Farming We prepared training content aimed at agricultural advisors and practitioners. The materials cover key topics such as soil quality, the carbon cycle, carbon farming principles, and carbon credit schemes. An online course format was proposed, including presentations, brochures, videos, and interactive elements such as quizzes and a glossary. Activity 2.5 – Policy Impact Assessment We initiated dialogue with national stakeholders to assess the project’s relevance at both European and regional levels. Efforts focused on raising awareness of sustainable soil management practices and collecting feedback through meetings and consultations. The groundwork for long-term policy integration of carbon farming was established. Implementation of WP3 Activity 3.1 – Monitoring and Offset of the Project Carbon Footprint We started the implementation of this activity by preparing a detailed action plan and communication tools for project partners. Data for the first and second reporting periods were collected, verified, and entered into the official Carbon Footprint Monitoring Tool. We participated in the Carbon Footprint Coordinators Group meeting and provided supporting materials, including a presentation and shared Excel templates to ensure methodological coherence. During the third reporting period, partners worked jointly on different project activities. A project meeting was held in Skopje, North Macedonia, between 21–23 January 2025. The project website and social media channels were regularly updated with relevant and engaging content. In addition, partners actively participated in external events where the project topic was of interest, presenting project results and working on increasing its visibility and recognition. Within WP1, we successfully completed Activity 1.3 – Research about carbon farming techniques most appropriate for the Euro-MED area and finalized Deliverable 1.3.1 – Guidelines for carbon farming techniques. Furthermore, we initiated the implementation of Activity 1.5 – Study of carbon credit schemes and environmental certification systems. Within WP2, the following milestones were achieved: Completion of Activity 2.2 – Preparation of transferable carbon farming training materials and submission of Deliverable 2.2.1 – Training material for carbon farming. Completion of Activity 2.3 – Planning actions for testing carbon farming schemes in the Euro-MED area and submission of Deliverable 2.3.1 – Action plan for testing carbon farming. Currently, preparations for the Final Conference are ongoing.
