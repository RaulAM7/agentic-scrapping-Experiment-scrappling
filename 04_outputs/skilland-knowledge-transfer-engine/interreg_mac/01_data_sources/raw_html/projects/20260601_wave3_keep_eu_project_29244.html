Home / Projects and documents / NaTour4CChange

Governing sustainable tourism in territories with high environmental value: reconnecting tourism and nature for addressing the climate crisis with an ecosystem-based approach

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The Mediterranean is a vulnerable hotspot in the current Climate Emergency. Tourism is and will be one of the most impacted economic sectors, with heightened risks, including flooding and erosion of key coastal assets. The recent Transition Pathway for Tourism and the Glasgow Declaration are building a global momentum for Climate Action in Tourism, but policy makers and destinations need support and new approaches. Nature can be a cornerstone to address this challenge, while ensuring the protection of key destination assets, such as beaches. How? Through climate mitigation and adaptation using ecosystem-based approaches, which can only be accomplished through strong governance, robust planning, and the engagement of the private sector and citizens. NaTour4CChange will build on successful experiences at Med and global level to test solutions for increasing the resilience of coastal destinations in the Mediterranean. The project will set common methods to allow participating regions to assess their tourism-related climate adaptation and mitigation priorities, and take climate action via plans and strategies, supported by cooperative governance. In coastal destinations, cross-sector teams will deliver specific tourism climate action plans, focusing climate adaptation, where Nature-based Solutions will be tested to ensure their feasibility, while innovative destination marketing and communication approaches will engage private stakeholders, visitors and residents in climate action. Finally, NaTour4CChange will promote cross-fertilization among participating regions and destinations, to achieve common methods and to compare the different tested plans and solutions, which will lead to lessons, best practices and policy recommendations. Associated partners and external stakeholders —at regional, national and international scale— will benefit from these learnings in order to make a significant Med contribution to this global challenge.

Description (FR):
La Méditerranée est une zone vulnérable dans l’état d'urgence climatique actuel. Le tourisme est l'un des secteurs économiques les plus touchés, avec des risques accrus, notamment les inondations et l’érosion côtière. Le parcours de transition pour le tourisme et la Déclaration de Glasgow ont créé une dynamique mondiale pour l’action climatique dans le tourisme, mais les décideurs politiques et les destinations ont besoin de soutien et de nouvelles approches. La nature peut être une pierre angulaire pour relever ce défi, tout en assurant la protection des atouts essentiels, tels que les plages. Comment? Grâce à l'atténuation et l'adaptation au changement climatique avec des approches écosystémiques, qui ne peuvent être accomplies que grâce à une gouvernance forte, une planification solide et l'engagement du secteur privé et des citoyens. NaTour4CChange s’appuiera sur des expériences réussies pour tester des solutions visant à accroître la résilience des destinations côtières en Méditerranée. Le projet établira des méthodes communes pour permettre aux régions participantes d'évaluer leurs priorités d'adaptation et d'atténuation liées au tourisme, et d'agir pour le climat à travers des plans et des stratégies, en s'appuyant sur la collaboration. Des équipes intersectorielles fourniront des plans d'action climatique spécifiques au tourisme, en se concentrant sur l’adaptation, où des Solutions fondées sur la Nature seront testées pour garantir leur faisabilité, tandis que des approches novatrices en matière de marketing et de communication engageront les acteurs privés, les visiteurs et les résidents. NaTour4CChange encouragera la fertilisation croisée entre les régions et les destinations pour parvenir à des méthodes communes, ce qui conduira à des leçons, des meilleures pratiques et des recommandations. Les partenaires associés et les parties prenantes externes bénéficieront de ces apprentissages afin d'apporter une contribution de la Méditerranée à ce défi mondial.

Actual Achievements (EN):
Natour4CChange partnership began meeting regularly online in December 2023, after the Programme's approval, to manage the contracting process and initiate project activities. These meetings were organized, coordinated, and moderated by the LP. The kick-off meeting took place via Zoom on March 5, 2024, and an in-person PSC meeting followed in Zagreb on June 17–19, 2024, where further steps for each WP were defined and training for Regions and pilot sites was also provided. In WP1, the first activity focused on launching Act. 1.1 (WP Coordination), resulting in the realization of Deliv. 1.1.1, i.e the Coordination Plan. Carbon footprint monitoring (Act. 1.2) is ongoing, with all partners submitting necessary data following the Community4Tourism meeting in Rome and the June meeting in Zagreb. The first deliv., led by Plan Blue, issued from Act. 1.3, was the designing of an online survey, implementing it, and processing survey results on best practices in climate action for coastal tourism destinations. The survey aimed to identify gaps in climate resilience actions in Mediterranean tourism, and the findings will serve as a baseline for future activities. Deliv. 1.4.1, i.e. the Methodological Framework for assessing coastal tourism-related issues concerning climate change (CC) mitigation, and Deliv. 1.4.2, focused on CC adaptation, were developed to be used by Regions and pilot destinations. These frameworks contain criteria and indicators for climate change mitigation and adaptation tailored to territorial dynamics in each region and pilot site. A training session on these methods was conducted during the June 2024 PSC meeting in Zagreb. Progress has also been made on Act. 1.5 and 1.6, which focus on drafting plans and strategies at the regional and destination levels and identifying enabling conditions for their success. Act. 1.9 (coordinated by IUCN), related to capacity development for pilot regions and destinations and experience exchange, has delivered its first output, i.e. the Capacity Development Plan (Deliv. 1.9.1). This plan outlines training approaches and events throughout the project and includes several activities aimed at facilitating the exchange of best practices and experiences. In Act. 1.11, the partnership has consistently published project-related news on the web, Facebook, and LinkedIn. Additionally, LP and HSPN launched in June 2024 two social media accounts for the project (LinkedIn and YouTube), and the project website was finalized and published on June 18, 2024. The partnership has attended several events, including the Community4Tourism Kick-Off Meeting and EuroMed training events. Internal communication remains active through regular emails and monthly online meetings. The project's Communication Strategy (part of Act. 1.11), developed by HSPN, has been also approved by the partnership. In WP2, the Coordination Plan (Activity 2.1) was developed by CPMR and subsequently approved by the partnership. In Act. 2.2 all the five Regional Coordination Units (RCUs) were established. Each region has provided documents outlining the context, tasks, responsibilities, and functioning of their respective RCUs. In Act. 2.3 the selection of pilot destinations and "Pilot Site Coordinators" is nearly complete, with all pilot destinations chosen except for France, where alternatives are still being considered. Project Regions are finalizing the geographical scope of these destinations and appointing coordinators, under the supervision of the RCUs. in parallel each RCU has started to work for the identification of main CC-related issues in coastal tourism (Act. 2.4) in order to start the draft of an integrated “Regional Tourism Climate Strategy” for mitigating and adapting the whole Regional coastline to the Climate Change. IUCN (WP coordinator), developed the WP3 coordination plan. The first task involved defining for the PP Regions a common set of criteria for selecting and defining pilot destinations based on the IUCN Nature-based Solutions standard. This set of criteria was approved by the partnership. IUCN and CPMR were overseeing the selection of pilot destinations to ensure alignment with project goals. The appointed Pilot Site Coordinators have started establishing the Tourism Climate Action Task Force (Activity 3.2), which aims to engage key public and private stakeholders at the local level. Additionally, they have begun analyzing climate change-related issues, that will allow them to draft their Destination Tourism Climate Action Plan (Activity 3.4), and design/kick off at least one Nature-based Solution. Through these ongoing efforts, the Natour4CChange project is making significant progress across all WPs, setting the stage for impactful climate resilience actions in Mediterranean tourism regions and destinations. The NaTour4CChange project has made significant progress in advancing climate action within coastal tourism. Through active collaboration and coordination among partners, the project has successfully implemented key activities, delivering impactful outputs and fostering regional cooperation. Natour4CChange partnership is meeting regularly online each month. These meetings are organized, coordinated, and reported by the LP. In the 2nd semester 5 PSC meetings took place online and one was held in-person (Almeria - Spain, on 22.11.2024). On 28th of November IFT has organized and moderated an online meeting with 14 Associate partners, to get them informed about first project results developed so far As far as the WP1 “Methods and Learnings is concerned, Carbon footprint monitoring (Act. 1.2) is ongoing, with key data input by each PP in the Programme CO2. Deliv. 1.3.1 “Report on best practices” has been issued in the 1st semester, as well as Deliv. 1.4.1 “methodological frameworks for assessing climate change (CC) mitigation and adaptation for the Regions” and Deliv. 1.4.2 “Methodological framework for assessing the main coastal tourism-related issues concerning CC, for the pilot Destinations”. In this 2nd semester it was decided to merge Deliverables D.1.5.1, D.1.5.2, and D.1.6.1 into one document for improving their friendliness and usability. Deliv. 1.5.1 “Technical report describing the methods and brief on how to apply them to develop the Regional Tourism Climate Strategy/Plan”, Deliv 1.5.2 “Technical report describing the methods and brief on how to apply them, to develop the Destination Tourism Climate Strategy/Plan”, and Deliv 1.6.1 “Technical Report describing the identified enabling conditions, with a checklist for supporting regions” were issued, thanks to the contribution of all the PPs. A specific training session on these methods and tools was held in person in Almeria (Spain) on 21.11.2024, and repeated online on January 15th 2025. As far as the WP2 “Regional Tourism Climate Governance & Policy” is concerned, "Establishment of the Regional Coordination Unit for Climate Action in Tourism” (Act. 2.2) was implemented in all the 5 Regions (although its formalization was delayed in Sardinia and Crete – see below). Pilot destination selection and "Pilot site Coordinator" appointment (Act. 2.3) were accomplished in 5 out of 6 countries (in France the process took longer time – see below). D.2.4.1 “Reports compiling the assessment results in project regions” were finalized by 4 out of 5 Regions (Crete had some troubles – see below). Concerning the “Coordination with the missions governance projects (TCP & IDP) and JS” (Act. 2.8) project’s representatives attended the Dialogue4Tourism conference in Poreč – Croatia on 10 April 2024 and the MED Cluster Climate Change & Biodiversity meeting in Rome on 11.4.2024. Finally, about WP3 “Pilot Destination Resilience”, D.3.1.1 “Criteria for pilot destinations selection” were developed in the 1st semester, while the establishment of a Tourism Climate Action Taskforce (Act. 3.2) took place in 4 out of 6 pilot areas (Crete and French pilotes having some delays – see below). D.3.3.1 “Reports compiling the climate assessment results of each pilot Destination” were finalized by 3 out of 6 pilot areas (Spanish, Crete and French pilotes having some delays – see below). The drafting of the “Destination Tourism Climate Action Strategy/Plan” (Act. 3.4) started in all the 6 pilot areas with the training held in Almeria in November, supported also by a individual support from WP Coordinator and Plan Bleu. As additional contribution (not planned in the project AF), Plan Bleu - with internal resources, using its innovative, open-access tool, MapX - developed interactive online maps on vulnerability degree of project pilot Regions and destinations, based on the analysis carried out by each concerned PP. This represents an added value for Plan Bleu (that is the "Mediterranean Observatory on Environment and Sustainable Development of the Barcelona Convention") but also for the project, that gains a worldwide known "window" on its outputs. During the third reporting period, the NaTour4CChange (NT4CC) partnership strengthened coordination and implementation across all Work Packages, ensuring effective monitoring, technical cooperation, and dissemination of results. Governance was maintained through regular monthly online meetings of the Project Steering Committee (PSC) and Technical Committees. Five PSC meetings were held online and moderated by the Lead Partner, complemented by an in-person meeting in Marseille on 9–10 April 2025. This event was a milestone, combining a formal PSC session with training on Nature-based Solutions (NbS, Act. 1.9), an exchange workshop among pilot destinations, and a study visit, reinforcing learning, peer exchange, and project visibility. Under WP1, activities advanced steadily. Carbon footprint monitoring (Act. 1.2), coordinated by HSPN, was consolidated: partners submitted data on schedule, while reporting for Periods 1 and 2 was finalised and analysed, producing trends and recommendations. Awareness-raising visits to pilot sites were also carried out. NbS work (Act. 1.7) culminated in the publication of Deliverable 1.7.1, NbS for Coastal Destinations Climate Action Manual, prepared by IUCN with Plan Bleu, followed by in-person training in Marseille to strengthen partner capacity. IFT advanced Activity 1.8 on destination marketing and visitor communication, defining methodology, draft structure, and partner consultations. Dissemination and communication (Act. 1.11) were actively pursued: IFT, CPMR, IUCN, and Plan Bleu promoted results at events such as the Dialogue4Tourism session (Brussels, May 2024), the UN Ocean Conference (Nice, 2025), and the EUSAIR Conference (April 2025). Natura Jadera updated its website and social media, uploaded the project video, and prepared content for the official webpage. HSPN produced new communication tools (video, roll-up banner), managed the website and LinkedIn updates, coordinated event logistics, and drafted the first NT4CC Newsletter. In WP2, major progress was made on Regional Tourism Climate Strategies. Five regional assessments (Act. 2.4) were delivered, providing a solid foundation for strategic planning. Based on these, three regions finalised their strategies (Zadar County, Andalusia, and Crete) under Activity 2.5. All partners contributed by preparing inputs on enabling conditions and working through their Regional Coordination Units. Capacity-building was reinforced with a second online training on the Climate Strategy Toolkit (January 2025), complemented by bilateral follow-ups. Cross-learning was promoted through the 1st Transnational Exchange Workshop for Regional Cross-Fertilisation (18 June 2025), organised by CPMR with over 40 participants, including associated partners such as the Balearic Islands. The workshop enabled presentation of strategies, exchange of practices, and peer review by The Travel Foundation. WP3 saw tangible progress in Destination Climate Action Plans and NbS pilots. Six technical assessment reports (Act. 3.3) were delivered, covering Cabo de Gata (Spain), Crete (Greece), Dugi Otok (Croatia), Hutovo Blato (Bosnia and Herzegovina), Villasimius (Italy), and Cap Ferrat (France). These identified climate vulnerabilities and tourism pressures. Work then advanced on Destination Climate Action Plans (Act. 3.4): Deliverable 3.4.1 was submitted by AndTour (Andalusia) and Natura Jadera (Dugi Otok), while four other plans are underway and scheduled for Period 4. NbS design and testing (Act. 3.5) was launched, guided by the NbS Manual and Marseille training. Preparations for interventions began, including Natura Jadera’s wooden walkway at Sakarun beach to address erosion at a high-pressure site. Local workshops, such as Beaulieu-sur-Mer, validated assessments and engaged stakeholders. The first Destination Exchange Workshop (Act. 3.7), held in Marseille, allowed pilot destinations to share methods, identify challenges, and build synergies. Dissemination remained strong and cross-cutting. CPMR promoted NT4CC through its commissions and political meetings, while IUCN and Plan Bleu ensured visibility in global fora. Natura Jadera and AndTour supported outreach via institutional channels and media coverage. The Region of Crete promoted the project locally through World Wetlands Day at Alatsolimni, co-organised with HSPN and local authorities, raising awareness of pilot sites and project objectives. Overall, the third reporting period consolidated NT4CC’s analytical and strategic foundations. Regional and destination assessments were largely completed, three regional strategies finalised, and preparatory steps for NbS interventions launched. Capacity-building and exchange activities reinforced the Mediterranean community of practice on climate-resilient coastal tourism. These results have set the stage for the next period, when remaining strategies will be completed and NbS pilots more fully implemented, ensuring that NT4CC delivers on its objectives.
