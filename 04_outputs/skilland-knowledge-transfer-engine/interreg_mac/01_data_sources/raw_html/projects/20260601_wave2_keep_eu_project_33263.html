Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 160. Measures to improve the accessibility, effectiveness and resilience of healthcare systems (excluding infrastructure) ~ SUMMARY: SALAM project, led by Polyclinic of Bari, brings together technological universities from Italy (POLIBA), Spain (University of Barcelona), Jordan (JUST), and Lebanon (University of Balamand), and the two Caritas from Lebanon and Jordan, intertwining scientific-technological and humanistic-social demands. It addresses the crisis of public health systems in the countries involved in the project, expressed differently but with common aspects such as shortage of healthcare providers and resources,impacting on equity of access to health. Human complexity requires a more holistic approach to healthcare, including highly specialised, competent, flexible, resilient, and cooperative healthcare professionalism. Within Open Innovation methodological framework, which underlies synergies between different, complementary disciplines, SALAM will develop a comprehensive solution that will bring mutual benefits to involved countries, envisaging: Two hospital hubs in Lebanon and Jordan, integrated with telecardiology service, Helis Infarct.NET platform and related equipment, in use at the Polyclinic of Bari, which will be scaled up and uptaken with the support of academic partners. The hubs will include Medical Mobile Units and will contribute to making right to health concrete. Innovative sanitaire protocols for medical staff training and for raising awareness among caregivers and local populations, to ensure the functioning of the HUBs, promote knowledge exchange and empower communities on new medical practices. Transnational Research Center among partner universities on both sides of Mediterranean, which, moving from health needs of territories, will work together to develop accessible medical bio-devices, benefiting everyone - especially those affected by CVDs - so that "no one is left behind" and the value of inclusion can find substance across different nations, in a relationship of mutual enrichment where peace will be direct consequence of well-being for all. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Health and social services, ICT and digital society, Innovation capacity and awareness-raising

PHASE Project, “Promoting e-Health in cb Area by Stimulating local Economies”, INTERREG IPA CBC Italy-Albania-Montenegro Programme 2014-2020 (EN) | Infarct.NET, pilot developed within PHASE project, is a territorial network of ICT structural nodes and equipped ambulances aiming at a prompt and appropriate clinical intervention in early phases of heart-attack (emergency/urgency); it is the first output to be exploited, given its scalability, feasibility and replicability. At the heart of this network stands the platform Helis.
  • Its strength lies in the fact that it represents a solution already fully operative and validated in Italy, Albania and Montenegro, allowing it to process over 16,000-17,000 electrocardiograms per month, which are handled by the telecardiology operational centre, thus detecting 9 to 10 acute heart attacks, which can be immediately directed to cardiac emergency care points or to hemodynamics centres. As the validation and multidimensional analysis of results of this output show, Infarct.NET optimises time of intervention in case of heart attack, reducing complications and improving outcomes, anticipating the first cares even in the ambulance or at patient’s house with the available healthcare operator. It allows the transmission in real time of ECG signals and other biometric data to control rooms with cardiologists remotely supporting the decisions of local nurses or operators.
  • The interconnection between the control rooms in involved countries enables real time second opinion among doctors. The system is also equipped with a clinical Decision Support System allowing automatically an initial report, afterwards validated by the cardiologist, thus making a first diagnosis of the ECG.
  • Within SALAM the Helis platform underlying the operation of Infarct.NET, along with the necessary functional equipment for its operation, will be integrated within the Hospital Hubs in Lebanon and Jordan, providing a tangible contribution to improving health access equity, especially among vulnerable populations and in remote areas. Project In Silico medicine and omics, funded by the Italian Ministry of University and Research under the PNRR call. Total project funding: € 15.628.450,00. (EN) | The primary objective of In Silico Medicine, which will be capitalised within SALAM, is to establish a fully integrated digital data flow between High-Performance Computing (HPC) centers and healthcare facilities.
  • Besides this, its main objectives are:
  • ● To use HPC solvers to develop vertical solutions for In Silico Medicine
  • ● To develop artificial machine learning algorithms to analyze omics data (including radiomics) efficiently, with the final goal to provide reliable correlations and associations.
  • ● To stratify patients for improved diagnoses and more efficacious pharmacological treatments.
  • ● To deploy user-friendly and ready-to-use software packages for the medical and biological community.
  • PoliBA is responsible for the radiomics component of the project, which involves utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze medical images and provide diagnostic and prognostic information, such as predictions of cancer recurrence. This radiomics solution can complement or even replace traditional clinical lab analyses. By enhancing the efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and accuracy of diagnostic and treatment processes, radiomics offers a significant advantage for delivering sustainable healthcare within the SALAM project. By leveraging both local computing resources and cloud-based infrastructures, these digital medicine technologies can scale according to the computational capacity available in each country, ensuring equitable access to advanced analytics. This reduces dependency on physical lab infrastructure, which is often lacking or overburdened in crisis-affected regions, and enables remote diagnostics, allowing specialists to support local clinicians through shared analyses. Moreover, digital medicine technologies as the ones developed for In Silico Medicine, rely substantially on software components that are susceptible to be tailored to the specific needs of the project, exploiting data coming from clinical use, patient outcomes, and user experiences. (EN), 6. Mechanical ventilator of low cost and simple construction with description in open mode: Development and evaluation in a test bank and in a porcine model. Official Code 1381. Project Type Period 06/01/2023 - 05/31/ 2026 (EN) | TN Research Centre will build on experience gained within this project aiming to design, build, and evaluate a low-cost mechanical ventilator for intensive care and publish all the necessary technical information in open-source in view of its replicability without intellectual property costs in low- and middle-income countries. A ventilator will be built that can operate in control and support modes for adult and pediatric patients with severe respiratory failure, built with low-cost electromechanical components available on the online market.
  • This project is expected to finish in 2026, with an in vivo validation in a porcine model. University of Barcelona will also leverage its experience in adapting the device’s design to the specific needs of the local health care users and to the technical and materials availability in the local market.
  • The main outputs of this work-in-progress project can serve as a solid background for SALAM as an example of a medical device for potential development in involved territories, encompassing: the stage of education and training of technicians, ventilators deployment in their hospital hubs, stimulating the local industry of medical devices.
  • (EN), 7. CARDIORG: DEVELOPING A NOVEL MECHANOACTIVE PATCH TO RESTORE CARDIAC TISSUE ORGANIZATION AFTER MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION. Official Code 2021 BP 00106 Grant Type Period 04/01/2023 - 03/31/2026 (EN) | Heart failure following myocardial infarction (MI) is a leading cause of death, increasing due to a sedentary lifestyle and population ageing. Current regenerative approaches to restore myocardial function are focused on adding new functional tissue to the damaged zone. These strategies mostly fail because they are unable to restore the organization of the damaged tissue. This project addresses a key issue that will revolutionise cardiac regenerative medicine: it will develop a mechanoactive cardiac patch able to restore cardiac tissue structure and function after MI. To achieve this goal, the project will understand the tissue structural organisation restoration mechanisms influenced by the mechanical environment and the impact of restoration of tissue organisation on the contractility and electrophysiology of myocardial tissue. University of Barcelona will develop a 3D in vitro model that mimics post-MI tissue. To mimic post-MI tissue in vitro, a novel biomaterial based on decellularised cardiac tissue will be developed, then translating the results to generate a mechanoactive cardiac patch that will be tested in vivo. The results of the project would be the first essential step towards the development of more effective therapies for MI. This project is more focused on research than on immediate application to patients. Within SALAM the results will be scaled leveraging the synergies among partners, incorporating the project knowledge into consortium research. University of Barcelona will provide the PPs with support in training on the project topic of regenerative medicine for CVDs, adapting its collaboration to the actual background and needs of beneficiaries. This project is a background to SALAM since it will make it possible to advance in novel cardiac therapies by promoting advanced education/training for the development of novel medical technologies, potentially applicable in the mid-term in all countries since HF from MI is and will be worldwide increased.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other SALAM Project contributes to:
    • UN SDG 1, target 1.5 strengthening resilience of people in vulnerable situations, and Goal 3 target 3.8 increasing equitable access to healthcare in complex geopolitical scenarios through hospital hubs integrating telecardiology units and the Research Centre to optimise and produce new low cost biomedical devices. Target 3.4 is equally relevant, as the capitalization of the Infarct.net will lead to detect early signs of heart attacks, reducing mortality from CVDs and optimising their treatment. Target 10.2 is also relevant: the increased equity of access to care that SALAM aims for, will contribute to greater social inclusion for all.
    • EU Global Health Strategy, improving equitable access to health services following a human-rights based approach, fostering digitalisation as a fundamental enabler and contributing to shaping the digital health ecosystem globally, supporting countries in training and putting into action healthcare workers,applying a One Health approach by fostering a holistic paradigm to healthcare delivery.
    • European Pillar of Social Rights, principles 11 with reference to the provision of good quality care for children, and 16 (and art. 35 EU Charter of Fundamental Rights as well) as it gives concreteness to the right to timely access to affordable preventive and curative quality care (establishing the hospital hubs and implementing protocols of sanitaire education for population and healthcare professionals).
    • New EU Agenda for Mediterranean with reference to “strengthen resilience, build prosperity and seize the digital transition”, including R&I enhancement, considering SALAM focus on unleashing the potential of telemedicine, in terms of technology and capacity of healthcare personnel to exploit it to ensure equitable access to care in crisis contexts, also in line with EU-Jordan/EU-Lebanon Partnership priorities adopted within the EU Neighborhood Policy.
