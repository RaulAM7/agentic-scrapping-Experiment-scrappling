Systemic circular bioeconomy solutions to support start-up and social driven innovation in rural areas

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
BIOSTARS soutient les communautés rurales de la Méditerranée en les aidant à construire des économies locales plus fortes grâce à des entreprises durables basées sur la biotechnologie. Le projet réunit des startups, des investisseurs et des communautés afin de créer des emplois, de réduire l'impact sur l'environnement et de promouvoir l'innovation sociale. À l'aide d'une boîte à outils spéciale développée dans le cadre de projets antérieurs, BIOSTARS guidera les régions dans la mise en place de villages de startups bioéconomiques. Il propose des solutions pratiques et sociales, des formations et un soutien pour renforcer les compétences écologiques, encourager l'esprit d'entreprise et relier les acteurs locaux dans un effort commun pour développer des économies rurales circulaires et économes en ressources.

Description (EN):
BIOSTARS supports rural communities in the Mediterranean by helping them build stronger local economies through sustainable, bio-based businesses. The project brings together startups, investors, and communities to create jobs, reduce environmental impact, and promote social innovation. Using a special toolbox developed from earlier projects, BIOSTARS will guide regions in setting up bioeconomy startup villages. It offers practical and social solutions, training, and support to boost green skills, encourage entrepreneurship, and connect local players in a shared effort to grow circular, resource-efficient rural economies.

Assembling the place-based innovation ecosystem in transfer territories Provide a common understanding of the Start-up village concept Identify the challenges and development pathways for developing and switching of start-ups, SMEs and social enterprises for a sustainable bioeconomy Selecting outputs from ‘Givers’ based on the identified project portfolio for defining key areas in the development of the BIOSTARS toolbox Deliver an effective BIOSTARS toolbox and validate the proposed toolbox with actors-takers from different sectors and maturity levels, evaluating their feedback and producing the final configuration of the toolbox Creating rapport between givers and takers Delivering tailored training for public authorities to use the BIOSTARS toolbox Delivering tailored training for entrepreneurs to use the BIOSTARS toolbox Providing support to local authorities to capitalize on the BIOSTARS toolbox, establishing the virtual help desk as a hot spot to provide support to SMEs and innovative companies. Setting up a Transnational cluster for transnational and cross-sector collaboration Supporting innovative SMEs, start-ups and social enterprises by organizing workshops on “hot topics” Workshops on the empowerment of youth in the bioeconomy sector bridging the gap between young talent and the bioeconomy industry Creation of the Youth platform, a Repository of ideas and proposals made by local youth as contributes to youth involvement in the bioeconomy Understanding cultural differences and assessing societal readiness in the transfer territories, assisting in strategizing and operating in a place-based ecosystem responding to local needs and dynamics, and addressing society's readiness to adopt and use innovation. Supporting the use of the BIOSTARS toolbox by providing guidelines and recommendations to replicate successful cases across the MED Scaling out of project results developing a transferring framework providing step-by-step guidelines for capitalizing on the transnational cluster Providing support for the development of startup villages on sustainable and circular systemic bioeconomy solutions Leveraging on the Start Up Village Initiative through the Regional Hubs and fostering knowledge exchange and mutual learning Transferability plan for the capitalization and exploitation of the project results beyond the partnership Launch of the BIOSTARS Manifesto to sustain the Transnational Cluster after the project’s end High-level conference on financing innovation in rural green sectors in BiH Contribute to the Result amplification strategy and engage in capitalisation activities with the Institutional Dialogue and Thematic community project.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The project consolidates and delivers solutions for the implementation of systemic circular bioeconomy solutions, start-up and social innovations, emphasising the uptake of sustainable development pathways addressing directly the EUSAIR 3rd pillar: Environmental quality - by ensuring sustainable economic models that contribute to the protection of biodiversity and valorisation of ecosystem services; The partnership and territories of the AIR involved in BIOSTARS (Croatia, Greece, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and through CEI all Western Balkan countries) have the capacity to connect the results of the project to those developed in the framework of the EUSAIR, contributing directly to the fulfillment of these objectives.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion ...
    • EuropeanGreenDeal Through its actions, BIOSTARS contributes in tackling climate and environmental-related challenges set by the European Green Deal. First of all, the project will directly contribute to preserving and restoring ecosystems and biodiversity through the development of economic models that aim to respond to territorial challenges, among which is the abandonment of territories. Depopulation is one of the first causes of impoverishment and loss of biodiversity. Further, digital innovation solutions as a horizontal approach to systemic bioeconomy activities will contribute to creating a toxic-free environment through the set-up of indicators to monitor and measure the relation between products/services and environment variables. Finally, BIOSTARS adopts a participatory approach, involving local communities and enhancing transnational cooperation to work towards a more sustainable future, in initiatives that seek to combine economic competitiveness, societal pull, and technology push.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 BIOSTARS addresses the need to boost innovation capacities through the implementation of systemic bioeconomy solutions that enhance economic tissue and bridge the gap between MED territories. It will do so through transnational cooperation and coordination of stakeholders with the aim of accelerating the smart and green transitions of the Mediterranean rural areas. Through its actions, the project will contribute to both pillars of the Territorial Agenda 2030: 1) Just Europe - by strengthening innovation capacity and competitiveness of the Mediterranean internal areas, by strengthening territorial cohesion across borders, by encouraging all governance levels to unleash the unique potential of territories through integrated and cooperative approaches; 2) Green Europe - by strengthening innovation capacities and empowering local and regional communities to protect, rehabilitate and utilise their environments, landscapes, material and immaterial cultural assets.
    • Other The rural areas are the main target territory of BIOSTARS. The project will contribute directly to the EU Rural Action Plan by delivering actions and solutions for stronger, more connected, resilient and prosperous rural areas and communities. The project will contribute by: 1) enabling the communities to take an active part in the economic and social development of their territories, also by applying place-based and integrated approaches; 2) providing innovative services and making the most of biobased, societal and digital tools to encourage innovation pathways; 3) connecting rural areas by strengthening cross-border and transnational cooperation; 4) by diversifying economic activities to new sectors with positive effects on employment and making an attractive environment for companies.
    • Other EU Adaptation Strategy. By delivering solutions to fight depopulation and the consequent abandonment of territories, the shortage of jobs, the poor digitalization knowledge, and the difficulty in conserving cultural and environmental heritage, BIOSTARS will contribute to adaptation to climate change. It will do so thanks to improved territory maintenance and the introduction of new economic activities based on economic and social sustainability.
    • Other 2030 Greener Med Agenda. BIOSTARS, by means of the BIOSTARS toolbox and all accompanying capacity-building activities, is aligned with the core objective of the 2030 Greener Med Agenda which is to set the framework to coordinate, streamline and promote the efforts in the Mediterranean region – involving UfM member countries and other relevant stakeholders, through a participatory approach, to contribute to the agenda's THEMATIC AXIS 1 to support the transition to a green, circular and socially inclusive economy based on sustainable consumption and production practices and nature-based solutions.
    • THEMATIC AXIS 2 to prevent and reduce pollution on land, air, and sea.
    • THEMATIC AXIS 3 to protect, preserve, manage, and restore natural resources in the Mediterranean region within an integrated ecosystem approach, including terrestrial, marine, and coastal dimensions.
    • Other BIOSTARS' core concept will build on the idea of the Startup Village Forum initiative and its pillars (innovation, entrepreneurship, rural space, multiple scales (place-based approach), and ecosystems) by directly involving two start-up villages, one HR and one IT, and by promoting the use of the BIOSTARS toolbox.
    • The Startup Village Forum is part of a larger vision for rural areas in the EU, which recognises the enabling role of innovation to empower citizens and entrepreneurs to seize opportunities offered by current societal, environmental and digital transformations. It includes a flagship action on Research and Innovation for Rural Communities aiming at contributing to the development of a strong ecosystem, which brings together public and private players for rural communities to thrive and be an attractive place for innovators to work and live.
