enhancing SUStainable tourism attraction in small fascinating med TOWNS

Programme 2014 - 2020 INTERREG VB Mediterranean
Date of latest update: 2025-01-25

Description

Description (EN):
The charm of the Mediterranean space resides also in the fascinating small towns which have increasingly become tourist destinations for large numbers of visitors, favouring both the development and growth of its territories. Thanks to press campaigns, fashions and youth trends, such tourist flows are gaining ever more ground. However, Mediterranean fascinating small towns are subject to the decline in fashion and notoriety by tourists. In addition, these towns are at risk of intensive exploitation of their territories and may become over-equipped with infrastructures and services whenever their tourist attraction decreases. The project aims to intervene at the peak of the growing curve of tourist flows, making sure that these towns maintain the highest tourist flows, averting any risk of alteration of their fragile ecosystems. The main expected outcomes are the planning documents to address tourism marketing to produce new methodologies and technological tools which contribute in the strategy focused on stabilizing tourism flows. Therefore, the outputs are addressed to preserve the heritage of such towns which otherwise would see their existence compromised by the effects of overtourism. The challenge of the project is to associate small towns to jointly govern these processes, avoiding that the tourist flows cause collapse of the public services for excessive anthropization. In return, the preservation of local traditions and cultural heritage, has to be ensured.

Description (FR):
Le charme de l'espace méditerranéen réside aussi dans les fascinantes petites villes, devenues de plus en plus des destinations touristiques pour un grand nombre de visiteurs, favorisant le développement de ses territoires. Grâce aux campagnes de presse, aux modes des jeunes, ces flux touristiques ont gagné de terrain. Pourtant, ces petites villes méditerranéennes sont soumises à un déclin de la mode des touristes. En outre, ces villes risquent l’exploitation intensive de leurs territoires en devenant sur-équipées en infrastructures et services, chaque fois que leur attrait touristique diminue. Le projet vise à intervenir au sommet de la courbe croissante des flux touristiques, pour que ces villes maintiennent les flux touristiques les plus élevés et évitent tout risque d'altération de leurs écosystèmes fragiles. Les principaux résultats attendus sont de nouvelles méthodologies et outils technologiques qui contribuent à la stratégie axée sur la stabilisation des flux touristiques. Par conséquent, les outputs sont destinés à préserver le patrimoine de ces villes qui, autrement, verraient leur existence compromise par les effets négatifs du tourisme de masse. L’enjeu du projet est d’associer les petites villes à une gestion commune de ces processus, en évitant que les flux touristiques entraînent un effondrement des services publics à cause d’une anthropisation excessive. En contrepartie, la préservation des traditions locales et du patrimoine culturel doit être assurée.

Achievements (EN):
Despite of the COVID-19 sanitary emergency, the PPs have been able to launch all the planned pilot actions effectively involving 19 pilot Municipalities, one more than planned in the AF.The project methodology has been updated and produced in the final version containing the lessons learnt during the testing activities in the pilot areas. The Project methodology has been produced in English and translated in Italian, Slovenian, Albanian, Spanish and Croatian.59 meetings with the Local Focus Groups (out of 36 in AF) and 23 Training Sessions (out of 18 in AF) have been organized.19 Action Plans have been finalized and submitted to the related Local Authority (out of 18 in AF).29 Tourist packages have been produced (out of 18 in AF) and 22 National Marketing Plans (out of 18 in AF).19 Evaluations reports have been produced (out of 18 in AF).Under WP4, in this period have been realized: 6 National Seminars, 6 National Training for new Municipalities, 3 Participation in International events, 3 Technical meetings have been organized involving international tourism agents. 10 MoUs have been already signed by new municipalities/public authorities interested to adopt the project methodology.A very good visibility to the project has been assured during the first 3 periods of implementation.We have achieved 57 appearances on media (100 in total during the three semesters out of 40 planned in the AF for all the project life).18 Storytelling videos have been produced during the period (21 in total during the three semesters out of 20 planned in the AF for all the project life).62 Vox Pops have been produced during the period (72 in total during the three semesters out of 100 planned in the AF for all the project life).Passing to WP1, the COVID-19 has caused a significant increase of the work necessary to coordinate the project activities. We have had to organize more coordination meetings than planned to deal with the situation. Great efforts have been assured by the Technical Committee members and by the Project Steering Committee.
