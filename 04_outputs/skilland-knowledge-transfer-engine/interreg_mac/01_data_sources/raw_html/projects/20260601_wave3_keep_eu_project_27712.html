Dialogue4Nature, the Institutional Dialogue Project of the Interreg Euro-MED Natural Heritage Mission

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Mediterranean society is dependent on natural resources, being densely populated and one of the world’s most visited areas. The basin is a biodiversity hotspot hosting 18% of the world’s marine biodiversity with high levels of endemism. It is also a climate hotspot. Chronic climate change is heating the Mediterranean Sea three times faster than the global mean, with changes to biodiversity, habitats, migration of endemic species and invasion of non-indigenous species. Intensifying acute extreme events result in storm surges, heat waves, floods, and droughts. The current decline of biodiversity is unsustainable, loss must be halted, and society must learn to adapt and mitigate climate change. The governance of the Mediterranean is fragmented. Many regional challenges are poorly identified and addressed through ad hoc policy measures at local, regional, or national level. This project will take best practices from the associated thematic projects and upscale to Mediterranean and EU policy level, engaging with decision makers and relevant authorities at the science-policy interface. In addition, through the global networks of the partners results will be showcased at the international level. Inspired by successful policy initiatives such as the Stockholm resilience centre and the iberoamerican network of climate change, the Dialogue4Nature project will establish a Mediterranean Resilience Network (MRN). This network will coordinate cooperation at the broader Mediterranean scale on climate change, biodiversity, and sustainable development policies. Dialogue4Nature project will foster cooperation and to promote a more participatory and integrated policy process for climate change and biodiversity, including sub-national and civil society. In this manner, natural capital will be mainstreamed into social and environmental policy, not only helping to protect the environment but also to improve people’s health and well-being and to ensure a more resilient socio-economic model.

Description (FR):
La Méditerranée, étant densément peuplée et l'une des régions les plus visitées au monde, dépend des ressources naturelles. Le bassin méditerranéen est un point chaud de biodiversité, abritant 18% de la biodiversité marine mondiale avec des niveaux élevés d'endémisme. C'est aussi un point chaud climatique : la mer Méditerranée se réchauffe trois fois plus vite que la moyenne mondiale, avec des changements dans la biodiversité, les habitats, la migration d'espèces endémiques et l'invasion d'espèces non indigènes. Le déclin actuel n'est pas durable, la perte doit être arrêtée et la société doit apprendre à s'adapter et à atténuer le changement climatique. La gouvernance en Méditerranée est fragmentée. De nombreux défis régionaux sont mal identifiés et abordés par le biais de mesures politiques ad hoc au niveau local, régional ou national. Ce projet reprendra les meilleures pratiques des projets thématiques associés pour atteindre les politiques méditerranéennes et de l’UE, en s'engageant avec des décideurs politiques et des autorités compétentes à l'interface science-politique. De plus, grâce aux réseaux mondiaux des partenaires, les résultats seront présentés au niveau international. Inspiré par des initiatives politiques réussies telles que le centre de résilience de Stockholm et le réseau ibéro-américain sur le changement climatique, le projet Go4Med Nature établira un réseau méditerranéen de résilience (RMR). Ce réseau coordonnera la coopération à l'échelle méditerranéenne en matière de changement climatique, biodiversité et politiques de développement durable. Le projet Go4Med Nature favorisera la collaboration et encouragera un processus politique plus participatif et intégré. De cette manière, le capital naturel sera intégré dans la politique sociale et environnementale, contribuant non seulement à protéger l'environnement, mais aussi à améliorer la santé et le bien-être des personnes et à garantir un modèle socio-économique plus résilient.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The implementation of WP1 has advanced according to the plan. Specifically, the preparation and adoption of D.1.1.1 “Joint C4N & D4N REUSE Work Plan in collaboration with JS & NCP” has been concluded in the framework of Activity 1.1 “Coordination of Work package and with TCP and JS”. In parallel, the preparation of D.1.2.1 “Database of target groups and available solutions” has started in close collaboration with C4N. In the framework of D.1.3.2 “Communication campaign”, the implementation of a Communication and Dissemination Plan has started as there is a high dissemination of any action taken (project website, social media etc.) and active participation in external events. Furthermore, the implementation of D.1.4.2 “Awareness Raising Campaign” has started, as a campaign has been launched during this reporting period. The big milestone of WP1 during this reporting period was the joint organization of the hybrid event “Making the Mediterranean Transition Happen” that took place in Barcelona during the EU Green Week (06/2023). During the event, project partners, associated partners and other key stakeholders met with the objective of presenting the Mission to the community. The implementation of WP2 has advanced according to the plan. Specifically, the preparation and adoption of D.2.2.1 “Conceptual framework” has been concluded in the framework of Activity 2.2 “Conceptual Framework for transformative changes” as well as of D.2.3.1 “Management tools for WGs” in the framework of Activity 2.3 “Consolidating and creating networks to increase the outreach of the results”. Also, in the framework of Activity 2.1 “Coordinating the work package and with TCP and JS”, a joint plan with C4N has been created. During this reporting period, the PPs established the WGs and created the corresponding factsheets outlining the scope, the principles and the plan of each WG in order to call the APs to join the WG they prefer. The implementation of WP3 has advanced according to the plan. Specifically, the preparation and adoption of D.3.1.1 “Coordination strategy and action plan for the WP and collaboration with TCP and JS” has been concluded in the framework of Activity 3.1 “Coordination strategy for the WP including TCP and JS”. The PPs have advanced with the first steps for the preparation of D.3.3.1 “State of art of cooperation opportunities” while they prepared a draft version of D.3.4.1 “TORs and road map for the Mediterranean Resilience Network”, which included the scope, approach, objectives, and activities of MRN. Last but not least, the PPs have started implementing Activity 3.6 “Mainstream key policy and governance approaches within and beyond the Med area” (announcement document) and Activity 3.7 “Advocacy and representation of Mission 2 within and beyond MED area” (Liaising activities monitoring file). Throughout this reporting period, significant progress has been made in advancing the objectives and outputs of the Dialogue4Nature (D4N) project. Coordinated by the LP, the project management and coordination have been effectively executed, ensuring alignment between the D4N and C4N projects. The coordination efforts facilitated the smooth implementation of Mission4Nature's objectives, fostering collaboration among project partners and enhancing efficiency. Major achievements during this period include: Project Management and Coordination: The LP and the PPs diligently managed and coordinated project activities, ensuring adherence to the planned work plan and timeline. The kick-off meeting and subsequent online coordination meetings, including the 1st and 2nd Steering Committee meetings, provided platforms for partnership discussions and decision-making. Alignment and Coordination: Partners actively participated in various events and meetings to ensure alignment and coordination between the Dialogue4Nature (D4N) and Community4Nature (C4N) projects, as well as among the partnership of the D4N project itself. These efforts aimed to facilitate the smooth implementation of Mission4Nature's objectives. Throughout the reporting period, partners engaged in regular online coordination meetings for both the D4N and Mission coordination meetings with C4N. These meetings provided opportunities to discuss project progress, share updates, and address challenges. Partners also provided technical assistance and support during meeting discussions, clarifying roles and ensuring efforts were not duplicated across work packages. Additionally, they participated in significant events such as webinars, seminars, and the Annual Institutional Dialogue Event, networking with stakeholders and co-developing collaboration roadmaps. Working Groups Establishment: Working Groups (WGs) were established, and corresponding factsheets were developed to outline the scope and plans of each WG. This initiative aimed to encourage Associated Partners' involvement and facilitate collaboration on project activities. Joint Preparation and Events Organization: Partners collaborated on organizing events such as the Annual Institutional Dialogue Event and technical workshops, providing opportunities for networking and discussion on project objectives and activities. Furthermore, the organization of the first face-to-face Mission4Nature event facilitated in-depth discussions and strengthened relationships among project teams. Communication and Dissemination: Partners, including PP2, PP5, and PP6, actively contributed to communication efforts, ensuring proper dissemination of project objectives and activities. Communication materials were developed and disseminated through various channels, including dedicated websites, social media, and press releases. Overall, the collaborative efforts of project partners have led to significant achievements in advancing the Dialogue4Nature project's objectives. Through effective coordination, communication, and collaboration, the project is on track to achieve its goals and deliver tangible results for nature conservation in the Mediterranean region.
