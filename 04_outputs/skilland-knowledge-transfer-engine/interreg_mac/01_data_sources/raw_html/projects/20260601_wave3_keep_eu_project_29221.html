Accelerating Carbon Market Development for climate change mitigation and adaptation in Mediterranean agriculture

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Parmi les principaux défis identifiés dans la loi européenne sur le climat figure la neutralité climatique de l'ensemble du secteur foncier d'ici 2035. Pour atteindre cet objectif, les mesures à mettre en place doivent suivre une approche associant eau, énergie et sécurité alimentaire. Cette approche correspond aux principes de la gestion durable des terres et aux pratiques qui y contribuent en augmentant progressivement le captage à long terme du CO2 de l'atmosphèredans les écosystèmes. En outre, elle favorise la restauration de l'environnement et participe à une exploitation plus résiliente et équitable des terres par les communautés locales. L'objectif général de CARBON FARMING MED est de développer un système agricole méditerranéen résilient et d'aider les agriculteurs à mettre en œuvre l'agriculture carbone comme un nouveau modèle de gestion écologique permettant d'obtenir des revenus supplémentaires, d'optimiser les pratiques agricoles et agroforestières régénératrices et de fournir les outils nécessaires à l'adoption d'un marché des crédits carbone (vendeur et acheteur) grâce à: 1. La création d'un cadre théorique de l'agriculture carbone en Méditerranée 2. La quantification de la capture du carbone dans le sol et des émissions de GES évitées 3. La promotion d'un marché méditerranéen des crédits carbone générés par l'agriculture régénérative 4. Le transfert des résultats du projet Tout cela nécessite une approche transfrontalière réunissant acheteurs et vendeurs en Méditerranée pour qu'ils puissent tirer profit d'un marché commun de crédits carbone. CARBON FARMING MED sera la première plateforme de ce type dans la région qui permettra d'une part d'acheter et de vendre des crédits carbone et d'autre par d'aider les exploitations dans le processus de vérification en capitalisant sur les modèles et les bonnes pratiques existants, créant ainsi un cadre pour l'agriculture carbone en Méditerranée qui permettra à l'UE d'atteindre la neutralité climatique d'ici 2050.

Description (EN):
Among the main challenges identified in the European Climate Law is achieving climate neutrality of the entire land sector by 2035. Effective means to reach this target follow a water-energy-food security nexus approach. This is also in line with the principles of sustainable land management, and practices that are responding to the challenge by helping to gradually increase the capture of CO2 from the atmosphere and store it in the long term in ecosystems. Those practices also foster the restoration of the environment and contribute to a more resilient and fair exploitation of the land for local communities. The general objective of CARBON FARMING MED is to develop a resilient Mediterranean agriculture system and to help farmers to implement carbon farming as a new green business model to obtain additional revenues (e.g., voluntary market of carbon credits, CAP funding, etc.), optimizing regenerative agricultural and agroforestry practices and providing the necessary tools to facilitate the carbon credits’ market adoption (from seller and buyer perspectives), through: 1. Establishing a theoretical framework of carbon farming in the Mediterranean 2. Quantifying carbon capture in the soil and avoided GHG emissions 3. Promoting a Mediterranean market of carbon credits generated by regenerative agriculture 4. Transferring and mainstreaming the project results and lessons learned The above requires a cross-border approach that brings Mediterranean carbon buyers and sellers together to benefit from a common market of carbon credits. The CARBON FARMING MED will be the first platform of its kind in the region, allowing to buy and sell carbon credits, while offering a tool that helps farms with the verification process, by capitalising on existing models and best practices and creating a carbon farming framework in the Mediterranean. This will tackle the joint challenge of enabling the EU to achieve climate neutrality by 2050, through carbon removals.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the first reporting period, all consortium members actively participated in meetings organized by the coordination team, including the first consortium meeting (February 1st) and the KoM developed in Vic, Spain (February 19th - March 1st). Additionally, members took part in key coordination events such as the ISE MED Innovation Day in France (June 2024), the 7th EURAF Congress in the Czech Republic (May 2024), and the 1st European Carbon Farming Summit in Spain (March 2024). A total of 32 technical meetings were also held between consortium members, either coordinated by the lead or requested by partners (see project calendar on Basecamp). The presentation of the Carbon Farming Projects Catalogue (D4.1, June 2024) has been a key resource for establishing connections with other ongoing European-funded projects, (It was developed a bilateral meeting with Life Carbon Farming project, led by the Institut de l'Elevage, France- April 2024). It has also helped to develop a shortlist institutions and project coordinators for conforming the Advisory Board (invitations sent). The project's communication strategy, developed by IHE in close collaboration with EMP (June 2024, see WP4 on Basecamp), serves as a guide to enhance communication efficiency and stakeholder engagement. Additionally, the official website and social media accounts (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram) were launched in June 2024 to expand the project's visibility and encourage community involvement. The open call inviting Spanish and Italian farmers to join the project's pilot farms was published on the project’s website and social media channels (July 2024). This resulted in significant interest from both countries. Over the next 6-7 months, the selection process will be conducted to identify farms that meet the call's eligibility criteria. Following this, agreements will be signed, and technical recognition visits will commence. Finally, the Carbon Footprint Assessment and Offsetting Guidelines (D.4.8.1) were developed by CENER 21 to assist partners in understanding and utilizing the ZEROCO2Med tool (Activity 4.8 - Carbon Footprint Assessment and Offsetting). A meeting was held with the entire consortium to explain the process of data collection for each reporting period, ensuring all members are aligned on the methodology. In WP1, the following progress was made: UVIC completed the Report on Carbon Farming as a Business Model (A1.1 and D1.1), which was reviewed, approved by partners, and submitted to the JS. IHE began A1.2 in month 3, analyzing MRV requirements and standards, forming the basis for further research. CREA started A1.3 in month 1, with the report (D1.3 – June 2024) expected by the second period's end. CREA also initiated A1.4, developing a SOC estimation model for the Mediterranean region's agroclimatic conditions. WP2 focused on defining criteria for regenerative agriculture and agroforestry practices. Led by AZOLLA (A2.1) with support from CREA, TUC, EURAF, UVIC, and IHE, the D2.1 document (June 2024) was developed, forming the basis for the open call. CREA and AZOLLA actively encouraged local farmer participation, while IHE led a coordination meeting to synchronize efforts The key activities of WP3, led by UVIC, included the A3.1 report on the "Definition of Functionalities and Requirements to Develop the Carbon Farming MED Tool" (D3.1 – June 2024). Several technical meetings with partners were conducted to identify the minimum technical requirements for the MRV system and to define the core functionalities of the tool. Activity 3.2 commenced in month 4, though additional technical meetings are planned to finalize the frontend programming, aiming to prevent significant changes in the upcoming months. In WP A4.1, led by EUROREGIO, the focus was on identifying existing European carbon farming projects to leverage available data and foster synergies. The European Carbon Farming Projects Catalogue (D4.1 - June 2024) was presented and thoroughly reviewed by the partners involved (IHE, CENER 21, CRITT, UVIC, AZOLLA). Learning pathways and potential collaborations were identified, including the recommendation to involve leaders from similar projects in the advisory board. In A4.4, IHE drafted the communication strategy with significant input from Euroregio, which also contributed to the development of communication tools such as the project poster, institutional website, and project website. Under A4.8, CENER 21 developed an Excel tool for carbon footprint assessment and provided training on its use, with all partners completing the file to test the tool's functionality. During the second reporting period, the Carbon Farming MED project made substantial progress across all work packages, focusing on research, pilot testing, digital platform development, communication, and project management. WP1 Significant efforts advanced research on Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification (MRV) standards at global, EU, and national levels. A review of CO₂ offsetting platforms assessed real-world MRV applications and challenges. These findings form the basis for a tailored MRV framework adapted to Mediterranean carbon farming practices in the next phase. Carbon farming practice selection identified agroforestry and regenerative agriculture as the most suitable approaches for the region. Predictive soil sequestration models were adapted to assess carbon storage potential under Mediterranean conditions. A Soil Sampling Protocol was developed to ensure data collection consistency across pilot farms, alongside testing RothC soil carbon sequestration models. A preliminary review of techno-economic models for agro-system performance led to drafting the methodology for Activity A1.5. Collaboration strengthened research integration, aligning with project goals. Deliverable D3.1: Selection of Best-Suited Carbon Farming Actions was successfully completed. To enhance Output 1.1, a proposal was approved to postpone delivery to the next period, ensuring it fully incorporates WP1 findings and Mediterranean stakeholder needs. WP2 Fieldwork intensified, assessing and implementing pilot testing activities across multiple Mediterranean sites. Site visits in Spain and Italy evaluated farm characteristics, land access, and production types, providing the foundation for selecting sampling plots and standardizing field data collection. From October to December 2024, systematic soil sampling followed Chronosequence and Contrasting Land Use approaches. Samples are undergoing laboratory analysis to determine carbon content and sequestration potential. Pilot farms implementing agroforestry and regenerative agriculture were monitored for carbon sequestration performance and economic feasibility. A combination of modeling and direct measurement techniques assessed their impact, contributing to project objectives. WP3 A major milestone was the continued development of the Carbon Farming MED digital platform, a key tool for farmers, researchers, and policymakers. Work advanced on frontend and backend functionalities, integrating complex data processing with a user-friendly interface. A prototype user interface (MOOC in Figma) was designed to collect farmer data on Soil Organic Carbon (SOC) and greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. The platform's architecture supports carbon removal project development aligned with certification schemes like Verra and the International Carbon Registry (ICR). Backend development established a secure login and registration system. Preliminary soil carbon accumulation modeling using the CAST model yielded initial results, presented at the Soil Health: Current Status and Future Needs conference in Chania, Greece. WP4 Efforts focused on strengthening visibility, fostering stakeholder engagement, and ensuring smooth administration. A key achievement was establishing the Advisory Board (AB), with its first formal meeting scheduled for January 2025, bringing together external members, associated partners, and the steering committee. A Framework for Effective Project Communication was developed to streamline information sharing. Communication materials included a project roll-up and a promotional video (pending approval). The project actively participated in national, European, and Mediterranean-level events, including: - 1st European Carbon Farming Summit (Valencia, Spain) – presenting to a European audience. - Simposio Internacional del Carbono en México (Online) – presenting to North American institutions. - CONAMA (National Congress of Environment, Spain) – presenting to a national audience. - ISE Community of Practice Gathering & MedTalks – engaging in knowledge-sharing discussions. Monthly technical and coordination meetings ensured partner alignment and Interreg project adherence. The Carbon Farming Projects Catalogue was expanded, identifying new initiatives and engaging key stakeholders for advisory board participation. Project sustainability efforts progressed in carbon footprint assessment, with data collection and submission compliant with reporting guidelines. Preparations for the EuroMED Academy & Community Activities began, with an initial work plan and content gathering. Request to delay Output O.1.1 Regarding O.1.1, after reviewing project progress, it was determined that delivering this output in Period 3, after WP1 completion, would be more appropriate. This adjustment ensures the framework incorporates consolidated results, enhancing quality and applicability. The Project Officer was officially informed on 30/01/2025. . During the third reporting period, the Carbon Farming Med project advanced significantly in administrative, technical, and dissemination activities. The consortium successfully postponed the delivery of Output 1.1 to June 2025 to ensure integration of all WP1 results. On the technical side, WP1 advanced the Carbon Farming Framework, delivering progress on documents, models, and databases: a conventional agriculture baseline scenario, statistical tools in R, and a database of over 100 studies assessing carbon farming externalities. WP2 completed soil analyses and advanced farm diagnostics across Spain and Italy, developing a standardized farmer description form to harmonize data collection. WP3 progressed with the integration of Roth C into the IT platform, including tools for farm polygon uploads and interface development. WP4 strengthened dissemination, expanding LinkedIn outreach, producing educational content, and initiating synergies with projects such as Carbonica, LILAS4Soil, and IRISCC. The first Advisory Board meeting provided constructive feedback and fostered synergies. The consortium also prepared contributions to major events (EU Carbon Farming Summit, Bioeconomy Congress, Eurosoil 2025), strengthening policy links and Mediterranean-wide collaboration.

  • ZeroCO2MED – Transition towards climate-neutral Interreg MED projects 2021-2022 (Interreg MED programme 2014-2020).

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The European Green Deal is the EU’s growth model and response plan to climate change and environmental degradation. To overcome these challenges, the European Green Deal will transform the EU into a modern, resource-efficient and competitive economy. The project will contribute to different key areas of the EU Green Deal, especially:
    • 1. “Climate neutral” Europe. This is the overarching objective of the European Green Deal. The EU will aim to reach net-zero GHG emissions by 2050, a goal that has been enshrined in the European Climate Law (Regulation (EU) 2021/1119). CARBON FARMING MED will directly contribute to this objective through the development of a Mediterranean Market of carbon credits, which will contribute to balancing GHG emissions from all land, livestock and fertiliser use through sustainable land management practices, which will increase the capture of CO2 from the atmosphere and store it in ecosystems.
    • 2. Circular Economy. This is another main objective of the European Green Deal and will be tackled through the EU circular economy action plan. This action plan aims to:
    • - make sustainable products the norm in the EU
    • - empower consumers and public buyers
    • - focus on the sectors that use most resources and where the potential for circularity is high
    • - ensure less waste
    • - make circularity work for people, regions and cities
    • - lead global efforts on circular economy
    • CARBON FARMING MED will directly contribute to the circular economy action plan through the optimization of regenerative agricultural practices in a Mediterranean context and promote a circular model of carbon credits in the Mediterranean.

A core part of the European Green Deal, it will also support a green recovery following the Covid-19 pandemic. The strategy aims to put Europe’s biodiversity on the path to recovery by 2030 for the benefit of people, climate and the planet.

One of the ways of restoring degraded ecosystems at land across the whole of Europe will be by increasing organic farming and biodiversity-rich landscape features on agricultural land.
    • Therefore, the implementation of regenerative agricultural techniques in the Mediterranean region will directly contribute to the biodiversity strategy and its goals. Regenerative agriculture focuses on increasing biodiversity, strengthening the health of farm soil and topsoil regeneration.

    • The European Commission adopted its new EU strategy on adaptation to climate change on 24 February 2021. The new strategy sets out how the European Union can adapt to the unavoidable impacts of climate change and become climate resilient by 2050. The Strategy has four principal objectives: to make adaptation smarter, swifter and more systemic, and to step up international action on adaptation to climate change.
    • One of the focus areas of the strategy is promoting nature-based solutions for adaptation, in order to increase climate resilience and contribute to multiple Green Deal objectives.
    • In its Adaptation Strategy, the Commission commits to proposing nature-based solutions for carbon removals, including accounting and certification in upcoming farming initiatives; as well as to develop the financial aspects of nature-based solutions and foster the development of financial approaches and products that also cover nature-based adaptation.
    • CARBON FARMING MED directly contributes to the afore-mentioned focus areas of the strategy, through the implementation of regenerative agriculture techniques in Mediterranean agriculture, a nature-based solution to capturing carbon in soil.

    • Moreover, CARBON FARMING MED strengthens the interrelations between our health, ecosystems, supply chains, consumption patterns and planetary boundaries, which is at the core of the Farm to Fork strategy, through the creation of a transboundary carbon market.
