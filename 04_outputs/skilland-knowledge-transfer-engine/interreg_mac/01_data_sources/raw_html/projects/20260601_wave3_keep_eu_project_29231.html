Innovative and Green Procurement towards sustainable economy in MED area

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Every year, over 250 000 public authorities in the EU spend around 14% of GDP (around €2 trillion per year) on the purchase of services, works and supplies. In many sectors such as energy, transport, waste management, social protection and the provision of health or education services, public authorities are the principal buyers. High quality public services depend on modern, well-managed and efficient procurement. However, 55% of procurement procedures use lowest price as the only award criterion for public contracts. This indicates that public buyers are probably not paying enough attention to quality, sustainability and innovation. In order to tackle the problem, European Commission, encourages Public authorities to widely adopt Strategic Procurement (including Green Public Procurement and Public Procurement of Innovative solutions) to boost jobs, growth and investment in green innovation leading Europe toward Innovative sustainable economy. In this frame born ProcuraMED with the aim to support the transition towards competitive, innovative, sustainable and resilient ecosystems in the Mediterranean developing innovation capacities of enterprises and local authorities and the uptake of advanced green technologies by means of Strategic Procurement Procedures. ProcuraMED, upscaling and transferring the tools, strategies and network developed in the framework of PROMINENT MED and GRASPINNO, will 1. Develop reliable tools, advanced training and tailored counselling to public authorities and enterprises to boost the process of green innovative technology application in public services 2. Create an Euro-Mediterranean coalition boosting value chain and transnational cooperation between public procurers (demand side) and private enterprises (offer side)

Description (FR):
Chaque année plus de 250 000 autorités publiques de l'UE dépensent environ 14 % de leur PIB (environ 2000 milliards d'euros/an) pour l'achat de services, de travaux et de fournitures. Dans de nombreux secteurs tels que l'énergie, les transports, la gestion des déchets, la protection sociale et la fourniture de services de santé ou d'éducation, les pouvoirs publics sont les principaux acheteurs. Des services publics de haute qualité dépendent d'un approvisionnement moderne, bien géré et efficace. Cependant, 55 % des procédures de passation des marchés utilisent le prix le plus bas comme seul critère d'attribution des marchés publics. Cela indique que les acheteurs publics n'accordent pas trop d'attention à la qualité, à la durabilité, à l'innovation. Afin de s'attaquer au problème, la Commission européenne encourage les pouvoirs publics à adopter les marchés publics stratégiques (y compris les marchés publics écologiques et les marchés publics de solutions innovantes) afin de stimuler l'emploi, la croissance et l'investissement dans l'innovation verte menant l'Europe vers une économie durable innovante. Dans ce cadre est né ProcuraMED dans le but de soutenir la transition vers des écosystèmes compétitifs, innovants, durables et résilients en Méditerranée en développant les capacités d'innovation des entreprises et des autorités locales et l'adoption de technologies vertes avancées au moyen de procédures d'approvisionnement stratégiques. La montée en gamme et le transfert des outils, des stratégies et du réseau développés par PROMINENT MED et GRASPINNO permettront: 1. Développer des outils fiables, des formations avancées et des conseils sur mesure aux autorités publiques et aux entreprises pour dynamiser le processus d'application des technologies innovantes vertes dans les services publics 2. Créer une coalition euro-méditerranéenne stimulant la chaîne de valeur et la coopération transnationale entre les acheteurs publics (demande) et les entreprises privées (offre)

Actual Achievements (EN):
The project has successfully navigated the initial phases, including the pre-contract phase, partner adjustments and project start-up. Just before the signing of the PA and SC, the Gozo Regional Council (PP9) withdrew from the project and was replaced by a new partner PP10 REAN.The LP formalised the withdrawal of the old partner and the entry of the new one by sending a written procedure. The contracting process was then completed by signing the PA between the LP and the project partners and the SC between the LP and the programme. All partners worked on and contributed to all the activities as per the AF and BDGT of the project. 1) Project Management and Coordination: Narni organized the pre-kick-off online meeting on 25 January 2024 and the hybrid kick-off meeting (KoM) in Lisbon on 2 and 3 April 2024. These events facilitated discussions between partners, ensuring that everyone was aligned with the project's objectives and prepared for the work ahead. The LP also oversaw the development and signing of the SC Rules of Procedure. Several online meetings were held with the WP Leaders and Task Leaders, focusing on strategic planning for the WP1 and WP2 work packages (30/01/2024 – 14/05/2024 – 24/05/2024 – 30/05/2024) and then on 12/06/2024 was organized an online meeting with all partners to report to them as well the planning activities. 2) Financial and Contractual activities: partners worked on financial performance and efficiency management, procurement procedures and selection of external service providers, signing of contract documents, preparation of financial reporting documents, ensure that the NC were designated. Narni was responsible for providing the required financial information and checking the partners' reports prior to submission to their FLC. 3) Communication: these activities, led by IrRADIARE (PP4) and supported by the LP and other partners, were instrumental in beginning to raise awareness of the project's objectives and engage stakeholders in the pilot regions. IrRADIARE worked on drafting the communication plan, designing the project's visual identity and promotional materials (A.1.2: development of a video presentation of the project, a project brochure in print and electronic format, role up for the event) creating the website and social media, and producing weekly newsletters. The partners have started to distribute these materials to ensure that the objectives of the project reach a wide audience and have displayed the project poster in their offices. On WP1 as said the LP organised several online meetings with the WP Leader (UPatras) and Task Leaders (TSL, InRA) to plan the activities. On A.1.1 Narni, in collaboration with the WP Leader UPatras (PP2) and the Task Leader Terre di Siena Lab (TSL, PP3), coordinated the development of the platform and ensured its alignment with the project objectives, starting with the integration of the Green Public Procurement tool developed in the Graspinno project with the tools and guidelines on PPI developed in PROMINENT MED. In addition, questionnaires were developed and disseminated to assess the background conditions for the use of green and innovative tenders in the partecipated countries. It was agreed that all partners would collect the responses to the questionnaires and send a short report in English with the results of the analysis. This baseline work will be used to adapt the Green Public Procurement (GPP) and Public Procurement of Innovation (PPI) guidelines to local contexts, thereby increasing their applicability and effectiveness. For Activity A.1.2 the partnership started mapping stakeholders to define target roles and objectives. Narni supported InRA in the development of information material and shared with all partners a calendar for the organisation of 1 info-day and 2 thematic sessions in each country. For Activity 1.3 Narni shared with all partners a calendar – discussed with WP and Tasks leaders - for the organisation of all events. For WP2 Narni shared with all partners a calendar for events to further disseminate knowledge and foster collaboration as A.2.1 and UPatras began planning the first international workshop. The Partners have been proactive in building a transnational public-private coalition for an innovative sustainable economy reaching out to key stakeholders in their regions and exploring synergies with other thematic projects, participating in the 2nd Innovation Camp (Barcelona) - 28-29 May and in the Interreg Euro-MED ISE Mission: Shaping Sustainable Innovation in the Mediterranean – Marseille 18 & 19 June 2024 (A.2.2 and A.2.3). Partners have started to collect data for the project's carbon footprint monitoring tool (Activity 2.4). In conclusion, although no specific deliverables/outputs are expected for this reporting period (1-6), the project has taken several important steps and laid a solid foundation for future work. The partnership has demonstrated strong coordination and effective communication. Throughout the second reporting period, the ProcuraMED project has made significant progress towards its objectives by fostering collaboration among partners and advancing key strategic initiatives. The Municipality of Narni played a central role in coordinating project management activities: at the beginning of the semester ensured timely submission of first project report ensuring that all obligations were met on time and at the end of the reporting period, coordinated the reporting procedures for the second semester. As per the technical activities the LP, WPs and Task Leaders were in constant communication to discuss and ensure the proper implementation of the activities with other partners by organising several online meetings (16/07/2024 - 10/09/2024 - 24/10/2024 - 26/11/2024). The 2nd SCM (physical version) was held on 09/10/2024 in Brussels, hosted in the premises of IrrADIARE. Act. 1.1 - Significant collaboration between UPATRAS, the lead partner, and TSL led to the completion of background analysis by the end of September. All partners collected responses from the ProcuraMED questionnaires, compiling them into a report that TSL integrated into a cumulative analysis. At the second SC meeting in Brussels, partners decided how to review the project tools accordingly. Narni, in collaboration with UPATRAS, led the adaptation of GPP and PPI guidelines, creating templates for their implementation. A major achievement in this period was the successful integration of the GPP tool from the GRASPINNO project with PPI guidelines from PROMINENT MED and new ProcuraMED materials, resulting in the launch of the Strategic Procurement Unified Platform (Output 1.1 and D.1.1.1), which is now available online. Act. 1.2 - Partners conducted extensive stakeholder mapping and disseminated project materials through media campaigns, institutional websites, and social media. Some partners have started organizing info days and thematic sessions for both public authorities and enterprises to promote GPP and PPI, attracting strong participation (see all details in the dedicated section of this activity). These events facilitated discussions among stakeholders, further supported by strong engagement through local networks, newsletters, and detailed event programs. Act. 1.3 - Partners began planning training courses for public procurement technicians, enterprises, and academic institutions. These courses will introduce the key concepts of PPI and GPP, highlight their benefits, and provide guidance on using the Unified Strategic Procurement Platform. The training sessions are scheduled for the next semester. IrRADIARE continued leading communication efforts, maintaining regular updates on the project website and social media platforms. The ProcuraMED brochure was produced in English and translated into the national languages of the partners. Partners supported Irradiare on the realisation of communication material for the Info Day and thematic meetings. According to financial and contractual activities, partners properly managed financial performance and efficiency, procurement procedures and selection of external service providers, signing of contractual documents, managing data and entering into the system, preparing documents for validation, providing required financial information, financial reporting. In conclusion, the collaboration between the partners has significantly increased both the effectiveness and the impact of the ProcuraMED project by fostering knowledge sharing and increasing visibility. The exchange of expertise and best practices has improved the quality and relevance of the project outcomes, while participation in international forums and events has raised the profile of the project and strengthened engagement with key stakeholders. The ProcuraMED project has made significant progress towards its objectives by fostering collaboration among partners. Several online meetings were organized: on 23/01/2025 and 16/04/2025 with MedCities to discuss WP2; on 24/02/2025 with UPATRAS and TSL to address the implementation of the project tool; from 03–12/03/2025 with BSC Kranj; on 26/03/2025 to prepare the ISE Mission (3rd ISE Community of Practice in Sarajevo, 2–4 April 2025) with partners participating in person; and on 30/04/2025 with all WP Leaders. On 6/02, Narni attended an online meeting with Loukia Prentza on the ISE Mission – Peer Review, and on 10/02 joined a meeting with Haris Paliogiannis regarding the ISE Mentoring and Twinning Programme. Moreover, an online SCM was organized on 4/02 and the Narni hosted the third physical SCM in Narni on 19 May. At the beginning of the semester the LP ensured timely submission of second project report and at the end of the reporting period, coordinated the reporting procedures for this third semester. WP1: Act. 1.1 - Narni worked closely with UPATRAS (LP) and TSL to update the “Strategic Procurement Unified Platform” (D.1.1.1), building on work started in the previous reporting period. Feedback from all partners on the GPP and PPI application guidelines in each country was collected and integrated. The platform is now online, with several new user accounts created (both Public Authorities and SMEs). Act. 1.2 All partners have organized the info days and thematic sessions for both public authorities and enterprises to promote GPP and PPI and explain their opportunities and the project foreseen activities. Act. 1.3 Almost all partners finished to organize the training courses. The missing partners will finishd them at the beginning of the next reporting period. These courses introduced the key concepts of PPI and GPP, highlight their benefits, and provide guidance on using the Unified Strategic Procurement Platform. Act. 1.4 Partners started the first preparatory meetings between partners’ staff and external experts for organization of the mentoring activities scheduled for the next reporting period. In June Medcities is the only partner that already organized it. Narni provided the template for the AP to partners (key deliverables D.1.4.1). The deadline to it to LP is the end of September 2025, in order to present them at the International EXPO of Green Innovation (Barcelona, 4–6 November 2025). WP2:Act. 2.1 The LP organised the 2nd International Workshop “How Strategic Procurement Can Drive the Activation of Innovative Circular Value Chains” held in Narni on 20 May 2025. IrRADIARE organized the 1st international webinar “Green and circular procurement: a way to reach strategic goals”, organised during the EU Green Week (27 June 2025). MedCities began preparations for the International Expo of Green Innovation, to be held during the Smart City Expo World Congress 2025 in November. Act. 2.2 A dedicated project webpage was created within the MedUrbanTools repository. Each project partner contributed one good practice on GPP and PPI. Medcities prepares the Terms of Reference for the development of the Euro-Mediterranean Coalition on Strategic Procurement (Output 2.1), started to prepare the template for key deliverable D.2.2.1 (MoU) and identified the international expert responsible for supporting the coalition's development. Act. 2.3 In addition to the good synergies reached with the 2nd ProcuraMED International Workshop in Narni in May 2025 inviting representative of other projects, multiple partners, including Upatras, MedCities, DDIP ZDC and IrRADIARE, contributed to external networking efforts by participating in the 3rd ISE Community of Practice gathering – Sarajevo 2-4 April 2025. UPatras organised on May 5, 2025, the event “Shaping the future of the Adriatic – Ionian Region: Cooperation, Innovation and Sustainability” as part of the 10th EUSAIR Annual Forum in Crete. The event was powered by ProcuraMED and AZA4ICE projects of the Interreg Euro-MED Programme and gathered key projects focused on green and blue growth, sustainable procurement, digitalization and regional resilience. Act. 2.4 Narni collected data from partners for the Carbon Footprint monitoring tool of the Programme, to ensure accurate completion at the end of the reporting period. IrRADIARE with th support of all partners continued leading communication efforts, maintaining regular updates on the project website and social media about the events and activities organized in each country. According to financial and contractual activities, partners properly managed financial performance and efficiency, procurement procedures and selection of external service providers, signing of contractual documents, managing data and entering into the system, preparing documents for validation, providing required financial information, financial reporting.
