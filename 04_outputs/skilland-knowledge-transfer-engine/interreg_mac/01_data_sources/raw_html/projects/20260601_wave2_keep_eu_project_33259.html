Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 067. Household waste management: prevention, minimisation, sorting, reuse, recycling measures ~ SUMMARY: THE PROBLEM Growing consumption is generating changes in the composition of waste, including 'new' waste streams such as electronic. The Mediterranean Strategy for Sustainable Development 2016-2025 is based on the principle that economic growth needs to be harmonized with the protection of natural resources. In EU countries, the WEEE is managed within a strict legislative framework, however, only one third of the WEEE produced is collected separately and destined for appropriate treatment. Furthermore, countries not belonging to EU territories have a less restrictive WEEE waste management legislation, contributing to improver disposal and treatment methods of discarded electrical equipment. E-waste exposes a high environmental risk for NEXT MED area, as informal recycling, and e-waste abandoned in dumping sites, provide hazardous substances seed into groundwater, affecting water sources, aquifers and sea water. THE OBJECTIVE WEEE VALUE aims to promote local and regional policies for better e-waste management, to reduce WEEE environmental damage in the selected areas, while promoting: Comprehensive awareness campaigns, through the solution WEEE AWARE, New innovative eco-business models and an efficient online collection platform, through the solution WEEE COLLECT, Social inclusion in WEEE management companies, through the solution WEEE SOCIAL. TRANSNATIONAL COOPERATION The unification of waste management criteria in the transnational MED area is essential, since otherwise it could facilitate the dynamization of the illegal movement of e-waste from areas with stricter regulations towards areas with more permissive regulations. To stop this threat, it is necessary to take into account common joint planning, and coordinated business promotion. WEEE VALUE gathers 8 partners and 5 associated, covering 8 countries, which will approve 8 new Action Plans for e-waste management. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: SME and entrepreneurship, Social inclusion and equal opportunities, Waste and pollution

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other WEEE Value contributes to the following Strategies:
    • UN SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOAL 12 “ENSURE SUSTAINABLE CONSUMPTION AND PRODUCTION PATTERNS”
    • WEEE Value contributes to this priority by encouraging more sustainable production patterns, focused on the recycling and reusing of e-waste. The main goal of this priority is to promote sustainable growth and development, which requires minimizing the natural resources and toxic materials used, and the waste and pollutants generated, throughout the entire production and consumption process.
    • UN SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOAL 14 “CONSERVE AND SUSTAINABLY USE THE OCEANS, SEAS AND MARINE RESOURCES FOR SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT”
    • WEEE Value strongly contributes to this priority by promoting policy changes to better manage the e-waste, as electronic equipment is commonly disposed of in open oceans. The leaching of hazardous chemicals from electronic components is very contaminating, affecting ecosystems and human health.
    • EU CIRCULAR ECONOMY ACTION PLAN (CEAP)
    • The “Circular electronics initiative” inside the New Circular Economy Action Plan aims to promote longer product lifetimes. WEEE Value is well aligned with the main objectives of this plan, as it seeks a prevention of contamination of natural resources through better waste management policies, at the same time that promotes a swift to more circular ecobusiness which reuse already exploited resources instead of getting more resources from the nature.
    • UFM MINISTERIAL DECLARATION ON ENVIRONMENT AND CLIMATE CHANGE, AND THE 2030 GREENERMED AGENDA
    • WEEE Value fully aims at the main objective of the 2030 GreenerMed Agenda, which is to promote the transition to a circular and socially inclusive economy, and the objective of depollution and pollution preservation in NEXT MED AREA.
