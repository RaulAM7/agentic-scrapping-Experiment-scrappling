A multitude of inclusive Renewable Energy Communities in the Mediterranean Region

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
A clean and just energy transition can only rely on the direct involvement of all the actors operating in the territories: citizens, public authorities, private enterprises and scientific institutions. A stronger cooperation on a transnational level is required to enhance an integrated approach to innovate current models on the basis of the best practices and studies performed in different areas so far. RECinMED, through a partnership composed by 11 project partners coming from 9 countries and three associated partners, aims at testing innovative solutions in order to foster the creation and good functioning of inclusive Renewable Energy Communities. The project will develop a new cooperation forum and coordination activities on a transnational level to strengthen the networks and positive spillovers among projects regarding energy communities. In addition, three Working Groups will be established in order to analyze existing knowledge, design and implement pilot actions in three different contexts: urban areas, rural territories and islands. Each WG will face specific challenges and will be able to identify the best way to transfer and upscale validated solutions in new areas. Methodologies and tools already developed in previous EU projects have been selected. They will be analyzed and integrated according to best practices and available real-data to make them more effective and transferable in geographical contexts with similar traits and necessities. Testing reports, stakeholder engagement initiatives and a Transferability Plan will be delivered together with an ongoing quality assessment both for technical actions and project management, including communication.

Description (FR):
Une transition énergétique propre et juste ne peut reposer que sur l'implication directe de tous les acteurs agissant sur les territoires : citoyens, pouvoirs publics, entreprises privées et institutions scientifiques. Une coopération plus forte au niveau transnational est nécessaire pour renforcer une approche intégrée afin d'innover les modèles actuels sur la base des meilleures pratiques et des études réalisées jusqu'à présent dans différents domaines. RECinMED, à travers un partenariat composé de 11 partenaires de projet provenant de 9 pays et de trois partenaires associés, vise à tester des solutions innovantes afin de favoriser la création et le bon fonctionnement de communautés d'énergies renouvelables inclusives. Le projet développera un nouveau forum de coopération et des activités de coordination au niveau transnational pour renforcer les réseaux et les retombées positives entre les projets concernant les communautés énergétiques. En outre, trois groupes de travail seront créés afin d'analyser les connaissances existantes, de concevoir et de mettre en œuvre des actions pilotes dans trois contextes différents : les zones urbaines, les territoires ruraux et les îles. Chaque groupe de travail sera confronté à des défis spécifiques et sera en mesure d'identifier la meilleure façon de transférer et de mettre à l'échelle des solutions validées dans de nouveaux domaines. Des méthodologies et des outils déjà développés dans de précédents projets de l'UE ont été sélectionnés. Ils seront analysés et intégrés selon les meilleures pratiques et les données réelles disponibles pour les rendre plus efficaces et transférables dans des contextes géographiques présentant des caractéristiques et des besoins similaires. Des rapports de test, des initiatives d'engagement des parties prenantes et un plan de transférabilité seront livrés avec une évaluation continue de la qualité à la fois pour les actions techniques et la gestion de projet, y compris la communication.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The pre-contracting negotiation phase took a particularly long time and ended with the signing of the Partnership Agreement, at the end of March, and the signing of the Subsidy Contract, in mid-April. In the meantime, the project had begun its activities, with the Kick Off Meeting held online on 4 March 2024 (Minutes attached), and a second technical meeting, also held online, on 21 March 2024 (Minutes attached). During the KOM, the Steering Committee was set up (Minutes attached). On 4 and 5 June, the first F2F Meeting was held in Granada, Spain (Minutes attached) and also the 2nd Steering Committee Meeting (Minutes attached). The Quality Plan and Communication Plan were also presented during this meeting. (both attached) During the Granada Meeting, the 3 Working Groups met for the first time and started working. The report of the work done is contained in the Minutes of the Meeting itself. On 10 April, the Kick Off Meeting of the Green Living Areas Community took place in Turin, Italy. For RECinMED, Igor Doler (LP) and Marco Affronte (PM) were present. Marco Affronte (PM) and Francesco Suzzi (PP6) participated to the online GLA's Thematic Working Groups focusing on policy, on June 11th. 8 PPs out of 11, hung posters of the project in their offices, and uploaded photos to Basecamp. Attached photos. Although the official project start date remained at 1 January 2024, the project really got underway with the technical work at the end of the negotiation and fulfilment of the pre-project tasks (Subsidy Contract, Partnership Agreement). The technical specifications of WP1 were first entered into at the meeting on 21 March. Then, in Granada, the work of the WGs started concretely and continued until the end of the year (and beyond) with the analysis of the previous projects and solutions already developed (A 1.3) and contact with the stakeholders to be involved in the 3 WGs. Of course all the PPs were involved in this activities. At the same time, constant contact and coordination with Renewable Energy Thematic Community, IDP and JS (A1.1) was maintained. At the end of the year, the Pilot Transnational Cooperation Forum for the development of RECs was activated (A1.2). Activity 1.4, namely the design of the 3 Pilot Actions, builds on the work done in this second semester, in particular that of A1.3, and will be developed in the following months. During this first year, the project's Editorial Communication Plan was also drawn up by the LP, as well as all the material for the visual identity. Likewise, the RECinMED Project Carbon Footprint Reduction Strategy was prepared and the first Project Carbon Footprint_Report; both of them by the responsible Partner, PP10. The LP was also involved in the development of the Quality Toolkit, which was shared and elaborated in consultation with the entire Consortium. During the first year the following meetings were held (Minutes attached in the Report Annexes section): - 4.3.2024 - KOM (online) + 1st Steering Committee Meeting - 21.3.2024 - Technical Project Meeting (online) - 4/5.6.2024 - 3rd Project Meeting (1st F2F) in Granada, Spain + 2nd Steering Committee Meeting - 3.7.2024 Capacity building meeting (online) - 27/28.11.2024 - 4th Project Meeting (2nd F2F) in Pafos, Cyprus + 3rd Steering Committee Meeting As in the first year of the project, constant contact and coordination with the Renewable Energy Thematic Community, IDP, and JS (A1.1) was also maintained during Period 3. Members of RECinMED’s partnership participated in three events in the first semester of 2025: - Green Living Area Online Thematic Working Groups on policy (27.02.2025, online) - Clean Energy for EU Islands Forum (14–15.05.2025, in person) - 2nd Gathering of the Green Living Areas Thematic Community, Barcelona (28–29.05.2025, in person) The Transnational Cooperation Forum for the development of RECs (A1.2), launched in Period 2, continued to engage both the community and the public interested in RECs, including through the implementation of an editorial plan whereby partners take turns posting topics and news on the Forum to stimulate comments and debate. For Activities 1.3 and 1.4, the three WGs, after analysing in detail previous projects and already developed solutions, and after discussion and identification of the three potential Pilot Actions to be implemented in the study areas (urban, rural, and islands), developed the three Action Plans, which together also constitute Output 1.2. Regarding Deliverable 1.4.1: during the meetings of 23.04.2025 (online) and 27–28.05.2025 (in person), there was extensive discussion on how to organise the work for the training course. In Smolyan, the final structure was agreed (six modules), and the partners responsible for developing them were assigned. The LP will then compile the six modules into a single final product. This work was not completed in Period 3 but will be finalised in Period 4. For Activity 1.6, during the Smolyan Meeting in May 2025, the Technical Qualitative Assessment was carried out. The assessment focused on analysing the effectiveness of the three Action Plans delivered by the project partners, grouped into three clusters, each addressing a Pilot Action. A report was produced. During the third semester, the following meetings were held (minutes attached in the Report Annexes): 18.02.2025 – 5th Project Meeting (online) + 4th Steering Committee Meeting 27–28.05.2025 – 6th Project Meeting (3rd face-to-face) in Smolyan, Bulgaria + 5th Steering Committee Meeting - Communication - RECinMED actively strengthened its dissemination efforts during Period 3: The project’s social media presence grew steadily: the Facebook page reached 54 likes (+3 compared to P2) and 81 followers (+8 compared to P2), while the LinkedIn page achieved 108 followers (+32 compared to P2). All partners contributed to dissemination through their institutional channels, enhancing project visibility. The “Euro-MED Transnational Cooperation” Forum was launched on LinkedIn in January 2025 and arrive to 70 members at the end of Period 3. Each partner is responsible for leading a monthly thematic discussion and actively engaging in the debates initiated by others. The project video was produced and disseminated through both the project’s and all partners’ institutional channels. A press release covering the 3rd Transnational Project Meeting in Bulgaria was prepared and shared by the hosting partner, Municipality of Smolyan. RECinMED was also represented at the 2nd Gathering of the Green Living Areas Thematic Community of the Interreg Euro-MED Programme, held on 28–29 May 2025 in Barcelona, Spain, further strengthening its visibility within the programme community.

Relevant linked projects:

  • ETU (INTERREG MED)
  • The Ecosystemic Transition Unit (ETU) is a multilevel governance model developed by the Interreg MED Renewable Energy community to integrate the energy transition into territorial planning, using a holistic approach. It is based on the outcomes of six modular projects that ran from 2016-2019 across the Mediterranean. The modular projects – carried out in the framework of the Interreg MED Renewable Energy project – supported rural and island municipalities to implement renewable energy projects, while identifying and finding solutions to common challenges.
  • The ETU brings together governance, economic competitiveness and social innovation to revitalise rural areas and increase their resilience to climate change.
  • It is based on four pillars (Energy Planning, Energy Communities, Energy Governance, Energy Facilities) and five principles (Holistic response to climate change, Territorial equity, Social innovation, Green economy, Cooperation and commitment).

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The production and use of energy account for more than 3/4 of the EU’s greenhouse gas emissions. Addressing the decarbonisation of the EU’s energy system is therefore critical to reach EU emissions reduction objectives towards carbon neutrality by 2050.
    • Among the several objectives the EU Commission has set, two perfectly fit with those perpetrated by RECinMED:
    • - build interconnected energy systems and better integrated grids to support renewable energy sources;
    • - empower consumers and help EU countries to tackle energy poverty
    • The EU Energy System Integration Strategy promotes a model with energy flows between producers and consumers and Renewable Energy Communities having at their core the role of prosumers will help to go in the right direction.
    • Moreover, a more distributed generation and distribution of energy based on renewable power plants requires a higher involvement and awareness of consumers who shall use innovative digital tools and equipments such as smart meters for residential buildings.
    • RECinMED will add remarkable contribution to research and innovation in this field, testing already existing solutions in order to adapt them to different areas and social frameworks.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 RECinMED follows a transnational approach to act at the local level, putting together partners coming from different EU countries who share common territorial challenges. The Territorial Agenda 2030 calls for this kind of multilevel cooperation and polycentric development models.
    • The valorization of the geographical specificities of different areas to face bigger challenges, namely energy transition, is at the core of RECinMED.
    • At the same time, cooperation forums, engagement activities with stakeholders and coordination activities with JS, Renewable Energy Thematic Community, IDP and Euro-MED Academy facilitate the dialogue with decision makers at every level as desire and stimulated in the Territorial Agenda 2030.
    • The diffusion of renewable energy communities will shape the decarbonization path of the EU according to a place-based approach combined with cooperation between territories.
    • Testing solutions, in particular tools and methodologies to create and manage energy communities in three distinct target areas that represent most of the territorial framework in the EU, that is urban, rural and islands will be beneficial for those communities involved and for the external actors involved.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion RECinMED contributes to the EU Strategy for the Adriatic and Ionian Region (EUSAIR), addressing in particular Pillar 2 - Connecting the region
    • Regarding pillar 2, one of its specific objectives is to achieve a well-interconnected and functioning internal energy market contributing to the EU energy policy objectives: competitiveness, security of supply and sustainability.
    • RECinMED, especially in WP2, will test innovative solutions studied and developed in previous projects but integrated and enforced, aimed at reaching the same objectives.
    • The Partnership comprises five Project Partners coming from EUSAIR area:
    • SCV (Slovenia), UNIBO and ASSO (Italy), Argonauta and RERA (Croatia).
    • Argonauta and RERA will share the same Working Group focused on testing solutions for RECs development in islands. This effort contributes to the EUSAIR, that shows a strong effort on maritime and coastal sustainability as testified by its Pillar 1 which concerns Blue Growth
