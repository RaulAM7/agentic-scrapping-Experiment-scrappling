Enhancing Marine Protected Areas as Nature Based Solutions for adaptation to climate change: from local actions to Mediterranean basin strategy

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Le bassin Méditerranée est considéré comme un haut lieu du changement climatique (CC), dont les effets se superposent à d’autres changements environnementaux causés par l’activité humaine comme la surpêche, la perte d'habitat et la pollution. Tout cela affecte les écosystèmes marins méditerranéens et les personnes dont les moyens de subsistance en dépendent. Afin de contribuer à inverser cette tendance à la dégradation, MPA4Change cherche à renforcer l'efficacité des aires marines protégées (AMP) et de la restauration comme solutions fondées sur la nature clef pour l'adaptation au changement climatique en déployant une approche multi-niveaux, allant de l’action locale des AMP aux stratégies méditerranéennes, afin d'assurer leur durabilité sur le long terme. Pour ce faire, MPA4Change travaillera sur 3 axes principaux : 1) promouvoir et affiner les outils préalablement testés d'évaluation des risques, de suivi, de partage des données, de restauration, de science citoyenne, d'approches participatives et de définition de plans d'action pour l’adaptation, de communication ; 2) encourager le dialogue politique et la coordination entre stratégies européennes, méditerranéennes et mondiales de PSM/GIZC comme la CGPM/FAO, le PAM/PNUE, CAR/ASP, UpM ; et 3) établir une équipe d'experts pour rendre pleinement opérationnelle la mise en œuvre des politiques et outils d’adaptation et de mitigation du CC et en assurer la viabilité à long terme. Le résultat final attendu de MPA4Change est le renforcement de la résilience des zones côtières méditerranéennes afin de garantir la conservation et la valorisation durable des écosystèmes marins. Pleinement aligné sur les défis du SO2.4 d'Interreg Euro-MED, MPA4Change augmentera la capacité d'adaptation au CC en i) renforçant l'efficacité des AMP et de la restauration, ii) améliorant la coordination et la coopération des parties prenantes au-delà des frontières et iii) accroissant la sensibilisation au CC et la culture environnementale.

Description (EN):
The Mediterranean Sea is considered a hot spot for climate change. The impacts of climate change are interacting with other environmental changes caused by human activities (e.g. overfishing, habitat loss and pollution). As a result, Mediterranean marine ecosystems and human livelihoods that rely on them are being affected. To contribute to reverse this severe degradation trend, MPA4Change will look to enhance the effectiveness of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) and restoration as key Nature Based Solution for climate change adaptation by deploying a multi-level approach, from local MPA actions to Mediterranean strategies, in view to ensure its long-term sustainability and capacity. To do so MPA4Change will work on 3 main axis: 1) promote and fine-tune consolidated tools for risk assessment, monitoring, data sharing, restoration, citizen science, participatory approaches and defining adaptation action plans, communication, 2) support the Policy dialogue and coordinate with EU, Mediterranean and Global ICZM/MSP Strategies (e.g., FAO/GFCM, UNEP/MPA, RAC/SPA, UfM) and 3) develop a Roster of experts to fully operationalise the implementation of policies and tools ensuring the long-term sustainability and capacity for adaptation and mitigation to climate change. The final expected main outcome of MPA4Change is to enhance the resilience of Mediterranean coastal areas in view to support the good environmental status and sustainable use of marine ecosystems. Fully aligned with Interreg Euro-MED specific objective 2.4’s challenges, MPA4Change will increase the capacity to adapt to climate change by i) enhancing the effectiveness of MPAs and restoration, ii) improving the coordination and cooperation of stakeholders across borders to tackle them and iii) reinforcing raise awareness and environmental culture to climate change.

Actual Achievements (EN):
Since its approval at the end of 2023, the MPA4Change project has made substantial progress toward enhancing Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) as nature-based solutions for climate change adaptation. The project launched with a productive Kick-off Meeting (KoM) in January 2024 in Barcelona, where partners set the groundwork for the project and established a collaborative framework for future actions. The KoM allowed partners to clarify key project objectives and roles, responsibilities, and upcoming activities, providing a clear roadmap for the project’s implementation. The project has focused on the fine-tuning and adaptation of existing tool-kits to support the adoption of adaptation measures in MPA face to climate change, mainly based on those developed in the previous project MPAEngage but adding as well tools from other EU projects, for wider use across Mediterranean MPAs. These tools, including those for vulnerability assessments, ecological monitoring, restoration protocols, participatory approaches, development of adaptation action plans and communication materials have been identified and optimized in collaboration with key partners. A key part of this was the development of training materials, such as manuals and video tutorials, to support end users in the independent implementation of these tools. This process ensures their adaptability to different regional contexts, strengthening their potential for broader application. One of the highlights of this period was the starting of trainings with the support of online tools and meetings plus acapacity-building workshop held in Cyprus in May 2024, where the MPA4Change consortium transferred critical monitoring and citizen science tools to partners, including MPA managers and local authorities. The training introduced partners and local stakeholders to the use of monitoring protocols such as Local Ecological Knowledge (LEK), citizen science, species-specific protocols, and temperature monitoring. These efforts aim to equip MPAs with the tools necessary to monitor and respond to climate change impacts effectively, highlighting how these tools can be integrated into day-to-day management and long-term climate resilience strategies. Significant progress was made in communication and outreach activities, which are essential for fostering community engagement and policy advocacy. The project launched its communication channels, including a website and social media platforms, ensuring visibility and information sharing among a wider audience. A structured communication strategy was developed to promote the project's tools and achievements, aiming to engage both local stakeholders and the broader Mediterranean region. In addition, efforts have been focused on mapping potential MPA “takers” (a list of potential MPAs and stakeholders) who can adopt the project's tools, further expanding the impact of MPA4Change across the Mediterranean. The project maintained continuous coordination and quality assurance through regular online meetings: a monthly “Coordination Team” meeting, and specific WP leaders meetings when needed, besides the day-to-day communication between partners. Close collaboration has allowed for the effective exchange of knowledge and the development of synergies, particularly during the training and tool adaptation processes. Moreover, the consortium has also initiated efforts to assess its carbon footprint in line with its sustainability goals. Using the ZeroCO2Med calculator, the project is tracking the carbon footprint of its activities, in order to measure its impact and implement mitigation strategies where necessary. In conclusion, the MPA4Change project has successfully laid a solid foundation for achieving its objectives by refining critical tools, building capacities, enhancing communication strategies, and establishing strong cooperation among partners. The MPA4Change project has made significant progress during its second reporting period, focusing on scientific monitoring, capacity-building, stakeholder engagement, and policy advocacy across Mediterranean Marine Protected Areas (MPAs). One of the key areas of advancement has been the fine-tuning of the toolkits developed to enhance the effectiveness of Nature-Based Solutions (NbS), mainly the MPAs to face the climate emergency. . The toolkits support the MPAs across the entire process ensuring that MPAs have access to standardized, science-based methodologies for climate adaptation. During this period capacity building workshops on the fine tuned toolkits to equip MPA managers have been organized during this period: i) monitoring protocols to track ecological climate impacts (temperature monitoring, benthic health assessments, and climate fish indicator surveysIi) vulnerability assessment methodologies, iii) GIS tools and iv) citizen science program, , This trainings enhanced the ability of MPAs implementing the tools to track climate impacts and start the design of adaptive management strategies. The project is preparing for its 2025 blended learning training program, combining online courses with practical field sessions in multiple MPAs to ensure effective knowledge transfer. It is worth noting that the Citizen Science training program saw significant progress, engaging eight diving centers and 15 instructors in climate-sensitive fish species monitoring, particularly within Atlantic MPAs. This initiative strengthens public participation in conservation and provides MPAs with valuable long-term ecological data. Besides, a major milestone in capacity building was the Vulnerability Assessment (VA) Training, which included online sessions and an intensive in-person workshop in Faro, Portugal. Participants worked with real datasets, conducted vulnerability assessments, and refined methodologies to improve their accuracy and applicability. The integration of case studies from previous adaptation projects further supported the practical application of MPA4Change tools in real-world conservation efforts. Beyond technical advancements, the project strengthened policy engagement and stakeholder collaboration. A key policy meeting in Faro brought together MPA managers and decision-makers to explore the practical application of MPA4Change tools in daily marine governance. The project further expanded its policy influence through collaboration with existing Mediterranean initiatives, integrating some of MPA4Change toolkits into wider networks of MPAs. Participation in international and national conferences, including the 5th International Conference on Climate Change in Zadar and the Croatian Nature Protection Managers' Conference, further reinforced knowledge exchange. A survey using Mentimeter helped assess opportunities for collaboration and future implementation strategies. A structured policy advocacy strategy was developed, including a policy roadmap, key messages, and a comprehensive event and stakeholder database to support long-term engagement with decision-makers. Policy discussions were further strengthened through participation in high-level EU and Mediterranean conservation meetings, ensuring that MPA4Change outputs contribute to broader marine governance frameworks. The project also prioritized public outreach and communication to maximize awareness and engagement. In addition, diving protocols and promotional materials were developed to encourage recreational divers to contribute climate monitoring data, fostering citizen science engagement in MPAs. To ensure long-term impact and sustainability, the project advanced in the design of the “100 MPA MedAlliance”. This initiative builds on three main pillars: - Ready to use toolkits to guide MPAs in deploying adaptation action plans - Dedicated long-term assistance mechanism with a roster of experts - Policy & advocacy actions to enhance the role of MPAs to effectively face the climate change crisis Moving forward, MPA4Change will focus on consolidating knowledge, expanding the implementation of tools, and ensuring long-term policy integration for Mediterranean MPAs through the “100 MPA MedAlliance”. Through continuous collaboration and engagement, the project is well-positioned to strengthen marine conservation efforts and enhance resilience to climate change, making MPAs key actors in Nature-Based Solutions for climate adaptation across the region.

  • MPA-ENGAGE: Engaging Mediterranean key actors in Ecosystem Approach to manage Marine Protected Area to face Climate Change (programme Interreg MED 2014-2020), 2019-2022.
  • Climate Change is dramatically affecting the Mediterranean Sea, which is warning at a rate 20% faster than the world’s average. MPAs, despite the nature-based solution they offer to support efforts towards climate change adaptation and mitigation, are the first ones to feel the climate change effects, several Mediterranean MPAs already facing major biodiversity and functional alterations due to climate change, the others likely facing them in the next few decades. There is, therefore, an urgency to mitigate these risks and to consider adaptation options in partnership with local communities, decision-makers, civil society organizations research bodies, and other socio-economic actors at local, national, and regional level.
  • MPA-ENGAGE's objective was to promote the role of MPAs as nature-based solutions to adapt and mitigate the ongoing climate change effects in the Mediterranean Sea. Through a participatory approach (quintuple helix - MPAs, socio-eco actors, local/regional authorities, scientists and citizens), MPA-ENGAGE monitored the climate change impacts, elaborated vulnerability assessments plans and developed climate change adaptation actions plans in 7 Marine Protected Areas located in different Mediterranean countries: Albania, Croatia, France, Greece, Italy, and Spain. It engaged recreational divers and small-scale fishery actors in citizen science activities. Through trainings, twinnings and data sharing activities, it transferred its operational know-how to key coastal actors to develop a strong and resilient strategy. MPA-ENGAGE also promoted a Med Policy Dialogue among an assembly of associates (scientists, MPAs, & Networks/regions), with the support of organisations such as MEDPAN, RAC-SPA,UNEP/MAP, FAO; GFCM, CPMR-ICM, WWF and UfM.

    • MPA4Change will contribute to Integrated monitoring and Assessment Program (IMAP) by promoting a better coordination, technical and political cooperation across the Mediterranean countries to track climate change effects through the monitoring toolkits. The derived indicators can directly feed the Ecological Objectives (EO) to assess the Good Environmental Status (GES) of marine ecosystems. It is worth noting the harmonization efforts in assessing the GES in the Mediterranean since, the EO are aligned with the MSFD descriptors. Thus, by enhancing the resilience to climate change effects, the MPAs will contribute to the assessment of IMAP-GES.
    • Other New EU Strategy on Adaptation to Climate Change
    • The new EU Strategy on Adaptation to Climate Change (2021) aims to realise the 2050 vision of a climate-resilient Union by making adaptation smarter, more systemic, swifter, and by stepping up international action. Focusing on Mediterranean marine protected areas, MPA4Change will contribute to promoting the implementation of efficient Nature Based Solutions to tackle climate change and preserve marine ecosystems. In the process MPA4Change will contribute to improve our knowledge on climate change impacts, provide a harmonized framework for systemic adaptation planning and risk assessment in the MPAs, accelerate the adoption adaptation actions in the management plans Improve knowledge of climate impacts and solutions and strengthen climate resilience by building strong international cooperation on climate adaptation. These activities are completely aligned with the main goals for the strategy.
    • Other Union for the Mediterranean’s 2030 GreenerMed Agenda
    • MPA4Change is totally aligned to the 2030 GreenerMed Agenda objectives and will particularly contribute to the Axis 3 “Protect, preserve, manage and restore natural resources in the Mediterranean region within an integrated ecosystem approach, including terrestrial, marine and coastal dimensions”. The partnership will thus participate to UfM Working Group on Environment and Climate Change by providing operational tools in a wide array of identified key actions on the Axis 3, for instance but not only KA 3.1, Support actions that preserve, protect and/ or restore terrestrial, marine and coastal ecosystems, natural capital and biodiversity, KA3.3. Promote an integrated ecosystem-based approach to managing terrestrial, costal and marine natural resources, and KA3.9 . Promote nature-based solutions.
    • Other EU Marine Spatial Planning (MSP)
    • The EU MSP Directive aims to establish a framework for maritime spatial planning aimed at promoting the sustainable growth of maritime economies, the sustainable development of marine areas and the sustainable use of marine resources. The MSP must consider among others objectives “Preserving, protecting and improving the environment, including resilience to climate change impacts”. MPA4Change will contribute to tackle the challenge to incorporate the climate change effects in the design of local and networks of MPAs. This will be key to comply with the directive main goal.
    • Other Post-2020 SAPBIO (UNEP-MAP)
    • The Post-2020 Strategic Action Programme for the Conservation of Biodiversity and Sustainable Management of Natural Resources in the Mediterranean Region (Post-2020 SAPBIO) proposes activities tailored towards realistic objectives and targets to achieve a healthy Mediterranean Sea and coast in the context of sustainable development. Among the main objectives is to effectively protect 30% of the Mediterranean by 2030. MPA4Change will contribute to improve the effectiveness of MPAs specially face to the climate change effects by supporting key actors across different levels from local MPAs management bodies to international organizations and policy makers. Likewise, MPA4Change through the adoption of harmonized toolkits will enhance the cooperation and synergies across the MPA networks.
    • Other Post-2020 Global Biodiversity Framework (UNCBD)
    • The Post-2020 Global Biodiversity Framework is expected to be adopted during the UN CBD COP15. The framework establishes goals and targets to be achieved by 2030 in view to reach the Convention on Biological Diversity's vision of ‘Living in harmony with nature’ by 2050. The framework is still under discussion but include some targets especially relevant for MPA4Change activities to contribute to such as the increase of protection for the ocean up at least 30%, protect areas to support adaptation and mitigation to climate change and enhance effective management processes.
    • Other Maritime Strategy Framework Directive 2008/56/EC (MSFD)
    • MPA4Change will contribute to Marine Strategy Framework Directive (MSFD) by promoting a better coordination, technical and political cooperation across the Mediterranean countries to track climate change effects through the monitoring toolkit. The derived indicators can directly feed the MSFD Descriptors included to assess the Good Environmental Status (GES) of marine ecosystems. Thus, by enhancing the resilience to climate change effects, the MPAs will contribute as well to the assessment of MSFD-GES.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin The WestMed initiative aims at defining and implementing the Mediterranean Sea basin strategy. It is the result of years of dialogue between ten countries in the western Mediterranean region involved in the “5+5 Dialogue”: 5 EU Member States (France, Italy, Portugal, Spain and Malta), and 5 Southern partner countries (Algeria, Libya, Mauritania, Morocco and Tunisia). Several key areas from the Blue economy are covered through national hubs and dedicated working groups.
    • MPA4Change can contribute to WestMed by promoting the coordination and cooperation to enhance the governance face to climate change of coastal areas in general and in particular of MPAs through the adoption of participatory processes and development sustainable socio-economic activities (citizen science) supporting the resilience of MPAs face to climate change. Besides the data sharing and training programs may be of interest of the WestMed community.
