Promoting shared stewardship for Mediterranean Coasts

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The Mediterranean basin is one of the most important hotspots of biodiversity in the world in terms of richness of species and ecosystems. However, human activities and overexploitation of natural resources are resulting in ecosystem degradation and species lost at an alarming rate. The scale of these threats urges for a coordinated transnational approach. COASTRUST addresses the need to sustainably use, manage, protect or restore species, habitats, or ecosystems with the involvement of stakeholders from the connected economic sectors and of citizens, consistently with the European policy framework promoting participatory approaches to foster biodiversity conservation. Besides the prime conservation tool for biodiversity maintenance, represented by protected areas and their harmonized management, the project focuses on environmental stewardship as a form of governance acting at local and community level that reflects the rising influence on economic sectors exerted by environmental principles. In the project, environmental stewardship is applied in coastal areas undergoing anthropogenic pressures and results in the establishment of multistakeholder governance mechanisms aiming to support the management of natural resources at land and sea. The project acts at the strategic methodological level, by providing the conditions to enable environmental stewardship and by releasing a methodology for further transferring COASTRUST approach beyond the project scope, benefiting every Mediterranean citizen; and at the implementation level, by supporting and monitoring pilot activities development. A coastal stewardship strategy and a set of actions are developed for each target area according to its specificities, needs and environmental objectives such as the structure of local economy (fishery, tourism, agriculture, …), stakeholders, natural assets (individual and multiple species, individual habitats, entire ecosystems, integrated human-environment landscapes, …).

Description (FR):
Le méditerranéen est considéré comme l'un des plus importants hotspots de biodiversité au monde en termes de richesse en espèces et en écosystèmes. Les activités humaines et la surexploitation des ressources naturelles entraînent une dégradation des écosystèmes et la perte d'espèces à un rythme alarmant. COASTRUST répond à la nécessité d'utiliser, de gérer, de protéger ou de restaurer durablement les espèces, les habitats ou les écosystèmes avec la participation des acteurs des secteurs économiques connexes et des citoyens, conformément au cadre politique européen promouvant les approches participatives pour favoriser la conservation de la biodiversité. Outre le principal outil de conservation pour le maintien de la biodiversité, représenté par les aires protégées et leur gestion harmonisée, le projet se concentre sur la gérance de l'environnement en tant que forme de gouvernance agissant au niveau local et communautaire qui reflète l'influence croissante sur les secteurs économiques exercée par les principes environnementaux. L'intendance a été principalement mise en œuvre en tant qu'intendance des terres et sera déclinée de manière innovante par COASTRUST dans les zones côtières subissant des pressions anthropiques. La coopération transnationale permettra de construire, à partir d'expériences développées localement, une approche coordonnée pour soutenir la gestion des ressources naturelles à terre et en mer. Le projet agit au niveau stratégique et méthodologique, en fournissant les conditions pour permettre la gérance environnementale et en libérant une méthodologie pour transférer davantage son approche au-delà de la portée du projet; et par la mise en œuvre d'activités pilotes. Dans le cadre du projet, une stratégie d'intendance côtière et un ensemble d'actions sont développés pour chaque zone cible en fonction de ses spécificités, besoins et objectifs environnementaux tels que la structure de l'économie locale, les parties prenantes, les atouts naturels.

Actual Achievements (EN):
COASTRUST progress in the 1st reporting period has been highly satisfactory, with key activities and deliverables achieved in line with the planned timeline. Strong cooperation between Project Partners (PPs) and effective coordination by the Lead Partner, the Region of Western Greece (RWG), facilitated the smooth initiation of the project. RWG ensured timely communication with the project officer and guided PPs through the administrative pre-contracting phase, resulting in the swift finalization of all contracting procedures, which allowed the project to start without delays. Following the project’s approval, RWG led detailed discussions to map out the next steps for project implementation. The Kick-Off Meeting (KOM) in March 2024, organized online by RWG, successfully set the stage for partners to plan and execute their activities for the 1st reporting period. Regular monthly meetings have been held to ensure that each partner stays on track and contributes meaningfully to the project. The Steering Committee and Rules of Procedure have been established, ensuring smooth governance and collaboration among partners. Communication efforts have been a central focus, with partners such as DUNEA playing a pivotal role in designing the project's communication strategy, which includes the creation of the COASTRUST website and social media platforms. These channels, including accounts on Facebook and Instagram, are actively being used to engage a wide range of stakeholders—from local authorities and NGOs to the general public. Communication materials, such as posters and flyers, have been developed and are in the process of being translated into local languages, ensuring that the project reaches diverse audiences across all participating regions. One of the core activities under WP1 has been the development of Methodology 1.0, which provides a unified approach to marine and coastal stewardship initiatives. Partners like SUBMON and UNIROMA3 played a key role in shaping this document, ensuring it addresses the unique challenges and needs of each region's pilot area. The online capacity-building sessions facilitated by SUBMON have empowered partners with the knowledge and tools necessary to implement stewardship activities effectively in their respective regions. These sessions have also helped to define objectives and identify barriers in the coastal areas, ensuring that the methodology is practical and targeted. In WP2, focused on Carbon Footprint Monitoring, significant strides were made with the delivery of the carbon footprint monitoring, coordinated by UNIROMA3. During this reporting period, PPs began collecting carbon data from their pilot areas, which will be integrated into the Programme’s online tool for future analysis. This output aligns closely with COASTRUST’s environmental objectives, emphasizing the project’s contribution to broader European and global climate initiatives. In addition to these technical milestones, COASTRUST has been closely involved with the Natural Heritage Mission, contributing feedback to the Joint Secretariat (JS) and engaging in governance-related activities. Partners participated in Natural Heritage Mission events, including webinars and in-person meetings, fostering collaboration with other projects under the Mission and exploring synergies for future cooperation. This engagement helps position COASTRUST within a wider network of environmental and stewardship projects, promoting knowledge sharing and alignment with overarching sustainability goals. COASTRUST progress in the 2nd reporting period has been highly satisfactory, with key activities and deliverables achieved in line with the planned timeline. Strong cooperation between Project Partners (PPs) and effective coordination by the Lead Partner, the Region of Western Greece (RWG), facilitated the smooth initiation of the project. Under RWG's leadership, the project has seen a smooth initiation and adept coordination of various activities. RWG not only managed key administrative and financial aspects but also organized several online progress meetings to ensure continuous collaboration. Key events include the Progress and Steering Committee meetings held in Seville in October, which were instrumental in refining project strategies and timelines. The technical contributions have been robust, with contribution from all partners. In WP1, under the guidance of WP1 leader -SUBMON PP continued advancing in the development of Activities 1.1, 1.2 and 1.3, supporting and accompanying partners as they develop and begin their coastal/marine stewardship initiatives in their demonstration sites. Since the first in person meeting was delayed until after the summer (as explained in the report for the first reporting period), a first online capacity building session was organized for the 5th of July 2024. This session was one morning long, and all partners attended. A report of this capacity building session was uploaded to Basecamp. During this period, the partners has continued working in the development of the Methodology 1.0 and the final draft of the methodology was presented and explained during the in-person capacity building in Seville, and some priority actions discussed with partners. After this meeting, the draft was shared among all partners for further comments, and in December 2024 the final document was uploaded to Basecamp as an internal deliverable. The stakeholder mapping methodology, which was the first step of Activity 1.3, was explained to all partners during the online capacity building in July, so that partners had enough time to work on it before the meeting in Seville, and share doubts with SUBMON during their work. Partners have finished their preliminary stakeholder mapping during this second reporting period (it will be a living document throughout the project as each stewardship initiative advances and evolves), and there is a brief analysis of this work for each site in the final deliverable of site-specific reports D.1.3. This Deliverable was carried out as a shared effort of all partners: SUBMON drafted the guidelines to complete each site-specific report, and all partners completed the report for their sites in due time. In WP2, focused on Carbon Footprint Monitoring, significant strides were made with the delivery of the carbon footprint monitoring, coordinated by UNIROMA3. During this reporting period, PPs collected carbon data from their areas, which was integrated into the Programme’s online tool for future analysis. Alongside these efforts, the period also marked the initiation of key stakeholder engagements, essential for the creation of the Local Action Group in the pilot area. These preliminary contacts and bilateral meetings set the stage for more structured activities. Subsequently, partners engaged in drafting a stakeholders' engagement plan and organized the first local event aimed at formally establishing the Local Action Group and beginning the joint definition of coordinated stewardship initiatives. Some partners have already started their local events. This output aligns closely with COASTRUST’s environmental objectives, emphasizing the project’s contribution to broader European and global climate initiatives. Communication efforts have been a central focus, with partners such as DUNEA playing a pivotal role in designing the project's communication strategy, which includes the creation of the COASTRUST website and social media platforms. These channels, including accounts on Facebook and Instagram, are actively being used to engage a wide range of stakeholders—from local authorities and NGOs to the general public. Communication materials, such as posters and flyers, have been developed and are in the process of being translated into local languages, ensuring that the project reaches diverse audiences across all participating regions. In addition to these technical milestones, COASTRUST has been closely involved with the Natural Heritage Mission, contributing feedback to the Joint Secretariat (JS) and engaging in governance-related activities. Partners participated in Natural Heritage Mission events, including webinars and in-person meetings, fostering collaboration with other projects under the Mission and exploring synergies for future cooperation. This engagement helps position COASTRUST within a wider network of environmental and stewardship projects, promoting knowledge sharing and alignment with overarching sustainability goals.
