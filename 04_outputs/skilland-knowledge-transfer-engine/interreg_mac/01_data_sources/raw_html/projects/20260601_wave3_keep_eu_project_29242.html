PreVEnting and ReDucing the tExtiles waste mountain in the MED area

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Dans la période actuelle où les challenges et les opportunités liées aux textiles usagés sont de plus en plus dans la ligne de mire des gouvernements, de l’industrie et de la société civile, des changements importants doivent être faits pour une transition vers une économie circulaire pour les textiles et les vêtements (T/V). Les exécutifs locaux, régionaux et nationaux ont un rôle primordial à jouer afin de créer les bonnes conditions et inciter à accélérer cette transition. Pour clore la boucle textile, tous les acteurs de la chaine de valeur T/V doivent trouver ensemble le bon modèle économique. Dans leur processus, les institutions doivent réussir à inclure des organisations qui n’ont pas encore trouvé la solution ; celles qui sont chargés de la gestion de la fin de vie des textiles, mais aussi celles qui collectent, trient, recyclent, et celles qui s’orientent vers la ré-utilisation et les process de création de nouveaux produits. VERDEinMED a pour objet de proposer un cadre de travail pour étudier des réussites de business modèles circulaires, en identifiant les besoins d’innovation dans les modèles économiques, en matière technologique et sociale, ainsi que dans les contextes législatifs et d’usage. En commençant par la refondation de l’industrie reposant sur des pratiques durables, et orientant la demande vers une consommation consciente, ce projet aboutira à la définition du cadre nécessaire à la transition du secteur des T/V vers la durabilité et la circularité. L’écosystème ainsi créé sera soutenu au travers de la Bussola Verde, une « boussole verte » qui intégrera la création de hubs régionaux, de plateformes de savoirs et de connaissances, et un service support en une seule entité, regroupant des experts et des expertises venues de différents horizons, différents territoires, afin de préparer la constitution de centres de connaissance et de compétences, sortes de lieux uniques de convergence, dans chaque région.

Description (EN):
While the challenges and opportunities of used textiles are increasingly in the spotlight of governments, industry, and civil society, considerable system changes are required to transition towards a circular economy for textiles and clothing (T/C). Local, regional, national governments can and should play a pivotal role to create the right conditions and incentives to accelerate this transition. In order to close the textiles loop all players within the T/C value chain need to be able to create a viable business case. In their engagement processes, governments will need to include those organisations that have not yet been part of the solution: those in charge of the end-of-use textiles value chain such as waste management companies as collectors, sorters, recyclers, but also end users who will be involved in the co creation process of new products. VERDEinMED will present an analytical framework to study the successful implementation of circular business models, identifying the needs in terms of business model innovation, technological innovation and social innovation, and the enabling conditions of policy and behavioural change. VERDE project will show the full pallet of a threefold support service addressed to civil society, industry and policy makers indicating tailor made interventions needed for this transition. Starting with the reformation the industry to use sustainable practices, orienting the demand towards conscious consumption it will conclude by defining a policy framework to support the transition of the T/C sector towards sustainability and circularity. The ecosystem thus created, will be sustained through the Verde Bussola, a Green Compass integrating the creation of regional hubs, knowledge platform and support service into a single entity, bringing together experts and knowledge from different locations in order to prepare the framework for the constitution of knowledge and competence centers as a one stop shop in each region.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the initial reporting period, PPs successfully executed all planned activities without exception. LP1 CERTH, organized the first in-person kick-off meeting involving both Project Partners and Associated Partners. This meeting took place in Kastoria, GR on the 6th and 7th of February 2024. The primary purpose of this kick-off meeting was to coordinate and monitor the tasks and activities outlined in the work packages, ensuring the timely and quality delivery of results and deliverables. Throughout the first six months of the project, several online meetings were held among the project partners to monitor and coordinate the implementation of the work packages. All PPs played an active role in Work Package 1, particularly in the development of the deliverable titled “D.1.2.2. Regional Reports of State of the Art of Textile Waste Management and Recycling.” PPs contributed significantly by providing a comprehensive overview of the European approach to textile waste management and recycling. This included addressing various issues, relevant legislation, and socio-behavioral aspects across Europe. Additionally, PPs gathered feedback specific to their national/regional context, detailing the regional and national framework for waste management and recycling. The deliverable has been finalized and uploaded to the basecamp. PPs contributed to deliverable D.1.1.1, known as the Project Management and Financial Handbook, which has been finalized and uploaded to the basecamp. This handbook serves as a crucial support tool, outlining the fundamental guidelines related to the VERDEinMED Programme procedures. It details the management structures, tools, functions, and the daily management and communication procedures necessary for the project's success. Moreover, all PPs actively participated in the “train the trainer” webinars organized by CITEVE, within Act. 1.4. Within the reporting period, two out of seven webinars were organized, namely: “Global Dimension of Textiles” and “Ecodesign Strategies”. In relation to Work Package 2, PPs contributed to the development of the deliverable D.2.5.1, the Communication Strategy, which is finalized and uploaded on the basecamp. As part of this effort, PPs created and disseminated several posts on social media networks to enhance the project's visibility and outreach. For Work Package 3, within Act. 3.4, Staff Members from CERTH and CTA as communication officer participated in person in the 2nd MED Innovation Camp which took place in Barcelona, from May 27th - 30th. In this Innovation Camp they represented the VERDEinMED Project and participated in the co-creation workshops on the Smart specialisation strategies supporting Mediterranean green and just transition. On the 17th of June, CERTH, CTA and TTA-TC participated in the Innovation Days in Marseille. Key challenges identified based on a co-creational approach: Regulatory Alignment -Cross-Border Collaboration - Sustainable Funding. For Act. 3.5, LEG prepared the necessary Excel file to calculate the carbon footprint associated with travel, accommodation, and event organization. This was accomplished using the tool provided by the Euro MED programme, ensuring accurate and comprehensive environmental impact assessments. All PPs provided the required information by filling out the provided file. In summary, during the initial reporting period, all PPs fulfilled their responsibilities across multiple work packages, contributing to critical deliverables and ensuring effective project management, coordination, and communication. The activities undertaken by WP and Activity Leaders were essential in laying a strong foundation for the VERDEinMED Project, ensuring its alignment with planned objectives and fostering collaboration among partners. The VERDEinMED project has made significant progress in its mission to promote sustainability within the textile and clothing sector. Several important activities have been completed, contributing to the transition towards more sustainable practices in the industry. One of the key milestones in the project was the finalization of the Project Management and Financial Handbook by CERTH, which provided essential guidance on the project's financial and operational management. Along with this, the development of the HUB Stakeholder Engagement Guidance document helped to create a stronger connection between the various partners and stakeholders involved in the project. All partners were engaged in the creation of the VERDEinMED Hubs and participated in numerous online meetings, ensuring active communication and collaboration. A critical part of the project focused on building a robust Knowledge Platform to support the transition toward more sustainable textile practices. Activity 1.2, defined the core components of the platform, which included the creation of regional reports on textile waste management and recycling. CERTH worked closely with E-ZAVOD to shape the platform's structure and ensured that it integrated seamlessly with the project's website. Training and upskilling were another key aspect of the project. Activity 1.3, led by CITEVE, involved the design and delivery of a comprehensive training program aimed at improving the skills of PPs and APs in the textile sector. This program, which concluded in December 2024, featured seven workshops that covered essential topics such as ecodesign strategies, circular business models, and promoting sustainable consumption practices. A total of 187 participants were trained, contributing to the final deliverables including training materials, Knowledge Platform content, communication campaigns, and living labs. These deliverables will help ensure that the project's objectives, which include fostering sustainability in the textile industry, are met and maintained in the long term. A crucial part of the project’s success is the establishment of the Support Service, a tailored technical assistance service for a diverse range of stakeholders, including SMEs, startups, research organizations, consumers, and policymakers. This service aims to provide targeted solutions to specific challenges within the sector, leveraging tested methodologies and insights from three Proofs of Concept (PoCs). These PoCs, which focus on technical assistance, B2C living labs, and capacity building for public authorities through the VERDE Academy, provide a strong foundation for the Support Service. For the Technical Assistance, CONF developed and circulated a questionnaire, translated into native languages, to assess the specific needs of regional textile companies. The responses were collected and analyzed, allowing the project team to tailor the solutions more effectively to regional needs. In addition to these efforts, Activity 2.3 saw the creation of a guidance document by LEG, which outlined strategies for preparing communication campaigns and organizing living labs. These initiatives are critical for disseminating knowledge and engaging with a broader audience. In Activity 3.4, CERTH, DIADYMA, TTA-TC and CTA participated in the1st and 2nd ISE Community of Practice events which took place on 18-19 of June and 14th of November respectively. Within these events the partners had the chance to provide opportunities for networking, knowledge exchange, and collaboration across different stakeholders. CTA also participated in online events such as Innovative Sustainable Economy Community (ISEC) Hub Launch Event that took place on Wednesday 2 October 2024 online; as well as in the dissemination of the ISE mission content in the project social media. In Activity 3.5, all project partners contributed to the calculation of the project’s carbon footprint, with LEG proposing strategies to reduce and improve the project’s environmental impact. The added value of the cooperation between all partners in the VERDEinMED project is evident. Each partner brought their expertise to different aspects of the project, ensuring a well-rounded approach. Through the development of the Knowledge Platform and the implementation of living labs, the project is promoting knowledge-sharing across the textile sector, helping to drive long-term sustainability. The regional surveys and customized technical assistance provided by the Support Service ensure that solutions are tailored to specific regional challenges, making the project’s impact even more significant. Furthermore, the project is not only focusing on the practical aspects of textiles' sustainability but also on improving the project's overall environmental footprint, as evidenced by the carbon footprint calculations and proposed improvements.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The ‘take-make-use-dispose’ linear economic model has been called into question many years ago and all industries, including the T/C industry have been taking into consideration that field. The main approach taken on the European level, to mitigate the risks connected with todays’ current wasteful and polluting system of production and consumption of T/C, is the circular economy. With the European Green Deal even more, due to the fact that circularity of the product is one of its most important pillars. This circular approach towards products manufactured in EU holds enormous potential when it comes to sustainability, where not only environment benefits but also people’s and the countries’ prosperity is an ambitious aim. VERDEinMED will contribute to a change in the production systems - from a linear to more circular and regenerative – which is a challenge but also an opportunity to support and create more innovative processes that make greater use of resource efficiency (A2.4, A3.2 & O2.1). To achieve that, VERDEinMED will include the 4 helix actors, in an attempt to recognize the urgency of developing the end-of-use value chain and the possibilities to gain momentum to drive investment in the collection, sorting and recycling practices of T/C products and to create awareness around the availability and potential of circular T/C products in order to drive a shift in both consumption and production practices.

Moreover, a close interdisciplinary collaboration between technical, behavioural and regulatory measures is needed to address the complex issues associated with microfibre pollution from textiles. VERDEinMED will facilitate a move towards the more sustainable production, use and end-of-life management of textiles and a gradual move away from fast fashion, short garment lifespans and waste generation.
