SMart Industrial Tourism in the Mediterranean

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Before COVID-19, tourism accounted for about 15% of Med countries GDP and 11.5% of their total employment. With the lockdown and travel restrictions the pandemic totally disrupted the sector. Now that the pandemic is subsiding and the sector is recovering, a new risk has arisen, that old unsustainable tourism patterns are re-proposed, generating undesired effects like over-tourism and seasonalization, with their unbearable pressure on the environment and the communities. The challenge in the post-pandemic time is to change tourism, by e.g. focusing on alternative destinations and by exploiting technology (particularly those that have been boosted by the pandemic) to make the tourist experience more rewarding and yet more sustainable. Many Med regions and cities with important industrial sites are considering Industrial Tourism as a way to attract tourists to their areas. On the other hand, the common-place visit to productive sites could be greatly enhanced by integrating additional cultural and historical elements through the use of advanced and immersive technologies that can make the experience more informative and enticing for the visitor (Smart Industrial Tourism). The project aims to exploit transnational cooperation to explore the social and economic potential of Smart Industrial Tourism (SMIT), as a sustainable economic activity that can benefit tourism actors, SMEs, the industry, CCIs, R&D centers, HEIs and technology providers, and the local communities. The project will create transnational working groups involving 4helix stakeholders to explore industrial tourism and related advanced technologies applications. It will eventually define a common strategy and Action Plan for the development of SMIT in the Med regions. The result will be a diversification of the economy and of the sector, a reduction of over-tourism and seasonalization, and a boost to social and economic growth and to technological innovation of tourism at large.

Description (FR):
Avant le COVID-19, le tourisme représentait environ 15 % du PIB des Pays Med et l’11,5 % de leur emploi total. Avec les restrictions de voyage la pandémie a totalement perturbé le secteur. Maintenant que la pandémie s'est calmée et que le secteur s’est rétabli, un nouveau risque apparu est que les vieux modèles de tourisme non durables soient reproposés, en générant des effets indésirables - surtourisme et saisonnalisation avec leur pression sur l'environnement et les communautés. En cette période post-pandémique le défi est de changer le tourisme, en se concentrant sur des destinations alternatives et en exploitant les technologies (en particulier celles qui ont été stimulées par la pandémie) pour rendre l'expérience touristique plus gratifiante et durable. De nombreuses régions et villes Med ayant des sites industriels importants, considérent le tourisme industriel comme un moyen d'attirer les touristes. D'autre part, la visite conventionelle à des sites productifs pourrait être grandement améliorée en intégrant des éléments culturels et historiques supplémentaires grâce à l'utilisation de technologies avancées et immersives (Tourisme Industrial Intelligent - TII). Le projet vise à exploiter la coopération transnationale pour explorer le potentiel social et économique du TII, en tant que l’activité économique durable peut bénéficier aux acteurs du tourisme, aux PME, à l'industrie, aux CCI, aux fournisseurs de technologies et aux communautés locales. Le projet créera des groupes de travail transnationaux pour explorer les bonnes pratiques dans le tourisme industriel et dans l'utilisation de technologies avancées et immersives. Il définira une stratégie et un plan d'action communs pour le développement du TII dans les régions Med. Le résultat sera une diversification du secteur, une réduction du surtourisme et de la saisonnalisation, ainsi qu'un coup de pouce à la croissance sociale et économique et à l'innovation technologique du tourisme dans son ensemble.

Actual Achievements (EN):
Following the project approval, the Lead partner Municipality of Prato (MoP) established the project management team and set up administrative and financial procedures within the internal organisational structure. At the very beginning, the LP coordinated the implementation of the pre-contracting procedure by gathering information from project partners and providing inputs to the Interreg Euro-MED secretariat. The subsidy contract was signed by the legal representative. The partnership agreement was signed by all partners. After the pre-contracting phase was completed, the kick off workshop and the 1st steering committee (SC) meeting were organised. The two day event took place in Prato on 5-6 March, 2024. During the event, the project was presented to the local and regional stakeholders, as well as the general public. During the SC meeting, Steering Committee regulation was discussed and approved by the partnership. The first phase of the project was focused on assessing the social & economic potential of Smart Industrial Tourism in the partner regions by analysing their tourism systems and collecting of existing good practices in industrial tourism. Based on a template for the country survey provided by PP2, ISCTE-Lisboa partners collected data on industrial tourism that include information on the current state of Industrial Tourism, government policies and support, data and statistics, technology integration, marketing & promotion and the future outlook. The first draft of the Country surveys that are the base for the D.1.1.1 Baseline study of tourism systems in the partner regions, were completed in the first semester. In parallel, partners started the collection of good practices that will feed the D.1.2.1 Catalogue of Industrial Tourism Practices. According to what was agreed during the 1st SC meeting, the sample of GPs is as follows: Italy: 20 responses, Spain: 25 responses, Slovenia: 15 responses, Greece: 15 responses, Croatia: 15 responses, Portugal: 15 responses. At the time of drafting the 1st report, all partners collected the foreseen number of GPs and both deliverables are being finalised. In June PP2 ISCTE hosted the 2nd Steering Committee and technical meeting in Lisbon (18 – 19 June 2024). During the meeting partners discussed the 1st results of the WP1 and the most appropriate methodology for the implementation of WP2 activities, in particular the stakeholders engagement that will take place in Semester 2 and the organisation of the upcoming Innovation Camps. A public workshop, entitled “Challenges and opportunities of Industrial Tourism in Portugal” was organised on 19th June, with the participation of relevant stakeholders and the scientific community. Throughout the semester, partners participated also in the activities organised by the Thematic Project of the ST Community of the Interreg Euro-MED. The LP representatives participated in several online meetings, delivered initial project information and documentation and invited the Barcelona Provincial Council to the project kick off meeting in Prato. MoP also participated in the Kick-off meeting of the Community that took place in Rome on the 10 and 11 of April. PP4 MDT, coordinator communication activities set up the social media (Facebook, LinkedIn) and the project website. 19 Facebook posts, 18 LinkedIn posts and 5 news articles on the web site were published during the first semester. 1 press release, resulting in 7 articles were published on occasion of the kick off meeting. All partners contributed in drafting of the contents and disseminated information about the project through their communication channels. Partners published a dedicated web page on their institutional web sites and placed the project poster in their premises. PP6 E- Zavod assigned a carbon footprint manager that presented the Carbon Footprint activity and templates to partners and instructed them on how to collect the data. Partners collected the data for the 1st semester and submitted it to PP6 for further analysis and reporting . The first results will be reported in the full report (after M12). In the first year of activities, LP Municipality of Prato (MoP) has established the project management team and set up the administrative and financial procedures for the internal organisation, through which the approved workplan has been coordinated and implemented. The kick off and the 1st steering committee (SC) meeting were organised in Prato on the 5 - 6 of March 2024. On this occasion, the project was presented to the stakeholders and the general public. At the 1st SC, its regulation was approved. The 1st year mostly focused on WP1, that is, on the assessment of the social & economic potential of Smart Industrial Tourism in the partner regions (see: D.1.1.1 Baseline study of tourism systems in the partner regions). The partners' tourism systems were analysed and existing GPs collected in 2 dedicated catalogues (see: D1.2.1 Catalogue of Industrial Tourism Practices and D1.3.1 Catalogue of Advanced Technologies Apllications for Tourism). By using the templates developed by PP2 ISCTE for the country surveys, the partners reported the current state of Industrial Tourism in their respective regions, their governmental policies and support, data and statistics, technology integration, marketing & promotion, and future outlooks. The overall survey was finalised as "D.1.1.1 Baseline study of tourism systems in the partner regions". Through another template, GPs were collected by the partners for "D1.2.1 Catalogue of Industrial Tourism Practices", which includes several GPs focusing on successful experiences in Industrial Tourism. At the 2nd Steering Committee and technical meeting hosted by PP2 ISCTE in Lisbon (18 – 19 June 2024), the partners reviewed the outcomes of WP1 as a basis to kick-start WP2 activities, particularly as regards the engagement of stakeholders and their participation in the Innovation Camps. The public workshop on “Challenges and opportunities of Industrial Tourism in Portugal” organised on the 19th June in Lisbon, saw the participation of relevant tourism stakeholders and the scientific community. Regarding "A1.3 Application of advanced technologies in the tourism sector", partner in charge PP7 Faculty of Economics and Business Rijeka Hrvatska (HR) EFRI, provided a dedicated template after the summer break, whereby partners collected GPs in the area of Advanced Technologies Applications for Tourism. According to EFRI's instructions, at least 5 GPs were collected by each partner in the following areas: Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), Artificial Intelligence (AI), Chatbots, Internet of Things (IoT). The information has been included in "SMITour WP1 D1.3.1 Catalogue of Advanced Technologies Apllications for Tourism ". In the 2nd semester "WP2 Smart Industrial Tourism development strategy and Action Plan for the Mediterranean regions" kicked-off, with the first Innovation Camp held in Terrassa on the 20 – 21 November 2024. This was organized and managed by PP3 Industrial Tourism Network of Catalonia España (ES) XATIC, with participating stakeholders elaborating roadmaps in four separate groups. The outcomes (roadmaps for the development of SMIT activities in the regions) of the Innovation Camp are being assessed and will be finalized in the coming semester. This year the project also participated in the activities of the C4T Governance Project of the Interreg Euro-MED Programme. The LP participated in several online meetings, providing information and documentation, and inviting the Barcelona Provincial Council - head of the C4T project - to the SMITOUR kick off meeting in Prato. The LP also participated in the C4T kick-off meeting held in Rome on the 10 and 11 of April 2024. SMITOUR's coordination with the governance projects continued in the 2nd semester, through the participation in an online workshops with other partner projects of the "MED Cluster Innovation and Technology Capacity of the Interreg Euro-MED Sustainable Tourism Mission" on the 7th October 2024. PP4 MDT, communication coordinator for the project, launched and animated the project's social media channels - Facebook, and LinkedIn - and the website. Posts on Facebook and LinkedIn, as well as news articles on the website were posted throughout the year. 1 press release, resulting in 7 articles, was deleivered for the kick off meeting. All partners published further contents about the project on their respective communication channels. Each partner has a dedicated page on its institutional website, and hosts the project poster at its premises. PP6 E- Zavod's carbon footprint manager for the project, managed the Carbon Footprint monitoring activity through the templates provided to the partners, with instructions on how to collect the data. The carbon footprint data for the entire year have been analysed by PP6, with results included in the present progress report. The 3rd semester activities were focused on WP2, in particular on the Activity 2.2 Focus & Working Groups for the design of new Smart Industrial Tourism activities and products. Two transnational Innovation Camps were organised respectively by PP5 HICA and PP6 eZAVOD: 2nd Innovation Camp I 5 - 6 March 2025, Athens 3rd Innovation Camp I 10 - 11 June 2025, Maribor Both project partners and relevant stakeholders from hosting territories participated in ICs and contributed to the discussion and co-design of ideas for SMIT products.The two ICs were organised alongside the steering committee meetings and study visits. Following the ICs PP5 and PP6 drafted the Joint Thematic Roadmaps for Greece and Slovenia (D.2.2.1). The roadmaps were then shared with partners that reviewed them and contributed to their finalisation. On the occasion of the SC meeting in Maribor, MoP delivered a first proposal for the structure and the contents of the WP2 outputs, O2.1 Common Strategy for the development of Smart Industrial Tourism in the Mediterranean and O2.2 Action Plan for developing Smart Industrial Tourism in the Mediterranean. Throughout the semester, partners participated in the activities organised by the Thematic Project of the ST Community of the Interreg Euro-MED. The LP representatives participated in several online meetings. The Municipality of Prato also participated at the event organised by several Interreg programmes on sustainable tourism in Split (25-26 June), entitled “Paths that last” PP4 MDT, communication coordinator for the project, regularly updating the website with both news and upcoming events related to SMITour activities, including meetings, focus groups, and innovation camps. SMITour social media channels (Facebook and LinkedIn) were regularly updated on a weekly basis. This included creating content specifically about the project and its activities (such as meeting follow-ups), while also supporting content, events, and meetings from the Interreg Euro-MED Program and the Sustainable Tourism Mission. The project presentation video was produced in January and published on all communication channels. PP6 E- Zavod's carbon footprint manager for the project, managed the Carbon Footprint monitoring activity through the templates provided to the partners, with instructions on how to collect the data. The carbon footprint data for the semester have been analysed by PP6, with results that will be included in the next full progress report.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The project implements regional and transnational strategies that tackle directly the specific objectives of the EUSAIR 4th pillar: diversification of the tourism offer and the responsible and sustainable tourism management. The partnership and territories of the Adriatic-Ionian Region involved in SMITour (Croatia, Greece, Slovenia) have the capacity to connect the results of the project to those developed in the framework of the EUSAIR, contributing directly to the fulfilment of these objectives.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 SMITour addresses the need to study the social, economic, technological and environmental potential of industrial tourism, through transnational cooperation and coordination of stakeholders with the aim to accelerate the smart and green transitions of the Mediterranean industrial districts. Through its actions it promotes inclusive and sustainable future and helps the involved areas to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals. The project contributes directly to key territorial priorities of the Territorial Agenda 2030: 1) Just Europe - by strengthening innovation capacity, competitiveness of both tourism and traditional industrial sectors of the Mediterranean cooperation area, by encouraging all governance levels to unleash the unique potential of territories through integrated and cooperative approaches; 2) Green Europe - by strengthening innovation capacities and empowering local and regional communities to protect, rehabilitate, utilise and reutilise their (built) environments, landscapes, material and immaterial cultural assets
    • EuropeanGreenDeal European Green Deal provides a strategic framework for the green transformation of the tourism ecosystem. SMITour firstly studies and analyses and then proposes measures and actions that make the tourism sector more sustainable and resilient, by developing alternative tourism offer and by promoting and valorising the sustainable production processes of the industrial productive areas in the Mediterranean. Through its actions the project contributes to the achievement of objectives on ecological footprint reduction and to meeting the aims of the European Green Deal.
    • Other EU strategy for sustainable tourism. The project contributes to the key priority areas of the Strategy: 1) Strengthen: transition to sustainable, responsible and smart tourism - by studying and proposing the solutions for more sustainable forms of tourism, diversifying the offer, boosting new initiatives for cooperation and developing new digital services; by addressing the need to study the resilience of cultural heritage and notes the liaison between sustainable tourism and cultural heritage; by addressing need to protect industrial heritage of regions to enable new economic and professional opportunities; 2) Rethink: planning the future of the tourism industry - by studying the applications new technologies, such as artificial intelligence, robotics and virtual and augmented reality, and their impact on the tourism industry; by addressing the need to facilitate their uptake by accessing the adequate funding for tourism establishments, in particular micro enterprises and SMEs
    • Other The Urban Agenda for the EU is an integrated and coordinated approach to deal with the urban dimension of EU and national policies and legislation. By focusing on several concrete priority themes SMITour contributes to the work done by several EUA partnerships: digital transition, jobs and skills in local economy, culture and cultural heritage and the new thematic partnership on the sustainable tourism . By promoting multi-level governance approaches and bottom-up instances of collaboration among a variety of actors in delivering its action plan it will tackle several themes addressed by the EU Agenda, such as: 1) Redistribution of tourism flows; 2) Collaborative governance of tourism destinations (networking, good practice exchanges and promotion focused on similar attractors and themes, itineraries, common challenges); Innovation in tourism (implementation of new technologies and supporting new entrepreneurship and technological start-ups)
