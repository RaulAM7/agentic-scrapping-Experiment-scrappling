Planning for autonomous mobility on demand in the Euro-MED area

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Dans la zone Euro-MED, 40.6M d’habitants vivent dans des territoires de faible densité avec peu ou pas de transports publics (IEMED, 2021) en raison du coût de ces services rapporté au nombre d’usagers par kilomètre parcouru. Cette absence de transports publics conduit à l’isolement des populations ou à une forte dépendance automobile (ESPON, 2020) entrainant d’importantes émissions de CO2 (156M de tonnes) et des problématiques d’accès à l’emploi et aux services publics. Les recherches et initiatives européennes ont mis en avant le potentiel des Transports autonomes à la Demande (TaD) pour offrir un service de transport public de qualité avec des couts opérationnels réduits (41% d’amélioration du rapport qualité/prix, Malmö Uni. 2022) et un moindre impact environnemental (baisse des émissions de 72% selon K2Centrum, 2022). Malheureusement, il est difficile pour les collectivités et les entreprises de transport public de mettre en place un service autonome à la demande sans connaitre au préalable l’impact que celui-ci aura à l'échelle locale. Les projets AVENUE (H2020) et MultiDEPART (EIT UM) ont développé des méthodologies pour calculer l’impact local des TaD mais ces dernières restent difficilement exploitables directement par les décideurs publics. ArtMED a pour objectif l’optimisation d’un outil d’évaluation d’impact de TaD par l’Ecole Central de Lyon (AVENUE) et CARNET (MultiDEPART) et son transfert à 4 autorités publiques, en Slovénie (PO), au Portugal (TML), en Italie (ALOT), en Grèce (PF) et au réseau d’iED. ArtMED aboutira à la production de : 4 déclarations d’intention au niveau politique, 4 plans d’investissement et 2 plans de transport pour déployer les TaD. Les TaD étant très peu développés dans la zone Euro-MED, ArtMED est le premier projet à compiler les connaissances existantes en un outil de planification à destination des décideurs publics pour offrir une solution de transport public aux populations des zones faiblement peuplées en Euro-MED.

Description (EN):
In Euro-MED, 40.6 million people live in sparsely populated areas with poor or without public transport services (IEMED, 2021). These areas are expensive for Public (Transport) Authorities (PTAs) to operate due to low number of paying passengers per kilometre. This leads to high private car dependency or isolation of people (ESPON, 2020), resulting in high carbon emissions (156M tons of CO2) or inaccessibility to public services and labour market. A solution recognised by researchers and pilot projects in Europe is autonomous mobility on demand (AMOD), offering higher quality services while reducing operating costs (41% quality-cost ratio improvement, Malmö Uni, 2022) and CO2 emissions up to 72% (K2Centrum, 2022). However, it is difficult for PTAs to start planning AMOD without the ability to easily estimate its potential socio-economic and environmental impact. The AVENUE (H2020) and MultiDEPART (EIT UM) projects successfully developed methodologies to assess local impact of AMOD. Currently, these methodologies cannot easily be applied by PTAs outside of these projects. Therefore, the primary objective of ArtMED is enable PTAs to plan for AMOD by transferring methodologies from École Centrale de Lyon (AVENUE) and CARNET (MultiDEPART) to 4 PTAs in Slovenia (PO), Portugal (TML), Italy (ALOT), Greece (PF) and beyond through jointly optimising and implementing a publicly accessible AMOD impact assessment tool in a transnational approach. The tool will support PTAs in ArtMED to analyse the potential AMOD impact for local use cases and develop: 4 vision statements (for political support), 4 investment plans (for budgeting) and 2 transport model designs (for planning AMOD systems). As AMOD hardly exists yet in Euro-MED, ArtMED is the first project to make state-of-the-art insights available to PTAs through a user-friendly tool, that assesses AMOD impact to plan for more accessible, inclusive, and sustainable public transport for people in sparsely populated areas.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The ArtMED project is dedicated to exploring and advancing Automated Mobility on Demand (AMOD) in the Euro-MED area. The project has made significant progress, focusing on developing best practices, creating comprehensive vision statements, and crafting tools for impact assessment and implementation. In the 1st reporting period we reached the objectives as follows: Best Practices Collection (WP1): We produced a comprehensive document detailing the levels of flexibility and stages of AMOD development across different regions, including state-of-the-art examples of automated and on-demand mobility, with examples of successful implementations. In this document, we also analysed drivers and barriers for the implementation of AMOD in public transport. Vision Statements (WP2): We started developing detailed Vision Statements outlining the relevance, potential impact, and feasible deployment timelines for AMOD in partners specific use cases. Then, these documents were validated by an internal version of the impact assessment tool to enhance the final version of the partners' Vision Statements. Tool Development (WP1): We defined a list of inputs and outputs for the AMOD impact assessment tool, and integrated methodologies from AVENUE and MultiDEPART projects to optimise the tool. By doing so, we are developing a flexible tool for contextualising service scenarios, assessing social relevance, and creating implementation roadmaps. The working version of the tool was sent to all the partners for testing and is currently awaiting feedback for further development. Promotional Material and Outreach (WP3): We finalized the project website and maintained an updated the to-do list with direct deliverables. We also developed a PowerPoint template, a Branding Manual, a LinkedIn page, and a Report template. Main Outputs Delivered: Deliverable 1.1.6 - AMOD Best Practices Collection: We completed a detailed document covering the stages of AMOD development, state-of-the-art examples, drivers, barriers, and executive summaries Added-Value of the Cooperation in the 1st reporting periode: Interdisciplinary Collaboration: The project fosters interdisciplinary collaboration, bringing together experts from various fields to enrich the development of AMOD solutions. This cooperative approach ensures diverse perspectives are considered, leading to more robust and adaptable outcomes. Knowledge Sharing: Partners actively share feedback and experiences, leveraging each other's work to enhance the project's overall quality. This exchange of knowledge is crucial for developing well-rounded and effective AMOD strategies. Stakeholder Engagement: Engaging a wide range of stakeholders, from local municipalities to technology providers, ensures that the developed solutions are practical, scalable, and aligned with real-world needs. This engagement is pivotal for the successful implementation of AMOD in the Euro-MED area. In the 1st reporting period, the ArtMED project has made substantial progress towards its goal of advancing Automated Mobility on Demand in the Euro-MED area. Through the comprehensive Best Practices Collection, detailed Vision Statements, and the development of robust tools, the project is paving a strong foundation for the future implementation of AMOD solutions. The added value of interdisciplinary cooperation and stakeholder engagement has been crutial in achieving these milestones, ensuring that the project's outcomes are both innovative and practical. During the reporting period, the ARTMED Consortium engaged in a series of virtual and in-person meetings, including four conference calls, to ensure alignment on key project deliverables and to address any concerns related to the modification request. These calls were vital in keeping all partners up-to-date on progress, discussing the next steps, and resolving any challenges that arose. The calls also provided an opportunity to review timelines, share updates from working groups, and assess any modifications or adjustments required to meet project goals. In addition to the conference calls, an important milestone was two in-person meetings held in Palaio Faliro, Greece, October, and Lyon, France, in February 2025. The face-to-face interaction allowed for more dynamic exchanges on specific project components, facilitating a clearer understanding of any technical and administrative questions in response to the evolving nature of the project. It also enabled members to reinforce their commitment to the project’s objectives and ensure that all required tasks were being addressed in the most effective manner. A significant event during this period was the participation in the Green Living Areas (GLA) Mission 1st Institutional Policy Dialogue held on 10 January 2024. The dialogue served as an essential platform for engaging with institutional stakeholders, policymakers, and experts on the project’s goals and policy implications. Further contributing to the ongoing discourse on green living areas, the consortium also participated in the Green Living Areas Mission Thematic Working Group Online Sessions, held on 24 December 2024. These online sessions provided a space for ongoing projects and external stakeholders to exchange ideas and best practices on key thematic areas related to green living spaces, sustainability, and urban resilience. The next major online event, that project partners attended, within GLA Online Thematic Working Groups focusing on policy, scheduled for Thursday, 27 February 2025. In terms of deliverables, during this reporting period, the consortium successfully completed and submitted four investment plans. These plans are integral to the project’s ability to attract funding and investment, ensuring that the necessary resources are in place for implementation. Each investment plan was meticulously developed, incorporating both short-term and long-term financial considerations, and ensuring that the project’s goals could be met within the agreed timeline. Alongside the investment plans, four vision statements were finalized, reflecting the consortium’s aspirations for the ArtMED project and its long-term impact. These vision statements outline the project’s overarching goals, including the creation of sustainable urban spaces, the integration of green technologies, and the promotion of resilient infrastructure. They also highlight the importance of community engagement, policy alignment, and the fostering of partnerships among local, national, and international stakeholders. Furthermore, the consortium made significant progress in the development of the AMOD tool. A working version of the tool was produced during this period, marking a key achievement in the project’s technological component. The AMOD tool is designed to support decision-making processes and provide a robust framework for evaluating the environmental, social, and economic impacts of various interventions. This tool has been essential in the creation of the four investment plans and the four vision statements. By utilizing the AMOD tool, the consortium was able to ensure that these plans and statements were well-informed, evidence-based, and aligned with the project's goals. The tool's ability to integrate and analyze complex data played a pivotal role in shaping the strategic direction of the project, facilitating more informed decisions that will contribute to its long-term success.
