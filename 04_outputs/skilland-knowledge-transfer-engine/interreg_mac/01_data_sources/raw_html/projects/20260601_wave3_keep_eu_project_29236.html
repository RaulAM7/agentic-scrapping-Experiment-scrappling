Composing Local Green Energy Transition

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Les autorités locales (AL) EuroMED sont de plus en plus engagées dans la transition énergétique mais elles n'ont que peu souvent les capacités adéquates pour mener à bien les processus d'évaluation et de planification lors de la préparation de leurs stratégies locales de transition énergétique durable (en particulier les petites municipalités). Dans le cadre d'Interreg MED 2014-20, les projets COMPOSE, PRISMI et LOCAL4GREEN ont permis aux AL de rédiger des stratégies locales de transition énergétique durable testées avec succès, grâce à des processus coopératifs qui ont révélé leur potentiel de capitalisation. Malgré cela, les outils de ces trois projets ont été utilisés séparément et le manque d'intégration a été un défaut qui a empêché un transfert ultérieur. Le projet fournira une boîte à outils optimisée pour soutenir la planification et la mise en œuvre des plans énergétiques locaux, construite sur les résultats des projets COMPOSE, PRISMI et LOCAL4GREEN et déploiera un processus de formation pour l’appliquer dans les nouvelles AL. En outre, le projet soutiendra les principaux acteurs multiplicateurs (autorités régionales, associations de municipalités, agences de l'énergie, etc) afin qu'ils intègrent la nouvelle boîte à outils dans les services de soutien qu'ils fournissent habituellement aux autorités locales. Ce processus de transfert aux parties prenantes multiplicatrices est une caractéristique clé de l'approche du projet et il sera déployé en étroite collaboration avec le projet communautaire thématique (TCP). Des recommandations politiques visant à améliorer les stratégies locales de transition énergétique durable seront rédigées et les décideurs politiques seront interpellés en collaboration avec le TCP et le projet de dialogue institutionnel (IDP). La capitalisation des projets COMPOSE, PRISMI et LOCAL4GREEN nécessite la coopération transnationale des partenaires pour produire une boîte à outils optimisée et soutenir les nouveaux territoires impliqués.

Description (EN):
The EuroMED Local Authorities (LAs) are increasingly engaged in the energy transition but in general they do not have adequate capacities to carry out proper assessment and planning processes when preparing their local sustainable energy transition strategies (particularly, the small municipalities). Many dispersed efforts have been done to tackle such problems, even by former Interreg MED projects. In the Interreg MED 2014-20, the projects COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN produced relevant outputs for LAs to draft their local sustainable energy transition strategies that were successfully tested, even in cooperative processes that revealed their potential for joint capitalization. Despite this, the PRISMI, LOCAL4GREEN and COMPOSE tools were used separately and the lack of integration was a shortcoming that prevented a further transfer. The project will deliver an optimized toolbox to support planning and implementation of local energy plans, built on the COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN projects’ outputs and will deploy a training process to apply the novel toolbox in new Local Authorities. Furthermore, the project will support key multiplier stakeholders (regional authorities, associations of municipalities, energy agencies, etc.) to integrate the novel toolkit as a tool for the support services they normally provide to local authorities. This transferring process to multiplier stakeholders is a key feature of the project’s approach and it will be deployed in close collaboration with the Thematic Community Project (TCP). Policy recommendations to improve local sustainable energy transition strategies will be drafted and policy makers properly addressed in collaboration with the TCP and the Institutional Dialogue Project (IDP). The capitalisation of the COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN needs the transnational cooperation of the partners in charge of such previous projects to produce the optimized toolbox and support the new involved territories.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The Kick-off and 1st Steering Committee Meeting was held in Valencia on 7-8 March 2024, organized by FVMP (LP), where partners met in person fostering their transnational cooperation to implement the project first activities and agreed the next steps. During the KoM, the SC rules and members were approved. Also, representatives of Green Living Areas Mission participated. Regarding WP1, in the framework of the KoM, the 1st Technical Committee Meeting was held on 7 March. The Technical Committee, composed by MUSOL, TUC-ReSEL and UNIZAG FSB, plays a crucial role in the elaboration of the Deliverable 1.2.1 and Output 1.1, both to deliver an optimised toolkit for local authorities to plan and deploy local energy transition plans, based on the synthesis and upscaling of PRISMI, COMPOSE and LOCAL4GREEN outputs. In this meeting, the technical partners and the LP discussed about the potential areas for updating and upgrading the previous tools, and, considering COMPOSE toolbox the most comprehensive and integral tool, they discussed about the integration of PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN results into the COMPOSE toolbox and interface. After several technical meetings, TUC-ReSEL, WP1 manager, jointly with MUSOL and UNIZAG FSB, in order to define the key areas for optimization of a novel integral tool based in the capitalized results (Activity 1.1), produced a questionnaire to gather feedback from key stakeholders regarding their priorities, challenges, needs and capacity on green transition planning, and also their perception and recommendations on the usability and effectiveness of COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN toolkits. For this, TUC-ReSEL asked the partners to interview 4-5 stakeholders from their countries, both associated partners organizations and municipalities that participated as ETU/Flagships developed by the Interreg MED Renewable Energy Project. All partners started the contacts with the associated partners and other key stakeholders to ensure their early involvement in the project, and to interview them for collecting their valuable feedback, essential for the toolkit’s development. With the information collected, TUC-ReSEL is carrying out the cross-country analysis whose results are expected by the 2nd period to move forward in the optimized toolkit. At the same time, the technical partners have been working to update and upscale the COMPOSE, LOCAL4GREEN and PRISMI outputs, and to integrate them in the COMPOSE toolbox to generate the novel tool, which will be user friendly and easy to use for the local authorities (Activity 1.2). As planned in the AF, they are still working on the new tool, that was not expected to be delivered in the 1st period. Regarding WP2, FVMP, as Carbon footprint coordinator, has supported partners in the carbon footprint reporting process for the 1st period. All partners have contributed with their data using the Excel file template provided. FVMP has filled in all the partners’ data in the online carbon footprint tool (Activity 2.4). Regarding WP3, on 11 February 2024, FVMP jointly with MUSOL had an online meeting with the Community for Green Living Areas (GLA) to know the role of the Thematic Community Project (TCP) and Institutional Dialogue Project (IDP), and their relationship with the thematic projects. Later, FVMP and MUSOL attended the GLA Kick-off Meeting held in Turin, on 10-11 April 2024, where both had the opportunity to participate actively in the KoM sessions. Also, FVMP participated in the Thematic Working Group: Green Energy Systems, on 11 June 2024, meeting organized by GLA IDP (Activities 3.3 and 3.4). At the end of this 1st period, MUSOL agreed with FVMP to draft a shared methodology to identify windows of opportunity to provide policy recommendations to enhance the local energy transition planning. MUSOL is currently working on a draft of the methodology (Activity 3.2). In order to support partners in management issues, FVMP produced the Project Management Plan that defines the role, organization and procedures for the management of the project, including relevant information as the work plan, project tools, activity and financial reporting, eligibility of expenditures and reporting on carbon footprint. Regarding project communication, FVMP as Communication manager, attended the Communication Meeting on 13 March 2024 and the WordPress training on 30 April 2024 organized by the JS. FVMP prepared the contents for the project website and published it, and also created the project social networks. In Basecamp, FVMP created the folders to allow partners to share project documents and work in collaboration. Moreover, FVMP is part of the Communication Working Group created by the GLA TCP and participated in their 1st meeting on 25 June 2024. As well, FVMP has produced the project poster using the Programme’s template and delivered to all partners, that have printed and displayed it in a visible place in their organization headquarters, meeting the publicity rules. The 2nd Steering Committee meeting was be held in Rome on 17-18 October 2024, organized by UNCEM Lazio with LP FVMP, where partners met to review the state of the art of the 3 WP project activities, and to catch up regarding the management and communication actions. The SC meeting was attended by all partners fostering the transnational cooperation for the better development of the project. Regarding WP1, the Technical Committee, composed by TUC-ReSEL, MUSOL and UNIZAG FSB, jointly with LP FVMP, have worked on the integration of PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN tools in the COMPOSE interface, and also on the improvement of the COMPOSE toolbox, in order to produce the LOGREENER toolkit (Deliverable 1.2.1 and Output 1.1). Several contacts and online meetings have been produced between the Technical Committee and FVMP to move forward. During the 2nd SC, a technical meeting with the participation of all partners was held to discuss about the production of the LOGREENER novel toolkit and the elaboration of tailored training materials for local authorities and key multiplier stakeholders. In order to define the key areas for the optimization of the LOGREENER novel toolkit, all project partners have made interviews to key stakeholders, using a template and guide produced by TUC-ReSEL, WP1 leader, to gather feedback on their priorities, challenges, needs and capacity on green transition planning, and also their perception and recommendations on the usability and effectiveness of the COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN tools. The interviews results have been analyzed by TUC-ReSEL to get the necessary information to define how to capitalize COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN results into LOGREENER toolkit. The LOCAL4GREEN and PRISMI tools have been updated and adapted to be included as new sections in the COMPOSE interface in order to produce the LOGREENER toolkit during 3rd period. In addition, the LOCAL4GREEN, COMPOSE and PRISMI training materials have been revised to be adapted and integrated in the teaching pack on LOGREENER toolkit. Regarding WP2, following the indications of UNIZAG FSB, WP2 leader, before starting the tailored training and technical support to apply the LOGREENER toolkit, partners have made the previous contacts with their respective associated partners municipalities and key stakeholders to explain them the foreseen project activities, and also to know what specific needs they have in order to adapt the application of the toolkit. In relation to Carbon footprint reporting, LP FVMP has supported partners in the process for the 2nd period. All partners have contributed with their data using the Excel file template. Regarding WP3, MUSOL, the WP leader, has defined the methodology for Activities 3.2, 3.3 and 3.4, drafting a practical approach to identify policy windows and draft policy recommendations extracted from the LOGREENER toolkit. This approach is inspired by Kingdon’s “Policy Windows/Multiple Streams Framework” and was presented to the partnership at the 3rd SC meeting in Rome, October 2024, being approved. The partners were asked to provide the first policy windows mapping by December 2024. By the end of the period, approximately 17 policy windows had been identified by the partners and will be assessed in the next period. Regarding the collaboration with TCP and IDP of GLA Mission, LOGREENER project worked to maintain proactive communication with them. In this sense, the project invited to the 2nd SC Meeting in Rome on October 2024, to Alexia Boulanger, as representative of GLA TCP to be aware of the activities developed by the C4LA. Moreover, Project representatives participated in the Thematic Working Group on Green Energy Systems on 12 December 2024, organized by the C4LA, with the objective to plan jointly activities for 2025. In December 2024, FVMP and MUSOL were contacted by MedCities (Partner of GLA TCP) to organize the first meeting of the Spanish Antenna of the TCP, to be a meeting forum for the partners of Thematic Projects of the Green Living Areas Community in Spain. FVMP and MUSOL have collaborated with MedCities to organize the meeting that was held on 27 March 2025 at FVMP headquarters. Regarding management, partners received technical support from LP FVMP in the preparation of the 1st Partner Reports. The LP prepared the 1st Project Report, collecting Partners National Certificates to produce the 1st Payment Claim. The 1st Project Report and Payment Claim were submitted within the deadline of 30 September 2024. After, the LP worked with the JS and partners in the Q&A document on the 1st Reporting, that finally was verified by the JS. Regarding communication, LP FVMP, as Communication manager, prepared the Communication Strategy for LOGREENER Project. Also, the LP participated in the 2nd meeting of the Communication WG of GLA TCP on 23 October 2024. Moreover, the LP has fed the project communication channels: project website, social networks and LOGREENER Basecamp. The 3rd SC meeting was held in Rethymno, Crete, on 8-10 April 2025, organized by TUC-ReSEL jointly with LP FVMP, where partners met to review the state of the art of the 3 WP project activities. Jointly with SC meeting, TUC-ReSEL organized 2 tailored local events with its associated partners: Anogia and Amari Municipalities, where LP and technical partners presented the project and the toolkit. The SC meeting and events were attended by all partners fostering the transnational cooperation for the better project development. In WP1, TUC-ReSEL, as WP leader, jointly with technical partners and FVMP, have worked to produce the LOGREENER toolkit (D.1.2.1 and O.1.1), making the last updates and the final revision to check that it complies with the Programme rules. The toolkit was officially launched and is available in: reselplan-toolbox.eu/logreener, offering open access to local authorities and stakeholders. Regarding the teaching pack on the toolkit application (D.1.3.1), TUC-ReSEL with technical partners and LP prepared and revised the training materials. The teaching pack was produced, including a tailored mix of resources and being designed to address the needs of different user groups, ensuring practical guidance on how to use the toolkit effectively. In WP2, UNIZAG FSB, as WP leader, developed structured and adaptable concepts: for local authorities, a two-stage approach combining introductory workshops and follow-up sessions tailored to data collection and specific planning needs, and for key multiplier stakeholders, a three-step engagement involving stakeholder mapping, small-group consultations, and tailored training sessions. Partners have started to work in this sense for the training to local authorities and key stakeholders (A.2.1) and to support their associated municipalities in the application of the toolkit (A.2.2). In relation to Carbon footprint reporting, LP has supported partners in the process for the 3rd period (A.2.4), contributing all partners with their carbon footprint data. In WP3, MUSOL as WP leader has coordinated the entire partnership to prepare and implement the dissemination and policy impact strategy. MUSOL designed and validated a “policy windows”-based approach with the partners. Following MUSOL’s guidelines, each partner identified several potential policy windows to which the LOGREENER toolkit could contribute. Each policy window was assessed by the Technical Committee, and one policy window per country was prioritised out of 69 potential policy contributions initially identified, targeting 20 different policy instruments managed by 19 local, regional, or national authorities. Once one policy window per country was prioritised, partners began to draft the policy recommendations, and also, MUSOL with FVMP started to draft an international policy recommendation for the Covenant of Mayors (D.3.2.1). Regarding the presentation of the toolkit in relevant events (A.3.1), FVMP and MUSOL presented the LOGREENER project and toolkit in a training session on transferring, organized within the 2nd Gathering of GLA Thematic Community in Barcelona on 28-29 May 2025. Also, other partners presented the LOGREENER toolkit in different relevant events. Regarding the actions to address the policy makers and to enable the exploitation of the toolkit by key organizations (A.3.3), partners made the mapping of national policy makers and key stakeholders in each country, and LP made the mapping at international level. Once the mapping was made, the project started the toolkit’s dissemination by email, project website and social networks, partners’ websites and social networks, and other dissemination channels. Regarding the LOGREENER collaboration with TCP and IDP of GLA Mission, that is very close, the project participated in the GLA Thematic WG focusing on policy, on 27 February 2025. Also, FVMP with MUSOL’s support hosted the Launch event of the Spanish Territorial Antenna of GLA Mission, organized by the C4LA, in Valencia on 27 March 2025, where the Spanish partners of Thematic Projects took the opportunity to meet and share information about their main outputs. Furthermore, MUSOL with FVMP’s support submitted the international policy recommendation for the CoM to the Call for Policy Outputs of Thematic Projects launched by the GLA Mission, that was selected as policy champion to be presented in the 2nd Institutional Policy Dialogue, on 15-16 October 2025 in Brussels. Regarding management, partners received technical support from LP in the preparation of the 2nd Partner Reports. The LP prepared the 2nd Project Report and the 2nd Payment Claim, being submitted both within the deadline of 31 March 2025. Regarding communication, LP FVMP, as Communication manager, produced the video for the presentation of LOGREENER Project. Also, LP fed the project website and social networks and participated in the meeting of the Communication WG of GLA TCP on 7 May 2025.

  • LOCAL4GREEN – LOCAL Policies for GREEN Energy (2016-2019) and LOCAL4GREEN PLUS – LOCAL Fiscal Policies for GREEN Energy Mainstreaming (2021-2022). Cofinanced by Interreg MED Programme 2014-2020. (FR), LOCAL4GREEN – LOCAL Policies for GREEN Energy (2016-2019) and LOCAL4GREEN PLUS – LOCAL Fiscal Policies for GREEN Energy Mainstreaming (2021-2022). Cofinanced by Interreg MED Programme 2014-2020. (EN) | The LOCAL4GREEN project provides one of the main outputs to be capitalized in the LOGREENER project, together with PRISMI and COMPOSE projects. Therefore, LOCAL4GREEN outputs play a prominent role in the capitalization and transferring process entailed by the LOGREENER project.
  • LOCAL4GREEN supported Local Authorities to design and implement innovative fiscal policies to promote renewable energy sources both in the public and private sector and households, mainly in the framework of the Sustainable Energy and Climate Action Plans adopted by the Local Authorities signatories of the Covenant of Mayors for Climate and Sustainable Energy. In the LOCAL4GREEN project, the project involved 78 Mediterranean municipalities, designed 173 green fiscal policies and approved 79 policies. In the LOCAL4GREEN PLUS project, 17 Mediterranean municipalities have adopted 26 fiscal policies to promote RES. As relevant tools, the project produced the “Handbook on Green Fiscal Policy Formulation”, and the “International Handbook on Green Fiscal Policy” where the best practices on policy models implemented in the partners’ countries were collected. LOCAL4GREN proved that local green taxation is an effective and very useful tool to promote RES for Local Authorities, and it will be integrated in the new tool that will be created with LOGREENER Project. The FVMP was the lead partner of both LOCAL4GREEN and LOCAL4GREEN PLUS and MUSOL was the partner in charge of the elaboration of the outputs to be capitalized and provided support to the local authorities and receiver partners to adapt and make adopt the green local fiscal policies. The lead partner FVMP and the partner MUSOL will be in charge of the capitalization of the LOCAL4GREEN outputs. (FR) | The PRISMI project provides one of the main outputs to be capitalized in the LOGREENER project, together with LOCAL4GREEN and COMPOSE projects. Therefore, PRISMI outputs play a prominent role in the capitalization and transferring process entailed by the LOGREENER project.
  • Islands and rural areas decision-makers generally find difficulties in planning the transition to low-carbon energy systems for many reasons, such as lack of knowledge and data. PRISMI developed a decision-making tool for increasing local Renewable Energy Sources (RES) penetration based on a multicriteria analysis and a transnational approach, considering economic, technical and environmental parameters, to identify optimal solutions respecting local features and social needs. PRISMI PLUS implemented the tool in the process of elaboration of 2 Sustainable Energy and Climate Action Plans (SECAPs) for rural areas and 2 Clean Energy Transition Agendas (CETAs) for islands.
  • The UNIZAG FSB was involved in the creation of the decision-making tool and in its concrete application, therefore it is the partner in charge of the capitalisation of the PRISMI tool.   • As COMPOSE provides the most comprehensive tool, the capitalisation process is mainly based upon the COMPOSE output interface.
  • COMPOSE supported local authorities to build low-carbon communities and increase the renewable energy share in the local/regional energy mix in 11 Med regions. A holistic, step-by-step, methodological approach was developed to foster efficient implementation of local energy plans, considering not only technical, but also socio-economic and environmental aspects.
  • The TUC ReSEL developed a prototype model for sustainable energy projects design, implementation and assessment, which consists of 6 planning steps (Setting the baseline, Creating Local Action Groups, Local Plans and Local Partnerships, Implementation, Monitoring and Evaluation) and 2 critically important horizontal ones and a practical web-based toolbox (Sustainable Energy Planning toolbox). 18 sustainable energy demonstration projects were implemented, testing on the ground the COMPOSE Common Methodology for Development in Albania, Bosnia & Herzegovina, Croatia, Cyprus, France, Greece, Italy, Montenegro, Portugal, Slovenia and Spain. In the COMPOSE PLUS a special emphasis was put to mainstream and transfer of the tested COMPOSE toolbox and the respective validated methods, in 7 additional MED territories with a focus on building capacities for local authorities/stakeholders/networks at EU/national/regional/ local level for sustainable development and clean energy transition. The partner TUC ReSEL developed the COMPOSE methodological approach and the Sustainable Energy Planning toolbox (COMPOSE TOOLBOX) and it is in charge of the capitalisation of such tool in the LOGREENER.   • (FR) | REScoop PLUS aim was to harness power of European REScoops to go beyond their activities of producing and supplying energy and to take up energy savings for their members as a new pillar in their organisation. Specific strategies were developed to challenge cooperatives to implement evidence-based energy efficiency actions and services beyond RES production. 5 Cooperatives adopted 6 different tools and pilot tested them and delivered a toolkit with a range of best practice products like communication tools, ICT tools for better measurements, or new business models that support energy savings and can mobilise EU Citizens to invest in Sustainable Energy.

  • The REScoops PLUS toolkit and best practices can provide useful elements for the update of the COMPOSE tool and its upgrade in the project LOGREENER by the partner TUC ReSEL.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion 5 countries involved in the project are part of the Adriatic and Ionian Region (Italy, Slovenia, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina and Greece). Our project is aligned with the EU Strategy for The Adriatic and Ionian Region, particularly with the Connecting the Region pillar and Energy Networks thematic area. The cross-border cooperation and the networks that will be promoted in the framework of this project fits this Strategy. Furthermore, Supporting the transition to zero-carbon electricity generation supports the competitiveness of the economy in the ADRION region (lessons learned from the Interreg ADRION projects).
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion One of the defined challenges of the regions of the strategy that includes two partnership countries of this project (Slovenia and Italy) is climate change and its foreseeable effects on the environment, biodiversity and living conditions of the inhabitants. The project will contribute to boost the local climate change mitigation. We will contribute to 3rd Thematic Policy Area - "Environment and Energy" of this strategy, which is the Action group 9 - Energy. Specific objectives of Action group 9 is to support energy management systems in the Alpine Region by developing, sharing and installing energy efficiency and decentralised monitoring systems at the local level and by promoting regional energy monitoring.
    • Other Fit for 55% package
    • Part of the Green New Deal, the Fit for 55% package is a revision and adaptation of the existing climate and energy legislation, in order to meet the New UE objective to reduce by 55% the GHG emission by 2030.
    • The Fit for 55% package stipulates that every member state should:
    • · Ensure that the amount of renewables in transport leads to a GHG intensity reduction of at least 13% by 2030.
    • · Increase the share of renewables in the heating and cooling sector by at least 1.1% points a year.
    • · Aim for a minimum 49% share of renewables in buildings in 2030.
    • The tools developed in previous projects (PRISMI, LOCAL4GREEN and COMPOSE) have proven their potential for capitalization, in facilitating innovative methods and efficient guidance, enabling the development of energy efficiency solutions, increased shares of renewable energy sources in the local energy mix in several European territories and therefore contributing to GHG emission reduction. This project will provide the local authorities with a comprehensive set of (separated) tools to plan and implement local plans of RES promotion in line with the Green New Deal and the Fit for 55% package, specifically tackling the so-called diffuse sectors.
    • Other REPowerEU Plan:
    • REPowerEU is the Plan proposed by the European Commission as a response to the difficulties and perturbations in the global market due to the Russian invasion of Ukraine.
    • Developed to save energy, produce clean energy, diversify our energy supplies, the REPowerEU would augment the total capacity of renewable energy generation, increasing again the target stipulated in the Fit for 55% package. This new plan aims to duplicate the production of photovoltaic solar energy and accelerate the transition to cleaner energy sources. Our project, with the Energy Transition Planning Tool, will maximize the local production of green energy and the percentage of local energy needs satisfied with local renewables with the best payback time, yet aligned with this new Plan.
    • EuropeanGreenDeal This project strongly contributes to the European Green Deal long term strategy of achieving carbon neutrality by 2050. Indeed, this proposal focuses on
    • ensuring a secure and affordable EU energy supply developing a fully integrated, interconnected and digitalised EU energy market prioritising energy efficiency, improving the energy performance of our buildings and developing a power sector based largely on renewable sources.
    • The focus on this plan is on clean energy transition, reduce the greenhouse gas emissions and enhance the quality of life of European citizens, our project participates to its objectives, with the transfer of tools to design and implement local fiscal policies to promote RES in European territories.
    • Amongst the Commission’s main objectives to achieve is to “build interconnected energy systems and better integrated grids to support renewable energy sources” (40% new renewable energy target for 2030) and to boost energy efficiency (36-39% new 2030 energy efficiency targets for final and primary energy consumption) through its transferring mission, our project will contribute to it with toolkit for local authorites to plan and deploy local sustainable energy transition plans, clearly aligned with the European Green Deal clean energy transition objectives.
    • Other The New European Bauhaus (NEB) Communication of the European Commission.
    • The NEB inspires a movement to facilitate and steer the transformation of our societies along sustainability, aesthetics, and inclusion. Also, to bring together citizens, experts, businesses, and institutions together to reimagine sustainable living in Europe and beyond. The COMPOSE Project was selected to participate in the inauguration of the NEB strategy in Brussels last June 2022 and the TUC ReSEL team is involved in the NEB promotion. The LOGREENER project will enable the update of the tools produced by the COMPOSE, PRISMI and LOCAL4GREEN projects’ outputs and the elaboration of a novel toolkit based on such outputs. The updated tools and the new integrated toolkit will consider the NEB. Actually, the integration of NEB is one of the most prominent update of the mentioned tools. Therefore, the integral toolkit produced by the LOGREENER will include the NEB and it will embed specific actions in order to ensure that the local sustainable energy transition strategies will be drafted taking into account the NEB.
