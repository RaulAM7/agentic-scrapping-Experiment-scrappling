Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 067. Household waste management: prevention, minimisation, sorting, reuse, recycling measures ~ SUMMARY: In the Mediterranean, ORIENTATE tackles the challenges caused by climate change. It focusses on the agricultural sector, a vital artery of the region's livelihoods. In this region agriculture is vulnerable and sustainability is always under threat. ORIENTATE develops targeted solutions to waste streams generated by olive oil extraction and citrus fruit processing. Drawing inspiration from the innovative practices of pioneering Italian and Spanish partners, cutting-edge circular economy models are used to revolutionise waste management. ORIENTATE unfolds across Turkey, Jordan, and Lebanon. It mitigates environmental harm and aims to spark ripple effects of socio-economic empowerment. By harnessing agricultural waste, ORIENTATE opens the door to economic opportunities, job creation, and environmental stewardship, while enriching the lives of farmers, entrepreneurs, and communities alike. ORIENTATE also forges strategic alliances and fosters supportive regulatory frameworks, laying the groundwork for sustainable development and aligning seamlessly with the vision of a resilient, eco-conscious Europe and its neighbouring regions. The project establishes three pilot labs in Jordan, Turkey, and Lebanon, each focussing on converting agricultural by-products like Citrus Pulp Depectinized (CPD) and Olive pomace into animal feed and cosmetics ingredients, promoting a circular economy. These labs will develop bioproducts from olive pomace for animal nutrition and cosmetics in Jordan and Lebanon, and transform CPD into animal feed in Turkey. Training materials and joint activities across these countries will educate at least 90 professionals in the citrus and olive supply chains on sustainable waste management practices. Additionally, six Memoranda of Understanding will be signed with stakeholders such as farmers, food industries, and public authorities to adopt these sustainable models and promote regional agricultural innovation, thus tackling unemployment. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Agriculture and fisheries and forestry, Knowledge and technology transfer, Waste and pollution

Valorisation of Bio-waste from the Olive Oil Extraction Process: LIFE OLEA REGENERA (LIFE17 ENV/ES/000189) (2018-2022) (EN) | The Project LIFE OLEA REGENERA offers valuable resources that PPs will leverage to promote sustainable agricultural practices, especially in MPCs.
  • LCEF (PP2) was one of the main stakeholders of it. The project aims to design and develop a new process for olive oil mills to valorise waste generated during olive oil extraction. The project's process produces two new by-products from waste, utilized for eco-friendly items, enhancing alperujo value. The generated zero-waste biostimulants and bioprotectors improve crop growth and protection, while functional animal feed is also produced. Additionally, this process reduces polluting emissions. Outputs from this project have been analysed by PPs and will be effectively utilised in ORIENTATE, as follows:
  • a. An innovative physical process has been developed, enabling olive mills to fully utilise olive pomace (OP) waste by transforming it into valuable by-products. This has completely resolved the OP issue in the mills, as it is now fully reused. Approximately 12.495 tons of olive pomace were transformed into by-products, exceeding initial expectations. These by-products include a liquid fraction (L3) and a solid fraction (S1+S2), which are used as raw materials for other products such as bio-stimulants and animal feed. Additionally, this process has significantly reduced the amount of waste generated, decreasing gas emissions and energy consumption during transportation.
  • b. Six new eco-friendly and zero-waste products were developed, proving effective as bio-stimulants and bio-protectors in various crops.
  • c. New pig functional food, rich in olive-derived monounsaturated fatty acids & nutrients, boosts health, growth & meat antioxidants.
  • PP2 (LCEF) will assist PPs in developing a Pilot plan for MPCs (JO & LB) and integrating results into training. Policy proposals will be included in MoUs (WP6).

(EN) | CORERAS (PP1), along with its esteemed member, University of Catania (https://www.coreras.it/chi-siamo/partner), leverages the expertise acquired from the project SMART-UP to enhance ORIENTATE. The pilot actions of SMART-UP project, led by the University of Catania, have been analysed by the PPs and will be utilised within ORIENTATE. The project's goal is to recover valuable substances with medicinal properties from food and non-food waste. Citrus pulp and olive leaves were used to make a dietary supplement, while snail slime and edible flower scraps were used to create a cosmetic formula. This waste was repurposed to develop innovative products like a moisturising, antioxidant lotion with anti-aging effects and a nutraceutical food supplement. This approach boosts production profitability, diversifies product offerings, and opens up new markets. The project's activities significantly reduced costs and environmental impact for the participating farms, enabling them to reinvent themselves and capture new markets through waste reuse. The new formulations developed have been standardised and accompanied by certificates of analysis, making them suitable for use in both the nutraceutical and cosmeceutical sectors. This innovation has also expanded the application of products obtained through nanoformulations, which hold potential interest for the pharmaceutical industry. During the Jobshadowing (WP3), PP3, PP4, and PP5 and their experts will visit local farms engaged by SMART-UP to learn about existing technologies and sustainable processes adopted by them in this project.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other ORIENTATE is in line with the Specific Objective “RSO2.6 Promoting the transition to a circular and resource efficient economy”. In particular, it matches the UN SDG 12 “Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns” that requires less waste of natural resources and pollutants while encouraging the reduced use of toxic materials.
    • In this regard, ORIENTATE is mainly aimed at promoting and sharing the importance related to the circular economy model by supporting the creation of bio-products obtained from agricultural waste treatment through the use of innovative and sustainable technologies applied to two agricultural supply chains, namely Citrus and Olive.
    • ORIENTATE is also in compliance with the ‘Farm to Fork’ strategy aimed at accelerating the transition to a sustainable, healthy, and fair food systems due to deployment of new technologies within the food value chain in order to increase the demand of such food as well as the public opinion with regard to the benefits for the human nutrition.
    • It will contribute to this strategy by planning awareness raising campaigns in order to inform citizens and SMEs on the development of local bio-economy in the agricultural sector, and by arranging thematic workshops in each of the participating countries and training courses where farmers, representatives of feed manufacturing industries, agricultural operators, organisations of meat chain, managers of quality management system will be given the opportunity to learn how to adopt circular economy techniques related to the citrus and olive supply chain (for instance the reuse of pomace).
    • Finally, ORIENTATE develops joint activities in line with the recent policies adopted by the Union for the Mediterranean (UfM). Indeed, it contributes to boost the cross-border cooperation and dialogue between the EU and Southern Neighbourhood countries in the Mediterranean area by maximising the implementation of best practices in the field of agricultural waste valorisation.
