DigitalLife4ALL: An Adult & Lifelong Learning initiative to enhance the digital skills & smart competencies of Seniors, Older Adults, People with Disabilities & Persons in Vulnerable Social Groups ...

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 145. Support for the development of digital skills ~ SUMMARY: DigitalLife4ALL’s aim is to build a synergetic network of Digital Skill Hubs across all participating regions. The Hubs’ task: to continuously foster learning in modern digital skills & competencies in all citizens, especially targeting vulnerable social groups. Our Hubs will be designed to address common challenges observed both empirically & through research in partner regions: drastic lack of digital skills in seniors, older adults, people with disabilities & vulnerable groups, discomfort using digital tools offered in modern society, lack of safety against a plethora of digital threats. The Digital Era is advancing, but it’s leaving parts of our society behind, especially the already disadvantaged. This is the exact problem we set out to tackle: to build resilient infrastructure to teach digital skills & competencies to local communities, especially to vulnerable persons with respect to their sensitivities, to allow citizens of all social strata to use modern digital tools & services efficiently & safely. To augment their quality of life & enhance employability and to continuously support our common journey towards a Smart Mediterranean, leaving no-one behind. All this will take place by creating self-sufficient Digital Hubs and a unified Digital Education Curriculum, to be taught in Hubs continuously, both physically & digitally to maximize our reach.Our aim is to construct a network, a Community of Practice set towards strengthening Digital Skills in our societies, that will continuously, effectively disseminate modern Digital Competencies to citizens, stakeholders & policymakers. Through informal training & teaching cycles, community engagement & events, DigitalLife4ALL seeks to create a constant place of digital literacy in every country: a place that will always support smart expertise for beneficiaries & support horizontal digital learning in all social groups, be it adults, seniors, the disabled or socially-vulnerable, as we navigate the new Digital Era. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Education and training, ICT and digital society, Social inclusion and equal opportunities

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other The synergies of DigitalLife4ALL with NEXTMED are deeply embedded & in full alignment with pertinent EU policies, strategies and other initiatives. Specifically:
    • DigitalLife4ALL aids towards building a more Social & Inclusive Mediterranean (Priority 3) with the specific objective of enhancing digital literacy among vulnerable groups, incl. seniors, older adults, people with disabilities and other marginalized communities. The project seeks to bridge the digital divide in selected target groups & support their social inclusion by providing tailored educational programmes through Digital Skill Hubs. Our aim is to boost equitable access to digital services & employability opportunities and enhance participation in social & economic life, thus supporting the broader goal of creating an inclusive Mediterranean. The project's focus on inclusivity addresses the immediate needs of these target groups and contributes to long-term social resilience by infusing beneficiaries with important digital skills.
    • DigitalLife4ALL will utilize existing frameworks, outcomes and best practices from previous EU-funded projects to develop its curriculum, thereby ensuring scalability and enhancement of educational content. This not only avoids duplication of efforts, but also leverages previous investments to create greater value. Moreover, by collaborating with key stakeholders and partners already active in the region, DigitalLife4ALL will enhance its impact through shared resources, expertise and networks. In addition, activities will be designed to directly support and promote the policies and strategies defined by NEXTMED, in particular those focusing on social inclusion & digital competitiveness. Finally, by focusing on the establishment of self-sustainable Digital Skills Hubs, the project ensures long-term sustainability and continued contribution to regional digital literacy policies, reinforcing the broader strategic objectives of social cohesion and economic competitiveness.
