Food Governance and Policies at local level

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 3 - Governance Projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 173. Enhancing institutional capacity of public authorities and stakeholders to implement territorial cooperation projects and initiatives in a cross-border, transnational, maritime and inter-regional context ~ SUMMARY: The FOOD GaP project aims at providing a significant contribution for a better cooperation governance in the Mediterranean, thus grasping the extraordinary opportunity offered by this novel priority in the NEXT-MED programme. The project aim is to support local public authorities and stakeholders to adopt improved governance models for local food policies development in the Mediterranean. Food policies' focus is key to address complex and intertwined challenges threatening cities in both shores of the programme area (such as urbanization, resources depletion, food insecurity, import dependency, as the international crisis and COVID-19 demonstrated, rural-urban disconnections) and a coordination is needed both with local stakeholders and cross borders to reduce the pressures on local communities wellbeing, welfare and social and political stability. FOOD GaP objective will be achieved by: i. fostering local public authorities capacities to coordinate their efforts within their administrations and with the local actors (institutions, civil society, academia, and economic and social partners), ii. designing local participatory governance models, iii. piloting them by testing the improved services, embedded by the local food policies’ development, iv. establishing the Mediterranean Coalition for local food policies development. Ciheam Bari will lead the project with 4 local public authorities (Tyre, LB, the metropolitan City Mersin, TR, the Metropolitan city of Rome, IT, Houmt Souk, TN), EPLO, EL, and 5 Associate partners, representing EU and international networks and organizations. All together will contribute advocate for a city food diplomacy and create new avenues for extended collaborations within the programme area, other cities, international networks and higher policy levels. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Governance, partnership, Institutional cooperation and cooperation networks, Urban development

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other The FOOD GaP project has the ambition to be a flagship initiative toward improved governance models in the programme area and beyond and has a cross-cutting entry point with other Next-Med priorities and specific objectives. An enhanced social and institutional environment in fact facilitate the attainment of a green, social and digital transition, strongly claimed by the EU strategies.
    • Moreover, Food GaP intends to complement the EUSAIR strategy and the WestMed Initiative by addressing the food system sustainability in a synergic way with the blue economy pillar, finally demonstrating the intimate connection between blue economy and rural development, as both determine the sustainable and inclusive growth in the area.
    • The project is complementary with the renewed Agenda for the Mediterranean above all considering the turmoils due to the recent Palestinian crisis. In this unexpected emergency, local communities need to mange humanitarian aid and cooperate with multi-level actors and across-sectors on equal footing. The renewed Agenda also relaunched the need of the untapped potential of Southern Mediterranean countries on key themes such as inclusive societies, and economies for all.
    • Moreover, the Food GaP, is synergic with Union for the Mediterranean (UfM) EDILE initiative as it highlights the need to catalyze funding from regional and central public institutions.Actually, the project contrIbutes to the achievement of UN AGENDA2030 (SDG17,1,2,3,11,12), in terms of capacities and supports alliances with the civil society; food security, nutrition and poverty eradication in cities; inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable cities; sustainable production and consumption.
    • At the European level, the new Green Deal and its Farm to Fork strategy, the cohesion funds, the European Agricultural Fund for Rural Development (EAFRD), the Territorial Cooperation programmes all prioritize the need for a coordinated governance, including cities, rural and coastal communities.
