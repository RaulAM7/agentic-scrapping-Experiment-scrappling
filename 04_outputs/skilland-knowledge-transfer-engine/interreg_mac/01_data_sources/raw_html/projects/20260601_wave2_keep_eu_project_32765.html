Creative craft for industrial symbiosis in blue-economy sectors - RECONNECT

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
RECONNECT vise à stimuler l'économie circulaire en Méditerranée en réutilisant les déchets de la pêche et de l'aquaculture pour créer des produits biosourcés de valeur. Il encourage la collaboration entre les industries, les entreprises artisanales et les communautés en utilisant des technologies innovantes et des modèles commerciaux durables. Le projet met en évidence le rôle de l'artisanat dans la mise en œuvre d'approches créatives et sociales de la circularité. Il soutient la coopération entre des secteurs qui interagissent rarement, au bénéfice des grandes industries comme des petites entreprises. RECONNECT testera ses outils auprès de 130 PME et créera des réseaux et des ressources pour garantir un impact à long terme sur les pratiques durables de l'économie bleue.

Description (EN):
RECONNECT aims to boost the circular economy in the Mediterranean by reusing waste from fisheries and aquaculture to create valuable bio-based products. It promotes collaboration between industries, craft-based businesses, and communities using innovative technology and sustainable business models. The project highlights the role of crafts in driving creative and social approaches to circularity. It supports cooperation between sectors that rarely interact, benefiting both large industries and small firms. RECONNECT will test its tools with 130 SMEs and create networks and resources to ensure long-term impact in sustainable blue economy practices.

Expected Achievements (EN):
WP1 establishes the foundation of the RECONNECT project, focusing on laying the groundwork for subsequent activities. This work package involves several key preparatory activities designed to ensure a robust and well-informed start to the project. It includes creating comprehensive regional reports that map the current state of industrial symbiosis and circularity, developing an open innovation methodology for clustering within fisheries, aquaculture (including algae/micro-algae-based activities), and conducting thorough stakeholder mapping. To complete the toolkit, an AI-based tool to assess SMEs capabilities and create collaborations will be finalised for testing. Additionally, WP1 organizes study visits and experience exchange programs to foster knowledge transfer and capacity building. If applicable, the specifications for an open call to recruit firms will be defined, ensuring a transparent and inclusive engagement process. COORDINATOR: PP8 UNIRI WP2 focuses on the practical application and testing of the toolkit composed by OI methodologies and the AI-based tool developed in WP1. This work package is dedicated to piloting innovative approaches through the recruitment of firms and the establishment of innovation brokerage units. These units will be piloted both transnational by sector and intersectorally, fostering cross-border and cross-sector collaborations. SMEs from fishery (Pilot n.1) and aquaculture (Pilot n.2) sectors from all project areas will be assessed for innovation needs and to identify potential re-use of residues from their or others production processes. The results of the assessment will be matched with existing solutions, creating further synergies and opportunities of transectoral collaborations. The activities in WP2 aim to demonstrate the feasibility and effectiveness of the proposed solutions in real-world settings, providing valuable insights and refinements for scaling up. WP2 Coord: PP5 KINNO WP3 is centered on ensuring the sustainability and broader impact of the project’s outcomes. This work package focuses on transferring the knowledge and best practices developed during the project to a wider audience, enhancing the resilience of the participating sectors, and capitalizing on the project’s successes. It sees the development of guidelines to help SMEs navigate symbiotic systems by integrating open innovation practices and providing consulting services to SMEs to help them adapt and consolidate their positions within symbiotic ecosystems. Additionally, the creation of a position paper outlining transition pathways through open innovation will summarise lesson learnt, future trends, etc to be endorsed by public authorities for the follow-up activities. WP3 aims to ensure that the benefits of the project extend beyond its duration, promoting long-term sustainability and growth in the Mediterranean blue-economy sectors. WP3 coordinator: PP2 CCIAADL

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal It contributes to the Circular Economy Action Plan by demonstrating practical applications. Through the development and implementation of resource-efficient business models and the transformation of waste into high-value products, RECONNECT supports the plan's objectives of fostering sustainable economic growth and reducing environmental pressure
    • RECONNECT starts from the fundamental premise that Research and Innovation (R&I) is a central driver for promoting a sustainable blue economy, as well as for achieving the objectives set out in the European Green Deal. Lead, navigate and accelerate the trasformative Green Deal agenda through transparent, comprehensive and balanced scientific evidence and innovative solutions.
    • To make this happen, RECONNECT plans to: invest in technologies that respect the environment;
    • restore degraded ecosystems and increasingly expand land and marine protected areas; reduce the use of pesticides; promote the sustainability of food production;
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion RECONNECT supports EUSAIR by enhancing economic and social prosperity in the Adriatic and Ionian region through sustainable blue-economy practices. The project’s waste management and circular economy initiatives align with EUSAIR’s pillars of Blue Growth and Environmental Quality. RECONNECT project contributes to the flagship initiative “Fostering quadruple helix ties in the fields of marine technologies and blue technologies for advancing innovation, business development and business adaptation in blue bioeconomy”. The project contributes as follows: Strengthening quadruple helix ties in the field of blue technologies in the region through: a) Stronger RDI and cooperation among SMEs and between SMEs, large enterprises and research centres operating in the Adriatic-Ionian Macroregion; b) Increased networking between researchers, SMEs and clusters; c) Increased joint research papers and number of researchers exchanged within the macro-region-Internationalization of SMEs in the region
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion Contribution: While primarily focused on coastal and marine areas, RECONNECT’s principles of resource efficiency and sustainable practices can be adapted to the Alpine region’s circular economy strategies. The project fosters innovation and cross-regional collaboration, which are central to EUSALP. The partnership will get in touch with the crossing-group on Circular Economy and will contribute to its workplan.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 Contribution: The project contributes to the Territorial Agenda 2030 by promoting a sustainable, balanced, and harmonious development of the European territory. RECONNECT’s focus on integrating craft sector innovation with industrial practices supports territorial cohesion and enhances regional competitiveness.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin Contribution: RECONNECT directly supports this initiative by promoting sustainable blue-economy practices that improve marine and coastal ecosystem health, enhance economic development, and foster social inclusion in the Western Mediterranean region. In the frame of this Mediterranean initiative, RECONNECT focuses on the fishery/aquaculture sector and capitalises experiences and practices. Additionally, RECONNECT's objectives are fully in line with the BLUEfamsa project's objectives, particularly: a) create added value from waste; b) use discarded fish/shellfish in other phases of the production cycle, c) highlight synergies with other sectors, agriculture included.
