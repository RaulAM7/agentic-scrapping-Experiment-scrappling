Mediterranean Waste Innovations for Sustainable Environments

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 067. Household waste management: prevention, minimisation, sorting, reuse, recycling measures ~ SUMMARY: The Mediterranean grapples with waste management issues, with most municipal waste ending up in landfills untreated. Organic waste, a major component, and a valuable energy and nutrient resource, is often contaminated, making it inadequate for usage. The imminent climate change challenge worsens these effects. The Ellen MacArthur Foundation stresses the need for a circular economy to address this. However, the region faces inconsistent variations of legislative, technical, and funding gaps hindering the implementation of circular strategies. Public perception of waste as a disposal issue adds to these challenges. In response to this context, the MedWISE project aims to accelerate the transition to a circular resource-efficient economy within the region through the sharing of proven-efficient solutions. The project will therefore optimize organic resource management using two main strategies: enhancing cross-border testing of innovative technological resource efficiency solutions and increasing transnational knowledge sharing of technologies through a triple helix approach. The methodology comprises 6 WPs implemented in Italy, Spain, Lebanon, Jordan, Turkey, and Tunisia. While WP1 prioritizes efficient project management, WP2 develops a robust communication strategy and communities of practice for widespread result dissemination. The technical WPs focus on promoting cradle-to-grave solutions. Specifically, WP3 maps 3 types of innovations: industrial symbiosis, AI-powered sorting at the source, and decentralized composting by focusing not only on technological but also environmental social and economic transferability. Then, WP4 pilots the solutions while enhancing parallelism between northern and southern contexts. The remaining WPs target cross-border solution adoption. WP5 builds the capacities of public and private entities while raising awareness at the societal level while WP6 concentrates on policy integration to ensure regional and local policy mainstreaming. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Green technologies, ICT and digital society, Innovation capacity and awareness-raising

ENEA will use the experience in the developing of such kind of platforms, e.g., the Symbiosis® platform and the DECORUM platform. The IS Platform will integrate specific tools with the following functionalities:
  • ▪ Explore all the possibilities of IS in agro-industrial districts;
  • ▪ Check the compliance with environmental, health and technical standards and regulations;
  • ▪ Provide a digital passport and guarantee the treaceability;
  • ▪ Monitoring the supply chain in terms of sutainability and circularity.
  • On one hand, this output will be exploited in the MEDWISE project to engage stakeholders, build and extend IS networks, and practice IS round tables. On the other hand, it will serve as a starting point for the design of the MEDWISE IS platform, which will be developed and implemented with its own specific contents and tools. Moreover, the experience acquired with the pilot testing (considering several producers and several end users) within the Sicilian territory will support the MEDWISE activities on the management and valorization of urban organic waste with resource-efficient and circular approaches.   • By leveraging the insights and accomplishments from Med4Waste, ESDU-AUB aims to enhance its initiatives in these specific realms. (EN), SURFOLY (EN) | ESDU-AUB will leverage the expertise acquired from SURFOLY projectin the following domains:
  • SURFOLY serves as a pioneering model of circular economy, converting olive oil by-products into premium-quality animal feed suitable for small ruminants, such as sheep and goats, in the Mediterranean area. Through the utilization of olive pomace, stones, and polyphenol-rich wastewater in an advanced pyrolysis procedure, SURFOLY generates biochar capable of absorbing pollutants and acting as a beneficial feed supplement. This feed not only enhances animal well-being and product quality but also mitigates methane emissions. The project seamlessly integrates these processes within mixed crop-livestock systems, where grazing ruminants aid in carbon sequestration within olive groves. This comprehensive approach not only fosters sustainable agriculture and bolsters the olive industry but also underscores significant environmental advantages, advocating for replication across various socio-economic landscapes throughout the MENA region.
  • By employing the circular economy framework to transform waste by-products, the project initiates a harmonized and sustainable approach.   • ENI CBC Med Programmes by EU 2021-2023 (EN) | The main objective of the project is to promote social inclusion and combat poverty by providing underprivileged NEETs (youth not in employment, education, or training) and women with marketable skills based on circular economy business models applied to the hotel, food retail, and household sectors. It is estimated that the project will create 8 new jobs for NEETs and women in Aqaba, Jordan. These pilots will continue to develop circular economy business models and serve as ongoing training platforms, even after the project ends, fostering a sector expected to generate more opportunities in the future. Additionally, the project will facilitate job placements for vulnerable groups in new services created by the partners, primarily in waste management and farming. Beneficiaries include unemployed women of all ages and NEETs aged 18–24 at risk of exclusion, Aqaba’s civil-based organizations, and targeted hotels. Project outputs include promoting a cross-border network and establishing local mechanisms involving public, private, and civil society sectors to assist NEETs and women through supported employment schemes in circular economy jobs; delivering a training program for NEETs and women focusing on soft and hard skills related to biowaste management and entrepreneurship using both theoretical and on-the-job methods with digital tools; and implementing circular biowaste management pilots that create jobs and foster sustainable circular economy employment in partnering countries. (EN), GIMED: Green Impact MED Project - Positive Investments for Positive Impacts - ENI-CBC Med (EN) | The development of a green economy in the Mediterranean area to combat environmental degradation represents a great opportunity for jobs creation, particularly for young graduates and women who are three times less likely to be employed than men. Strengthening eco-innovative entrepreneurship is a recognised solution even though green ventures face a complex set of challenges including limited access to funding, underdeveloped markets and fragmented support from sector stakeholders. GIMED aimed at boosting the development of eco-innovative ventures to create employment and drive the green and circular economy in the Mediterranean. The project implemented a United Nations-backed ‘Green Entrepreneurship Standard’ that supported entrepreneurs in green economy to generate successful ventures those ventures a mark of quality to better access finance and markets. On the ground, the project trained and coached eco-innovators and encouraged financiers to invest in the Mediterranean eco-innovation sector. Some of the project’s beneficiaries were in the waste-management sector. (EN)

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other On a strategic level, MedWISE aligns with several strategies and initiatives as outlined below:
    • - UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 12, 14, and 15: It aligns with these SDGs by promoting sustainable consumption and production patterns, conserving marine resources, and protecting terrestrial ecosystems. This is done through the dissemination of circular economy principles, waste reduction strategies, and sustainable resource management practices

    • - European Raw Materials Alliance (ERMA): It complements ERMA's aim to diversify supply chains, create jobs, and foster innovation in the raw materials value chain by promoting resource-efficient practices, enhancing technology transfer, building the capacity of engineers and students, and boosting the recovery and recycling of critical raw materials.

    • - EU Mission: Restore our Ocean and Waters: It synergizes with this mission by promoting sustainable practices that protect and restore ocean health. It complements this mission by increasing the adoption of innovative resource management solutions within the Mediterranean region resulting in polluted waterbodies
    • - Union for the Mediterranean Ministerial Declarations on Energy, Environment, and Climate Action: It aligns with these declarations by promoting clean waste management solutions, environmental protection, and climate action. It complements these efforts by implementing sustainable practices, fostering collaboration across borders, and contributing to a greener and more resilient Mediterranean region.
