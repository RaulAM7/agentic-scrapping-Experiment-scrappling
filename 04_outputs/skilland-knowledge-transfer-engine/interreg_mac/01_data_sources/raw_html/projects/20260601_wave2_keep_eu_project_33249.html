Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 064. Water management and water resource conservation (including river basin management, specific climate change adaptation measures, reuse, leakage reduction) ~ SUMMARY: The disruption of natural water cycles caused by human activities, such as land use changes, agricultural practices, and urbanisation, is leading to ecological damage in the Mediterranean (Med) region which is particularly vulnerable to the impacts of global warming. Despite this, water management policies often focus on large-scale infrastructure projects, such as dams and water treatment plants that do not adequately consider the importance of Small Water Cycles (SWC). This disconnect has negative consequences as SWCs play a critical role in maintaining healthy ecosystems and supporting agricultural practices. Additionally, it appears that there is limited knowledge among farming communities on the concept of restoring SWCs and its benefits, including conserving water sources, improving soil health and increasing agricultural productivity. The Med region offers a suitable context to address these global challenges at a regional scale and serves as a real test bed for identifying effective solutions. In this respect, MAENA is designed to build capacities and support the adoption of practices targeting SWC restoration to improve on farm water use efficiency. The project will be implemented based on a participatory approach while actively involving farmers and surrounding local communities. Four demonstration farms will provide proof of concept of SWC restoration that will be deployed in twenty farmers’ lands in partner countries. MAENA will transfer knowledge and skills on SWC restoration to the targeted community through transnational training and capacity building with onsite, online and peer exchanges formats. MAENA will form the first core of a Hub fostering SWC restoration in the Med region, enabling decision makers to replicate and scale-up the project outcomes, and carry forward this new paradigm. This approach will result in more resilient and sustainable farming communities supporting both people and the environment by replenishing our water (مائنا). ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Agriculture and fisheries and forestry, Soil and air quality, Water management

  • H2020-MSCA-RISE (EN) | - Improved remote sensing-based soil moisture, vegetation, and evapotranspiration products, as well as agricultural management tools
  • These tools, developed by isardsaT in collaboration with INAT, use innovative algorithms to estimate high spatial resolution soil moisture (SM), evapotranspiration (ET), and vegetation status (VEG) from Earth Observation data. SM, ET and VEG estimates are built based on several synergies between multi-sensor/multi- resolution/multi-wavelength remote sensing data.
  • The tools provide information at both high spatial (field scale) and high temporal (weekly) resolutions to enable early estimation of yields and identification of critical periods where water stress may have a significant impact on yield.
  • Besides, ACCWA has developed several relevant water and agricultural management tools. The first concerns irrigated agriculture where a methodology was developed to quantify irrigation water amounts based only on remote sensing data. This tool will derive this key variable in fields not equipped with water meters during MAENA implementation. Another tool concerns the estimation of cereals yield well ahead (between 2 to 3 months) the end of the season.
  • Access will be given to MAENA partners to deploy these tools to monitor soil and vegetation status and manage irrigation. In fact, the tools will be applied in the specific cases of demonstration and beneficiary farmers' lands. Reports with all relevant data and recommendations will be shared with all beneficiaries.
  • - MAENA will be benefiting also from the extensive network for inter-sectorial knowledge sharing developed in the framework of the ACCWA project. (EN)

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other MAENA supports sustainable water management and green agriculture by promoting practices that reduce environmental impact and support biodiversity.
    • It will contribute to UN SDGs, including SDG 13 by reducing water-related risks, improving soil health and reducing soil erosion, and to SDG 15 as restoring water cycles can help to support healthy agricultural ecosystems and to improve soil fertility.
    • It is in line with the EU Biodiversity Strategy 2030 (points 2.2.2, 2.2.3 & 3.3.4) and EU Adaptation Strategy that focus on protected soil (nature restoration plan), improved water availability and increased resilience to extreme weather events. It is also in accordance with the UfM Ministerial Declaration on Environment and Climate Action (points 1, 3, 7 & 8).
    • In fact, Small Water Cycle (SWC) restoration helps to improve water availability, reduce the risk of water scarcity and ensure that water resources are used more efficiently. It also helps to build resilience to extreme weather events, such as droughts and floods, by promoting greater water infiltration, reducing soil erosion, and supporting healthy ecosystems. The solutions proposed include conservation agriculture techniques, rainwater harvesting, drip irrigation powered with solar, cultivation under controlled conditions, advanced monitoring solutions, and other nature-based practices that will be implemented while actively engaging:
    • · Farmers, within a participatory approach, to be able to propose user-driven solutions,
    • · Authorities and decision makers to ensure future scaleup by adopting field-scale strategies
    • MAENA is in line with the Farm to Fork strategy that aims to promote fair, healthy and environmentally-friendly food systems. In particular, the transnational training & capacity building package, as well as the engaging events and the created hub will support healthy and sustainable agricultural practices, and public awareness of the importance of ecological sustainability and food security.
