Transformation of Schoolyards to Combat Climate Change Effects and Promote Healthy and Active Environments

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Les effets croissants du changement climatique représentent des menaces importantes pour les villes, en particulier les fortes pluies, les inondations, la hausse des températures extrêmes et les effets des îlots de chaleur urbains ont un impact significatif sur l’environnement urbain, la qualité de vie et la santé en Méditerranée. Le projet Re-Act Schools aborde ces défis en mettant l’accent sur l’adaptation au changement climatique et la prévention des risques dans les espaces extérieurs des écoles. Les cours d’école, en particulier dans les villes où les espaces verts publics sont limités, sont des atouts précieux qui offrent des opportunités uniques pour tester et promouvoir des solutions technologiques innovantes et basées sur la nature pour l’atténuation et l’adaptation au changement climatique. Pour atteindre cet objectif, le projet rassemble un partenariat diversifié et complémentaire qui comprend 2 institutions de recherche, 5 organismes publics et une agence de développement territorial de 6 pays méditerranéens (Italie, Espagne, France, Croatie, Bulgarie et Chypre), qui travailleront ensemble pour transformer 6 cours d’école grâce à des actions pilotes en espaces verts, résilients au changement climatique et innovants qui favorisent des modes de vie sains et actifs parmi les élèves et la communauté au sens large, selon les objectifs du nouveau programme européen Bahuahus et Green Deal. L’activité de test sera accompagnée d’une phase de capacity building finalisée à définir une méthodologie de conception innovante pour atténuer le stress thermique, gérer efficacement les ressources en eau, améliorer la biodiversité, et offrir des abris climatiques aux citoyens dans le contexte urbain méditerranéen sur la base de l’utilisation des simulations microclimatiques ENVI-met et des modèles Digital Urban Climate Twin pour tester des méthodes avancées d’analyse, de conception et de surveillance pour des cours d’école résilientes au climat. Les connaissances développées au cours du projet seront recueillies dans un plan d’action innovant et un outil multimédia pour aider l’administration publique de la région Med à gérer la transformation régénératrice des cours d’école et des espaces publics.

Description (EN):
The escalating effects of climate change pose significant threats to cities; in particular, heavy rainfall, flooding, rising extreme temperatures, and urban heat island effects significantly impact the urban environment, life quality, and health in the Mediterranean. The Re-Act Schools project addresses these challenges by focusing on climate change adaptation and risk prevention in school outdoor spaces. Schoolyards, especially in cities where public green living areas are limited, are valuable assets that offer unique opportunities to test and promote Nature-based and innovative technological solutions for climate change mitigation and adaptation capable to reduce the climate hazard. To achieve this goal, the project brings together a diverse and complementary partnership that comprises 2 research institutions, 5 public bodies, and a territorial development agency from 6 Mediterranean countries (Italy, Spain, France, Croatia, Bulgaria, and Cyprus), that will work together to transform 6 schoolyards through Pilot Actions into green, climate-resilient and innovative spaces that promote healthy and active lifestyles among students and the wider community, according to the goals of the New European Bauhaus and Green Deal Program. The testing activity will be accompanied by a capacity-building phase finalised to define an innovative design methodology to mitigate heat stress, manage water resources efficiently, enhance biodiversity, and offer climate shelters to the citizens in the Mediterranean urban context based on the use of ENVI-met microclimatic simulations and Digital Urban Climate Twin models to test advanced methods for analysing, designing, and monitoring climate-resilient schoolyards. The knowledge developed during the project will be collected in an innovative Action Plan and a multimedia tool to support the Public Administration of the Med Area in managing the regenerative transformation of schoolyards and public spaces.

Expected Achievements (EN):
WP1, coordinated by the Province of Lucca, enhances the capabilities of PA and local governments across the MED area in effective planning for climate change adaptation and climate change risk management of school outdoor spaces, improving the occupants' outdoor comfort and health, through the exchange of knowledge at interregional level (interregional workshops), the definition of local guidelines and action plans, and the production of a digital tool kit WP2, coordinated by Department du VAR, aims at transforming the existing schoolyards located in different Mediterranean territories into climate- resilient spaces by designing and implementing six Pilot Actions applying innovative Nature-based and technological solutions. WP3 coordinated by UNIFI DIDA, aims to improve the schoolyards' thermal comfort and the user's health conditions by increasing the level of schoolyards climate resilience through the identification of climate-related risks and the development of targeted interventions. WP4, coordinated by CYI, will develop a model for data-driven management and optimization of school outdoor spaces by comparing the environmental quality of the schoolyards before and after the transformation due to the Pilot Actions.

The aim was to improving, through technical solutions and participatory methodologies, energy efficiency in secondary schools in the Mediterranean area.
  • Teenergy Schools has set up an Action Plan and a common Strategy based on the experimentation of energy saving techniques, integration of innovative materials and renewable energies for reducing costs and consumptions. The project has implemented Best Practice Benchmark based on data from Energy Audits conducted on more than 80 school buildings in MED area, to provide representative values and compare the secondary schools’ actual energy performance.
  • (EN), N.A. (FR) |
  • The Re-Act Schools project leverages the findings from TEENERGY SCHOOLS, which focused on enhancing energy efficiency in Mediterranean schools, to enrich its own approach towards creating climate-resilient outdoor environments. In particular, Re-Act Schools will build on the shared experience of three project partners, DIDA, CYI, and the Province of Lucca in designing the policy guidelines for climate resilient schoolyards design.
