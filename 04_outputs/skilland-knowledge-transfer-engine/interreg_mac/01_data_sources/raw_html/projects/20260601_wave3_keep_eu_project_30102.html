Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Les îles méditerranéennes vont entamer leur transition vers la neutralité climatique grâce au projet CO2 Pacman. Atraver les résultats et les outils des précédents projets (BLUE DEAL, COMPOSE/COMPOSE Plus, FP7 CityZen), CO2 Pacman fournira les connaissances et les outils nécessaires pour les stratégies et de politiques intégrées pour relever le défi de l'atténuation du changement climatique et de l'adaptation à celui-ci ; à cette fin, il engagera les autorités publiques, les citoyens et les parties prenantes dans la co-création de scénarios pour la transition vers des îles climatiquement neutres. Les activités du projet seront présentées dans 3 îles: Brac (HR), Crète(EL), Elbe (IT). Le projet développera un cadre coopératif de planification intégrée et fournira une stratégie/un plan d'action conjoint intégrant des politiques et des stratégies d'atténuation du changement climatique et d'adaptation à celui-ci, qui pourront être reproduites dans les îles et les zones côtières. Le nouvel outil d'évaluation et de visualisation du CO2 Pacman sera perfectionné et utilisé pour évaluer les défis actuels (e.g. l'empreinte carbone et ses facteurs), puis pour identifier un ensemble de solutions multisectorielles (dans les domaines de l'énergie, des bâtiments, de la mobilité, de la gestion de l'eau et des déchets, du tourisme, de l'industrie), basées sur des approches écosystémiques, pour quantifier leurs effets en termes d'atténuation et de compensation de l'empreinte carbone pour l'objectif de neutralité climatique dans les îles. L'approche de coopération et de co-création ciblera les autorités publiques, les entreprises, les groupes d'intérêt et les citoyens pour soutenir l'innovation et le renforcement des capacités. Le passage à une société climatiquement neutre nécessite une transition culturelle: le projet intègre des informations scientifiques et techniques dans un nouveau système de communication capables d'aider à planifier la transition climatique pour les îles.

Description (EN):
Mediterranean islands will start their transition to climate neutrality thanks to the CO2 Pacman project. Building on outcomes and tools from the previous projects Interreg Med BLUE DEAL and COMPOSE Plus and the FP7 CityZen, CO2 Pacman will provide knowledge and tools to drive the implementation of integrated strategies and policies for the challenge of climate change mitigation and adaptation; with this purpose it will engage public authorities, citizens and stakeholders in co-creation of scenarios for the transition towards climate neutral islands. Project activities will be showcased in three target islands: Brac in Croatia, Crete in Greece and Elba in Italy. Based on results from testing, the project will develop an integrated cooperative planning framework and deliver a joint strategy/action plan embedding policies and strategies for climate change mitigation and adaptation to be potentially replicated in islands and coastal areas. The new CO2 Pacman assessment and visual tool will be developed to assess current challenges (i.e. Carbon Footprint and its drivers) and then to identify multi-sectoral solutions and to quantify their effects in terms of Carbon Footprint mitigation and compensation to achieve the goal of climate neutrality in the islands. These solutions, including ecosystem based approaches, will concern the sectors of energy, buildings, mobility, water and waste management, tourism, industry, etc. The cooperation and co-creation approach will target: public authorities (increasing their capacity in effective planning), enterprises, groups of interest and citizens to foster both transnational and local cooperation, support innovation and capacity building. The shift to a climate neutral society requires a cultural transition: the project integrates scientific and technical information in a new clear and fun communication and social engagement system, composed by new visual tools able to help plan climate neutrality transition roadmaps for islands.

Actual Achievements (EN):
CO2PACMAN project officially started on November 21st 2023. The Kick-Off Meeting was held on 20-22 March 2024 in Siena (Italy), and established the groundwork for project implementation, with discussions on roles, responsibilities, and timelines. Before that several informal online meetings were organized to prepare activities. The project management structure was presented and has been fully operational since the project kick-off meeting. The Project Management Team has been established by UNISI and includes representatives from all partner organizations, ensuring efficient coordination and communication. As LP, UNISI has continually assured the flow of communication between PPs ensuring the circulation of the relevant information concerning the contents of the project, as well as administrative and financial issues. Since the beginning, all project partners were engaged and actively contributed to the project development, except for RAUSK who withdrew from the Partnership after a few months. The CO2PACMAN Steering Committee decided to replace RAUSK, with all its budget, with the new Bosnian partner CENER21. For this reason, the procedure to modify the AF has been finalized in delay by the LP in accordance with the SC members and the JS and the signature of the contract process has been concluded in July 2024. The LP and the WP leaders coordinated the co-working activities of PPs. Online meetings were held to discuss progress, address issues, and ensure alignment across all work packages. A budget Redistribution among UNIFI and UNISI Partners has been performed and approved by the Steering Committee. UNIFI asked UNISI to take over the coordination role of the WP1. This decision was derived due to the change in composition of the UNIFI team, and the loss of the previous reference person and related expertise. While minor challenges have arisen, they have been effectively managed without significant impact on the timeline or budget. Clear instructions and guidelines were sent to PPs to allow PP delegates to provide their contribution, collect coherent information, and assemble final deliverables by WP leaders. Partner UNISI worked closely with Partner UNIFI to Activity 1.1 implementation- Preparing Pilot activities: CO2PACMAN tool assessment system. The 1.1 activity has been performed based on remote meetings and several co-working sessions. The deliverable D1.1 has been produced and definitively validated by the quality Board of the CO2PACMAN Project. Partner EPLO submitted the first draft of D.2.5- Development of communication and dissemination strategy, contributing to the project's overall understanding of the communication strategy. Managing the project’s dissemination and communication efforts, ensuring that the project’s progress and results were effectively shared with a broader audience. The project website has been also created and populated with news and information. Social Media channels (LinkedIn, Facebook, YouTube, and Instagram) have been activated and populated with information on the project's activities. A Podcast Show was launched and video interviews and video presentations of the partnership were created and produced by the LP and launched in the project social community to communicate about the project. Activity 1.2- Preparing pilot activities: cooperative planning has been started by Resel-TUC Partner, and will be finalized in the coming months. A longer period was required to achieve a common understanding among project partners and to develop a joint motivation and strategy. For this reason ReSEL-TUC initiated a consultation matrix exercise, to integrate and streamline all views, incentives and expectations. Also, the team put some effort into making links with previous initiatives (i.e. BLUE DEAL, COMPOSE) to build on experiences, tested methods and, exploring potential synergies for a more effective implementation. The deliverable D1.2.1 will be soon available in Basecamp. In general, all the partners provided essential resources and expertise that enabled WP and task Leaders to finalize the specific deliverable of this first reporting period. Procedures for internal communication have been created and agreed upon between all PPs, including the creation of the Communication Team (made by one person per PP), and the shared Google Drive folder as the repository for all the project documents. The foreseen Communication activity for 2024 has been summarized by the CM and shared with all PPs. During the initial semester, the LP and the Communication manager participated in the Kick-off of the Green Living Areas Community project in Turin (in April 2024). Overall, the project is on track to meet its goals, and the consortium is committed to maintaining momentum in the next reporting period. Each partner has made substantial contributions, both independently and in collaboration with others, ensuring that the project remains on track and continues to deliver high-quality outputs. During this reporting period, key events and activities ensured active participation and coordination across the CO2 PACMAN partnership.The new Partner CENER21 participated to the all the activities of the project. Partners organized and facilitated the Rooting Labs in Brač (Sept 2024, co-organized by SDC and SDEWES) and Crete (Oct 2024, organized by ReSEL-TUC), promoting stakeholder cooperation and fostering community involvement in decarbonization. Additionally, several coordination meetings were held to ensure the smooth progress of project activities. The finalized Communication and Dissemination Strategy is the primary guide for the project's external communication efforts, ensuring consistency and outreach across all partners. The project website, particularly the News & Events section, was updated in this framework to keep stakeholders informed. Project partners presented CO2 PACMAN in some events to communicate the project's objectives, share insights, and foster new collaborations. In particular, UNISI participated in the 1st Institutional Policy Dialogue 1st Forum EuroMED Italia, organized by the Italian NCP. Besides, delegates of CO2 PACMAN attended some events organized by the GLA Mission. All PPs participated in the Rooting Lab workshops in Brač and Crete, contributing to the project's activities and visibility. EPLO supported the project's communication activities, providing input at the GLA Communication Managers meeting and contributing to the overall communication strategy. In addition, the project engages with the public through various initiatives, such as promoting CO2 PACMAN activities through media campaigns, online platforms, podcasts and video interviews. ReSEL-TUC engaged with local and national press, supporting campaigns for Crete's climate neutrality and ensuring widespread outreach across regional communities. SDC and SDWES Center promoted the 1st Rooting Lab across regional and local media. Besides, four new episodes of the CO2 PACMAN podcast shows were recorded and published (created in cooperation with UNISI’s AP URadio), all in English and the local language of the speakers. The topics covered were the role of the Ecodynamics group of the UNISI, the 1st and 2nd Rooting Labs, and the role of the SMEs. GIITT strengthened public engagement by facilitating in-person surveys and interviews of citizens during the labs, thus increasing the local community’s understanding of the project’s goals and fostering awareness among residents. UNISI and UNIFI has started with the engagement of local stakeholders and logistics for the 3rd Rooting Lab in Elba that is planned for February 2025. UNIFI has also organized a data collection on landscape planning in all three islands. Specific WP1 technical activities were integral to the project’s implementation, including developing the Cooperative Planning Guidelines (D1.2), the SWOT Analysis and Action Plan for Brač and Crete (D1.1), and preparing detailed CO2 datasets. ReSEL-TUC led Crete's and SDEWES led the Brac’s successful data collection efforts, assembling datasets on demographics, land use, and energy consumption, which will inform future climate neutrality strategies for the island. Additionally, preparations for the CO2 PACMAN tool are underway to support data analysis and collaborative planning efforts. Once finalized, the tool will be pivotal for stakeholder engagement, allowing local authorities to utilize the gathered data in designing and implementing tailored decarbonization strategies. In WP2, progress was made in Stakeholder Engagement activities, with CENER21 contributing to the development of engagement methodologies, CEEI Valencia leading the preparation for the Open Innovation Collaborative Actions; As part of WP2 communication objectives, social media campaigns were successfully launched by GIINT alongside offline activities such as Vox Populi interviews and questionnaires to increase awareness and gather input from local stakeholders. These efforts were complemented by creating engaging content, including event videos and press releases during the rooting Labs, ensuring wide dissemination of the project’s key messages and outcomes. The project has also made significant strides in participatory planning processes, with partners contributing to developing methodologies and tools that will be crucial for future planning. The use of Virtual Reality led by GEOIMAGING partner and showcased during the 1st and 2nd Rooting Labs were essential for the engagement phase. The engagement of stakeholders, including local citizens and decision-makers, continues to be a cornerstone of the project’s success. The reporting period saw considerable advancements in developing and disseminating CO2 PACMAN’s objectives and successful collaboration between partners. These efforts continue to lay a solid foundation for the project’s upcoming phases, paving the way for effective decarbonization solutions tailored to Mediterranean islands The project has made significant progress since its launch, achieving several of its specific objectives and delivered the KEY deliverables D1.3.1 and D1.4. 1.
