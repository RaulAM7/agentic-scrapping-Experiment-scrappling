Investing in Green and Just Transition for a New Sustainable Business as Usual Economy

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Urban and urban-rural areas in the MED region are under threat due to negative impacts of climate change and unsustainable development practices. This calls for a change from an old to a new economy with new consumer, business and financing models that will lead towards resource efficient New Business as Usual (BaU) Economy, making the Green and Just Transition (GJT) a functional reality. BauNOW project main objective is to improve the planning and implementing capacities of public and private stakeholders for financing of measures supporting the GJT to climate-neutral and resilient living areas. With new, integrated development practices and improved access to finances for GJT, both the public and private sector in the MED project areas will recognise the business and development potential of GJT, making it their new BaU. The GJT Dashboard is the project’s solution that will facilitate this process. An easy-to-use web-based tool with step-by-step methodology on how to design, finance and implement GJT on local or regional level will serve public authorities, practitioners and private sector. It will be validated through bottom-up processes in 9 pilot areas in 7 MED countries, leading to concrete measures in support of GJT. Transferability plan, focused on access to public and private investments for GJT, will transfer the Dashboard to other MED regions and beyond through Community of practitioners, investment forums and partnership networks. Project regions and partners represent the diversity of the MED area and aim to bridge its regional disparities by joint development of effective, easily transferable solutions for GJT. BauNOW is a new outlook on development. It delivers the Dashboard tool to support the implementation of a paradigm shift in development planning, going beyond sectoral approaches, and calling for and opening opportunities for public and private sector to re-evaluate investment principles and recognise the potential of GJT as viable investment.

Description (FR):
Les zones urbaines et urbaines-rurales de la région MED sont menacées par des impacts négatifs du changement climatique et des pratiques de développement non durables. Cela demande un changement d'une ancienne à une nouvelle économie - des nouveaux modèles de consommation, d'entreprise et de financement, portant à une économie “Business as usual” (BAU) nouvelle, économe en ressources, et une transition verte et équitable (TVE). Le projet BauNOW vise d'améliorer la planification et le financement des mesures de la TVE auprès des acteurs publics et privés vers les zones de vie climatiquement neutres résilientes. Avec des nouvelles pratiques de développement intégrées et un meilleur accès au financement, les secteurs public et privé dans les domaines des projets MED reconnaîtront le potentiel commercial et de développement de la transition, ce qui en fera leur nouveau “BAU”. Le tableau de bord pour la TVE -un simple outil de projets web- peut faciliter cela, offrant une méthodologie ascendante pour concevoir, financer et mettre en œuvre la TVE locale ou régionale. Il servira les autorités publiques, les praticiens et le secteur privé et sera validé par des processus horizontaux dans 9 zones pilotes dans 7 pays MED, conduisant à des mesures concrètes. Le plan de transférabilité, visé sur l'accès aux investissements publics et privés, le transférera à d'autres régions MED et au-delà, par le biais d'une communauté de praticiens, d'investissement et des partenariats. Les régions et les partenaires représentent la diversité de l'espace MED et visent à combler ses disparités régionales par le développement conjoint de solutions efficaces et transférables. BauNOW, une nouvelle perspective sur le développement, fournit un outil pour un changement de paradigme dans la planification du développement, au-delà des approches sectorielles, ouvrant des opportunités pour que les secteurs public et privé réévaluent les principes d'investissement et reconnaissent le potentiel de la TVE.

Actual Achievements (EN):
In this reporting period all the tasks were successfully conducted. The project started with the kick-off on February 16th. The purpose of the meeting was the presentation of lead partner (LP) Geodetic Institute of Slovenia (GIS) to acquaint all partners with the partnership, provide a general outline of the project's context and content, share administrative information and provide information on the kick-off meeting in Ljubljana. Also the short overview of the project was presented by the LP about the objectives, activities, main outputs per WP, project overall objective, project work plan and project time plan. The LP together with PPs organized In-Person two-day project meeting, which was scheduled in March 20th -21st in Ljubljana. Project partners participated in the meeting (a few of them were present online). The aim of the meeting was to introduce each other, to start the project, to unify the understanding of the project and start brainstorming about pilots. WP leaders presented activities in their WPs. LP presented key administrative and technical issues regarding project implementation including status of contracting activities, project reporting and management of the project. Project partners gave a short presentation of their work on the specific theme. They provided key information about their past and current activities, as well as potential ideas and initiatives for their future work regarding pilot activities, their experiences and knowledge .PPs presented the ideas and initiatives that are the drivers of individual “pilot” that will be developed and implemented through the BauNOW project. Project partners discussed the synergies between pilots, the type of cooperation that is needed and where they see the opportunities for connection. Some partners already made and presented drafts of implementation plans for their pilots. LP attended Green Living Areas Thematic Community Kick-off event on10 – 11 April 2024 in Turin where LP was presented project BaunOW and participated at GLA workshops. On May 2024 LP organized online working meeting concerning the general concept of the project with core PPs and KOM leader. Before the meeting a chart was sent to partners outlining a proposal for a general spatial planning and investment process into sub-phases, providing descriptions for better comprehension and the general investment life cycle process for green projects (activity 1.1). The chart serves as a step-by-step guide, detailing key phases and stakeholders involved in the participatory process. Based on the meeting, partners were asked to reflect on a concrete green project (such as climate change initiatives, nature-based solutions, mobility projects, energy initiatives, revitalization efforts, etc.) that they've either worked on or implemented in the past and evaluate its planning process. Partners provided feedback, some made an updated concept based on their project. The proposals have been taken into account in the upgrading of the concept. LP also participated at Green Living Areas BauNOW 1st Green Energy Systems Thematic Working Group Meeting 11.6.2024 and contribute to discussion. In September 2024, a joint working meeting was organised by LP to review project progress, outline the next steps, and to update the methodology for green investments. The agenda included presentations by REAN on the updated concept for the GJT Dashboard specifications, as well as discussions on administrative and reporting issues and future activities. At the end of September, further updates on the GJT Dashboard concept were presented. Following the presentation, the LP provided feedback to ensure the dashboard would be both feasible and aligned with the project's ambitions. In October 2024, the LP introduced the Euro-MED Programme’s new carbon footprint monitoring initiative. To simplify the process, the LP provided an Excel sheet for data collection. To better understand the progress of the pilot cases and offer support where needed, individual meetings were held with each partner, as well as with LP and partner Iskriva, in late September and early October. These meetings focused on discussing pilot-specific goals and planning the necessary steps to achieve them. All pilot cases were further developed by project partners, with meetings held with key stakeholders and territorial analyses conducted as needed. Some pilot cases were already launched, including an AI-powered public service solution offering a One-Stop-Shop service for citizens to receive tailored advice on energy renovation and renewable energy installations. In November 2024, the partnership held a working meeting to present an upgraded concept for the GJT Dashboard. The dashboard was developed by REAN with support from the LP and Iskriva. To facilitate the monitoring of target group engagement, the LP created a Google spreadsheet for partners to document the target groups engaged during the reporting period. In early December 2024, the LP organized a joint event in Ravne na Koroškem, attended by Judith Cazas from the programme office, who delivered a presentation on the requirements for implementing investments. During the event, each partner provided a brief five-minute presentation on their pilot activities, and the GJT Dashboard was further discussed. The event also included the Conference on Investments in the Green Transition in Remote Industrial Regions on December 4, 2024. This conference was a collaborative initiative between the Baunow project and the NONA (New Governance for New Spaces) project from the Interreg Danube Region. The conference aimed to bring together key stakeholders from local, regional, national, and international levels to foster dialogue on sustainable development in industrial and rural areas. Participants explored best practices through presentations, thematic site visits, and discussions focused on green investments, cultural heritage revitalization, and urban transformation projects, aligning with the goals of a green and just transition. The conference was covered by multiple newspapers, television channels, and other news outlets. By the end of December 2024, data was collected from partners regarding pilot areas to be included in the GJT Dashboard. Communication was maintained through online meetings, email exchanges, and an in person meeting. The LP also participated in the MED Talks (held on September 9, 2024, and December 16, 2024), which were organized by the programme to engage with other projects and stakeholders in the region. Up to this period, all tasks were successfully conducted. During this reporting period, the BauNow project achieved important progress across communication and pilot implementation. Collaboration in project was strengthened through online and in-person meetings. An online project meeting took place on 20 May 2025, complemented by several bilateral sessions with pilot partners. GI updated the project website with detailed news on pilot progress and major events, while the project’s Facebook channel was managed as a tool for wider visibility. Communication deliverables included monthly outreach reports to monitor partners. BauNow also gained visibility through participation in several major events. LP represented the project at the Interreg Euro-MED Green Living Areas Mission event in Barcelona on 28 May 2025. Slovenian partners organized the international capitalization event Inclusive Tourism as a Development and Investment Opportunity in the Danube Region on 2–3 June 2025 in Moravske Toplice. The event gathered multiple Interreg projects and hosted a BauNow information point. At national level, Slovenian, Croatian, and Albanian partners joined Green Living Areas mission events in April 2025, where they presented BauNow results and explored synergies with other initiatives. Important progress was also made on the common BauNow methodology, which continues to be refined through monitoring and evaluation of pilot cases, coordination meetings, and field visits. GI, REAN, and Iskriva advanced work on the Green and Just Transition (GJT) Dashboard, holding technical meetings to review functionalities and alignment with project goals. The Dashboard is being designed as a practical online tool to guide public authorities through all stages of the project lifecycle, from planning to financing and implementation. Pilot cases across partner countries demonstrated concrete achievements: - Croatia: Partners organized three workshops from March to June 25: Solar Potential Map to stakeholders, Financial instruments for household solar power plants, Municipal financing models for GJT measures. REA North further developed VIREAS, an AI-based chatbot providing personalized GJT information to citizens. Extensive testing and optimization were conducted during early 2025. VIREAS now integrates a technical library of guidelines and regulations, public co-financing schemes, and energy methodologies. It is accompanied by a user manual, a short citizen guide, and a one-pager for advanced users, making it fully operational and scalable. - France: Geres supported Aix-Marseille-Provence Métropole in establishing a cooperative financing structure for GJT projects, with a strong focus on governance and transparency in carbon financing. Geres prepared terms of reference for a study on the drivers of demand for local carbon projects, selected a consultant, and attended a high-profile carbon finance event in London on 3 June 2025. It also completed the internal process to become a founding member of the cooperative, ensuring long-term involvement. - Bosnia and Herzegovina: The City of Cazin progressed with solar energy cooperatives. Technical documentation for photovoltaic power plants on four public buildings was prepared, representing a significant step toward pilot implementation. - Montenegro: UDG launched its roadshow activities. The first event, the Entrepreneurial Ideas Market in Podgorica, selected two startups for continued participation. The initiative also built momentum for creating a community of investors and practitioners supporting green and just transition solutions. - Albania: AUT concentrated on Dajti National Park, where it secured funding for bicycles promoting green mobility, prepared investments for solar panels at the Visitor Center, and explored opportunities for solar-powered benches. A stakeholder questionnaire on renewable energy and green transition was developed for distribution in the coming months. - Italy: Kyoto club organised the first Green and Just Transition Hackathon, held in Bologna on 26 June 2025. This Italian pilot case brought together the Bologna Municipality, the Emilia-Romagna Regional Government, and local stakeholders. The Hackathon aligned with the Build Better Lives campaign, produced valuable inputs for the BauNow Dashboard, and generated concrete recommendations for local and regional authorities. It also strengthened connections with the broader GJT community, setting an excellent example of participatory innovation. - Slovenia: GI held several meetings with the Municipality of Kočevje to identify challenges and needs related to e-mobility and the purchase of e-cars. Peer learning remained a key component. During a site visit in Cazin (February 25), LP GI and Iskriva got familiar with the Cazin pilot case. This exchange promoted mutual learning, provided insights into local contexts, and encouraged on-site discussions about improvements and future opportunities.

    • EuropeanGreenDeal Project BauNOW with its New Businness As Usual approach fully corresponds to EU Green Deal as a growth strategy. It puts the new investment strategy and financial sustainability of the projects at the forefront of the green and just transition. Multi-sectoral and multi-level solutions provided by the Green and Just Transition Dashboard will contribute to the objectives: no net emissions of greenhouse gases by 2050, economic growth decoupled from resource use and complete inclusivity (no person and no place left behind). Some of the pilot activities demonstrate how this can be done:
    • - carbon community, solar cooperatives, sustainable mobility for decreasing greenhouse gases,
    • - program for start-ups, business community, series of hackathons, instruments of micro-finances for economic growth decoupled from resource use,
    • - no person and no place left behind with supporting public services available to all population groups: energy counselling, sustainable transition hub, sustainable mobility.
    • In addition, inclusiveness is reflected by different types of participating areas (small and middle sized cities (Cazin, Legrad, Kočevje), metropolitan areas (Marseille, Bologna) to peripheral rural areas (Koprivnica Križevci county, Una Sana county)) and diverse set of partners and ASPs (local, regional and national authorities, international organisations, chambers of commerce, associations for rural development, local action groups, NGOs, companies).
