REPair PERspective

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Why not? Re-thinking traditional business models, re-discovering the centrality of human value in the pursuit of innovation and competitiveness, re-viewing behaviors, all toward a circular economy model, is more urgent than ever. Why not? To support SMEs in the repair economy, increase repair skills, and pursue the transition to more sustainable choices by overcoming the existing repair barriers. The REPper project aims to address these challenges and propose concrete solutions to meet them. A global challenge such as sustainability, by reducing waste, increasing reuse, adopting regenerative practices and - heart of the project- implementing repair, can only be met by acting on a transnational scale. How? Through 3 action areas strongly focused on cross-border cooperation: 1. Setting-up a REPper HUBS NETWORK - a unique contact point, physical and/or digital - with the aim of designing and testing a transnational network of pilot services. 2. Creating a REPper FACTORY for upskilling and reskilling, providing training at the highest EU level, to reduce imbalances between Mediterranean regions. 3. Building a new repair culture through "gentle nudges" - NUDGING - that can trigger positive changes and new habits. These actions will inspire joint solutions such as business model canvas, blueprint, and Nudge; strategies and action plans (Roadmap, recommendations, and policy papers); and, finally, cooperative outputs with an eye to the future: an e-pledge and a letter of interest. To do this, REPper brings together highly qualified and diversified partners, capable of capitalizing on the past results, implementing them with new actions for public and private targets: SMEs, public authorities, sectoral agencies, business support organizations, schools and, last but not least, citizens. And if "Why not?" is a question that offers multiple solutions bringing us closer to a Greener Europe, REPper wants to take up the challenge by offering a different perspective.

Description (FR):
Pourquoi pas? il est plus urgent que jamais de repenser les modèles d'entreprise traditionnels, de redécouvrir la centralité de la valeur humaine dans la poursuite de l'innovation, et de revoir les comportements, le tout vers un modèle d'économie circulaire. Le projet REPper vise à relever ces défis et à proposer des solutions concrètes pour y répondre. Un défi global tel que la durabilité, en réduisant les déchets, en augmentant la réutilisation, en adoptant des pratiques régénératives et - cœur du projet - en mettant en œuvre la réparation, agissant à une échelle transnationale. Comment? A travers 3 domaines d'action axés sur la coopération transfrontalière: 1. La création d'une REPper FACTORY pour la montée en compétence et la requalification, offrant une formation au plus haut niveau de l'UE, afin de réduire les déséquilibres entre les régions méditerranéennes. 3. La construction d’une nouvelle culture de la réparation par le biais de "coups de pouces" - NUDGES - qui peuvent déclencher des changements positifs et de nouvelles habitudes. À cette fin, REPper réunit des partenaires hautement qualifiés et diversifiés, capables de capitaliser les résultats passés, de les mettre en œuvre avec de nouvelles actions pour des cibles publiques et privés: PME, autorités publiques, agences sectorielles, organisations de soutien aux entreprises, écoles et, enfin, le citoyenneté. Et si "Pourquoi pas?" est une question qui offre de multiples solutions pour parvenir à une Europe plus verte, REPper veut relever le défi en offrant une perspective différente.

Actual Achievements (EN):
In the initial phase of the project, MCBO took the lead in defining the operational and organizational structure. It established detailed guidelines for financial tracking, administrative setup, website adaptation, and coordinated communication activities. Additionally, MCBO integrated a shared folder system to optimize filing and task tracking. At the same time, it managed the contractual aspects by coordinating all partners to ensure timely updates of the necessary documentation on the JEMS platform. The partners (PP) followed MCBO's guidelines, adopting the established procedures and contributing to the smooth progression of the project from the outset. During the first period of the project, several activities were carried out across different WPs, focusing on the design, testing, and implementation of the REPper Hubs and engagement with key stakeholders. The start of period 1 took place with the Kick off meeting in Lisbon on 5 and 6 March 2024. In WP1, partners worked on developing the network of pilot services to support enterprises in the repair sector. Based on the feasibility and operational plans prepared by E-institute, partners assessed the characteristics and services of their regional hubs and shared knowledge about best practices. MCBO as LP, supported E-Zavod in creating a common methodology and template for feasibility and operational plans for regional hubs. Each partner filled in the template with information about its country and E-Zavod processed the information. E-Zavod, with the support of MCBO and the collaboration of PPs, worked also on structuring workshops to facilitate peer-to-peer (P2P) knowledge sharing, with the first workshop planned for September 2024. In order to meet the target and have more participation in the meetings, the first meeting was postponed to the second period. MCBO also helped the OCU launch activity A1.3, focusing on showcase hubs. In WP2, led by ANATOLIKI, stakeholder engagement was key to understanding and implementing the 5-helix approach for the repair hubs. ANATOLIKI compiled contributions from eight countries into the Joint Needs Analysis (D 2.2.1).and developed guidelines on engaging stakeholders in collaboration with PP7 FODSA. As part of the process for creating the Joint Needs Analysis, interviews were conducted with 3-4 training experts from each participating country. Furthermore, ANATOLIKI and DECOP conducted analyses of existing repair programs and training needs. DECOP created templates for monitoring co-creation activities with stakeholders and engaged key brands to assess their needs. These efforts contributed to deliverable D 2.1.1, outlining stakeholder engagement guidelines. In WP3, MCBO initiated the analysis of consumer behavior regarding repairs, which will lead to the creation of a transnational report on behaviors to change and a list of identified nudging strategies (Activity 3.1 REPper Nudge piloting). MCBO also collaborated with AVITEM on communication activities, creating a project website, and managed operational and financial coordination, organizing meetings and supporting partners with financial reporting. Throughout April 2024, key documents were finalized and uploaded, including templates for best practices and engagement activities. From January to December 2024, the REPper project made significant progress in fostering a repair economy through transnational cooperation and local engagement. The project has successfully aligned its activities with its key objectives, delivering impactful outputs and engaging a wide network of stakeholders. Project Coordination - The Metropolitan City of Bologna (MCBO), as LP, played a crucial role in coordinating project activities, ensuring smooth implementation, and overseeing key deliverables. The project started with the internal setup, including financial and administrative structuring, communication frameworks, and the launch of the project website. Initial meetings, both virtual and in Lisbon, facilitated alignment on guidelines, deliverables, and timelines. A major milestone was the development of feasibility and operational plans for the REPper Hubs and the regional assessment report, laying a strong foundation for the project. Work Package 1 - At the project level, feasibility studies and operational plans were developed to provide clear direction for the establishment of these hubs, setting shared operational criteria and procedures. In parallel, a testing and assessment report was produced to evaluate the effectiveness of the adopted solutions, identify best practices, and highlight potential areas for improvement. Moreover, the partners collaborated on defining a common methodology for hub implementation, contributing to the creation of the O1.1 Joint Canvas document, which serves as a key tool for ensuring coherence and standardization across transnational activities. Two key workshops were organized to facilitate peer-learning (A 1.2): the first, held on 10th September 2024, focused on building bridges in repair communities to overcome barriers, and the second, held on 20th November 2024, addressed effective communication strategies. Work Package 2 - Under the leadership of ANATOLIKI, the project has significantly contributed to capacity-building efforts for repair professionals. Key deliverables include the D2.1.1 Guidelines on how to define, map and engage stakeholders, D2.2.1 Joint Needs Analysis, and D2.2.2 Skills Portfolio for repair trainers and business supporters. All PPs actively participated in stakeholder mapping and the development of training resources, ensuring the five-helix approach was integrated into the creation of repair hubs. These efforts have laid the groundwork for structured training programs aimed at strengthening repair enterprises across Europe. Work Package 3 - The REPper project placed a strong emphasis on changing consumer behavior toward repair practices. MCBO led the REPper Nudge Piloting activity, analyzing behaviors and identifying effective nudging techniques to encourage repair habits. The drafting of D3.1.1 provided a comprehensive cross-country analysis of behaviors to be changed, offering valuable insights to refine and improve the project's nudging strategies. Communication, Dissemination, and Stakeholder Engagement - Project partners actively engaged in communication and dissemination efforts to ensure visibility and stakeholder involvement. The project's communication activities included the development of a comprehensive communication strategy and the creation of detailed guidelines, tools and templates. Partners received customized support to meet their individual communication needs, while a dedicated project website was created to host content, articles and resources. A project video was produced by AViTeM with all partner countries to showcase the collective results, while close coordination with AE and OCU ensured the development of effective communication tools for the hub showcase and REPper Factory. During the year, partners actively participated in coordination meetings, ensuring alignment with the Interreg Euro-Med governance framework. ANATOLIKI took a leading role in carbon footprint reporting, collecting and analyzing data to measure the project's environmental impact and suggest mitigation measures. These reports reinforced the project's commitment to sustainability. The first year of the REPper project was marked by strong cooperation, the establishment of core elements for repair centers, the development of capacity building structures, and significant efforts to promote behavioral change. Through effective governance, strategic dissemination, and environmental awareness, the project has laid the foundation for further progress in strengthening the repair economy in Europe. During this reporting period, the Metropolitan City of Bologna (MCBO), as Lead Partner, continued to provide guidance at European and local levels. Monthly online coordination meetings were organized with all partners, complemented by thematic sessions on communication (coordinated by AVITEM). In April 2025, MCBO represented REPper at the Interreg Euro-MED Innovative Sustainable Economy Community of Practice meeting in Sarajevo, and engaged in working groups related to resource valorisation. Together with AULEDA, MCBO co-organized the in-person partner meeting held in Vlorë, Albania, on June 3–4, 2025, where partners shared progress on work packages, deliverables, and next steps. WP1 Under the coordination of E-Zavod, partners advanced in the design and testing of the hubs’ network of pilot services, aimed at supporting new enterprises in the repair sector. Regular communication ensured alignment and timely delivery of outputs, while joint progress updates were supported by the continuous collection of hub characteristics, service testing results, and assessments. Knowledge exchange among partners and stakeholders was particularly valuable, especially the sharing of best practices and lessons learned across territories. As part of the hub-related activities, the project’s workshop series continued with the third online seminar in May 2025, focusing on empowering consumers with data to evaluate when repair is preferable to replacement. Partners agreed to continue the cycle with a fourth seminar in November 2025. In addition, an inventory of actions and materials created within the hubs is being consolidated to showcase practical solutions and inspire replication. WP2 As Work Package 2 leader, ANATOLIKI coordinated the consortium’s efforts to strengthen capacities for the repair economy. A key milestone was the development of the Joint Training First-aid Kit (Deliverable 2.2.3), created by Altroconsumo with the support of ANATOLIKI and other partners. ANATOLIKI ensured alignment across the partnership by providing guidelines and templates, and by preparing the ground for Deliverable 2.2.4 on pilot trainings and the cross-regional training assessment. Thanks to the support of DECO, partners updated and consolidated the list of key stakeholders, ensuring that local professionals, SMEs, start-ups, vocational training centres, and schools are properly engaged. This process contributes to embedding repair and circular economy concepts into local training circuits and curricula. A plan for local factory running and trainings was also established, paving the way for practical skill-building in the upcoming semester. WP3 During this period, the REPper project advanced activities in behavioural change, aiming to promote repair practices among citizens. The Nudge Piloting activity was centrally coordinated by MCBO to ensure a coherent approach across all participating regions. A common methodology and template were developed and shared with partners to guide the design and local implementation of nudges. Pilot activities are scheduled to launch in summer 2025, targeting diverse audiences such as tourists, residents, and young people, with first results expected in Thessaloniki in October 2025. The project started discussing during the Consortium Meeting activities to mobilize multi-sectoral and multi-level decision makers foreseen for the next semester, including the organisation of focus groups and roundtables to foster stakeholder engagement and policy support.

The project aims to decrease WEEE from consumer products by scaling up citizen repair initiatives through the use of digital tools: digital tools that stimulate and facilitate citizen repair by collecting repair solutions and making them easily accessible to citizens. The tools will target both consumers at home and more skilled repairers in repair cafés. They will also map and guide citizens towards high-quality professional repair services, and assemble, with the help of designers, a database of 3D-printing designs (open-source) for printing replacement parts. The objective is to decrease WEEE by 13,5 T per year in pilot repair cafés and by 175 T per year in the pilot cities. The project’s main output will be an integrated approach to supporting citizen repair: a digital infrastructure that supports self-repair, repairing together (in repair cafés or repair centers), and repairing with professional support. To sustain this infrastructure beyond the project lifetime, business and policy models will be developed with a view to setting up a European Open Repair Data Platform.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The project is aligned with the European Green Deal provisions about fostering the use of refurbished sustainable products in the EU, boosting circular business models in both production and consumption processes and empowering consumers towards a green transition. Based on the provisions and indications offered by the Circular Economy Action Plan (OJ C 465, 17.11.2021, which is part of the EU Industrial Strategy), products on the EU market have to be created to last longer, to be easier to reuse, repair and recycle.
    • The REPper project contributes to this objective by promoting a behavioral change towards the repair economy among consumers (including the youngest), and by supporting at the same time the development of creative and green entrepreneurship in the repair sector. Also, as auspicated and supported by the European Commission, REPper hubs will act as focal points for new entrepreneurs, existing SMEs and consumers increased knowledge & awareness about reparability and durability of products to help them make environmentally sustainable choices, both from the side of demand and offer creation. As only 12% of secondary materials and resources are being brought back into the economy (Circular Economy Action Plan, EU 2020), the project focuses on the great potential of the repair economy to fight climate change and achieve climate-neutrality by 2050, by strengthening at the same time economic competitiveness and support a full transition towards circular economy at territorial level.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 the REPper project focuses on key issues addressed by the agenda as general overarching objectives, namely the “Just Europe” and the “Green Europe”, and its specifically aiming at fostering the culture of repair as key element for local and regional strategies supporting a green transition towards circular economy. The project is coherent and intend to provide a clear contribution towards the actual achievement of the main aims of the territorial agenda: by supporting innovation about circular economy processes and policies at local/regional level, by encouraging and strengthening capacity building in the field of repair, as well as to support entrepreneurship and new business development within the territories involved. In particular, by supporting the creation of a Transnational network of REPper Hubs in partner Regions, including IPA, the project is also fully coherent with the Agenda's aim about implementing and fostering transnational cooperation between territories and governments of the EU and neighborhood Regions.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion the project concurs in achieving the expected results and main objective of Pillar 3 of the strategy, namely to improve environmental quality by reducing waste flows. The Adriatic and Ionian Region is vulnerable to  climate change, then there is a strong need to make it more resilient to such changes, limiting the carbon footprint and uses of resources. The strategy is embedded in the activities implemented by the project partners linked to the areas of interest of the strategy, namely Italy, Greece, Slovenia and Albania. Involvement  of a wide range of stakeholders in the project implementation, will further contribute to an integrated approach and cross-cutting issues: capacity building and communication can contribute to raise awareness and to align policies and to improve implementation where relevant, as well as boosting new sustainable business models. There is  a clear need for sharing existing knowledge and developing cooperation network and exchange platforms.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion thanks to the cooperation of the three project partners based within the Alpine area (Italy, France and Slovenia), the project contributes to achieving the thematic policy area’ objective 1 - fostering sustainable growth and promoting innovation in the Alps: from theory to practice, from research centers to enterprises - and in the cross-cutting policy area - governance, including Institutional Capacity. In WP3, the project aims at mobilizing relevant multi-sectoral and multi-level decision makers to increase their knowledge and awareness about the repair sector potentialities to both protecting the environment and facilitating green entrepreneurship at territorial level; in particular, decision makers will be engaged in discussion about how to foster repair solutions through green public procurement (GPP) and how repair economy should be included into existing Smart Specialization Research and Innovation Policies. The approach proposed by the REPper project will contribute to fostering an innovative economic development of the Alpine Region by balancing growth and environmental sustainability. To sum up, the REPper project will build of the following priorities of thematic policy area’ objective 1: developing innovation and research capacity and transfer into practice; improving and developing support for enterprises and promoting high levels of employment in the Region.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin REPper, although not focusing on the blue economy, will contributes to the WestMed Initiative, promoting sustainable growth and jobs, and in particular to the axis 3 "Sustainable consumption and production", pushing new business models and supporting new entrepreneurship, and 4 "Skills development and circulation". The project will facilitate cooperation and coordination also with Mediterranean neighborhood countries; AViTeM, which has in its mission to facilitate cooperation in the Euro-Med area, will act as an activator.
    • Other RIGHT TO REPAIR -
    • In 2021 the EC announced a legislative proposal on the "right to repair" by the end of 2022 under the European Green Deal, and as part of the Circular Economy Package II. The project is aligned with the priorities of the EC having acknowledged the growing importance of this branch of circular economy and the need to encourage consumers to use products for longer, by repairing defective goods and by purchasing more secondhand and refurbished items. Normally, goods and objects out of guarantee are not considered “repairable”, often because of the high costs of reparability that fall on the consumer, whom prefers to spend more to have a brand-new object. The REPper project aims at supporting an increased awareness about the benefit ad potentials of repairing among citizens, but also to facilitate the conditions for repairment becoming more economically convenient and long lasting.
    • In WP3 the project focuses on changing consumer behavior making repair choices more appealing through nudges, in order to create a new repair culture. As the European Parliament has long been supportive of the consumers’ "right to repair" and better reparability of products, the project is aligned in making it a central focus in the circular economy debate. The resolutions of the Parliament (OJ C 425, 20.10.2021, OJ C 465, 17.11.2021) prepared the way for the topical on the "right to repair" on 7 April 2022/2515(RSP) that concerned the whole lifecycle of products and considers product design, as well as the key ethical principles of production. The project aims as well in this direction and in making the repair economy an opportunity for a further implementation of the circular economy. In the strand of repair economy that's continuously growing, the project in WP3 proposes the organization of 3 transnational festivals during the project lifetime in occasion of the International repair day, established in 2017 and that takes place annually on the 3rd Saturday of October.

In line with these objectives, the project will run a carbon footprint monitoring in WP3, to ensure the project’s environmental sustainability.

The sector of electronic waste is one of the most problematic due to the fact that is rapidly raised also since the COVID-19 pandemic. Instead of discarding electrical and electronic equipment, making it part of a fast-growing waste stream that contains both valuable and hazardous materials, this project aims at preventing waste production by repairing items and trying to reduce its consumption, prolong short product life cycles and stimulate minor repairs.
