Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 066. Waste water collection and treatment compliant with energy efficiency criteria ~ SUMMARY: MedRESOURCE tackles the pressing transnational challenges of water scarcity, land degradation, and waste management in the Mediterranean region. Currently, despite existing standards, WW sludge is still inadequately managed, leading to environmental harm and missed opportunities for resource recovery. MedRESOURCE will develop and demonstrate four innovative solutions to reuse wastewater sludge to enhance soil fertility, reduce environmental pollution, and foster sustainable agricultural practices. The solutions will be jointly developed by a partnership of 6 organizations from 4 countries: Palestine, Italy, Tunisia and Jordan, supported by institutional and local associated partners. In each country a Living Lab will be set up to further develop existing solutions and to demonstrate their value to local communities, farmers, authorities and policy makers. The expected change from MedRESOURCE is profound: converting waste into valuable resources, the project will significantly improve soil quality, reduce the need for chemical fertilizers, and mitigate pollution. This will enhance agricultural productivity and resilience to climate change, benefiting local farmers, communities, and the environment. Key outputs are: - 4 Living Labs (LL), each demonstrating a specific reuse of WW sludge in agriculture - A capacity building programme carried out in each LL, reaching about 200 beneficiaries - 4 international study visist to the LL, promoting replicability and best practice exchange and reaching from 60 to 120 beneficiaries - Guidelines for policies development, providing references and standards at EU and MED level to integrate and better adapt regulatory frameworks to the innovative potential of WW sludge reuse. MedRESOURCE offers a compelling solution to critical environmental issues, providing tangible benefits and fostering sustainable development across the Mediterranean. Supporting this project means investing in a greener, more resilient future for the region. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Soil and air quality, Sustainable management of natural resources, Water management

Relevant linked projects:

  • MEDISS - ENICBCMED 2014-2020 (EN) | Pilot Plant in Jericho, Palestine: MEDISS developed advanced irrigation systems using blended treated wastewater, fresh water, and saline water from farmer wells. This infrastructure will be further utilized by MedRESOURCE to incorporate treated sludge into composting processes, improving soil quality and fertility.
  • Pilot Plant in Sardinia, Italy: MEDISS’s pilot plant demonstrated the efficacy of ammonia stripping from livestock waste, producing valuable ammonium sulfate fertilizer and soil improver. MedRESOURCE will scale up this technology, optimizing the production of these fertilizers to reduce nitrate pollution and enhance nutrient recycling. This will be achieved through partnerships with local agricultural agencies and research institutions.
  • Soil Study and Mapping in Jericho Valley: MEDISS conducted comprehensive soil studies and mapping to identify soil salinity and degradation issues. MedRESOURCE will build on these studies by implementing soil rehabilitation measures and integrating treated sludge into compost to enhance soil structure and fertility.
  • Collaborative Platforms and Networks: MEDISS established innovative platforms for data collection on water supply, water quality, soil composition, and crop situations. MedRESOURCE will utilize these platforms to monitor and evaluate the impacts of sludge-derived products on soil and crops, ensuring continuous improvement and adaptation of practices.
  • Capacity Building and Training Modules: MEDISS developed extensive training modules and capacity-building activities for local stakeholders, including farmers, water management authorities, and local organizations. MedRESOURCE will expand these activities, focusing on the sustainable use of treated wastewater sludge, composting, and biochar production, ensuring widespread adoption of best practices.
  • Public Awareness and Acceptance and Cross-border Collaboration. (EN), TRESOR (IEV Transboundary cooperation Itlay-Tunisia 2014-2020) Project duration: 2020-2023 (EN) | - Installation of an integrated hybrid pilot plant: constructed wetlands to improve the quality of secondary reclaimed water used for agricultural irrigation and reed beds for dehydration and stabilization of sludge to be used as fertilizer. Both, water and sludge systems, are connected as the sludge drainage water is partially feeding the hybrid wastewater treatment system. MedRESOURCE will use the existing infrastructure to fully operate the pilots and evaluate their performances. The reed beds will be operated in cooperation with ONAS through and MoU to provide the necessary quantities of sludge.
  • - An improvement of the physicochemical parameters including BOD, COD, TSS is observed and tangible results were obtained. The project will follow up on the monitoring of the constructed wetland during the full operation of the reed beds for sludge drying.
  • As the microbiological quality is still a challenge and is expected to improve through the time, the performances will be studied under these conditions.
  • - Nature-based solution are environmentally friendly and fit with circular economy. They do not require skills for maintenance and are easy to manage. An evaluation of the cost of operation of both pilots will be performed built on data acquired during the previous project. A socio-economic study will be realized including acceptance of farmers and potential adoption of the technology.
  • - Training material on nature-based solutions available for potential users including previous participants to training sessions and visitors from 24 governorates in Tunisia. MedRESOURCE will build on this material to produce comprehensive material to be distributed to potential beneficiaries, target population and possible participants in meetings and dissemination/training events. (EN), Sustainable Sludge Management in Jordan (GIZ) (EN) | - Biochar Production from Sludge: method to produce biochar from wastewater sludge, which is effective in improving soil fertility and structure. MedRESOURCE will upscale this innovation, integrating biochar production into its processes and conducting comparative studies to evaluate the benefits of biochar alone and combined with activated sludge and organic fertilizers.
  • - Training Modules and Capacity Building: comprehensive training to local farmers and stakeholders on the safe use of treated sludge and biochar. MedRESOURCE will build upon these training modules, expanding capacity-building activities to include new best practices and innovations in sludge management, ensuring that local communities are well-equipped to implement these sustainable practices.
  • - Stakeholder Engagement Strategies: engagement of local farmers, the Ministry of Water and Irrigation, and the Water Authority of Jordan. MedRESOURCE will continue this engagement, fostering strong relationships with these stakeholders to ensure smooth implementation and widespread adoption of sludge reuse practices.
  • - Monitoring and Evaluation Systems: systems for monitoring the environmental and agronomic impacts of sludge and biochar use. MedRESOURCE will utilize and enhance these systems to continuously evaluate the effectiveness of its interventions, ensuring data-driven decision-making and adaptation of practices.
  • - Policy Recommendations: policy recommendations for integrating sludge reuse into national agricultural and environmental policies. MedRESOURCE will further these efforts by advocating for the adoption of these policies, providing evidence from its expanded implementation to support regulatory changes.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other MedRESOURCE demonstrates significant synergies and complementarities with major strategies and initiatives aligned with the Interreg NEXT MED Programme. A key synergy is with the European Green Deal, which aims to make Europe climate-neutral by 2050. MedRESOURCE supports this goal by promoting sustainable water and sludge management practices, reducing environmental pollution, and enhancing soil health, thus contributing to climate resilience in the Mediterranean region. Additionally, the project aligns with the EU Circular Economy Action Plan, which seeks to foster sustainable resource use and reduce waste. By converting wastewater sludge into valuable products like compost, biochar, and soil improvers, MedRESOURCE exemplifies circular economy principles, reducing the need for landfill disposal and minimizing resource wastage. This not only addresses local environmental challenges but also supports broader EU objectives of resource efficiency and sustainability. MedRESOURCE also complements the Horizon 2020 initiative, particularly its focus on research and innovation in water management and sustainable agriculture. The project’s collaborative approach, involving research institutions and higher education organizations, fosters innovation and knowledge transfer, aligning with Horizon 2020’s objectives to enhance scientific knowledge and develop innovative solutions. Furthermore, MedRESOURCE aligns with the Union for the Mediterranean’s Water Agenda, which promotes integrated water resource management across the region. By establishing Living Labs and promoting best practices in sludge reuse, the project supports regional cooperation and capacity building, essential components of the UfM Water Agenda.
    • MedRESOURCE not only aligns with these major strategies but demonstrates practical, scalable solutions to shared environmental challenges, promoting active collaboration and knowledge exchange among project partners, stakeholders, and existing networks.
