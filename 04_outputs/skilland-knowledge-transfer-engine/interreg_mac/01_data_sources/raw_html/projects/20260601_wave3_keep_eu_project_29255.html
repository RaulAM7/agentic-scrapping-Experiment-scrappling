Enhancing MED sustainable cultural tourism through the creation of eco-itineraries inside European Cultural Routes

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Cultural Tourism is a key asset in MED economies, however it has a strong, mutually detrimental relationship with climate change when tourism industries do not effectively incorporate sustainable practices. The MED area therefore needs to strategically re-shape its cultural tourism offer by matching its focus on local cultural heritage with greater sustainability. Cultural Routes of the Council of Europe (CRs) represent a key instrument to sustain and promote cultural tourism all over Europe, from mainstream destinations to small-scale local contexts. MEDRoutes overall objective is therefore to establish and disseminate a local-based, low-impact model of sustainable tourism across CRs in the MED area, in particular, to establish a specific model of Mediterranean "slow tourism" rooted in the common heritage of 4 CRs. To tackle the project general objective, MED-Routes’ approach is rooted in a TRANSFER rationale: -transfer mechanisms are put into place to cross-reference the most relevant, field-tested outputs from INCIRCLE & EMbleMatic projects for the design of eco-itineraries; -transfer mechanisms are further encouraged in application to MED-Routes experience through capacity building and institutional networking, especially in WP4. By exploiting Cultural Routes’ plural and transnational dimension, MED-Routes will encourage the diffusion of its eco-itinerary model in the context of other CRs and by the initiative of similar institutional actors transnationally. The combination of all these elements defines transfer & capitalization as the core assets in MED-Routes’ approach in an innovative perspective for sharing the main outputs: -Transnational Strategy for sustainable tourism & eco-itineraries in the MED area -Green Label of Mediterranean CRs for the certification of eco-itineraries -MED-Routes Action Plans outlining key actions to apply the Transn. Strategy into CRs’ agenda.

Description (FR):
Le tourisme culturel est un atout essentiel des économies méditerranéennes, qui entretien une relation en même temps forte mais mutuellement préjudiciable avec le changement climatique lorsque les industries du tourisme n'appliquent pas des pratiques durables. L'espace MED doit donc remodeler sa strategie d’offre touristique culturelle en mettant l’accent sur la durabilité du patrimoine culturel. Les Itinéraires Culturels du Conseil de l'Europe (IC) représentent un instrument clé pour soutenir et élancer le tourisme culturel en Europe, toutes destinations incluses, grandes et petites. L'objectif global de MED-Routes est donc d'établir et de diffuser un modèle de tourisme durable à faible impact et à base locale à travers les IC de l'espace MED et d'établir un modèle spécifique de 'slow tourism' méditerranéen, enraciné dans le patrimoine commun des 4 IC. Pour atteindre son objectif, l'approche du Projet est ancrée à une logique de TRANSFERT: - des mécanismes de transfert sont établis pour croiser les résultats les plus pertinents des projets INCIRCLE et EMbleMatic avec la conception des éco-itinéraires; - des mécanismes de transfert sont encouragés par le renforcement des capacités et la mise en réseau institutionnelle, spécialement en WP4. En exploitant la dimension pluraliste et transnationale des IC, MED-Routes encouragera la diffusion du modèle d'éco-itinéraires dans le contexte d'autres IC et par les initiatives d'acteurs institutionnels similaires et transnationaux. Ces éléments associés font du transfert et de la capitalisation les atouts principaux de l'approche de MED-Routes pour une perspective innovante de partage des résultats: -une stratégie transnationale pour le tourisme durable et les éco-itinéraires dans l'espace MED -un label vert des IC méditerranéens pour la certification des éco-itinéraires -des plans d'action pour les MED-Routes décrivant les actions clés pour appliquer la Stratégie Transnational dans l'agenda des IC.

Actual Achievements (EN):
In PR1, MED-Routes successfully initiated activities to build awareness among tourism stakeholders and cultural services providers about the potential of sustainable tourism and increase understanding of how circular economy principles can be effectively applied to the cultural tourism sector in the MED region: -State-of-the-Art Research (A1.1): This task involved a comprehensive mapping of existing eco-friendly and sustainable tourism activities across the targeted regions. Each partner contributed an integrated report detailing local policies, best practices, and key stakeholders. The result was the production of Integrated Maps for the development of the local circular tourism sector (D.1.1.1), outlining the current state of circular tourism practices across the participating regions and opportunities for further development. The task was coordinated by BATTI, which was also in charge of finalizing and delivering the Integrated Maps. -Preliminary analysis for the transferability of INCIRCLE and EMBLEMATIC Outputs (A1.2): One of MED-Routes' key strategies is to transfer successful methodologies from previous EU-funded projects INCIRCLE and EMBLEMATIC. In PR1, under LIBERTAS guidance, PPs conducted an in-depth analysis of these projects’ outputs, focusing on their applicability to MED-Routes’ goals. The result was a comprehensive Catalogue of outputs to be transferred and capitalized from INCIRCLE and EMBLEMATIC (D.1.2.1), detailing tools such as the INCIRCLE Replicating Package and EMBLEMATIC Guidelines adapted for the creation of eco-itineraries and circular tourism models. -Cross-Route Analysis and Transnational Working Group (A1.3): Under BATTI supervision, a Transnational Working Group (TWG) was established to address the specific needs of each participating region, focusing on merging circular economy practices with cultural heritage valorization. PPs collaborated closely, meeting also in Varna (BG), using the INCIRCLE replicating package to explore solutions in waste management, water management, sustainable mobility, and renewable energy sources. A significant milestone was the development of the Transnational Working Group Toolkit (D.1.3.1), which will guide future activities and help embed circular economy practices into the cultural tourism offerings of the four CRs. -Carbon Footprint Calculation & Monitoring (A1.5 - in progress): BATTI designed a dedicated calculation tool to assess the carbon footprint of project activities, which was filled by PPs for PR1, providing relevant data for continuous evaluation of the project’s environmental impact. -Synergies with EURO-MED Projects (A4.4 - in progress): To enhance cross-project cooperation, MED-Routes established a monitoring committee composed of 1 representative per PP to identify relevant EURO-MED projects for fostering synergies, dialogue and knowledge exchange. As for the management & coordination tasks, in PR1 URF (LP) effectively coordinated the technical and financial oversight of the project. Internal communication was actively maintained, ensuring ongoing dialogue among partners and the JS. To facilitate collaboration, a shared Google Drive folder was established, providing PPs with access to project documentation and progress updates. The Project Management Team (PMT) was established and led by URF, ensuring continuous technical and financial monitoring. Additionally, the Management Handbook was delivered, laying the foundation for smooth operations in future periods. The consortium met in person at the KoM in Faenza (IT) on 7/2/24, during which the Steering Committee was established. A second TPM was held in Varna (BG) on 30/5/24, allowing partners to further discuss project progress and achievements. Through this collaborative and well-structured management system, URF ensured the smooth technical and financial progression of the project according to schedule. As for the communication activities, in PR1 INSULEUR (responsible PP) appointed a Project Communication Manager (PCM) to oversee all communication tasks, finalized the Project Communication Strategy and set up the Project Website, provided by the Programme, periodically updated with project news, events, and outputs in cooperation with PPs. All PPs created dedicated sections on their institutional websites to display key project info, logo, and EU co-financing details, while MED-Routes posters were produced and displayed at partners’ premises to enhance project visibility and awareness. In addition, INSULEUR developed a Periodical Communication Monitoring Tool to track communication activities across all partners. Partners contributed to the Result Amplification Strategy by participating in programme events: in particular, on the 10th and 11th of April 2024 the LP, together with INSULEUR’s PCM, participated in the KoM of the Interreg Euro-MED Sustainable Tourism Community in Rome, presenting MED-Routes and establishing contacts with other relevant EURO-MED projects. In its 1st year, MED-Routes made significant progress in integrating sustainability & circular economy principles into cultural tourism across the Mediterranean. Through research, strategic stakeholder engagement, and transnational collaboration, the project successfully carried out key activities to enhance sustainability in CRs: - State-of-the-Art Research (A1.1): PPs conducted a comprehensive assessment of sustainable tourism practices, mapping local policies, best practices & stakeholders, resulting in the Integrated Maps for the Development of the Local Circular Tourism Sector (D.1.1.1), providing insights into circular tourism opportunities. - Transferability of INCIRCLE & EMBLEMATIC Outputs (A1.2): coordinate by LIU, PPs analyzed outputs from INCIRCLE & EMBLEMATIC to assess their relevance for MED-Routes. This led to the Catalogue of Outputs to be Transferred and Capitalized (D.1.2.1), identifying key methodologies for eco-itinerary development. - Cross-Route Analysis and Transnational Working Group (A1.3): A Transnational Working Group (TWG) was formed under BATTI’s supervision, facilitating collaboration on waste management, sustainable mobility, and renewable energy solutions. The group developed the Transnational Working Group Toolkit (D.1.3.1) to support circular economy principles integration into CRs. - Roadmap for CRs (A1.4): PPs collaborated with the four participating CRs to design tailored sustainability strategies, culminating in the Joint Roadmap for Cultural Routes (D.1.4.1). This document provides a structured framework for embedding circular practices in cultural tourism. - Stakeholder Engagement and Participatory Labs (A2.1): Local participatory labs brought together tourism service providers, businesses, and community representatives to discuss sustainable tourism approaches. Insights were compiled in the Report of Consultation Groups (D.2.1.1), offering key recommendations for CRs. - Development of Eco-Itineraries (A2.2): A Transnational Workshop in Pafos allowed PPs to co-design 2 eco-itineraries per CR, applying circular principles and methodologies from previous EU projects. The outcomes were documented in the Report of the Joint Transnational Workshop (D.2.2.1) and the EMBLEMATIC MED-Routes Transferability Guidelines (D.2.2.2). - Green Label for CRs (A2.3 - ongoing): Work began on developing a Green Label for eco-itineraries, which will certify their sustainability. This label aims to increase visibility, encourage responsible tourism, and motivate stakeholders to adopt eco-friendly practices. - Synergies with EURO-MED Projects (A4.4 - ongoing): A monitoring committee was established to foster collaboration with relevant EURO-MED initiatives. MED-Routes representatives actively participated in multi-project seminars and clustering events, enhancing knowledge exchange and cross-project synergies. - Carbon Footprint Calculation and Monitoring (A1.5 - Ongoing): Documents and tools for assessing activities’ carbon footprint have been established. PPs will consistently update them until the project's completion. Concerning Management & Coordination, during PR1 & PR2 PPs were guided by the LP through the financial & technical reporting process, ensuring the timely submission of reports and certification of expenditures. The consortium met in person at the KoM in Faenza (IT) on 7/2/24, where the StC was established. A 2nd TPM was held in Varna (BG) on 30/5/24, allowing PPs to further discuss project progress and achievements, while on 5/11/2024 the 3rd TPM was held in Pafos (CY), to discuss the state of the project, activities’ implementation, and technical/financial reporting, ensuring alignment with project objectives. As for the communication activities, INSULEUR developed a comprehensive communication strategy focusing on promoting sustainable cultural tourism across the Mediterranean, through multimedia, stakeholder engagement, and transnational collaboration to raise awareness and encourage policy change. In addition, INSULEUR provided a Periodical Communication Monitoring Tool to collect information on partner-led communication activities, ensuring monitoring & overall coherence of the project’s dissemination efforts. To enhance project visibility, MED-Routes posters were designed for display at partners’ premises. Additionally, a social media presence was established, with a Facebook account created and managed according to a structured social media calendar and guidelines produced by INSULEUR to ensure consistent outreach. The project website was delivered and approved at the end of PR1. Throughout the second project period, the website was regularly updated with news, ensuring continuous engagement with stakeholders and the public. Throughout PR1 & PR2, MED-Routes demonstrated the added value of transnational cooperation in advancing circular economy principles within cultural tourism, paving the way for more sustainable & resilient tourism models across the Mediterranean. In its third semester of activities, MED-Routes made steady progress in embedding sustainability and circular economy principles into cultural tourism throughout the Mediterranean region. On May 19th and 20th, 2025 the Transnational Project Meeting (TPM) was held in Castellòn de la Plana, Spain. All activities to be conducted during this reporting period and the next one were discussed. Regarding WP1, throughout the semester, PPs continued to fill in the carbon footprint calculation and monitoring tool timely and carefully, accordingly to A1.5. Regarding WP2, the consortium completed all activities successfully: - Concerning A2.2, PPs developed two fundamental documents to transfer the knowledge of the two Euro-MED projects that MED-Routes is capitalising, EMBLEMATIC and INCIRCLE: D2.2.2 EMBLEMATIC MED-Routes transferability guidelines for the creation of eco-itineraries, and D2.2.3 MED-Routes Package for sustainable practices and eco-itineraries in Med Cultural Routes. Building on this knowledge, and on previous activities conducted in the previous semesters, PPs were able to define all eight small-scale eco-itineraries foreseen by the project. - Concerning A2.3, the Green Label was officially finalized and presented to other Cultural Routes of the Council of Europe during the 12th Training Academy on Cultural Routes, held on June 13, 2025, in Torres Vedras, Portugal - Concerning A2.4, each Cultural Route developed a promotional strategy for its eco-itineraries, with the goal of increasing the visibility of the eco-itinerary and consolidating its identity. - Concerning A2.5, the Transnational Strategy for sustainable tourism and eco-itineraries was delivered. The Strategy was developed by integrating 4 project deliverables (D2.2.2, D2.2.3, D2.3.1, D2.4.1), to provide a comprehensive framework for the development, promotion, and potential replication of the MED-Routes eco-itineraries. To ensure broader accessibility, the Strategy was translated into the languages of all partners developing eco-itineraries. Finally, a dedicated Letter of Intent was co-developed and signed by all CRs involved in the project. This document reaffirms their shared commitment to adopting the Strategy as a reference framework within their policies. Regarding WP3, during this reporting period, partners developed training materials for the dissemination of eco-itineraries (A3.1). Regarding WP4, during this reporting period, partners worked on some activities: - Concerning Activity 4.1, PPs and APs started to plan how to build the MED-Routes Action Plans in the next reporting period; - Concerning Activity 4.2, PPs started to plan the Forums they will hold next semester; - Concerning Activity 4.3, PPs presented the MED-Routes approach and products during the Training Academy of the Cultural Routes taking place in Torres Vedras (PT) from June, 11th to June, 13th 2025. - Concerning A4.4, PPs attended online meetings with the Euro-MED community and planned activities for cross fertilization with other projects. Lastly, PPs put efforts in communicating the project, in accordance with the Communication Strategy. During the last semester, MED-Routes clearly showcased the benefits of transnational collaboration in promoting circular economy approaches within the cultural tourism sector, setting the foundations for more sustainable and resilient tourism models throughout the Mediterranean.

  • (FR) | TouriSMEshare explores the potential of sharing economy for the growth of European SMEs and Social Enterprises (SE) in sustainable tourism and cultural heritage-related sectors. It promotes those tourism services with lower environmental and socio-cultural impacts, for a higher involvement of tourists in natural and cultural capital preservation.
  • In particular, the project focuses on matching demand &supply for sustainable tourism experiences in the context of sharing economy. From the demand perspective, tourists have wider and wider access to internet, smartphones and tablets which are used to seek individual and authentic tourism experiences; on the supply side, Tourism Destinations, Cultural Institutions and especially SMEs have a great potential to provide sustainable tourism services. Sharing economy is the core approach adopted by the project to support local development, especially in rural marginal areas and small islands, exploiting their natural and cultural heritage – both tangible and intangible -, supporting job creation and valorizing local traditions.
  • MED-Routes will look at the best practices produced and collected by TouriSMEshare in the field of collaborative economy in the tourism sector, with the aim to derive useful suggestions on how to interconnect the key businesses and services that will constitute the new eco-itineraries for the sake of local cultural heritage valorization.

  • (FR) | The CLIC project applies circular economy principles to cultural heritage adaptive reuse for achieving environmentally, socially, culturally and economically sustainable urban/territorial development.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The EU Green Deal states that energy transition towards greater sustainability should involve and benefit all consumers; it also affirms that the ecologic transition is an opportunity to expand sustainable and job-intensive economic activity, particularly in the area of circular economy for the creation of new activities and jobs. The proposal is in line with this vision as it emphasizes virtuous practices in sustainable and circular economy at local level to sustain local economies and generate benefits for local communities
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The proposal fits the strategies set by the Territorial Agenda 2030 in the sector of Nature, landscape and cultural heritage. In particular, the project objectives match the key principles set by the agenda under the following points: (a) Fostering better balanced territorial development utilising Europe’s diversity, and (b) Encouraging decision makers at all governance levels to unleash the unique potential of territories through integrated and cooperative approaches.
    • Furthermore, the proposal adopts the vision of the Territorial Agenda 2030 in application to CIRCULAR ECONOMY expressed in its goal to support Europe’s transition to a circular model through the development of place-based economic symbiosis processes, which are fostered by the creation process of eco-itineraries encompassing various sectors – transport, hospitality, services, waste managements, etc. The proposal also matches the agenda’s vision of strengthening innovation capacities inside regions, applying it to the multiple areas that make up the tourism sector.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The EUSAIR strategy presents a specific pillar dedicated to Sustainable Tourism.
    • The proposal fully shares the vision of the following specific objectives defined by EUSAIR:
    • -Diversification of the macro-region’s tourism products and services along with tackling seasonality of inland, coastal and maritime tourism demand.
    • -Improving the quality and innovation of tourism offer and enhancing the sustainable and responsible tourism capacities of the tourism actors across the macro-region.
    • In particular, the proposal contributes to the EUSAIR objectives by setting up a diversified tourism offer in the targeted territories that has a low impact on both the human and natural environment, re-orienting tourism flows from overcrowded, high-peak seasonal destinations to less conventional landscapes whose cultural heritage needs greater valorization. The proposal would thus contribute to the EUSAIR by fostering a more sustainable and responsible tourism management.
    • Other When it comes to facilitating a framework aimed to ensure that businesses integrate environmental and socio-cultural considerations, the EU Strategy for Sustainable Development assigns a key role to both to public policy and networks of businesses, who should coordinate their efforts with a joint, pro-active approach to expand sustainable development practices at many different levels. The proposal aims to establish a cross-sectoral cooperation for tackling challenges that affect multiple actors in the tourism sector, which is one of the most contaminated and multi-faceted in contemporary economies.
    • In particular, target-engaging activities across different WPs and culminating in WP4 are oriented to establish a systematic dialogue among representatives of different stakeholders, with the final aim to make sure that adopted policy proposals and strategic documents for local sustainable tourism respond to the multi-faceted needs of the tourism sector.
    • Other The EU Strategy for Sustainable Tourism attributes an utmost importance to the tourism sector as a cross-cutting economic activity with a wide-ranging impact on both the environment and the economy.
    • The proposal operates deeply within this twofold potential and particularly operates to follow the directives of the Strategy to include tourism as a horizontal priority in regional authorities’ operational programmes, with a particular focus on sustainable tourism action plans at national / regional level in consultation with stakeholders and civil society and in line with a future European roadmap for sustainable tourism.
    • Furthermore, the proposal highly valorizes the strategic characteristics of culture and cultural heritage in European tourism as suggested by the Strategy, which stressed the need to allocate sufficient funding to culture and cultural heritage sites through sustainable models that fight climate change and overtourism. The Strategy also clearly states the need to study the resilience of cultural heritage, as well as the liaison between sustainable tourism and cultural heritage, particularly within the framework of the Cultural Routes established by the Council of Europe. CoE’s Cultural Routes are indeed described as a key asset in highlighting Europe’s diverse history, promoting cultural heritage and ultimately for the valorization of small businesses through intercultural dialogue and transnational cooperation. The proposal fully adopts all such principles and works to implement the Strategy’s vision by connecting tourism promotion, cultural heritage preservation and the consolidation of local sustainable economies.
    • Other The European Commission’s 2022 Transition Pathway for Tourism details key actions, targets and conditions to achieve the green and digital transitions and long-term resilience of the tourism sector at EU level. In particular, the document highlights key areas in which strategic measures need to be taken, in particular:
    • - Investment in circularity to reduce energy, waste, water and pollution, and at the same time to better meet the increasing demand for sustainable tourism;
    • - Investment in skills to ensure the availability of qualified workforce and attractive careers in the ecosystem;
    • - Enhancing data sharing practices to allow for new innovative tourism services and improve the sustainable management of destinations.
    • MED-Routes contributes to the set of actions designed by the Transition Pathaway by carrying out detailed assessments in a plurality of economic areas within the local tourism sector, thus identifying the main fields where stakeholders should act, to accelerate the transition and promote more resilience in the tourism ecosystem from the micro-sphere up to higher levels in the national tourism industries. The project also responds to the identified need of skills investment by the means of the capacity-building activities targeted to the socio-economic operators acting in the tourism services through sustainable, future-oriented practices.
