Mediterranean Pilot Actions for Climate Change Adaptation

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 060. Adaptation to climate change measures and prevention and management of climate related risks: others, e.g. storms and drought (including awareness raising, civil protection and disaster management systems, infrastructures and ecosystem based approaches) ~ SUMMARY: The MedPROACT project aims to enhance the adaptive capacity of Mediterranean coastal communities to climate change by focusing on ecosystem resilience and sustainable management of marine resources. This initiative involves a transnational consortium of environmental NGOs, universities, and research institutions from Italy, Turkey, Jordan, and Tunisia. The activities are organised into three technical work packages that address, through demonstrated solutions and tested pilot actions, various aspects of climate change adaptation such as the management of invasive species, restoration of specific marine ecosystems, and development of local climate adaptation tools. Expected outcomes: - the adaptive capacity of coastal communities connected to fishery and seafood consumption is increased by promoting knowledge and strategies that address the invasive species proliferation, reducing pressure on traditional stocks and increasing scientific literacy; - improved ecosystem resilience of seagrass meadows and coral reefs through restoration and sustainable management practices taught to local communities, including the decision makers of marine protected areas, in order to allow the maintenance of their crucial role in carbon sequestration, coastal protection, and habitats supporting marine life; - enhanced community resilience: by increasing awareness of the local population and developing effective adaptation policies through participative processes, the project aims to equip the local communities and authorities with effective Adaptation Plans and Guidelines, governance tools that will safeguard people, activities, tourists and livelihoods, especially connected to marine and coastal resources. In summary, MedPROACT leverages a comprehensive, multilevel approach to build resilience against climate change in Mediterranean coastal communities and benefit local actors such as fishers, tourism operators, scientific community, governmental bodies, youths. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Agriculture and fisheries and forestry, Climate change and biodiversity, Coastal management and maritime issues

  • PLASTIC-BUSTERS INITIATIVE: for a Mediterranean free from litter (UfM) (EN) | The UfM labelled Plastic Busters Initiative (Led by UNISI) kick-started in 2013 to address scientific and policy gaps existing at the time in the Mediterranean region. Since then, improvements have been implemented on the science policy interface, with Plastic Busters contributing to the regional policy context as well as being informed by it. The initiative entailed actions that addressed the entire management cycle of marine litter, from monitoring to prevention and mitigation actions at basin scale. MedPROACT will capitalise the setting up of methodologies for bioindicator species and the multi-stakeholder approach fostered by Plastic Busters Initiative through the wide network enforced in the last decade among the Euro-mediterranean region.

The marine litter monitoring approach of Plastic Busters MPAs will be refined, expanded and extended in order to address the need for monitoring multiple stressors in marine ecosystems and evaluating their impact on marine biodiversity of Mediterranean coastal and marine PAs. The aim of this web platform is also to promote the elimination of any form of packaging (non-use packaging). (EN)

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other MedPROACT aims to support the development and implementation of EU and UN policies, focusing on Goal 13 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) from the 2020 Agenda 2030. This goal urges crucial actions to combat climate change and its impacts through effective and equitable adaptation and mitigation actions, reducing vulnerability and enhancing climate resilience. Goals 14 and 15 aim to conserve oceans, seas, and marine resources, and to protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial and marine ecosystems. These goals are central to MedPROACT's pilot actions as well.
    • Furthermore, the project supports the implementation of the Marine Strategy Framework Directive (MSFD), specifically targeting biodiversity maintenance, prevention of adverse alterations by non-indigenous species, ensuring healthy populations of commercial fish and shellfish, and maintaining sea floor integrity. Additionally, it aligns with the EU Green Deal's strategy on climate change adaptation, emphasising climate-proofing, resilience building, prevention, and preparedness.
    • MedPROACT is in harmony with the EU Strategy on Adaptation to Climate Change and the UNEP/MAP-Barcelona Convention, particularly focusing on the Regional Climate Change Adaptation Framework for the Mediterranean Marine and Coastal Areas (RCCAF), aiming to enhance resilience in the Mediterranean region's natural and socio-economic systems.
    • The project's results and outputs will contribute directly to the implementation of UfM 2030GreenerMed, endorsed by 42 UfM Member Countries. This agenda promotes a transition to a green, circular, and socially inclusive economy, emphasising sustainable consumption and production practices and Nature-based Solutions (NBS) as well as the protection, preservation, management, and restoration of natural resources in the Mediterranean region through an integrated ecosystem approach covering terrestrial, marine, and coastal dimensions.
