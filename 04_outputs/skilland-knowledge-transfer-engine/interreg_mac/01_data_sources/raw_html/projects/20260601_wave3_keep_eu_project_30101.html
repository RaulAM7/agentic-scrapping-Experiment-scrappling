Increasing capacities of PTO and PTA in the EURO MED area by testing novel solutions to effectively manage public transport’s transition towards energy-efficiency and resource-efficiency

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
E-MED tests and validates solutions to increase energy-efficiency and resource-efficiency of public transport systems to reduce public transport's environmental footprint drastically, and to react flexibly to changing energy prices caused by the current energy crisis. This challenge affects in particular local public authorities, public transport infrastructure and energy providers, but also industry suppliers of electric vehicles and service operators. Thus, E-MED solutions are developed to increase energy-efficiency and share of Renewable Energy Systems (RES), (e.g., tools for fleet, network planning, driver support, Artificial intelligence supported maintenance of energy systems), and to increase resource-efficiency and resilience, (e.g., re-use of batteries and washing water recycling systems, maintenance of tires, procurement guidelines) support in the evidence-based planning and introduction of e-bus systems to help objective decision-taking and smart planning, investing and procurement from the start. This way, E-MED solutions support the right design of a Public Transport (PT) system through smart organisation and planning of networks and operations, optimised charging concepts, optimal use of engineering technology, and capacity building around more “human” topics like eco-driving. As key enablers to reduce energy consumption and to increase the lifetime of PT fleet and infrastructure, E-MED solutions with focus on end-user needs provide orientation to both buyers and industry. Based on the testing and validation of E-MED solutions, strategies and action plans are optimised in a participatory co-design approach with stakeholders from the public transport and energy community. E-MED is composed of 10 partners ranging from public transport organisations and authorities to cities, as well as knowledge partners renowned in the transport and energy field. To enlarge this cooperation, associated paratners like UITP, University of Lisbon, and CARNET are strategically involved to maximise communication outreach and knowledge transfer of results.

Description (FR):
E-MED teste et valide des solutions pour augmenter l'efficacité énergétique et l'efficacité des ressources des systèmes de transport public afin de réduire l'empreinte environnementale des transports publics et de réagir de manière flexible à l'évolution des prix de l'énergie. Cet enjeu concerne notamment les collectivités locales, les infrastructures de transport public et les fournisseurs d'énergie, mais aussi les industriels fournisseurs de véhicules électriques et les opérateurs de services. Ainsi, des solutions E-MED sont développées pour augmenter l'efficacité énergétique et la part des énergies renouvellables et pour augmenter l'efficacité des ressources et la résilience, soutenant à la planification fondée sur des preuves et à l'introduction de systèmes d'e-bus pour aider à la prise de décision objective et à la planification, à l'investissement et à l'approvisionnement intelligents dès le départ. De cette façon, les solutions E-MED prennent en charge la bonne conception d'un système de PT grâce à une organisation intelligentes des réseaux et des opérations, et un renforcement des capacités autour comme l'éco-conduite. En tant que catalyseurs clés pour réduire la consommation d'énergie et augmenter la durée de vie de la flotte et de l'infrastructure, les solutions E-MED sont axées sur les besoins des utilisateurs finaux fournissent une orientation aux acheteurs et à l'industrie. Sur la base du test et de la validation des solutions E-MED, les stratégies et les plans d'action sont optimisés dans une approche participative de co-conception. E-MED est composé de 10 partenaires allant des organisations aux autorités de transport public ainsi que des partenaires de connaissances reconnus dans le domaine des transports et de l'énergie. Pour élargir cette coopération, des partenaires associés tels que l'UITP, l'Université de Lisbonne et le CARNET sont impliqués afin de maximiser la communication et le transfert de connaissances des résultats.

Actual Achievements (EN):
In the first weeks of Period 1, the E-MED partners prepared the Steering Committee and Partnership agreements, and modified the Application Form in Jems as per JS's pre-contracting instructions. These modifications were officially approved on April 17, 2024. The kick-off meeting took place in Barcelona on February 28, 2024, with representatives from each partner. Two additional General Assembly meetings (April 17 and June 19, 2024) were held to support project implementation, engage partners, and plan future activities. Partners set up coordination tools for online communication and project management (Basecamp, Google Meet, M. SharePoint) and created a project website and LinkedIn channel using the JS-provided template. Activities 1.1 and 1.2 of WP1 consisted of: - Conceptualizing the toolkit with integrated network-, vehicle-, and charging facility-level capabilities (all partners). - Defining ICT platform requirements to integrate Geographic Information Systems with modeling capabilities (CERTH, Theta). - Enhancing the vehicle-level planning tool by IDIADA, with AI-powered chat support for electric bus inquiries using IDIADA's data (IDIADA, CERTH, UPC). - Conducting a market analysis of charging-facility planning tools and identifying gaps (TUSGSAL, UPC). - Defining user requirements and toolkit capabilities (related to WP4). - Preparing content for a workshop in Thessaloniki to introduce the WP1 Planning Toolkit. In WP2, the structure and content for the State of the Art analysis based on Eco-driving were initiated in A2.1 (supervised by UM and MOM). The specific advancements were: - Reviewing tools and guidelines for diesel solutions and assessing their adaptation to electric fleets, as well as Eco-driving benefits (Carris, ATB, TUSGSAL, MOM). - Identifying solutions for vehicles, drivers, control centers, and technology (automated, semi-automated, training). - Reviewing monitoring, implementation, planning, and driver reward schemes, along with regulations and requirements. - Developing a questionnaire for the State of the Art analysis at pilot locations (Maribor, Barcelona, Lisbon). In Activity 2.2, UPC (Leader) and the PTOs of the E-MED project collaborated to analyze operational data from buses captured by each operator's telemetric systems. Sample datasets were obtained from TUSGSAL, Carris and ATB. The partners also undertook the following tasks: - Standardizing the datasets: Developed a code to translate raw data from each operator into a harmonized file format for consistency across cities. - Conceptualizing outputs: Defined data analytics outputs (powered by AI) in three areas: fleet analysis, driver analysis, and vehicle analysis. - Developing algorithms: Coded algorithms in Python for integration into the WP2 Toolkit to perform fleet analysis, focusing on headway and speed variations along routes. The achievements in WP3 (led by TUSGSAL) aligned with the procurement methodology for fleet and infra. (A3.1) and green and circular procurement for services and operations (A3.2). The specific contributions during Period 1 were: - Conducting a benchmark of the current situation through interviews and surveys with PTOs, PTAs, and other partners involved in the project. - Defining the requirements for small to medium pilot tests, incorporating learnings and insights from past procurement processes. - Developing guidelines for green and circular procurement. In addition, all partners collaborated on defining the content for the workshop to be held in Bergamo, focused on vehicle, facility, and green services procurement (Activity 3.4). In Activity 3.5, UPC developed the framework for evaluating the results of feasibility studies and pilot tests, which resulted in the internal publication of Deliverable D3.5.1: Instructions for Conducting Feasibility Studies and Pilot Activities. Finally, the achievements in WP4 (supervised by CERTH) were mainly concentrated in Activities 4.1 and 4.3. These include: - Identifying needs and requirements: Conducted surveys, interviews, and two workshops with Public Transport Operators and Authorities (IDIADA, TUSGSAL, MOM, ATB, Theta, CARRIS, CERTH) while engaging associated parties. This process led to Internal Deliverable 4.1.1: “Joint Requirements for Strategies & Action Plans, Strategic Impact Analysis of Pilots.” - Developing the Communication Strategy: Drafted by Redmint and UPC, currently under review by other partners. - Preparing the Project Amplification Strategy: Integrated knowledge and achievements from other work packages (Redmint, Carris, UPC). A video summarizing key contributions has been published on the E-MED website as part of Deliverable 4.3.4: Promotional Videos. All partners have initiated contact with the stakeholders at each site to develop Action Plans that will integrate E-MED solutions (Activity 4.2). Additionally, the partnership estimated the carbon footprint impact of all activities conducted during Period 1 under Activity 4.4. The project has been making steady progress across multiple work streams, achieving key milestones in assessment, solution mapping, and infrastructure readiness. Two surveys have been successfully launched—one targeting Public Transport Operators (PTOs) and Public Transport Authorities (PTAs), and another focused on bus manufacturers—to gather critical insights for project development. These activities have been subcontracted to EIT Urban Mobility (EIT UM), ensuring expert execution and analysis. Work continues on key deliverables, including DEL 3.5.1 (Developed frameowrk for real life testint, simulations and Feasibility studies) , DEL 4.1.1 (CERTH), and DEL 4.1.2, ensuring alignment with project objectives. Solution owners have mapped available solutions and identified essential capabilities. Additionally, infrastructure investments are in progress, with partners actively working on procurement. Offers have been requested, but delays have been reported in finalizing contracts due to supplier reservations. Pilot activities continue to progress, with updates on their celebration and validation phases. The project consortium has been actively engaged in critical meetings and networking event such as the launch of the Green Living Areas hosted in Torino on the 10th and 11th of 2024. A Consortium Meeting was held in Bergamo in December, fostering knowledge exchange and strategic alignment.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal EU Green Deal sets a vision for a decarbonised Europe and there are milestones in between to ensure all the sectors contributing, including transport (and public transport thereof) are in the right direction. Accordingly, by 2030, there will be at least 100 climate-neutral cities in Europe. To this end, the electrification of bus public transport fleets across Europe, including MED countries, is a major challengeand at the same time a huge opportunity. Many cities/ authorities across Europe have either already deployed e-buses or are in the process of doing so in the near future. E-MED adds to the ongoing development by including the elements of energy efficiency measures to the mix. Technologies like smart charging, integrated tools for monitoring and operations of e-buses, etc. are available off-the-shelf today but adapting these solutions to the local context is a challenge that E-MED addresses paving the way to achieve short-term milestones and objectives.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 Territorial Agenda 2030 recognizes the opportunity that the transport sector offers towards climatic, societal and technological goals. Reliable secondary and local transport networks which link to transnational networks and urban centres are essential for quality of life and for business opportunities. Infrastructural developments for transport are often expensive and that is exactly where efficiency of the operations of e-buses, operating the charging infrastructure intelligently, matters. Territorial frameworks are the building blocks of the very idea of EU and through transnational cooperation in E-MED between different countries and also organizations working in public transport, E-MED contributes to this core principle. On a more strategic level, synergies will be identified and the results are amplified through transnational cooperation through strategic guidelines and action plans developed in WP3 for the innovations that will be tested in WP1 and WP2 which strengthens “A Green Europe” making Europe fit for the digital age, sustainable mobility and a fully integrated European transport network, the transition to a circular economy.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion E-MED supports modernisation of the transport sector to make it more efficient hence more environmentally friendly. E-MED with its project activities around energy-efficiency through circular economy principles and circular energy flows in public transport systems EUSAIR’s strategic goals to develop reliable transport networks and to achieve a well-interconnected and well-functioning internal energy market supporting the three energy policy objectives of the EU – competitiveness, security of supply and sustainability. In addition, EUSAIR’s goal for sustainable growth incl. the need of modernisation of the transport sector (as the Adriatic and Ionian Region faces the same challenge as Central Europe in terms of public transport system modernisation) to make it more efficient hence more environmentally-friendly (e.g. by promoting inter-modality); incl. the production and integration of renewable energy sources into transport systems. Furthermore, E-MED promotes the strategic goal of creating new business opportunities, hence new jobs, through a circular economy perspective for public transport. E-MED’S strategies and pilots/joint solutions can be taken up by the the Adriatic and Ionian Region as policy framework and best practices to foster the transition towards a sustainable circular transport sector.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion Since EUSALP’s geographical scope encompasses one of the most fragile environments, E-MED’s activities in terms of improving the circularity and energy efficiency in public transport contributes to 2nd and 3rd thematic policy area, which cuts across mobility and energy. Furthermore, EUSALP aims at a better overall transport system. E-MED’s ambitious public transport operators and authorities’ test activities, looks at innovations to support the e-bus operations at vehicles, network and at the ecosystem level contributing to enhancing the capabilities for a better overall transport system. Another cross-cutting policy area intends to include enhancing the capacities. WP2 in E-MED focuses specifically on harnessing the transnational cooperation producing the strategic guidelines, action plans, an update of SUMPs to include the new innovations in public transport operations, etc. to give a functional meaning for macro-regional strategy like EUSALP.
    • Other Underlined by emission reduction goals, the EU's Sustainable & Smart Mobility Strategy goes hand in hand with the EU Green Deal. While some EU cities & regions are well in line with these strategic goals also in terms of PT electrification and energy efficiency measures, it is not enough to meet the zero-emission goal of EU which needs also cities in the Mediterranean region to charge forward the testing and implementation in the local context. EU research programmes and initiatives like Horizon Europe (2021-2027) and Connecting Europe Facility (CEF) among others, bring these innovations and new technologies from conception to large-scale business models. Specifically, Cluster 5 of Horizon Europe Programme (Climate, Energy & Mobility) and Mission calls, funds the research and innovation help achieve milsetones of carbon neutrality in 100 cities by 2030. E-MED’s consortium includes public transport operators/authorities in Lisbon, Barcelona, Bergamo and Thessaloniki among these 100 climate-neutral cities mission by 2030 and testing measures that improve circularity and energy efficiency will have a compounding effect in taking them to the rest of the EU. E-MED’s testing activities in public transport, produce evidence-based solutions and also develop capacities making them ready to be taken up by climate-neutral cities and public authorities along the Mediterranean region.
    • Amidst the energy crises brought upon the EU with Russia’s invasion of Ukraine, E-MED’s energy efficiency measures complement the new REPowerEU plan in terms of optimally using the available power to charge the electric bus operations, infusion of circularity to effectively use the available resources the process chain of e-bus operations where possible and strengthening these measures through trans-national cooperation across the Mediterranean region.
