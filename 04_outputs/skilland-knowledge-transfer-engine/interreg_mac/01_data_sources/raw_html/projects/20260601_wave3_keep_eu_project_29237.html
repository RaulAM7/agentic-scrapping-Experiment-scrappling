Promoting Green and Inclusive Mobility hubs for greener MED living areas by leveraging citizens' real needs

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
One of the most crucial challenges in modern Mediterranean urban areas is to reduce traffic congestion and air pollution towards achieving better living environments which are now burdened, given the citizens’ commuting demand, and the increasing seasonal traffic flows. In this context, new, greener, faster and more flexible transportation options with improved connectivity are needed, for the climate-friendly mobility adaptation away from private cars. To effectively promote low-carbon solutions, mobility hubs emerged mainly in Northern/Central Europe, aiming to concentrate public and shared travel modes while providing high-quality users’ experience. However, they are developed in a different context in relation to MED people’s transportation culture and habits, requiring further study and adaptation. Are MED citizens ready to adopt these new mobility habits? How can they been persuaded to change their traffic culture to a more green and efficient way? How familiar are the local policymakers with the efficient implementation of this concept after all? In order to address these question, the project will develop a joint Strategy for green and inclusive mobility hubs for MED (urban and interurban) areas after considering the vision, the pillars, the particular citizens’ real needs, habits and traffic culture. The overarching goal of the holistic approach adopted is to ensure the involvement of stakeholders, the active inclusion of local communities and citizens, while not neglecting the touristic element as well, for the participatory assessment and promotion of mobility hubs towards climate change mitigation. Considering that mobility hubs will be a trend for the next years in Europe, the key outputs of this project will be crucial for mobility hubs’ smooth implementation and consequently for the green transformation of MED areas.

Description (FR):
L’un des défis les plus cruciaux des zones urbaines méditerranéennes modernes est de réduire les embouteillages et la pollution de l’air afin d’obtenir de meilleurs environnements de vie, aujourd’hui mis à rude épreuve compte tenu de la demande de déplacement des citoyens et de l’augmentation des flux de circulation saisonniers. Dans ce contexte, de nouvelles options de transport plus écologiques, plus rapides et plus flexibles, avec une connectivité améliorée, sont nécessaires pour une adaptation à une mobilité respectueuse du climat, loin des voitures particulières. Pour promouvoir efficacement les solutions à faibles émissions de carbone, des pôles de mobilité ont émergé principalement en Europe du Nord et centrale, visant à concentrer les modes de déplacement publics et partagés tout en offrant une expérience utilisateur de haute qualité. Cependant, ils sont développés dans un contexte différent en relation avec la culture et les habitudes de transport des personnes MED, nécessitant une étude et une adaptation plus approfondies. Les citoyens MED sont-ils prêts à adopter ces nouvelles habitudes de mobilité ? Comment peut-on les persuader de changer leur culture de circulation pour une manière plus écologique et plus efficace ? Dans quelle mesure les décideurs politiques locaux sont-ils finalement familiers avec la mise en œuvre efficace de ce concept ? Afin de répondre à ces questions, le projet développera une stratégie commune pour des pôles de mobilité verts et inclusifs pour les zones MED (urbaines et interurbaines) après avoir examiné la vision, les piliers, les besoins réels des citoyens, les habitudes et la culture de la circulation. L'objectif primordial de l'approche holistique adoptée est d'assurer l'implication des parties prenantes, l'inclusion active des communautés locales et des citoyens, sans pour autant négliger l'élément touristique, pour l'évaluation participative et la promotion des pôles de mobilité vers l'atténuation du changement climatique. Considérant que les pôles de mobilité seront une tendance pour les prochaines années en Europe, les principaux résultats de ce projet seront cruciaux pour la mise en œuvre fluide des pôles de mobilité et, par conséquent, pour la transformation verte des zones MED.

Actual Achievements (EN):
GREENMO project is progressing very well so far, with dedicated and engaged active consortium, deliverable already concluded, as well as communication and dissemination achieved. Everyone is bringing high-energy vibe within the consortium, as well as everyone is genuinely interested in the topic which leads to excellent cooperation and always takes the implementation one step further ahead. During the reporting period 1, important pre-contracting actions were made, leading to the finalization of key legal and financial issues/documents, such as Subsidy Contract, Partnership Agreement, Steering Committee written rules, Project Guidebook, Quality Plan. Lead Partner and all partners were actively involved in all project activities which were officially launched by the kickoff meeting that was successfully held in February 2024 in Athens with the participation of all partners. In addition to that, aiming to engage and purposefully “activate” the partners, LP organized an online pre-kickoff meeting on 23.1.24 to bring closer together the partnership before the official kick off. After that, bilateral meetings between the LP and each PP were realized online on 31.1, 2.2 morning, 2.2 afternoon with two PPs who will co-lead specific activities, 12.2. The aim was to discuss in detail and to jointly draft the outline of each activity with the respective Activity Leader (AL). Indeed, Building on the added value of the cooperation achieved through the project, the first deliverable was produced based on the common definition of mobility hubs, the analysis of knowledge gathered, and the input and experience exchanged among partners on the status of their country. The visually appealing handbook "The concept of mobility hubs: Characteristics and Typology of Mobility Hubs in MED Areas" highlights the different types of hubs, the benefits, the stakeholders involved, the peculiarities of med areas and the current state of hubs in the GREENMO partner countries. The final result is a pdf handbook which will be used internally as training material for the participants of the WP2 activities and externally on the GREENMO website and LinkedIn page. It will remain available for the Euro-MED Academy and/or the training sessions within the TCP. All partners started involving main stakeholders in their territories by introducing them to the project and its main objectives and outputs, which is a task that further emphasis will be placed during the next semesters. In parallel, data to monitor the carbon footprint of the project was collected through the template that the Coordinator created in order to insert them to the calculation tool proposed by the EuroMED Programme. Regarding communication objectives and activities, the dissemination pack has been finalized in terms of website, poster, roll up, ppt templates, strategy, Linkedin account. Press releases, website articles and posts have been published both on the project’s website/Linkedin and on the partner’s website. Participation in external events and conferences is a task we are working on. The useful seminars provided for LPs and Communication Officers were attended to get acquainted with the procedures, the requirements, the rules and tips. In parallel, LP’s Communication Manager is part of the EuroMED Communication Officers Network and the Communication Working Group for Green Living Areas. Also, GREENMO representatives participated in the event of GLA Mission to share insights with all mobility projects, while being aware of the cross-fertilization opportunities that the IDP networks/community can provide. GREENMO project has made significant progress during the first year of implementation, laying the groundwork, as promised, for inclusive and sustainable mobility in the Mediterranean region. The efforts to date, have delivered concrete results, fostered valuable cross-border cooperation and engaged a broad spectrum of stakeholders across different levels of governance and society in all participating countries. One of the key achievements of GEENMO in the first year was the successful development of a comprehensive training package on the concept of mobility hubs. This training material, created in the form of a well-designed infographic, explains different typologies and characteristics of hubs relevant to MED areas. It has been widely disseminated to policy-makers, public authorities, and educational institutions and will also be available through the Euro-MED Academy, ensuring continued knowledge sharing and capacity building. In parallel, equally important was the organization of the GREENMO webinar and roundtable, which brought together top-level experts from Mediterranean countries and Northern Europe, including public authorities, research institutions, and private-sector players. The event facilitated a valuable exchange of best practices on governance models for mobility hubs, enhancing awareness of successful implementation strategies. Building on this exchange, the project developed a dedicated insight report on governance structures, offering practical guidance for MED regions aiming to implement or improve such hubs. The project’s inclusive approach has also been a key target. A series of interviews with local and regional authorities, infrastructure providers, NGOs, research institutions, SMEs, and interest groups allowed GREENMO to capture top-down insights into the planning and operation of mobility hubs. At the same time, the project organized participatory workshops in each partner country, directly involving citizens— of all ages, backgrounds and abilities—to ensure their needs are considered in GREENMO strategy. This dual-input process has been essential in shaping a holistic understanding of the current gaps, expectations, and opportunities. Another part of the project has been its commitment to sustainability, demonstrated through detailed monitoring of the project’s own carbon footprint. Reports showed that travel—especially air transport—was the major contributor to emissions. Recommendations were developed to help partners reduce their environmental impact, and further actions are planned to promote greener travel choices. GREENMO has also made solid progress in building interregional networks and strategic partnerships. Through its active engagement in the Interreg Euro-MED Green Living Areas community, participation in European conferences and forums, and visibility through social media and online content, the project has positioned itself as a key player in the transition to more sustainable and inclusive mobility across MED cities. Despite some minor scheduling shifts—such as the rescheduling of the webinar and the slight delay in completing some stakeholder interviews—the project timeline remains on track. These challenges were addressed through effective coordination and adaptive planning without compromising the quality or scope of the deliverables. These achievements showcase the added value of transnational cooperation, enabling partners to learn from each other, address shared challenges, and co-develop strategies that are more impactful than isolated efforts. As GREENMO moves into its second year, it is well-positioned to continue this momentum and contribute meaningfully to the creation of greener, more connected, and people-centered urban mobility systems in the MED region. During the third reporting period, GREENMO partners maintained a strong and coordinated effort across research, data analysis, gaps, communication, and community engagement, advancing the project towards its strategic objectives. Major progress was made in the frame of Deliverable 2.3.1, the comparative analysis of stakeholders’ and citizens’ views on mobility hubs. Across all GREENMO territories – Greece, Italy, Spain, Cyprus, Malta, and Bosnia & Herzegovina – the consortium put effort on the analysis of the input which was collected based on the extensive and inclusive data collection process within GREENMO, reaching: - 1,402 public questionnaire responses - 130 participants in participatory workshops - 60 stakeholder interviews. These figures are remarkable for a project of this kind and demonstrate the trust built with local communities, the strong commitment of partners, and the project’s dedication to participatory and evidence-based planning. This joint effort provided robust data from both bottom-up (citizens) and top-down (decision-makers) perspectives, now being consolidated by the University of Malta and the University of Cyprus to feed into the project’s overall strategy. On governance, early in semester 3, the consortium finalized Deliverable 1.2.2 on intergovernmental frameworks affecting the planning and operation of mobility hubs. This work provided insights into institutional barriers and enablers for implementing mobility hubs, as well as specific examples to be useful for interested policymakers. It was further disseminated through conferences and external meetings. Communication and amplification activities remained central. The Lead Partner, UniWA, submitted the official project presentation video to the Joint Secretariat (Deliverable D.3.3.1) and complemented it with web and social media dissemination. In parallel, many Linkedin posts and website articles were published covering different aspects of the project progress. Aiming to ensure close alignment with the Green Living Areas Mission (GLA) of Interreg Euro-MED, UniWA together with ALOT advanced the Results Amplification Strategy and they were actively engaged in GLA community events and thematic working group on mobility on international and local levels, strengthening synergies across projects and contributing to mutual learning. Additionally, UniWA’s Communication Officer took part in the Euro-MED Communication Officers Network and in a series of storytelling seminars organized under the GLA initiative (May–June), working on how to effectively translate technical data into understandable narratives for all. Taking a step further, GREENMO partners represented the initiative in several external events and networks, such as the RuralMED Mobility Forum, SMART LAND conference, THNS forum, UITP Summit, Product Club meeting, GLA national workshops, and the Barcelona community gathering. These opportunities allowed GREENMO to share preliminary results, connect with related initiatives, and position its approach within the broader European mobility and sustainability landscape. Consortium coordination was reinforced through regular online meetings and the third transnational project meeting hosted in Malta (8-9 May 2025). Partners presented technical results, co-created future steps, and jointly reviewed the draft agenda of the project’s final event to be held in Mantua in October 2025. The meeting also served to present interim findings from the carbon footprint assessment and stakeholder engagement activities. The carbon footprint monitoring was carried out by the City of Ljubuški, who collected and processed partner data and issued the report including results and respective recommendations to reduce impact. Looking ahead, the consortium has started preparing for the publication of project findings to follow, while also securing opportunities for broader policy impact. Notably, GREENMO’s strategy has been selected for presentation at the Institutional Policy Dialogue Days in Brussels (October 2025), providing visibility at EU level. Added value has come from the interdisciplinary and international cooperation: universities led methodological design and data processing, public authorities engaged their citizens and employees on sustainability, while innovation and consultancy partners steered governance analysis and amplification strategies. This complementary expertise ensured that activities were not only delivered on time and at high quality but also enriched by diverse perspectives, strengthening both local implementation and European-level relevance. In summary, Semester 3 was marked by successful and timely implementation. This way, the project achieves to keep building momentum towards its final output on strategy, ensuring relevance for policymakers, private actors, and the wider Euro-MED community.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The European Commission has adopted a series of proposals aimed at adapting the EU's climate, energy, transport and taxation policies to reduce net greenhouse gas emissions by at least 55% by 2030 compared to 1990. Transport is a critical element in this. Promoting sustainable transport, the EU Green Deal will improve the well-being and health of citizens and future generations by providing efficient, safe and environmentally friendly transport services, which is in the same direction as GREENMO. The project can contribute to the wider strategy by completing it with the element of inclusiveness that is missing.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The Territorial Agenda 2030 is a strategic framework document that underlines the importance of and provides orientation for strategic spatial planning and calls for strengthening the territorial dimension of sector policies at all governance levels. It seeks to promote an inclusive and sustainable future for all places and help achieve Sustainable Development Goals in Europe. One of main pillars is “Sustainable connections” targeting to the sustainable digital and physical connectivity of territories. Thus, it invites those responsible for spatial planning and transport planning to explore new socially and environmentally progressive models for local and regional mobility as a service and to work together for multimodal and environmentally friendly accessibility outside and inside urban centers.
    • All these are related to mobility hubs and citizens’ inclusion which are the two main pillars of GREENMO project. Mobility hubs are multimodal stations which exploit the urban space aiming to provide beautiful spaces and hi-quality services. The project aims to jointly develop a holistic strategic document which will incorporate the element of inclusiveness, just like the Territorial Agenda promotes. Thus, such an output, which is aligned with the goals of this wider strategy, can significantly contribute and complement it. The attempt of PPs will be to develop the document to become a reference document on the concept of mobility hubs following the principles of inclusiveness and sustainable mobility connections in urban living areas.
    • Other EU Sustainable and Smart Mobility Strategy is a landmark for mobility in EU. Based on the target of EU Green Deal to cut 90% the emissions by 2050 by delivering a “smart, competitive, safe, accessible and affordable transport system”, this strategy forms the basis for how the EU transport system can successfully implement this green and digital transformation while increasing resilience to future crises. The Sustainable Mobility Strategy puts in place key milestones for all modes of transport. GREENMO jointly developed strategy will be focused on green and inclusive mobility hubs in MED areas. Its uptake will contribute to the target of the wider EU strategy.
    • Other Recognizing that the environmental targets can only be succeeded with the people and communities, the European Climate Pact was launched as a citizen-oriented initiative towards green living areas, transport, buildings and skills. The European Climate Pact is an opportunity for people, communities and organisations to participate in climate action across Europe.
    • However, this initiative is on a theoretical level to learn, debate, connect. GREENMO can significantly contribute to enhance and strengthen this by placing citizens in the processes of developing the final project strategic document. Applying in practice the principle of public engagement and participation, GREENMO document aims to add value to the European Climate Pact and to become a reference point of its repository in order to be shared and disseminated by the members of the Pact to their regions, to be adapted, transferred, and uptaken, by them towards inclusively implementing mobility hubs as a solution for CO2 reduction.
