Eco-nautical sea routes

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
En tant que première destination touristique mondiale, les ports méditerranéens subissent une forte pression touristique. Cela rend leurs zones protégées plus vulnérables au surtourisme. Le projet ECO-SEAROUTES vise à créer un itinéraire éco-nautique reliant les ports qui s'engagent à : 1) respecter une norme commune de durabilité dans une approche transnationale ; 2) redistribuer les flux touristiques vers les zones côtières et intérieures voisines. Pour y parvenir, le projet encourage les décideurs locaux à créer et à développer de nouveaux itinéraires touristiques locaux vers des zones moins fréquentées et leur patrimoine culturel. Il utilisera une approche ascendante et participative, prête à être partagée et reproduite.

Description (EN):
As the world's largest tourist destination, Mediterranean ports are under intense tourist pressure. This makes their protected areas more vulnerable to overtourism. The ECO-SEAROUTES project aims to create an eco-nautical route linking ports committed to: 1) respect a common sustainability standard in a transnational approach; 2) redistribute tourism flows to neighbouring coastal and inland areas. To achieve this, the project encourages local decision-makers to create and develop new local tourist routes to less crowded areas and their cultural heritage. It will use a bottom-up and participatory approach, ready to be shared and replicated.

Expected Achievements (FR):
To develop a strategic approach aimed at creating a sustainable european nautical route, based on community engagement to build smaller-scale, more environmentally conscious and sustainable inbound nautical tourism in seasonally overcrowded Mediterranean ports. To reduce the human pressure on natural areas located along the costs of popular Mediterranean ports and islands (including Natura 2000 sites) by improving the quality and sustainability of the surrounding touristic offer with pilot itineraries. To consolidate the attractiveness and reputation of peripheral and inland destinations connected to Mediterranean ports with the piloting of Community Heritage Hubs in each itinerary. To keep track of GHG emissions due to the project, establish liaisons with and capitalise from other thematic and governance projects, transfer and disseminate project outputs outside the partnership.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The project responds to the EU Green Deal priorities of:
    • -protecting biodiversity and ecosystems
    • -reducing air, water and soil pollution
    • -ensuring the sustainability of the blue economy.
    • In particular, ECO-SEAROUTES proposes a new model for the protection of coastal environments based on a more conscious management of touristic assets and on a more balanced distribution of touristic flows. In this sense, it operates on a twofold level:
    • a) it sets out tailored criteria for nautical facilities and vectors to reach common standards of environmental sustainability;
    • b) it implements a set of actions and territorial-based strategies oriented to modify the approach to coastal & maritime tourism on visitors’ end.
    • The proposal also embraces the EC’s vision on a new approach for a sustainable blue economy in the EU, as it helps develop green tourism infrastructure in coastal areas that also contribute to the preservation of biodiversity and protected sites.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The Territorial Agenda 2030 (TA) identifies natural and cultural heritage as local/regional assets offering unique opportunities for development and high-quality living environments. It also warns against the over-exploitation of maritime space and mass tourism, which can compromise natural habitats.
    • ECO-SEAROUTES acts consistently with the TA’s vision of creating blue infrastructure networks that link ecosystems and protected areas in spatial planning and land management policies. The nautical ECO-SEA-ROUTE developed in WP1 precisely aims at better integrating fragile coastal areas into tourism destinations networks developed under environmentally conscious criteria.
    • Besides, the proposal also matches the TA’s focus on empowering local communities to protect and promote their environment (natural and cultural) by establishing Community Heritage Hubs as specific tools for a bottom-up re-elaboration of local assets in connection with an eco-sustainable tourism model.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The proposal responds to EUSAIR Pillar 4-Sustainable Tourism since:
    • - the nautical ECO-SEA-ROUTE connecting multiple MED ports and maritime areas contributes to improving the quality & innovation of sustainable tourism as envisioned by EUSAIR Topic 2;
    • - local-based ECO-SEAROUTES Itineraries helps diversify the touristic offer not just in terms of products and services, but also in application to a more homogeneous distribution of tourism flows along coasts-hinterlands linkages (EUSAIR Topic1).
    • Besides, ECO-SEAROUTES makes use of the SmartWays model implemented by PP7 RdF in the Routes4U initiative, which received EUSAIR endorsement for establishing a transnational path of excellence that enhances interregional tourism cooperation through shared quality standards.
    • By reinforcing environmental protection and with the creation of local tourist itineraries, ECO-SEAROUTES consolidates the SmartWay model envisioning an even wider tourism transition towards sustainability and diversification.
    • Other The proposal contributes to the principles set by the WestMED initiative in application to the targeted project countries (IT, ES, FR) and in their relationships with the wider WestMED area. In particular, ECO-SEAROUTES responds to the following items of the 2023 WestMED Ministerial declaration:
    • - it contributes to sustainable consumption in the sphere of maritime transport and carbon-neutral ports, thanks to the diffusion of its model targeting the sub-sector of coastal & maritime eco-tourism
    • - it strengthens the “maritime cluster” concept by interconnecting different actors along the tourism supply chain in WestMED coastal areas
    • - it introduces new patterns in the field of Maritime Spatial Planning (MSP) by seeking a new balance between boosting the growth of blue economies and ensuring the protection of fragile ecosystem and endangered natural sites, all while contributing to reduced marine pollution by means of a more sustainable approach to coastal tourism.
    • Other Thanks to its approach in strengthening sustainable tourism in direct connection with the environmental protection of fragile and endangered coastal areas, the proposal matches a set of goals established by the EU biodiversity strategy for 2030:
    • - it establishes a MED-wide network of maritime and coastal protected areas (based on existing Natura2000 sites) to ideate, share and spread new models for a better integration of conservation plans into a homogeneous green transition of the tourism sector;
    • - it contributes to intertwining the environmental protection of Natura2000 sites with the offset of tourism-based emissions and minimising tourism-derived environmental pressure;
    • - it aims to contribute to data collection & data analysis as per online tools established by the EU Biodiversity strategy (online actions tracker and targets dashboard) by making project results openly available as a case study for the quantification of EU-wide targets’ reach in ecosystem preservation.
    • Other The project fits the S3 approach regionally/nationally adopted in:
    • -Puglia Region in the macro-areas of environmental sustainability and blue growth, in specific application to improving the quality of life as it promotes an innovative touristic model applicable to both special economic zones (Ionian and Adriatic)
    • -Spanish RIS3 in the areas of innovation ecosystem applied to green transition. Concerning Andalusia, the project shares the regional focus on improving natural resources management in function of tourism innovation
    • -Montenegro’s S3, sharing its strategic vision on enhancing tourism competitiveness for a Healthy and Sustainable Montenegro through knowledge, environmental protection, social cohesion
    • -Slovenian S5, whose core principle is to enhance productivity in conjunction with reducing the pressure on natural resources. The project proposes a model to balance the two aspects, responding to the strategic priority “Networks for the Transition to a Circular Economy”.
    • Other The project fits the S3 approach regionally/nationally adopted in:
    • -Cyprus’ S3 for 2030, envisioning a new growth model based on a diversified economy by means of knowledge-intensive products. The proposal offers a new touristic model intended to protect environmental and natural resources as key assets for a thriving society
    • -Corsica’s S3 for 2021-2027 in its definition of green transition as the capacity to promote high value chains linked to environmental resources. The project offers a viable way to transform the rationale of local tourism and change consumption models
    • -Spanish RIS3 in the area of ecosystem biodiversity applied to green transition. In the Balearic Islands, this aspect is articulated as modernisation of tourism industries in function to the ecological transition, for which the project outlines a specific approach based on densely visited coastal areas
    • -Greek S3 (SYNOPSIS), which sets Environment and Circular Economy as a key priority area together with Innovation.
    • Other The Smart Islands Initiative is a bottom-up effort of European island authorities and communities, currently coordinated by PP3 DAFNI with its 44 islands’ local and regional authorities from Greece as members.
    • The initiative is oriented to making European and Mediterranean islands as laboratories for technological, social, environmental, economic and political innovation, setting the main priorities of preserving islands’ distinctive natural and cultural capital and encouraging the shift towards alternative, yearlong, sustainable and responsible tourism.
    • ECO-SEAROUTES represents a concrete model for DAFNI to implement solutions aimed at the consolidation of a more sustainable tourism model, proposing a set of tools and operational strategies that are easily adaptable and transferable to a wide range of insular territories facing environmental degradation, all while empowering civil society organisations and individual residents to contribute to the green transition locally.
