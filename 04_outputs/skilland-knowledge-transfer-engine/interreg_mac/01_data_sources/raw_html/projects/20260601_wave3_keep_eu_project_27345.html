LOCAL Fiscal Policies for GREEN Energy Mainstreaming

Programme 2014 - 2020 INTERREG VB Mediterranean
Date of latest update: 2025-01-25

Description

Description (FR):
Le projet capitalisera le livrable "Manuel International sur les Modèles de Politiques Fiscalese Écologiques Locales" du projet LOCAL4GREEN. Ce livrable décrit un ensemble de politiques fiscales qui peuvent être mises en œuvre au niveau local pour promouvoir les sources d'énergie renouvelables (SER), en relevant le défi de l'augmentation des SER dans les secteurs non SCEQE grâce à un outil flexible et innovant (fiscalité verte). Par conséquent, le projet LOCAL4GREEN+ se focalise sur l'intégration de ces politiques fiscales locales vertes dans les politiques des nouvelles autorités locales. Le projet mettra en œuvre un processus de formation et adaptera les modèles politiques aux caractéristiques spécifiques des municipalités bénéficiaires en collaboration avec des groupes de travail formés par des décideurs, des responsables politiques et d'autres parties prenantes de ces municipalités. En outre, le projet fournira un soutien technique et mettra en place un processus de liaison stratégique avec les décideurs des municipalités bénéficiaires. Deux anciens partenaires du projet LOCAL4GREEN (FVMP – partenaire principal - et MUSOL) sont impliqués en tant que partenaires donneurs et assureront le transfert des politiques à quatre associations de municipalités impliquées en tant que récepteurs. Les partenaires récepteurs travailleront avec les municipalités partenaires associées qui ont déjà exprimé leur intérêt pour la mise en place d'une fiscalité locale verte.

Description (EN):
The project will capitalize the deliverable “International Handbook on Green Local Fiscal Policy” of the project LOCAL4GREEN. Such deliverable describes a set of fiscal policies that can be implemented at local level to promote the renewable energy sources (RES), tackling the challenge of increasing RES in the non-ETS sectors through a flexible innovative tool (green taxation). Therefore, the project LOCAL4GREEN+ is a mainstreaming initiative focusing on integrating such green local fiscal policies into the policies of new local authorities. The project will carry out a training process and will adapt the policy models to the specific features of the receivers municipalities in collaboration with working groups formed by decision makers, policy makers and other stakeholders of such municipalities. Furthermore, the project will provide technical support and will deploy a strategic liaising process with the receivers policy makers to ensure the implementation of the green fiscal policies to promote RES. Two former partners of the LOCAL4GREEN project (FVMP – Lead partner - and MUSOL) are involved as givers partners and will ensure the transfer of the policies to four associations of municipalities involved as receivers. The receivers partners will amplify the capitalization process and will work with receivers associated partners municipalities that have already expressed their interest in implementing green local taxation and will concretely implement the capitalized policies.

Achievements (EN):
The Project has carried out the mainstreaming activities as planned in the AF, being some activities and outputs even ahead of schedule. Partners have trained the receiver municipalities which have created the working groups to adapt the green fiscal policies. They are currently working to prepare the viability studies with the concrete and adapted proposals of fiscal policies to promote RES. In some countries the process has been faster and partners have been able to reach substantial progress even drafting the relevant policy acts and supporting the policy makers for their approval. In Spain, 3 municipalities have approved 9 fiscal policies until 31/10/2021 (8 provisionally, 7 of which have been definitively approved in December 2021). Furthermore, according to the advanced situation of the policy act drafts in other receivers municipalities, additional approvals have been produced at the end of 2021, that will duly reported in the final report.Regarding the coordination and management, 2 supporting tools created by LOCAL4GREEN have been updated: the Handbook on Project Management and the Project Monitoring Guide, and also the Project Steering Committee has been created.On 24th March 2021, the Kick-off Meeting was held online due to the pandemic situation and jointly with a training session from the giver partners (FVMP and MUSOL) to receiver partners in order to develop the skills needed to mainstream into new municipalities the green fiscal policies tested by LOCAL4GREEN, included in the “International Handbook on Green Local Fiscal Policy”.In relation to communication, the Project website has been updated with new contents for LOCAL4GREEN PLUS, and the social networks have been nourished with Project activities. A Google drive folder has been created to share with partners the communication pack (logos, poster template, etc) and other issues related to communication, management and mainstreaming. Moreover, partners have collaborated with the RE Community, especially to support the flagship municipalities in Spain and Italy. The adaptation of the green fiscal policies in the RE Community municipalities is still ongoing.The Project has already reached some progress in the outputs 2.1 and 2.3. In total 3 municipalities have approved fiscal policies to promote RES until 31/10/2021 (3 of 12 planned in output 2.1 for June 2022), reaching 54.771 population of rural municipalities (54.771 of 77.265 population planned in output 2.3 for June 2022).
