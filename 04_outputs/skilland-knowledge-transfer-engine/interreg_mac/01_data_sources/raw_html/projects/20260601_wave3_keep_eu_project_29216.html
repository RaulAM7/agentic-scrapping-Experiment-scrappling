Allocated Zones for circular Aquaculture to trigger the transition to an Inclusive and Circular Economy in aquaculture sector fostering new business opportunities and eco-consciousness society

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Did you know that aquaculture represents a great contributor to aquatic food production worldwide? Especially because most fisheries are near or above sustainable exploitation limits. In Euro-MED, fisheries & aquaculture have been key socioeconomic drivers for millennia, providing livelihoods & MED diet’s basis. Aquaculture is still key economic sector for the area, but faces many challenges: lack of strategies for sustainable development & public sector’s relevant low capacity; linear economy practices/business models traditionally used; complicated legal/licensing framework; lack of dialogue between stakeholders; limited access to space. The overarching challenge of sustainable aquaculture is jointly tackled in AZA4ICE, specifically in close-to-coast & inland waters, where it has great potential for local economies & consumption system. AZA4ICE amplifies the transition to Inclusive & Circular Economy in the sector, implementing innovative ecosystem spatial planning approach embedded with circular practices-production systems in close-to-coast & inland waters. It brings together stakeholders of 5-helix to increase their knowledge/skills, exchange experience & collaborate in drafting their vision & Action Plan for sector’s transition; thus filling the lack of dialogue & blunting conflicts of different uses, leading to new business opportunities & eco-consciousness society. AZA4ICE aquaculture spatial planning approach, stakeholder engagement methodology & Action Plans are the main outputs to be used by public authorities, private sector & academia to benefit the whole society. AZA4ICE approach builds on partners’ existing knowledge & engages 5-helix actors increasing their capacity by exchanging cross boarder experiences; thus it critically depends on transnational cooperation. AZA4ICE is pioneer to suggest circular aquaculture spatial planning & test it in close-to-coast & inland waters going beyond existing practice of aquaculture planning in coastal sea areas.

Description (FR):
Saviez-vous que l'aquaculture contribue grandement à la production alimentaire aquatique dans le monde entier ? D'autant plus que la plupart des pêcheries sont proches ou au-dessus des limites d'exploitation durable. Dans la région Euro-MED, la pêche et l'aquaculture ont été des moteurs socio-économiques clés pendant des millénaires, fournissant les moyens de subsistance et la base du régime alimentaire méditerranéen. L'aquaculture reste un secteur économique clé pour la région, mais elle est confrontée à de nombreux défis : absence de stratégies de développement durable et faible capacité du secteur public en la matière ; pratiques économiques linéaires/modèles d'entreprise traditionnellement utilisés ; cadre juridique/de réglementation complexe ; manque de dialogue entre les parties prenantes ; accès limité à l'espace. Le défi global de l'aquaculture durable est abordé conjointement dans le cadre d'AZA4ICE, en particulier dans les eaux côtières et intérieures, où elle a un grand potentiel pour les économies locales et le système de consommation. AZA4ICE amplifie la transition vers une économie inclusive et circulaire dans le secteur, en mettant en œuvre une approche innovante de planification spatiale de l'écosystème intégrée avec des pratiques circulaires et des systèmes de production dans les eaux intérieures et littorales. Il rassemble les parties prenantes des 5-hélices pour améliorer leurs connaissances/compétences, échanger sur leurs expériences et collaborer à l'élaboration de leur vision et de leur plan d'action pour la transition du secteur, comblant ainsi le manque de dialogue et atténuant les conflits entre les différentes utilisations, conduisant à de nouvelles opportunités d'affaires et à une société éco-consciente. L'approche de planification spatiale de l'aquaculture d'AZA4ICE, la méthodologie d'engagement des parties prenantes et les plans d'action sont les principales réalisations qui seront utilisées par les autorités publiques, le secteur privé et le monde universitaire au profit de l'ensemble de la société. L'approche d'AZA4ICE s'appuie sur les connaissances existantes des partenaires et engage les acteurs des 5-hélices en augmentant leur capacité par l'échange d'expériences transfrontalières ; elle dépend donc de manière critique de la coopération transnationale. AZA4ICE est pionnier pour proposer une approche circulaire des aménagements aquacoles et la teste dans des eaux intérieures et proches de la côte, allant au-delà des pratiques existantes de planification de l'aquaculture dans les zones maritimes côtières.

Actual Achievements (EN):
AZA4ICE progress in the 1st reporting period was satisfactory to the greatest extent, since the planned activities and deliverables were carried out according to the set time plan. The close cooperation and efficient communication between Project Partners (PPs) was effective and led to the successful achievement of project objectives. Under LP coordination, who had a smooth and efficient communication with the project officer, PPs implemented the necessary administrative pre-contracting phase’s activities to ensure the quick finalisation of the contracting procedures and as a result to ensure than no delays will occur in the beginning of the project. Immediately after AZA4ICE approval, PPs, with LP’s guidance, discussed in detail the next steps for project’s implementation and the testing activities; drafting a detailed work plan and the responsibility assignment matrix – RACI. They also formed the Steering Committee and the Technical Committee at the very beginning of the project. AZA4ICE organised the project kick-off meeting with physical presence of the partners just on the 2nd month of project implementation. In this way, partners got known each other better and efficiently planned the activities to be carried out in the 1st reporting period. PPs formed also the Communication Team. The early activation of all the aforementioned committees was proven valuable for the effective management and project coordination. Regarding the communication of the project, PPs worked on the project’s promotional material and operationalisation of the AZA4ICE website and social media, which are being used for the dissemination of the content and objectives of AZA4ICE. The most important achievements were performed in WP1, contributing in project’s Output 1.1; and in WP2 contributing in project’s Output 2.1. In the frame of WP1 “Experiment the Allocated Zones for Circular Aquaculture (C-AZA) instrument in the pilot areas”, the scientific and technical partners developed the Del 1.1.1 “AZA4ICE methodology”. This methodology is the cornerstone of the project, since it details the steps that PPs will follow to define C-AZA in their pilot sites. The methodology defines the C-AZA common ecosystem approach to circular aquaculture spatial planning embedded with innovative circular production systems and described the techniques for environmental & socio-economic data collection, exchange with stakeholders, modelling-analyses-KPIs, the expected final results, etc. It is a highly transferable methodology and it is also the first project output, namely Output 1.1 “AZA4ICE methodology for circular aquaculture spatial planning embedded with innovative circular production systems”. Output 1.1 was delivered in the first reporting period, much earlier than initially planned, since the partnership identified that in this way there would be more time for the pilot activities ensuring their smooth and successful execution. PPs also developed the templates for the pilot reporting and the evaluation of the pilot results. In the frame of WP2 “Living Responsible Research Innovation Ecosystems (LiRRIEs) for quintuple-helix engagement”, the Del 2.1.1 “LiRRIE methodology” was delivered. LiRRIE methodology defines a common multi-level and multi-sectoral stakeholders’ engagement process to set up cooperation patterns and learning processes involving quintuple-helix (academia-industry-government-civil society-environment). It also details the necessary steps for LiRRIEs phases’ operationalization, members’ roles (PPs and 5-helix stakeholders) and their cooperation procedures. The LP, expert in such kind of stakeholders’ engagement processes, provided guidance to all PPs in order to start implementing the LiRRIEs in their territories, identifying the content and mapping the potential stakeholders/participants. The delivery of the LiRRIE methodology justifies the achievement of the Output 2.1 “Living Responsible Research Innovation Ecosystems (LiRRIE) methodology”, in the 1st reporting period as initially planned. Summarizing, AZA4ICE has already achieved 2 out of the 4 project outputs in the 1st reporting period, delivering two very useful and highly transferrable methodologies/solutions to the Euro-MED community. For both outputs, the transnational cooperation was an important element, based on which the specificities of all areas were taken into account and the knowledge gained in previous projects and initiatives was capitalised. Also, AZA4ICE PPs provided feedback to the JS and the governance projects under the Innovative Sustainable Economy Mission when requested, facilitating the creation of synergies between the Mission’s projects and beyond with other Missions, highlighting complementarities and potential actions towards common targets. Finally, with the aim to calculate the carbon footprint emissions caused by the project activities, AZA4ICE partners kept CO2 records to be integrated in the Programme’s dedicated online tool. AZA4ICE progress in the 2nd reporting period was satisfactory to a great extent, since the planned activities and deliverables were carried out according to the set time plan. The close cooperation and communication between Project Partners (PPs) were effective and led to the successful achievement of project objectives. Under Lead Partner’s (LP) coordination, who had a smooth and efficient communication with the project officer, PPs carried out the technical activities of this reporting period, as well as the necessary administrative procedures to report the outcomes reached since the beginning of the project. The most intense work was done under Work Package 1 (WP1) and Work Package 2 (WP2). In the frame of WP1 “Experiment the Allocated Zones for Circular Aquaculture (C-AZA) instrument in the pilot areas”, the pilot areas started the implementation of the Del 1.1.1 “AZA4ICE methodology” by collecting field and historical data for the space and species suitability analysis, described in the methodology. This methodology is the cornerstone of the project, detailing the steps that PPs will follow to define C-AZA in their pilot sites (aka the techniques for environmental & socio-economic data collection, exchange with stakeholders, modelling-analyses-KPIs, the expected final results). The PPs responsible for the pilots were supported by the scientific and technical partners and - depending on their capacity - they subcontracted external experts to further support them with the data collection and analysis. The exchange between PPs was extremely beneficial for clarifying how some steps in the methodology should be implemented and the lessons learnt in each pilot were transferred to the others. In the frame of WP2 “Living Responsible Research Innovation Ecosystems (LiRRIEs) for quintuple-helix engagement”, the PPs followed the Del 2.1.1 “LiRRIE methodology”. LiRRIE methodology defines a common multi-level and multi-sectoral stakeholders’ engagement process involving quintuple-helix, detailing LiRRIEs phases’ operationalization. By the end of the 2nd reporting period, the 2 first LiRRIEs phases were concluded, “Phase 1 – Identify and Map” and “Phase 2 – Motivate and Connect”. In Phase 1, PPs identified the current challenges, needs, barriers and opportunities to be tackled in the framework of LiRRIE and drafted the corresponding LiRRIE vision. They also mapped the relevant stakeholders from all economic sectors to be invited as LiRRIE members. In Phase 2, they formed the “LiRRIE forum” of complementary stakeholders of 5-Helix addressing the common problem/opportunity towards the achievement of the LiRRIE vision with clear understanding of LiRRIE leadership, stakeholder’s commitment, necessary activities and resources. The LP provided guidance to all PPs to implement successfully these LiRRIE phases in their territories. Another activity successfully concluded is the analysis of the aquaculture legal/regulatory framework and licensing processes in all participating countries (Act2.3). This report analyzes the regulatory frameworks and licensing processes shaping the aquaculture sector across the Euro-MED region, emphasizing sustainability, innovation and economic development. As part of Act 2.5 “Engaging civil society and consumers”, the LP and the communication leader started exchanging ideas about the AZA4ICE comic book, ensuring that it will be delivered successfully in the next reporting period. In the frame of WP3, PPs started conducting desk research for contributing to the development of the guide with circular aquaculture business practices and opportunities (D3.1.1). Also, the LP has started exchanging with the PPs about the common transnational strategy for drafting the AZA4ICE Action Plans (D3.2.1) for which input from the first LiRRIE events is needed. Moreover, PPs provided feedback to the JS and the governance projects under the Innovative Sustainable Economy (ISE) Mission when requested, facilitating the creation of synergies between the Mission’s projects and beyond with other Missions, highlighting complementarities and potential actions towards common targets. AZA4ICE participated in all ISE events, physical and online, e.g. the ISEC Hub Launch Event and the 2 ISE Community of Practice events, providing the requested input and feedback. Finally, with the aim to calculate the carbon footprint emissions caused by the project activities, AZA4ICE partners kept CO2 records to be integrated in the Programme’s dedicated online tool. In terms of communication and dissemination, PPs kept updated the AZA4ICE website and the social media accounts. Beyond the basic project communication material (branding, poster, roll-up, etc.), new material was designed as a scientific poster, videos presenting the PPs and the pilot areas, podcasts; and the overall video requested by the JS was drafted. AZA4ICE was also present in some external events prompting the project and its outputs reached till now. AZA4ICE progress during the 3rd reporting period was largely satisfactory, as the planned activities and deliverables were implemented in line with the established timeline. The effective cooperation and communication among the Project Partners (PPs) contributed significantly to the successful achievement of the project’s objectives. Under the coordination of the Lead Partner (LP), who maintained smooth and efficient communication with the Project Officer, the PPs successfully carried out both technical activities and necessary administrative procedures required to report the outcomes achieved during these months. PPs dedicated significant effort in implementing the activities across all four project Work Packages (WPs). In WP1 “Experiment the Allocated Zones for Circular Aquaculture (C-AZA) instrument in the pilot areas”, the pilot areas continued the AZA4ICE methodology implementation by collecting field and historical data for the space and species suitability analysis, described in the methodology. The PPs responsible for the pilots were supported by the scientific and technical partners, as well as their external experts, in data collection and analysis. Also, the scientific and technical partners, organised a training session during the 5th project meeting in Sibenik, Croatia, to train PPs and their external experts in using the templates which accompany the methodology for the data collection, analysis and results’ reporting and evaluation (A1.2 and A1.3). This training session was extremely beneficial for clarifying how some steps in the methodology should be implemented. In addition, it was a very good opportunity for exchange of challenges and lessons learnt by the PPs in each area. In WP2 “Living Responsible Research Innovation Ecosystems (LiRRIEs) for quintuple-helix engagement”, the PPs followed the LiRRIE methodology, which defines a common stakeholders’ engagement process involving quintuple-helix. By the end of the 3rd reporting period, “Phase 3 – Frame and Cross-Fertilise” and “Phase 4 – Deepen” were concluded. In Phase 3, PPs organised the 1st LiRRIE event in each pilot with the stakeholders’ participation. PPs fine-tuned the LiRRIE vision in collaboration with LiRRIE members, cross-fertilised their ideas on AZA4ICE methodology and collected data for the space use and the species, necessary for the AZA4ICE pilots. PPs also applied the BLUEfasma circularity self-assessment tool to the SMEs to identify their readiness and willingness to invest in Circular Economy (CE) in aquaculture. In Phase 4, PPs increased LiRRIE members’ knowledge in CE in the aquaculture sector, as well as in the current legal/regulatory context and licensing processes and relevant innovative circular production systems. This phase enhanced LiRRIE members’ relevant capacity which will facilitate later the co-development of the AZA4ICE Action Plans. LiRRIE initiators organised the 2nd LiRRIEs events. Another activity successfully concluded is the identification of the circularity baseline of businesses in the aquaculture sector using the BLUEfasma Circularity Self-Assessment Tool (Del 2.2.1). PPs faced some challenges in this task, since not all SMEs of the sector felt confident in using the tool by themselves. Due to this, PPs had to put more effort to guide them step-by-step filling the tool. As part of Act 2.5 “Engaging civil society and consumers”, the LP and the communication leader worked on the content creation and the design of the AZA4ICE comic book (D2.5.2), delivering it at the end of this period. They also started exchanging ideas about the AZA4ICE board game (D2.5.1). In WP3, PPs continued the desk research for the development of the guide with circular aquaculture business practices and opportunities (D3.1.1). Also, the common transnational strategy for drafting the AZA4ICE Action Plans (D3.2.1) was developed and agreed among all PPs. Moreover, PPs provided feedback to JS and the ISE Mission when requested, facilitating the creation of synergies between projects, highlighting complementarities and potential actions towards common targets. AZA4ICE participated in all ISE events, physical and online, e.g. the 3rd ISE Community of Practice. Finally, PPs kept CO2 records and integrated them in the dedicated online tool. In terms of communication and dissemination, PPs kept updated the AZA4ICE website and the social media accounts. Beyond the basic project communication material, new material was designed e.g. dedicated brochure per pilot area and podcasts. AZA4ICE was presented through the interview “Hands in the water, eyes on the future”, conducted with one member of the Portuguese LiRRIE. The interview was conducted as a collaboration action between Interreg Euro-MED and Interreg Atlantic Area and was published at the official interreg.eu website. AZA4ICE was present in external events (e.g. EMD2025, 10th EUSAIR Forum, Circle the MED Forum 8th ed.) reaching a wide audience, promoting the project outputs.
