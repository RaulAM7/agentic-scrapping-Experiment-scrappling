Home / Projects and documents / 2B-BLUE

Boosting the Blue Biotechnology community in the Mediterranean

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The Blue Biotechnology (BBt) sector can have a significant impact on the environment, human wellbeing and economic growth, however in the Mediterranean basin it is currently in its infancy. B-BLUE project has built a preliminary network of actors related to BBt and analyzed best practices, key drivers, barriers and readiness factors of EuroMed companies for adopting blue biotechnologies, concluding with the most promising value chains for EuroMed area. The project allowed the establishment of 5 interactive Blue Biotechnology Hubs (BHHs) to facilitate collaborations, knowledge transfer and spill-over effects to spur innovation and business within the marine biotechnology value chains and address the challenges detected. At once, a digital community has been developed, using an ICT tool called B-Blue MatchMaking Tool, to support new joint initiatives. Moreover, the MedIA - Mediterranean Innovation Alliance for Sustainable BlueBioeconomy - has been set-up in collaboration with Blue Bio Med and 2 specific collaborative working groups on BBt. 2B-BLUE aims to capitalize on B-Blue positive results to: 1) Exemplify evidence-based best practices identified to help communities turn BBt research into practice, 2)Build national demonstration sites (DS) to experiment emerging technologies or practices in local field conditions and bridge the gap among BBt research and industry for new technologies adaptation while help improve marine environment, 3) Establish strategic alliances of 5-helix stakeholders for the uptake of advanced blue biotechnologies by Med industries and 4)improve regional policies for enhancing sustainability, research and innovation capacities in Euro-Med area. Thus, the main challenges detected in the B-Blue work - most related to funding, normative and public and private collaboration- can be transformed into opportunities in 2B-Blue and result in more sustainable and efficient practices as well as better structuring of the BBt sector in the Mediterranean.

Description (FR):
Le secteur des biotechnologies bleues (BtB) peut avoir un impact significatif sur l'environnement, le bien-être humain et la croissance économique. En Méditerranée, ce secteur n’en est pourtant qu’à ses débuts. Le projet B-BLUE a rassemblé des communautés d'acteurs des BtB, analysé les meilleures pratiques, les principaux moteurs, les obstacles et les facteurs de préparation des entreprises euroméditerranéennes à l'adoption des BtB, en concluant par la définition des chaînes de valeur les plus prometteuses de la zone euroméditerranéenne. Le projet a permis la création de 5 hubs biotechnologie bleues interactifs (BHH) visant à stimuler l'innovation et les affaires au sein des chaînes de valeur des biotechnologies marines et de relever les défis identifiés. La communauté s’est digitalisée en utilisant l’outil B-Blue MatchMaking Tool qui soutient de nouvelles initiatives conjointes afin de : 1) Illustrer les meilleures pratiques pour aider les communautés à transformer la recherche en pratique, 2) Construire des sites de démonstration nationaux (DS) pour expérimenter en conditions réelles les technologies ou pratiques émergentes des BtB et combler l’écart entre l'industrie et la recherche pour l'adaptation des nouvelles technologies tout en contribuant à l’amélioration de l'environnement marin, 3) établir des alliances stratégiques d'acteurs de la quintuple hélice pour l'adoption par les industries de la zone euroméditerranéenne de BtB avancées et 4) améliorer les politiques régionales pour renforcer la durabilité et les capacités de R&I dans l'espace euro-méditerranéen. 2B-BLUE vise à capitaliser sur les résultats positifs de B-Blue et les principaux défis détectés (liés au financement, à la réglementation et à la collaboration entre entités publiques et privées) pour les transformer en opportunités dans 2B-Blue et qu’ils se traduisent par des pratiques plus efficaces et plus durables ainsi que part une meilleure structuration du secteur des BtB en Méditerranée.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The project is progressing on time and achieving the objectives, work packages, activities and expected results. In this first period the consortium agreement between the partners and the JS has been signed. In addition, the KoM has been held in Murcia (held on 14 and 15 March at the University of Murcia) and the rules of the steering committee of the project have been agreed for its correct implementation. On the first day were presented (1) the organisations participating in the project and their roles in the implementation of 2B-BLUE; (2) the most important issues related to the implementation and functioning of the project in the framework of the Interreg Euro Med programme (JS); (3) the Community4Innovation project which has given the necessary tips for the effective collaboration of 2B-BLUE with other thematic and governance projects under the umbrella of the sustainable economy priority of the programme; and (4) the 3 work packages, their activities and a work plan for the period 1. On Day 2 has been focused on the explanation and presentation of the aspects related to the coordination and management of 2B-BLUE, the administrative and financial issues, giving precise details about its procedures, the fundamental aspects for the communication and dissemination of the project and finally the rules of 2B-BLUE and its Steering Committee (SC) have been adopted. It has been generated a work repository in TEAMS to deposit and make accessible to the partners all the important information generated in the project. At the same time, it has uploaded the relevant information generated to the project's BaseCamp. During this period 1, monthly coordination meetings have been held in order to review the fulfilment of activities, deliverables and objectives and outputs expected (on 24 April, 24 May and 27 June). So, action points have been marked for each partner of the project according to what was established in the proposal and with a timeframe to carry them out. This has allowed the correct implementation of the planned activities and the correction of possible delays or deviations. 2B-BLUE partners participated in several events organised by the programme and other in line with the cooperation project objectives. For example, in (1) the Innovative Sustainable Economy Mission of the Interreg Euro-MED Programme 2nd Innovation Camp in Barcelona (28-29 May); (2) in Launch of the Community of Practice of the Innovative Sustainable Economy Mission and 1st edition of the MED Innovation Day in Marseille. 18-19 June; (3) or (4) in the ISBW15 Congress held in Napoli (17-21 June). In WP1 Study to facilitate the strengthening of the blue biotechnology sector in emerging value chains (lead by HCMR). A digital repository of good practices and actors has been created (http://medbbhub.cnr.it/) and serve to feed and develop successfully the organisation of WP2 pilot activities (At the moment, there are included 68 actors and 35 good practices). The action has been successfully coordinated by IRBIM-CNR and all the 2B-BLUE partners are collaborating to feed the repository. It is a digital tool to meet D1.1.1 (M6) on time and with the objectives of A1.1 related to the mapping of best practices and actors and A1.2 (and D1.2.1) finetuning pilot activities in BBHUBs of each country and in the Demo Sites (A2.1, A2.2, A2.3 and A2.4) and T-Labs (A3.1) while designing a BBt testing framework and pave the way for the implementation of the WP2 Development and implementation of testing activities in the blue biotechnology hubs (BBHs) and meet their objectives (starting in period 2). Here IRBIM-CNR has prepared a legal document to facilitate public-private collaboration in the framework of the pilot actions in the 6 BBHubs (Spain, France, Slovenia, Italy, Greece and Albania) and which will be applied in the framework of the A2.1 (methodology for testing in real environments), A2.2 (set-up of public-private Joint research units for demos sites and their implementation (A2.3). At the same time, ENEA is training (following seminars and indications of the JS) and coordinating successfully the carbon footprint monitoring action. All partners are following their indications to meet the foreseen targets and complete the information in the CO2 monitoring tool. Finally, NIB has prepared a dissemination and communication plan and strategy for the 2B-BLUE project in the framework of activity 3.4 (internal deliverable D3.4.1 uploaded on Base Camp). In this way, the strategy of amplification of results and dissemination of the project and the programme can be fulfilled. Also, NIB worked to keep the website (and social networks) alive in order to comply with the dissemination and cooperation commitments established by the JS with other thematic projects of the Innovative sustainable economy mission and other national and international strategies. The project is progressing on time and achieving the objectives, work packages and activities towards expected results. During period 2, monthly coordination meetings were held to review the fulfilment of activities, deliverables, objectives and expected results on the dates of 17 September, 25 November and 19 December. 17 October was the second physical Steering Committee meeting of 2B-BLUE (see information below). After each meeting, action points were noted for each partner according to what was specified in the proposal and with a timeframe for their implementation. This allowed the correct workflow of the planned activities and the correction of any delays or deviations. The second physical SC meeting was held on 17 October in Rome, Italy. Minutes report is uploaded in Basecamp. On this day (1) the former project B-BLUE, results and lessons learned, were presented to the new associated partners of the 2B-BLUE project; (2) the task status, main achievements, plan for the next deliverables and the next 6 months steps of the 3 work packages were presented, emphasising the Carbon Footprint Monitoring tool and its use; (3) each project partner explained the potential pilot activities to be carried out in each national BBHub (4) and the overview of the first period report. Also in Rome, the first international T-Lab was held on 18 October, as a side event of the Blue Planet Economy Expoforum, open to the public and visitors with 42 attendees. The aim of this T-Lab was to present 2B-BLUE to external key actors. Three guest speakers were invited to share inspiring examples with the public and the project partners. The main outputs of period 2 are: In WP1, Deliverables D.1.2.1, D,1.3.1 and D.1.3.2 have been finalised and uploaded to BaseCamp and JEMS, with the key role of leaders HCMR and CNR and the contributions of all partners. For Activity A1.4, all the PPs, with support from Enea, have recorded 2024 emissions using the Carbon Footprint Monitoring Tool and uploaded to Basecamp. D1.1.1 Dynamic database with map of BBt best practices and stakeholders is open access online (https://medbbhub.cnr.it/. It is a living tool during the project. The classification of the information in the repository is being improved to facilitate its use. Monthly meetings, SC meetings and preparation of T-Labs have contributed to provide a coordination framework for their fruitful transnational interaction between PPs. All reports are in the 2B-BLUE repository on Teams. In WP 2, activity A.2.1 Methodology for BBt testing in real environment has started. CNR and PMM have prepared a common template to collect structured information from national T-Labs for the methodology to be applied in the demo sites from period 3 to 5. CNR is developing an Expression of Interest model for selecting third parties to join JRUs; and a CRA framework. This effort goes to provide BBHs with the common regulatory framework for the testing of BBt in the different pilots. The aim is to test common methodologies of pilot actions for the alignment of national/regional policies & financing instruments in the field of the Blue Bioeconomy. Potential collaborations between BBt and demonstration sites have already been identified. The PPs will monitor the testing phase for potential multilateral initiatives & synergies for business. Regarding WP3, Activity A.3.1 'Organisation of Transformation Labs (T-Labs)', the first national T-Labs were held during this period in the 6 BBHubs led by PPs (Slovenia, 19 November; France, 19 November; Albania, 27 November; Spain, 28 November; Italy, 12 December; Greece, 16 December). The exchange of strategies and objectives prior to the T-Labs was relevant to gather structured information due to the different contexts of the project's BBHubs. The types of T-Labs were physical, online or hybrids, always in the format of a workshop with multiple attendees. The total number of participants in the six national T-Labs was 221, with representatives of the 5 quintuple helix. The project communication officer NIB prepared a dissemination and communication plan and strategy (internal deliverable D.3.4.1 uploaded to Basecamp). Also, the communication report was updated in January 2025. All PPs contribute to the strategy of amplification of results and dissemination of the activities of the project. NIB and UMU also participate in governance projects of ISE Mission: (1) the ISE Community Hub Launch Event (2 October), 2nd ISE Community of Practice (11 November) preparing cooperation in the Transfer of Results (Peer Review Process) and the Mentoring & Twinning Programmes. BBt days were organised in the local language to raise awareness on the BBt sector in collaboration with key actors. For exmple, UMU & CTN organised a Day of Aquaculture4.0 (18 September); ENEA &CNR- IRBIM organised a workshop per Favorire la creazione di un ecosistema dell'innovazione per sfruttare il potenziale delle biotecnologie blu (18 October). During the third reporting period, all project partners (PPs) actively participated in monthly progress meetings and attended the third Steering Committee meeting held in Montpellier in May 2025. They contributed to the methodological and technical development of all packages in particular WP 2 with the implementation of Demo Sites and planning of pilot actions in their respective territories. All partners were also involved in communication and dissemination activities through their institutional channels and in coordination with the communication lead. In particular: CNR and PMM, as co-leaders, focused on finalizing deliverable D2.1.1, titled “Model for the Development of Public-Private Joint Research Units; Specifications for the Set-Up of Public-Private Partnerships for Collaborative Research and Related Agreements”. PMM was responsible for organizing the third Steering Committee meeting, which took place in Montpellier. IFREMER also co-organized the 3rd. Steering comitee meeting and the visit to Palavas-les-Flots at the Palavas Marine Experimental Platform (PEMP). ENEA completed the reports for Periods I and II, led the carbon footprint tool assessment (T1.4), co-developed the Italian pilot action plan and monitoring activities with CNR, and coordinated input collection for D3.1.1 as WP3 co-leader. It also co-led deliverable D2.1.1 on public-private research models and reallocated budget to support two strategic studies on IMTA system scalability and best practices. ACEPSD contributed best practices linked to the Vlora Bay demo site, adapted the B-Blue demo-site model to establish a Joint Research Unit (JRU), and signed a collaboration agreement with the company "Alb-Adriatico 2013." It launched preliminary site preparations, infrastructure planning, and environmental assessments, and participated in key project meetings. ADF engaged external expertise for WP1 activities, carried out stakeholder mapping and best practices identification in Albania, and finalized the carbon footprint report. In coordination with ACEPSD, it organized the first online TLab in Albania and managed the publication of project content on its website, including materials sent to the communication lead.

  • Building the blue biotechnology community in the Mediterranean (B-Blue) (EN), Building the blue biotechnology community in the Mediterranean (B-Blue) (FR) | Interreg-MED project (ENEA coordinator, together with partners UMU, NIB, HCMR, PMM-TVT and IRBIM-CNR), this project set the precedent of the present project 2B-BLUE. While the previous project was focused in governance aspects, aimed at gathering the key actors of the Med BBt sector and increase their innovation capacity and their coordination to unlock the innovation potential in the field through joint transnational initiatives, also involving organizations from the Southern Shore of the Mediterranean, 2B-Blue was born as testing project to implement actions on B-Blue community and to create joint solutions tested in real life conditions. To this end, 2B-Blue will make use of the Network of Blue Biotechnologies Hubs (BBH), set by B_Blue, for involving MED BBt innovation communities in the testing phase. Moreover, it will capitalise on the B-Blue methodology for mapping best practices and stakeholders, fine tuning and integrating the related dynamic database; the research and analysis on regulatory frameworks, environmental sustainability evaluation frameworks, market perspectives and sustainable business models relating to BBt value chains; the Demonstration Sites models and methods to be tested on a wider and transnational scale; its tools & methodologies for the innovation transfer & capacity building in the BBt sector to be upscaled in a new knowledge transferring framework.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The project contributes to creating a more sustainable and efficient food and service production system in the use of marine resources. By providing fairer, more sustainable and local products and services in line with the Farm to Fork strategy. In addition to facilitating the preservation of marine biodiversity and offering technologies, tools and methods for a collaborative way to exploit in a sustainable way the marine biodiversity through marine biotechnologies. Also green financing and legal schemes in the sector and promote new businesses will be implemented that will favour a green transition through public and private funding for new green and sustainable initiatives.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin 2B-Blue will contribute by the promotion of sustainable products and services from the sea and provided by blue biotechnology. It will promote the adaptation of new standards for sustainable seafood production and providing new frameworks for the promotion of the products and services offered by blue biotechnologies. The project contributes offering solutions to points: (2.3) improve the state of biodiversity by supporting local participatory initiatives to improve sustainability through blue biotechnologies in the value chains adressed, and by improving investment in the promotion of nature conservation, (2.4) improve coastal resilience by stimulating innovation and bridging knowledge gaps with solutions to 2-B-B-Blue.
