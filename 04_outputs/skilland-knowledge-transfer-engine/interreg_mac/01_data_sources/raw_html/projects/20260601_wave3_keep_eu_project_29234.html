Novel Support tools to Mediterranean Governments Exploiting Behavioural Incentives

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
NUDGES - or more precisely, "climate nudges" - are defined as intentional modifications of the people's choice architectures that aim to alter citizen behaviours towards climate-friendly actions while maintaining their earlier alternatives. The NUDGES Study proposal aims at demonstrating the feasibility of leveraging the territorial diversities and cultural commonalities of Mediterranean countries for the successful integration of "nudging" strategies into the urban climate change mitigation and adaptation policies of local, regional and national public authorities. It will do so by delivering six proofs of concept, grounded on the results of 6 experimental pilots, each located in a different country of the programme area, whereby a PP and AP duo secure the active engagement of local public administration alongside citizens and communities. 5 of the 6 experimental pilots will be focused on the potential behavioural influence of collective sensorial experiences using Sight, Hearing, Smell, Taste and Touch; the sixth one will be transversal and focused on new generations.The Living Lab approach will serve as framework to ensure the needed level of realism and depth of engagement in experimentations by Quadruple Helix stakeholders. In so doing we expect to fill in 3 main gaps in the current scenario: 1) between climate responsible attitudes and behaviours within the MED population, 2) the lack of knowledge at policy level, on the potential of climate nudges as alternative tools to regulatory changes and financial incentives to reach ecosystemic change and community resilience, and 3) the lack of evidence on the intertwining of cultural and behavioural aspects as relevant for the effectiveness, and efficiency, of nudging strategies. A new and innovative, MED-wide community of interest will be created, paving the way to additional research action in this domain, in the framework of a dedicated Transferability Plan.

Description (FR):
Les "nudges climat" sont définis comme des modifications intentionnelles des architectures de choix des personnes visant à modifier les comportements des citoyens vers des actions respectueuses du climat, tout en maintenant leurs alternatives antérieures. NUDGES vise à démontrer la faisabilité de s’appuyer sur les diversités territoriales et les points communs culturels des pays méditerranéens pour l'intégration de stratégies de "nudging" dans les politiques urbaines d'atténuation et d’adaptation au changement climatique des autorités publiques locales, régionales et nationales. Il fournira 6 résultats, fondées sur 6 pilotes expérimentaux, chacun situé dans un pays différent du programme, gérés par un duo PP et AP. 5 des 6 pilotes expérimentaux seront axés sur l'influence comportementale des expériences sensorielles collectives utilisant la vue, l'ouïe, l'odorat, le goût et le toucher ; la sixième sera transversale et centrée sur les nouvelles générations. L'approche Living Lab servira de cadre pour assurer le niveau de réalisme et la profondeur d'engagement nécessaire dans les expérimentations par les parties prenantes de la Quadruple Hélice. 3 lacunes du scénario actuel seront comblées: 1) le gap entre les attitudes et comportements responsables face au climat au sein de la population méditerranéenne, 2) le manque de connaissances au niveau politique, sur le potentiel des nudges climatiques en tant qu'outils alternatifs à la réglementation et aux incitations financières pour atteindre le changement écosystémique et la résilience communautaire, et 3) le manque de preuves sur la pertinence des aspects culturels et comportementaux pour l'efficacité et l'efficience des stratégies de nudge. Une nouvelle communauté d'intérêt innovante à l'échelle de la région MED sera créée, ouvrant la voie à des actions de recherche supplémentaires dans ce domaine, dans le cadre d'un plan de transfert dédié.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the first period the main focus was to identify a suitable methodology for the "nudges" as well as to have the first discussions with the relevant stakeholders in each pilot area to define the scope and the perimeter of the 6 pilot actions that will start in the next period. More specifically, UdA who is responsible for this activity, has already shared with the partners the steps for the work do be done as well as had online b2b meetings with each partner to discuss and define the interventions and the next steps. Below is the recap from the first discussions on the pilot interventions: 1) e-Zavod: The intervention will include smell walks, scented dining experiences, culinary workshops, and online surveys (Approximately 9-15 people of age range: 10-40 years old). The aim is to promote sustainability, reducing food waste, and encouraging local food consumption. 2) PREDA: Intervention will be education and awareness programs for children on pro-environmental behaviours (Young generation, children (6-14), and their parents). The aim is to promote waste reduction, recycling and energy saving. 3) MHS: Organizing an artistic contest for billboard designs and outdoor materials to encourage reconsideration of reuse and recycling, specifically fabrics. Approximately 50 participants (both teenagers and adults). Age Range: Teenagers (14-19) and adults (19+). The aim is to inspire pro-environmental behaviours, specifically efficiency behaviour. 4) MUSOL: Promoting healthy lifestyles and reducing environmental pollution. Approximately 50-80 adults and 100 children of the age range: Adults (30-50) and children (3-11). The aim is to reducing car usage, increasing walking and cycling. 5) AUTH: "Mindfulness and Sustainability" program and appetite framework for 50 people over 18 years old with the aim to promote sustainable and healthy living practices. 6) UdA: Talent Garden, a multi-faceted educational and social project for about 30 kids of age 14-17 with the aim to promote positive emotions and well-being through various community activities and educational programs. Under the guidance of AUTH the systematic review of literature has started and is still under process. AUTH prepared a guidance of how to do the review, starting from the topic selection (nudges, sustainable behaviours, climate change) and then searching with specific keywords (behavioural economics, nudges, green nudges, sustainable behaviour, climate change) in different databases such as Scopus, PubMed, Science Direct. This resulted a large database of articles that after partners needed to screen based on inclusion and exclusion criteria so as to have the most relevant literature that answers to the question "How effective are Nudges in promoting environmentally sustainable behaviours related to climate change mitigation΄?". The data collection and extraction has been finalised in June and data analysis and synthesis as well as the deliverable writing will be done until September according to the work plan of the project. Another activity preformed this semester is the creation of the template for the good practise database. The database will be an online tool that will allow users to explore and filter through a multitude of nudges examples based on intervention type, sensory experiences, behaviour impact and geographic location. This online tool that is now under construction will provide an initial a collection of interesting cases to be explored but also it will be a live tool updated with the pilot activities as well as open to all giving the opportunity to people to join us in shaping sharing their examples of effective nudges towards environmentally responsible behaviour. That could be projects, products, services, and policies that harness the power of nudge theory to drive positive change for our planet. For communicating the concept of NUDGES a policy brief has been designed that is introducing nudges concept as well as explaining the rationale behind nudges in public policies. Also, the policy brief describes the way we will work with the pilots and the interventions to be implemented. The policy brief is almost ready, the last design touches are needed for its finalization. Since stakeholders are an important part the pilot process as well as for communicating the concept of nudges, an internal database has been created with all partners adding relevant contacts. This is also a live document that will be updated during the project life time. During this period, the project website (https://nudges.interreg-euro-med.eu/) and social media (LinkedIn) have been set up and 6 posts have been published. Also, we have created the project poster as well as template for the deliverables. Also, as a project we have participated to the Green Living Areas event in Turin and we follow all the relavant activities of the GLA Mission. The second period started with the continuation of the work that was initiated during the first period. First, under the guidance of AUTH the systematic review of literature was contacted with the contribution from the partners. The D1.1 titled “State of the Art Analysis of Cultural Nudges to Pro-Environmental Behaviour, which has been finalised, offers a comprehensive systematic review of nudging interventions aimed at encouraging behaviors that can help combat climate change, particularly in the Mediterranean region and Europe. The review highlights the most effective nudging strategies, underscores the influence of cultural factors on the success of interventions, and integrates insights from various geographical and cultural contexts throughout the European Union (EU). In regards to activity 1.2, PREDA (leading partner) with the contribution of all partners has prepared a database tool that contains a comprehensive collection of effective behavioural interventions aimed at promoting environmentally responsible practices. This tool allows users to explore and filter through a multitude of examples based on intervention type (such as culture or nudging interventions), sensory experiences (hearing, smell, touch, and taste), behavioural impact (awareness, behaviour change, long - term sustainability) and geographical location. A deliverable has been prepared that describes the database and its functionality. The tool can be found in the NUDGES official website, in the tab What we achieve. It consists of an introduction and a video of Nudges and then different tabs of "who we are", "what we do" and the tab "NUDGES with us", which is giving the opportunity to the users to share with us their different nudging experiences and also to give them the opportunity to join the NUDGES community. So far the tool consists of 120 best practises and the aim the online tool to be a living tool to be updated with the submission of new interventions and best practises as well as from the submission of the 6 pilots (when they will be implemented) activities and results. For activity 1.3, UdA as a responsible partner finalized the deliverable 1.3 "NUDGES Methodology Blueprint" that focuses on outlining a comprehensive approach to applying behavioural nudges and cultural interventions. This blueprint acts as a roadmap for designing and implementing strategies that encourage individuals and communities to adopt more sustainable practices, specifically targeting climate action. A distinctive aspect of this deliverable and of the NUDGES project is the emphasis on integrating cultural interventions, by recognizing that cultural identity and emotional connections are powerful drivers of human behaviour. Based on D1.3, nudges and cultural interventions has been prepared based also on relevant discussions with the relevant stakeholders The 6 pilot interventions have been defined as follow (more details in the deliverable): PREDA: uses multi-senses stimuli to engage children and parents in environmental protection through creative workshops. MUSOL: focuses on auditory cues to reduce carbon footprints by promoting walking over driving. eZAVOD: emphasizes the sense of smell, connecting citizens to nature and sustainable behaviours through herbal gardening. UdA: explores tactile experiences to encourage sustainable practices in marginalized communities. AUTH: highlights the sense of taste, promoting climate-friendly food choices linked to the Mediterranean diet. MHS: utilizes visual arts, engaging young artists to inspire climate-friendly behaviours through art and satire. NUDGES policy brief (Activity 1.4) has been finalized and it contains the introduction to nudges and why there is a need to have climate nudges in public policies, a description of the Nudges methodology and how the pilot interventions will work and some recommendations for action. The policy brief has been disseminated to the community of interest that contains more than 164 contacts from different stakeholders from the Mediterranean area. During this period, the project website (https://nudges.interreg-euro-med.eu/) and social media have been updated with news. In LinkedIn account we have 101 followers. Special emphasis was given in the 6 pilot interventions with interviews from the partners speaking about their pilot activities. Also, all the deliverables of the project have been uploaded to the website. As well all partners disseminate their activities on their institutional website and social media. We organized our 2nd meeting in Zagreb (MZ) where we also had the video shooting for the project video. Also we have participated to the Green Living Areas events such as the online working groups and institutional dialogue organized in Brussels. The 3rd period was focused on the full implementation of the pilot activities. Under the coordination of MUSOL and the guidance of UdA the partners implemented their pilot activities as follow: PREDA: implemented the pilot programme “Next Generation Citizens – Eco Heroes” in the City of Prijedor. They engaged children aged 6–14 in 4 creative workshops integrating behavioural insights. 1st: Watching the film Wall-E. 2nd: children drew their own eco-hero characters, building personal identity around environmental values. 3rd: introduction to circular economy principles. Children cut, sorted, and ground plastic bottles collected from previous workshops, used Tinkercad to design toys, and 3D-printed toys. 4th: an eco-garden was planted in PREDA;s premises. MUSOL: implemented the sound inspiration pilot to the city of Madridejos. They created a musical route with sound devices near the school, so that children and parents were motivated to walk this alternative route. They also implemented a regular nudging with talks on environmental sustainability in other schools of the area. The activity took place once a week, during the time of dropping off children from school, for a period of 3 months (between February-May 2025). Total participants 44 adults and 77 children. UdA: explored tactile experiences to encourage sustainable practices in marginalized communities. They implemented their activities in the Rancitelli, Talent Garden between February - May 2025 (every Wednesday Afternoon). The target group was teenagers of 15-17 years old. Total participants: 15. Two workshops were organized, the art and handcraft workshop focusing on hands-on creative activities to support motor and cognitive skills, social skills, emotional awareness and expression. The 2nd workshop was on creativity to climate awareness cognitive shift through tactile engagement. AUTH: implemented the pilot activity “Tasting the transformation. They focused on adult18, local restaurant owners, traditional food vendors, and producers in Thessaloniki. They had 45 participants split into 3 groups. The control group visited the partner restaurant and chose meals from the standard menu without any prior education. The cultural intervention group receive education on the Mediterranean Diet, sustainability, and the link between diet and climate change. They visit the same restaurant and choose from the same menu as the control group to assess the influence of prior knowledge. The nudging intervention group receive education on sustainable eating and co-design a new menu with nudging strategies. eZAVOD worked with smell. The activity was implemented in May in the Slovenske Konjice Monastery, that has a medicinal herb garden. Participants had a guided tour of the Žička Carthusian Monastery’s historic herb garden and herbal pharmacy. They explore how the heritage of herbal medicine connects with today’s efforts toward sustainability and mindful living. The cultural intervention was a series of three interactive workshops focused on smell, but also touch, and taste – all rooted in the monastery’s rich herbal tradition (smell walk, tea-tasting experience, introduction to the process of making herbal hydrolates. MHS: engaged young talent by organizing a contest focused on creating billboard designs to make ctizens to rethink how to reduce garbage and increase recycling. The designers’ contest was launched at the end of May. In total 35 participants participate to the contest. Also, cultural interventions were implemented. A documentary film with a guided tour to the Regional Waste Management System in Gabrovo, and the ”Climate fresk” game with a workshop with ecology experts was held in mid June (14-15 June). After the cultural interventions, the young designers had three weeks to prepare their designs. The expert jury made a selection and chose the best design for printing the billboard. For WP1, we supported the enhancement of the NUDGE4GREEN platform in line with the project’s evolving needs. The platform was technically and structurally improved to ensure complementarity with the main project website. During this period, the project website and social media have been updated with news. In LinkedIn account we have 141 followers and had 280 total page views and 143 unique visitors. All deliverables have been uploaded to the website and partners disseminate their activities on their institutional website and social media. NUDGES was presented at the EU Green Week 2025 conference in Brussels on 5 June. We participated to the parallel session on Engage for change: Exploring ways to engage citizens towards circular actions. The concept of NUDGES project was presented as well as the pilot activities followed by a workshop on nudging intervention. We organized our 3rd meeting in Pescara (17-18 June 2025) and participated to the GLA events such as the online working groups and the GLA gathering in Barcelona (28-29 May 2025).

Relevant linked projects:

  • INCIRCLE - Support INsular and low-density areas in the transition towards a more CIRCuLar tourism Economy.
  • Interreg Med Programme (EN), INCIRCLE - Accompagner les territoires INsulaires et peu denses dans la transition vers une Economie touristique plus CIRCuLaire.
  • Programme Interreg Med (FR) | INCIRCLE aimed at developing regional and municipal strategies for sustainable tourism, applying the principles of the circular economy in tourist destinations located in islands and low-density areas.
  • The project activities analysed the relationship between the principles of the circular economy and Mediterranean tourism policy, with a focus on the development and application of specific circular economy indicators that can improve the management and planning models of the tourism sector in the project partner territories, and in general in the programme area, with a concrete view to sustainability.
  • A knowledge platform has been developed that sets the premises for the exchange of already acquired knowledge, tools, methods, and good practices in the field on circular economy applying to the tourism sector, ensuring further transferring and capitalization of existing knowledge in the tourism sector. In addition the platform enables users to evaluate the circularity and sustainability of their policies and actions with a benchmarking and self-assessment tool.
  • Partner in charge: ANETEL.
  • (EN), INCIRCLE visait à développer des stratégies régionales et municipales pour un tourisme durable, en appliquant les principes de l'économie circulaire dans les destinations touristiques situées dans les îles et les zones à faible densité.
  • Les activités du projet ont analysé la relation entre les principes de l'économie circulaire et la politique touristique méditerranéenne, en mettant l'accent sur le développement et l'application d'indicateurs spécifiques de l'économie circulaire qui peuvent améliorer les modèles de gestion et de planification du secteur du tourisme dans les territoires partenaires du projet, et en général dans la zone du programme, dans une perspective concrète de durabilité.
  • Une plate-forme de connaissances a été développée qui établit les prémisses pour l'échange de connaissances, d'outils, de méthodes et de bonnes pratiques déjà acquises dans le domaine de l'économie circulaire appliquée au secteur du tourisme, assurant un transfert et une capitalisation supplémentaires des connaissances existantes dans le secteur du tourisme. En outre, la plateforme permet aux utilisateurs d'évaluer la circularité et la durabilité de leurs politiques et actions grâce à un outil d'analyse comparative et d'auto-évaluation.

  • Interreg Med Programme (EN), BLUEfasma - Renforcer la capacité d'innovation des PME, des clusters maritimes et des réseaux dans les îles MED et les zones côtières pour soutenir la croissance de l'économie circulaire bleue dans la pêche/l'aquaculture.
  • Programme Interreg Med (FR) | BLUEfasma integrates and implements Circular Economy (CE) principles in the key blue growth sector of fishing/aquaculture; to benefit Mediterranean insular, island and coastal areas in a novel, innovative way. Its overall objective is to empower the innovation capacity of SMEs, maritime clusters/networks, Public Authorities to boost blue CE growth in insular, island and coastal areas. Key outputs of the project are the following:
  • #1 Consolidated knowledge base: systematizing best practices, tools, solutions and methods in fishing/aquaculture
  • #2 Circular Economy readiness index: used as a unified MED measure of SMEs readiness & willingness to invest in CE and act as a decision support tool.
  • #3 BLUEfasma e-network: established to facilitate experience exchange and networking among all actors of fishing/aquaculture sectors
  • #4 BLUEfasma Living Labs: to integrate R&I and create user-centered open innovation ecosystems focused on circularity increase in fishing/aquaculture
  • #5 BLUEfasma Capacity Building Instrument: to integrate financing/mentoring/coaching opportunities for R&I business investment in fishing/aquaculture
  • #6 Systematized policy inputs by Thematic Working Group: to shift policies towards Circular Economy
  • Partner in charge: ANETEL. (EN), BLUEfasma intègre et met en œuvre les principes de l'économie circulaire (EC) dans le secteur clé de la croissance bleue de la pêche/aquaculture ; faire bénéficier les espaces méditerranéens insulaires, insulaires et côtiers d'une manière originale et innovante. Son objectif général est de renforcer la capacité d'innovation des PME, des clusters/réseaux maritimes, des pouvoirs publics pour stimuler la croissance du CE bleu dans les zones insulaires, insulaires et côtières. Les principaux résultats du projet sont les suivants :
  • #1 Base de connaissances consolidée : systématiser les bonnes pratiques, outils, solutions et méthodes en pêche/aquaculture
  • #2 Indice de préparation à l'économie circulaire : utilisé comme mesure MED unifiée de la préparation et de la volonté des PME à investir dans l'EC et agir comme un outil d'aide à la décision.
  • #3 BLUEfasma e-network : créé pour faciliter l'échange d'expériences et la mise en réseau entre tous les acteurs des secteurs de la pêche/aquaculture
  • #4 BLUEfasma Living Labs : pour intégrer la R&I et créer des écosystèmes d'innovation ouverte centrés sur l'utilisateur et axés sur l'augmentation de la circularité dans la pêche/l'aquaculture
  • #5 Instrument de renforcement des capacités BLUEfasma : pour intégrer les opportunités de financement/mentorat/coaching pour l'investissement des entreprises en R&I dans la pêche/l'aquaculture
  • #6 Contributions politiques systématisées par le groupe de travail thématique : pour faire évoluer les politiques vers l'économie circulaire

  • Interreg Med Programme (EN) | Le projet a testé et déployé une nouvelle approche adaptée aux spécificités MED protégeant le patrimoine naturel des effets néfastes du tourisme intensif. INHERIT a promu le tourisme durable en atténuant la saisonnalité et en s'attaquant au dépassement de la capacité d'accueil, en concevant et en mettant en œuvre une approche de protection «ascendante» reposant sur l'autorégulation et le suivi par la société locale et les acteurs du tourisme.
  • Le projet a testé et déployé une nouvelle approche de promotion du tourisme durable qui comprenait :
  • - Un nouveau mélange de mesures (par exemple, l'intégration ICZM-MSP, des initiatives de gestion des déchets «ascendantes») pour protéger son patrimoine naturel des effets du tourisme de masse (construction et déchets excessifs, désertification-érosion, capacité dépassée de destinations spécifiques, saisonnalité du tourisme) ;
  • - Une stratégie de coopération transnationale pour former un réseau d'aires MED, à savoir les aires INHERITURA, afin de donner la priorité à la protection renforcée de leur patrimoine naturel en tant qu'atout pour développer des activités touristiques durables (ex. activités) et d'assurer un développement responsable.
  • Partenaire responsable : ANETEL. (FR), The project tested and deployed a new approach adapted to MED specificities protecting natural heritage from the adverse effects of intensive tourism. INHERIT promoted sustainable tourism alleviating seasonality and tackling the surpassing of the hosting capacity, by designing and implementing a “bottom-up” protection approach relying on self-regulation and monitoring by local society and tourism stakeholders.
  • The project tested and deployed a new approach in promoting sustainable tourism that included:
  • - A novel mix of measures (e.g. ICZM-MSP integration, “bottom-up” waste management initiatives) to protect its natural heritage from mass tourism effects (excess construction & waste, desertification-erosion, exceeded capacity of specific destinations, tourism seasonality);
  • - A strategy of transnational cooperation to form a network of MED areas, namely INHERITURA areas, to prioritise the enhanced protection of their natural heritage as an asset to develop sustainable tourism activities (e.g. sea caving in the MED, MED inland tourism itineraries, remote islands activities) and to ensure responsible development.

  • (EN) | L'objectif de ConsumeLess Plus était d'améliorer la durabilité du tourisme dans la zone MED et d'évoluer vers une valorisation plus efficace des ressources naturelles et du patrimoine culturel. Le tourisme sera limité afin de promouvoir des destinations sûres. Les touristes ne rencontreront pas d'énormes foules mais pourront s'imprégner et expérimenter le style de vie communautaire de ces destinations, en d'autres termes découvrir des destinations ConsumeLess.

Faire partie de la communauté ConsumeLess signifie également qu'une plus grande attention est portée à l'économie et à la communauté locales, afin d'améliorer le bien-être des citoyens et des visiteurs.
  • Partenaire responsable : ANETEL. (FR), The objective of ConsumeLess Plus was to enhance the sustainability of tourism in the MED area and move towards a more efficient valorisation of natural resources and cultural heritage.   • Due to several factors relating to the COVID-19 pandemic, the tourism industry and economy have been brought down to a standstill. ConsumeLess Plus aimed to assist the Mediterranean region rebuild its destinations by encouraging SMEs to innovate and invest in sustainable principles. Tourism will be limited in order to promote safe destinations. Tourists will not come across huge crowds but will be able to absorb and experience the community lifestyle of these destinations, in order words experiencing ConsumeLess destinations.
  • Throughout the project, we showed that becoming a ConsumeLess destination means that you are part of a Mediterranean community that values natural resources, ecosystem services, and cultural heritage. Being part of the ConsumeLess community also means that more attention is focused on the local economy and community, to improve the well-being of citizens and visitors.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The project is in line with the Pillar 3 - Environmental Quality, which proposed 3 flagships with concrete actions EUSAIR countries will meet at national level to follow common goal/solutions for the region. It meets in particular
    • the objectives of the F2 - Protection and enhancement of natural terrestrial habitats and ecosystems aiming to establish protection and enhancement of natural terrestrial habitats and ecosystems, with particular attention to the
    • ecological connectivity of blue and green corridors/infrastructure.
    • Connecting all EUSAIR countries protected areas including Natura 2000 areas and other networks with tourism will have strong impact on the Adriatic-Ionian region with their joint managements and researches.
    • Capacity building and communication is of paramount importance under this pillar. Capacity building and communication can contribute to raising awareness of the issues and ownership of efforts to address them as well as
    • to aligning national environmental legislation to existing EU legislation, thereby also improving the implementation of laws.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion The project is coherent with the Objective 3 "Ensuring sustainability in the Alps: preserving the Alpine heritage and promoting a sustainable use of natural and cultural resources" of the EUSALP.
    • One of the main features of the Alpine Region is its outstanding natural and cultural heritage. Natural resources (in particular, clean and abundant water, minerals, a variety of landscapes and great biodiversity), and strong and diverse cultural life are major assets of this region. However, the use of natural resources and the exploitation of their potential need to be carried out in an environmentally friendly way. At the same time, climate change is already affecting the Alpine Region in terms of availability of resources and threats to population.
    • The project provides an evident contribution to achieve the main priorities of this policy area by:
    • - reinforcing Alpine natural and cultural resources as assets of a high quality living area
    • - building further on the position of the Alpine Region as world-class in terms of energy efficiency and sustainable production of renewable energy
    • - Alpine risk management including risk dialogue, to tackle potential threats, such as those of climate change.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin Le projet apporte une contribution claire à l'Initiative pour le développement durable de l'économie bleue en Méditerranée occidentale, en particulier à ses objectifs de promouvoir une croissance et des emplois bleus durables et de préserver
    • écosystèmes et biodiversité en Méditerranée occidentale.
    • La région est fortement affectée par le changement climatique et l'élévation du niveau de la mer représente une menace majeure pour les écosystèmes et les économies côtières. D'autres facteurs tels que la croissance et le vieillissement de la population, la migration et la mondialisation exacerbent encore ces pressions et pourraient potentiellement intensifier la concurrence actuelle pour les ressources et alimenter davantage la géopolitique et la stabilité de la région.
    • EuropeanGreenDeal The European Union’s Green Deal is the EU’s main new growth strategy to transition the EU economy to a sustainable economic model. Its overarching objective is to make EU the first climate neutral continent by 2050, resulting in a cleaner environment, more affordable energy, smarter transport, new jobs and an overall better quality of life. Within the EU Green Deal, the EU Biodiversity strategy for 2030 identifies the key drivers in biodiversity loss as changes in land and sea use, overexploitation, climate change, pollution, and invasive alien species. Biodiversity loss and climate change are intrinsically linked, and nature-based solutions will play an important role in mitigating, and adapting to, climate change.
    • The project contributes to Green Deal strategy through the creation of a permanent network of public authorities and stakeholders which will promote further action research on nudges and possibly also a capacity building programme addressed to policy makers and civil servants for public practices and policy making.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 Le projet s'inscrit pleinement dans l'Agenda Territorial 2030 qui souligne l'importance et l'orientation de l'aménagement stratégique du territoire et appelle à renforcer la dimension territoriale des politiques sectorielles à tous les niveaux de gouvernance.
    • Il contribue aux deux objectifs primordiaux, un juste
    • L'Europe et une Europe verte et cherche à valoriser le patrimoine naturel et culturel MED en tant qu'atout unique et diversifié qui doit être protégé, géré et développé davantage.
    • Le projet reconnaît l'importance d'un développement territorial équilibré pour réduire les vulnérabilités régionales au changement climatique et développer les capacités d'atténuation et d'adaptation aux impacts du changement climatique.
    • Other L'initiative WestMED est le résultat d'années de dialogue entre dix pays de la région de la Méditerranée occidentale impliqués dans le "Dialogue 5+5": cinq États membres de l'UE (France, Italie, Portugal, Espagne et Malte) et cinq pays partenaires du Sud (Algérie, Libye, Mauritanie, Maroc et Tunisie).
    • Ces pays sont prêts et désireux de travailler ensemble sur leurs intérêts communs pour la région : accroître la sûreté et la sécurité maritimes, promouvoir une croissance bleue et des emplois durables, et préserver les écosystèmes et la biodiversité.
    • Notre projet inclura l'initiative et en particulier ses partenaires du Sud parmi ses principales cibles de communication et de diffusion orientées vers les rives Sud et Est de la Méditerranée.
