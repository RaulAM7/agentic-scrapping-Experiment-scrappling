Testing Circular Economy solutions for the green transition of the Mediterranean area

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
CircleMED aims to convert the Euro-Med area into circular economy based territories, accelerating the diffusion of circular and climate-neutral practices and the green transition of the local civil, industrial and institutional systems. It points at reconciling our economies and human activities with the planetary boundaries. CircleMED’s integrated and systemic approach targets all the four helix stakeholders to promote the transition process to circular and sustainable production and consumption models, stimulating awareness and testing innovative circular solutions working closely with citizens, policy makers, business support organisations, academia, research entities and additional stakeholders, sharing knowledge and responsibilities for the green transition. Key elements CircleMED stress on are those considered fundamental for sustainable development: economic prosperity, environmental protection and social equity, balancing them to benefit current and future generations, targeting the three dimensions of citizens, SMEs and policy makers. CircleMED could be considered an evolutionary innovative project, testing solutions that already exist, but that have never been used together. It focuses on promoting circularity and testing resource efficiency particularly through an innovative circular assessment tool for SMEs, the creation of a transnational network of territorial circular hubs and the elaboration of plans for transferability of results in the whole Mediterranean area. The project aims at redesign, repair, reuse and recycle as driving scheme leading citizens, institutions and companies’ choices to a resource-efficient and competitive economy, further developing and scaling up circular approaches, methodologies and strategies easily adaptable to any kind of territory.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The kick-off meeting of CircleMED took place online on 5 February 2024. On this occasion the Steering Committee was formally established, administrative, financial management procedures and issues were reminded and the LP presented an overview of the project, highlighting the main activities, deliverables, outputs and upcoming steps. In addition, a Interreg Euro-MED Innovative Sustainable Economy (ISE) Mission representative presented to the partnership the mission and activities of the programme, the Thematic Projects and the Mission Governance Projects. In P1, the CircleMED partnership worked on refining the project’s internal organisation and management, as well as on developing initial activities for all WPs. In terms of communication, the CircleMED project adopted the official Interreg Euro-MED materials and logos, created its own brand identity, finalised its official website on the Interreg-EuroMED platform, and opened social media accounts on Instagram, Facebook and LinkedIn. WP1 aims to undergo analysis and develop tools to promote behavioural change and influence citizens' attitudes towards circular practices. During P1, the partnership worked on analysing the components of circular dynamics, identifying territories and circular domains. The document 'Guidelines for case study identification and initial description', elaborated by the Activity 1.1 leader, provided the framework for identifying the boundaries and components of the local circular dynamics under study. The guidelines included: - Objectives and main tasks of Activity 1.1 in WP1 - Identification of the area of interest - Identification of the circular economy processes - Circularity matrix - Reporting template Each partner began to scan and identify the areas of interest within which circularity can and should be analysed, considering administrative relevance, citizen representativeness, and geographical scope (urban vs. rural). At the same time, they identified possible areas of circularity to be considered (e.g. organic waste, water, textile, technologic waste, packaging). Following the analysis, each partner submitted a short report describing: 1) the identification and initial description of the area of interest and 2) the definition of the relevant circular process matrix, identifying the relevant circular domains. The reports should be presented during the consortium meeting in Turin in July 2024. During the analysis, the partners were asked to keep in mind and start “mapping” the stakeholders, which will be integral part of the Activity 1.2. The mission of WP2 is to enhance circular innovative business solutions for the optimisation and implementation of circular economy approaches in local enterprises, enabling circular and climate-neutral practices for the transition. CircleMED intends to create a tool that facilitates the matching between SME/enterprise development needs and market requirements while offering an opportunity to test the validity and effectiveness of circular solutions. The first step of such a project being the analysis of currently available circularity tools. The partnership identified and selected Decision Support Tools (DTS): interactive software-based tools that support managers in making decisions at both the strategic and operational level, a structured approach designed to decision-making among stakeholders. The partners worked on a comprehensive list of DTS available (mainly, but not necessarily only in the field of circular economy). The tools proposed by each partner, will be analyzed and categorized by the entire partnership, according to specific parameters. In vision of this task, the LP proposed an initial list of parameters for DTS analysis: "usability" (including “effectiveness”) indicators and "circular economy" criteria, to use as guidelines to analyse and categorise the DTS. The partners provided feedback on the preparatory document, to finalise the framework for the tools assessment, which will be carried out by all partners in P2. The leader of the WP3 Activity 3.1 started working on carbon footprint reporting, by exploring the Interreg Euro-MED Carbon Footprint Calculator platform. With regards to Activity 3.2, the partnership actively participated in the Thematic Community of the ISE Mission “Strengthening an innovative sustainable economy”, following the online communication and the Basecamp environment, and attending both the "2nd Innovation Camp of the ISE Mission", in Barcelona, on 28-29 May 2024, where the partners were actively involved in the activities of the event, sharing, exchanging and developing synergies between projects and stakeholders, and raising awareness of CircleMED, and the "Shaping Sustainable Innovation in the Mediterranean" event in Marseille, on 18-19 June 2024. CircleMED focuses on the following objectives: - to support the active participation of citizens and their understanding of the circular economy, to promote socio-economic practices and facilitate the transition; - to improve the implementation and optimisation of circular practices for local businesses thanks to an online circularity assessment platform; - and to work with local governments towards circular economy policies, while establishing a transnational system to promote the diffusion of circular economy practices in the MED region. Regarding the first objective, the CircleMED partnership has already launched a citizens' survey to analyse people's awareness and attitudes towards circular economy and sustainability. The results will be collected at local level, with feedback from 7 different territories in the MED region. The analysis of the results will be the basis for the “Report on behavioural barriers for consumers and citizens”. The survey was preceded by the identification of the target territories and the circular domains (the areas of interest within which circularity should be considered). Two "horizontal" (shared) circularity domains (organic waste and water) and one "vertical" (specific) domain per partner were selected. Each partner analysed and defined the relevant stakeholders in their area to be involved in the Local Advisory Boards: citizens' representatives, public authorities and academia, business and trade unions, with the role of monitoring and providing guidance to ensure that the actions identified in the project move in the direction of supporting sustainable solutions. The definition and structuring of the Local Advisory Boards was based on a stakeholder analysis that identified, for each partner, the most important and influential actors in their area. For the second objective of the project, a preliminary in-depth analysis of available online tools for circularity assessment was carried out by the partnership as a whole, in order to benchmark available solutions and identify areas for improvement and specifications for an upgraded / entirely new platform. The analysis resulted in a “Report on the assessment of tools for enterprises”. The partnership joined forces to research and identify all relevant platforms on the market (mainly in English and other languages) and to carry out the analysis. The results were compared and weighted to ensure maximum professionalism of the final result. With respect to the third objective, CircleMED Partnership actively participated in the activities of the Innovative Sustainable Economy Mission of Interreg-EuroMED, attending face-to-face and online meetings with the lead partner and other project partners, and actively engaged in activities and exchanges with the community. Other steps towards the achievement of the third objective, while based on the results and activities already in place, will be further developed in the next reporting Periods. CircleMED carried out its carbon footprint reporting according to the Interreg-EuroMED guidelines and platform. The CircleMED partnership met various times online and in-person. The official meetings have been: - Kick-off meeting and Steering Committee – online, 5 February 2024 - CircleMED first operative meeting – online, 19th of March 2024 - Partnership meeting and Steering Committee – Turin, 17-18 July 2024 - Partnership meeting and Steering Committee – Seville, 3-4 December 2024. On top of these, various online meetings were organised to ensure close collaboration and exchanges on the various activities: support was offered from the Activity Leaders to all partners with Q&A and explanatory session, feedback sessions made sure inputs from all partners were received and integrated at all stages and technical bilateral or collective online meetings made sure to tackle any technical aspect of the project, and ensure that the entire partnership was aligned and could contribute fully to the project. WP1, focuses on supporting the active participation of citizens to circular economy. In P3, the survey for citizens in the partners’ territories to analyse people's awareness and attitudes towards circular economy came to an end. The results have been analysed, resulting in the “Report on behavioural barriers for consumers and citizens”. The Deliverable was finalised by UNITO, supported by feedback by all Partners. The report integrated the responses, to explore shared trends and to assess potential space for improvement. The first section focuses on the core of the survey, dedicated to circular economy. The second section focuses on the part dedicated to global warming, providing essential contextual insights. From the outcomes of both sections, the conclusions are drawn. A section of the document is dedicated to a comparison across the target regions. The report assesses how the behaviour most associated with circularity among the respondents is related to recycling, self-reported as the most practiced action. Other actions (reuse, repair, reduction), are reported as much less common, indicating an overall rather vague awareness of the fundamentals of circular economy. The main barriers in the adoption of circular economy behaviours lie in resistance to behavioural change and limited availability and high cost of sustainable products. Respondents do not perceive the absence of regulations as major obstacle, and view policy interventions targeting production processes and economic incentives as the most effective measure to accelerate the transition to a circular economy. The results of the survey are one of the documents and tools of discussion with the Local Advisory Board (LAB). The results of the LABs will be integrated in the “Guidelines for local public support and citizens’ participation“ and in the “Capitalisation plan for circular sustainability”. In P3, the selection and contact of stakeholders to be involved in the LAB was finalised, under the coordination of SERDA, with a presentation during the Partnership Meeting in Sarajevo, 1 April 2025. Meetings of the LABs have already taken place (either in-person or online). Building from the findings of the survey and meeting of the LAB, some partners already started organising Future Labs: animation initiatives targeting citizens, to enhance societal dialogue. The Partners decided to structure the events as “gamification” workshops. UNITO organised a session on the benefits of gamification for engagement and information retention. Various board games were proposed. Finally, due to variabilities in the target groups and language constraint, it was decided that each partner assess the best board game to be used for its activity. The activities serve as base for the “Guidelines for local public support and citizens’ participation”, and for discussion for the LAB, to assess to what extent local policies fit-in at citizens level. WP2 aims at enhancing innovative solutions for the optimisation and implementation of circular economy in local enterprises. In P3, the “Report on the assessment of tools for enterprises” published in P2 was used as basis for the definition of the technical specification of the CircleMED circularity self-assessment platform. The characteristics, discussed by the partnership, were finally defined: - after evaluation of multiple “access profiles”, it was decided to create one only access type to streamline the process - The tool will collect information on the company via a questionnaire - Three options of “depth of analysis”with different length and detail of questionnaire will allow a flexible approach - The tool will provide respondents (businesses) with a benchmark and gap analysis with respect to pre-defined values (e.g. ISO 59020, ISO 59010), a report,and an Interactive Action Plan - The tool will integrate an Interactive User Portfolio, for companies to monitor their evolution in time. A tender was launched by CCI du VAR, to identify a suitable provider for the technical development. WP3 focuses on raising awareness on framework support for territorial deployment of circular economy principles, creating dialogue and synergies between stakeholders, and building political support. In P3, the Partners led by ANETEL developed a self-assessment framework based on the OECD Checklist for Action “The Circular Economy in Cities and Regions”. Using the tool as a base, with the support of the LAB, each partner will anayse regulatory obstacles and drivers in its local, regional or national policy instruments. In P3 CircleMED Partnership actively participated in the activities of the ISE Mission: - calls on the Mentoring and Twinning programme and C4I/DV - peer review process - ISE Mission CoP in Sarajevo, 2-3 April 2025 - “technical” meetings of the ISE Mission (e.g. communication) - 2nd International Workshop of the ProcuraMED Project, 20 May 2025. CircleMED partners keep monitoring its Carbon Footprint, to be reported in P4.

  • n/a (FR), Interreg MED 2014-2020 project “finMED” (EN) | n/a (FR), Interreg MED project “finMED” (UniTo, ANETEL, SERDA, CTA and CERTH have been partners of) has performed an analysis of existing instruments and mechanisms for financing innovation in green sectors and has developed a support service to be provided by clusters and business support organizations (in green technologies) to their members in order to improve their access to finance for innovation. All this knowledge generated will be a fundamental mechanism for the promotion of strategic planning as a tool for companies to adapt to current socio-economic threats. This result of finMED project (the tool is still working under the supervision of UNITO) will be capitalized as a working base for the activities of WP2, and partially for the development of the Network of Hubs in WP3. (EN), n/a (FR), Interreg MED 2014-2020 project “BlueFasma” (EN) | n/a (FR), BLUEfasma created an online platform that systematizes best practices, tools, solutions and methods in fishing/aquaculture. In particular, it created a tool for measuring SMEs readiness and willingness to invest in Circular economy and act as a decision support tool. The self-assessment tool asks for the SMEs/enterprises’ answers on the online survey provided through the tool. The tool is a circularity self-diagnosis tool; it is simple and user-friendly. Its main objective is to tackle the lack of data on the circularity level of Mediterranean SMEs/enterprises related to fishing/aquaculture and their willingness to invest in blue CE; and assist the SMEs/enterprises to review their current business practices, compare them with the provisions of the CE and identify areas where new/improved solutions may be developed and introduced. Based on the users’ answers the tool automatically generates the corresponding results and guidelines/recommendations towards Circular Economy
