Green Mediterranean through Solar Driven Decarbonization of Industries

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 041. Energy efficiency renovation of existing housing stock, demonstration projects and supporting measures ~ SUMMARY: The Mediterranean region faces a severe climate crisis and unemployment. The region, being blessed with abundant solar energy, must form effective international partnerships to promptly transit to greener, low-carbon and resilient economies. Industry contributes significantly to global warming of the region, which is estimated to be 20% higher than the rest of the world. Decarbonized and self-sufficient industries could promote sustainable development by alleviating unemployment rates. With 8 partners from Italy, Spain, France, Türkiye, Palestine and Egypt and 9 Associated partners, GREENOLIVE is committed to build a novel solar energy pilot partially addressing process heat of the plastics industry, which must undergo a deep energy transformation. The solar system will rely on 600 kWe PV system to provide heat at about 300°C through electric heaters using the Power to Heat (P2H) concept. Using P2H concept for process heat at 300°C is a novel approach. It is expected to decrease fossil fuel use in the plastics plant by 18-22%, through using solar energy efficiently. When solar energy is in excess, packed-bed thermal energy storage (TES), scaled-up based on previous EU project results, will store heat for periods when radiation is unavailable. Integrating TES in the system will avoid using expensive batteries and make it more energy efficient at lower cost. Locally available waste materials will be developed as TES materials. Low-cost and locally available components will be used in the system to deliver affordable clean energy and contribute to UN SDG7. The project will also prepare a replication plan for textiles and agri-products industries and the residential sector. The creation of GREENOLIVE Training Kit and white paper will optimize knowledge and skills transfer in solar energy systems and policy improvement, benefiting local industries, society, education, and engineering companies. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Energy efficiency, Green technologies, Renewable energy

Relevant linked projects:

  • Small-Scale Solar Thermal Combined Cycle. POLYPHEM. H2020-EU.3.3.2. - Low-cost, low-carbon energy supply. (2018-2022) (EN) | The objective of the Polyphem project was to improve the performance of small-scale Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) plants and their flexibility to generate power on demand. To this end, a new technology is proposed: a solar-driven combined cycle with integrated thermal energy storage.
  • - The outcomes of the project allow to reinforce the competitiveness of this new low-carbon energy technology, to favour its integration in the medium term in the Mediterranean energy mix and to contribute to the mitigation of climate change.
  • The project included testing a concrete TES tank, with concrete bricks as filler material. The models used for this project can serve as the basis for the development of the GREENOLIVE TES tank. We will access these models through our partner CIEMAT who is a partner in this project. (EN), Storage Research Infrastructure Eco-System. STORIES. H2020-EU.1.4. - EXCELLENT SCIENCE - Research Infrastructures (2021-2025) (EN) | The STORIES project fosters a European ecosystem of industry and research organizations on energy storage technologies aimed at developing novel concepts and technologies, by providing access to world-class research infrastructures and services, with a focus on improving materials for devices and optimizing hybrid energy systems (https://www.storiesproject.eu/)
  • In GREENOLIVE hybridization is present with the use of an electric heater and a Microwave applicator for converting electricity into thermal energy. The lessons learned in the STORIES project will be applied in the project. We will share these experiences through our partner CIEMAT who is a partner in this project. (EN), Techno-economical evaluation of different thermal energy storage concepts for CSP plants. CSPplus. CSP-Eranet 2020 (2021-2024) (EN) | This project aims at developing a new tool that will allow an easy but very complete economical and technical evaluation of the next generation CSP integrated with TES technologies. Therefore, the objective of this project is to reduce CAPEX and OPEX, which translates into a LCOE reduction (with a final LCOE lower than 10 c€/kWh, as indicated by the SET-Plan strategic targets) by evaluating different TES options compatible with operational temperatures up to 750ºC, with the aim of using more advanced power cycles such as supercritical CO2 (sCO2). CU and UB were partners in this project contributing with the packed bed concept, studying different secondary materials as TES materials and air or solar salt as HTF.
  • Outputs from UB are materials’ characterization data, LCA with precise allocation methodologies for electric arc furnace steel slag as TES materials and a thermodynamic modeling of a TES packed bed tank that explores the influence of different particle sizes. CU also worked on the radial and axial flow packed-bed thermal energy storage system for high temperature concentrated solar power plants. In GREENOLIVE, packed bed thermal energy storage modeling, scale-up methodologies and results will be used for the demonstration plant that will be designed and built in WP4 and WP5.We will access these through our partner CU and UB who were partners in this project. (EN), PhD Innovation Pathways for Thermal Energy Storage (INPATH-TES), EU Horizon 2020 (Grant agreement ID: 657466) (2015-2018) (EN) | Laboratory scale packed-bed thermal energy storage system was built and new sustainable and low-cost sensible thermal energy storage materials based on demolition wastes were developed to analyze its performance in industrial solar applications below 200°C. One Dimensional Continuous Solid Phase Model developed to evaluate its performance for industrial scale applications in the temperature range of 130 °C – 180 °C. Economic and environmental impacts of demolition waste-based packed-bed thermal energy storage system in industrial applications were evaluated through case studies. A PhD thesis was realized based on the coututelle agreement between CU and UB in this project. INPATH-TES also created a platform for education of PhD students to improve their skills.
  • In GREENOLIVE, the results of this project will be used in developing the lab-scale replicas for open living labs for capacity-building purposes in WP6. The education materials in GREENOLIVE will be developed based on the experiences from INPATH-TES outputs. We will access these through our partner CU and UB who were partners in this project. (EN), Advanced HYBRID solar plant with PCM storage solutions in sCO2 cycles. HYBRIDplus. Grant n° 101084182, HORIZON-CL5-2021-D3-03 (EN) | HYBRIDplus is introducing a high-density and high-temperature thermal energy storage (TES) system for concentrated solar power (CSP) that offers high dispatchability at low cost and lower environmental impact. The system uses Phase Change Material (PCM) technology in a cascade configuration, transforming non-dispatchable renewable electricity like PV into thermal stored energy. The HYBRIDplus CSP configuration uses a high-temperature supercritical CO2 cycle at 600 ºC, providing higher efficiency and lower LCoE than current technology. The system also offers full dispatchability, allowing a higher renewable share in the energy system, and lower environmental impact, including lower CO2 emissions and water consumption.
  • R2M leads the dissemination and exploitation work package of HYBRIDplus and therefore will be able to investigate synergies with respect to GREENOLIVE dissemination, capacity building, and replication activities. (EN), Customizable AI-based in-line process monitoring platform for achieving zero-defect manufacturing in the PV industry. Platform-ZERO. Grant n° 101058459, HORIZON-CL4-2021-TWIN-TRANSITION-01 (EN) | Platform-ZERO is a self-learning, modular, and customizable in-line process monitoring platform designed to improve the third-generation photovoltaic (PV) industry. The platform uses non-destructive inspection methods and AI-based tools to detect, correct, and prevent pre-critical production faults. The consortium, including partners with advanced characterization of complex materials and European-level development leaders, will demonstrate the technology at TRL7. The platform aims to increase the quality and reduce costs of high-tech PV devices, increasing the competitiveness of the EU's PV industry and enabling PV to become a key energy source for Europe's transition towards climate-neutral energy generation.
  • R2M leads the dissemination and exploitation work package of Platform-ZERO and therefore will be able to investigate synergies with respect to GREENOLIVE dissemination, capacity building, and replication activities. (EN)
