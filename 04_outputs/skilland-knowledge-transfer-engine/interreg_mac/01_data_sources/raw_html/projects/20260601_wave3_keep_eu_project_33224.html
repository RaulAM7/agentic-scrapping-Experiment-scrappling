Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 064. Water management and water resource conservation (including river basin management, specific climate change adaptation measures, reuse, leakage reduction) ~ SUMMARY: SWAMED targets Priority 2 Specific Objective 2.3 (RSO 2.5) Access to water and sustainable water management Climate change, droughts, increasing population cause water scarcity affecting agriculture and ecosystems, increasing competition for water resources between rural and urban areas. SWAMED tackles these common challenges across the Mediterranean Countries. SWAMED codesigns in a multi stakeholder approach a water monitoring and management system for irrigated agriculture adaptable to local conditions in 4 pilot demonstration in Tunisia, Turkey, Egypt, Greece, intended for farmers and water management entities.The system includes at the level of farms or group of farms commercial level sensors for basic water quality, flow, soil nutrients, humidity, temperature, weather station, actuators and controllers, aerial drones for crop status, and commercial (level. flow) and new wireless sensors by UCLVs for water afferents (canals pipelines) integrity and theft, monitoring, and for water quality (pollutants) Satellite observation tools are complementing field data for field monitoring and small catchment evaluation Data are input to a Decision Support System based on Artificial Intelligence enabling the optimal water use sustainable and climate change resilient practices. This will result in at least 30% reduction of irrigation water (mis-)use and losses. Additional technological assets for data streams are drones and satellite observation. The prompt availability of online information enables dynamic water allocation and pricing schemes, and evidence based governance and policies.SWAMED evaluates activities through a new micro and farm level WEFE framework, Life cycle assessment, multifaceted cost benefit analysis. Gender, discrimination, and fundamental rights issues are taken care of by specific activities all along the project lifetime. Beneficiaries are farmers, water management and quality authorities, local communities, governance and policy actors ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Agriculture and fisheries and forestry, Sustainable management of natural resources, Water management
