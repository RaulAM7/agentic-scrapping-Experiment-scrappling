Progressive solutions in greener optimization of public lightning in EURO-MED area

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The ProLIGHTmed project aims to contribute to the promotion of climate change mitigation through optimization and facilitation of public lighting energy efficiency solutions in the Euro-MED area. Direct beneficiaries of the project will use the step-by-step, methodological approach and get access to tools and resources that could foster a more efficient implementation of local energy plans, integrating not only technical, but also socio-economic and environmental aspects. As a result of working packages four main outputs will be created during the project lifecycle. Defined joint strategy for improvement of energy efficiency and reduction of CO2 emission in public lighting of Euro-MED region, that should provide a joint approach and jointly developed analytical and implementation tools. During the strategy planning processes involvement of all project partners, associated partners and other national and local level stakeholders, will be established. Joint action plan and methodology for financing public lighting optimization projects will be developed. Using the value chain approach and based on different experiences of financing the implementation of activities related to the optimization of public lightning, project partners will recommend best models for financing mechanisms, targeting and EU and IPA countries of Euro-MED region. Specific focus will be given to the banking sector and establishing subventions schemes for support of investments in public lighting; Upscaled solution with final recommendations on optimization of energy efficiency and reduction of CO2 emissions in public lighting in Euro-Med region will be based on testing activities and implemented cross-border knowledge transferability campaign targeting optimization of public lighting energy efficiency and reduction of CO2 emission in EURO-MED region.

Description (FR):
Le projet ProLIGHTmed vise à promouvoir l’atténuation du changement climatique par l'optimisation et la facilitation des solutions d'efficacité énergétique de l'éclairage public dans la zone Euro-MED. Les bénéficiaires directs du projet utiliseront l'approche méthodologique étape par étape et auront accès aux outils et aux ressources qui pourraient favoriser une mise en œuvre plus efficace des plans énergétiques locaux, intégrant non seulement les aspects techniques, mais aussi socio-économiques et environnementaux. Quatre principaux résultats seront obtenus au cours de la vie du projet. Une stratégie commune définie pour l'amélioration de l'efficacité énergétique et la réduction des émissions de CO2 dans l'éclairage public de la région Euro-MED, qui devrait fournir une approche commune et des outils d'analyse et de mise en œuvre développés conjointement. Au cours des processus de planification de la stratégie, l'implication de tous les partenaires du projet, des partenaires associés et d'autres parties prenantes au niveau national et local sera établie. Un plan d'action commun et une méthodologie pour le financement des projets d'optimisation de l'éclairage public seront développés. En utilisant l'approche par la chaîne de valeur et en se basant sur les différentes expériences de financement liées à la mise en œuvre d'activités d'optimisation de l'éclairage public, les partenaires du projet recommanderont les meilleurs mécanismes de financement, ciblant les pays de l'UE et de l'IPA de la région Euro-MED. Une attention particulière sera accordée au secteur bancaire et à l'établissement de programmes de subventions pour soutenir les investissements dans l'éclairage public ; Une solution à grande échelle avec des recommandations finales sur l'optimisation de l'efficacité énergétique et la réduction des émissions de CO2 dans l'éclairage public dans la région Euro-MED sera basée sur des activités de test et une campagne transfrontalière de transférabilité des connaissances visant à optimiser l'efficacité énergétique de l'éclairage public et à réduire les émissions de CO2 dans la région EURO-MED.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During period 1, two steering committee meetings were held, the first day after the kick-off event, organised in Tuzi on 29/2-1/3/2024, and the second SC meeting was held in Setubal on June 18-19, 2024. Also, joint project management procedures were adopted by all project partners. As a result of the fact that certain project partners could start implementation only after the signing of the Subsidy contract , the implementation of the planned activities in period 1 was delayed, the real work started from the beginning of April 2024. After completion of tendering process for the TA expert and Local expert in public lighting investments planning the implementation of activities from WP1 have started. As a part of activity 1.1 the Activity leader has developed a Questionary related to analyse of the present situation of energy efficiency in public lighting in the targeted countries. in relation to Tuzi/Montenegro the inputs for joint report on mentioned topic have been provided to Activity leader but the final report on regional level will be postponed till the end of October 2024. Also the basic analyses of examples of best practices on management and planning of public lighting have been started. That analyses will contribute to achievements of Activity 1.2 - Development of joint solution (best practice) in the sector including cross-border methodology upscaling energy efficiency and reduction of CO2 emission in public lighting of EURO-MED region. The Activity 3.3 - Organisation of wither targeted campaign on promotion of "best practice" in energy efficiency in public lighting stared as planned. The visual identity outputs have been created by the Leader of the activity PP7-DiTNE. Within the framework of WP1 – Joint approach for improvement of energy efficiency in public lighting of EURO-MED region and the Activity 1.1, an analysis of the current state of public lighting at both national and local levels across targeted countries have been conducted. ENA, the Energy and Environment Agency of Arrábida (PP2) produced a questionnaire aimed at collecting information regarding the current state. The data collected through this questionnaire was used to evaluate existing resources, reports, databases, and previous projects related to public lighting. Partners completed the questionnaire as part of a desk research exercise, relying on available local and national information sources. This report, therefore, provides a summary of the state of the art in public lighting for each country. More detailed insights can be found in the attached questionnaires. ENA contributed with TUC by: developing a table for classification of the current technological solutions based on different criteria (efficiency, acquisition costs, maintenance costs, type of use, safety considerations, aesthetic considerations, Eu policy compliance, environmental protection adequation and integration with other uses). PP2-ENA developed the Public Lighting GIS registration tool to be implemented in each partner countries. With the support of the pilot partners, the tool was translated on local languages and adjusted. The lack of financial resources has been identified as a limiting factor for further development in this area. Appropriate financial mechanisms are essential to enable the adoption of advanced technologies and energy-efficient solutions in public lighting systems. This analysis provides an overview of various financial options that can be applied to support the optimization of public lighting in the municipalities of Tuzi and Akamas. Municipality of Lezhe and City of Kaštela are working on their reports and all data will be compiled in one joint document. PPs participated in activities related to finalisation of deliverables of WP1. Additional effort has been given to finalisation of activity A.1.4 where additional alternative financing schemes for Portugal and Italy will be presented. During this period, PPs worked on WP2 on market researches of the companies who should do the Feasibility study on investment in public lighting sector in piloting municipalities. PPs agreed to finalize the feasibility studies in piloting countries till October 2025 and start with tendering process for investments in the last three months of 2025. From the management point of view, LP co-organised the 4th SC and PP meeting, hosted by DITNE, organised in Brindisi on 18-19. June 2025. The event was organized over two half-days, on the afternoon of June 18 and the morning of June 19, 2025, with the active participation of the Project Officer remotely. Held at Palazzo Guerrieri in Brindisi, the first half-day opened with a comprehensive review of project activities completed so far, pending tasks, and planned actions for period 4. As Communication Lead, PP7 delivered a key presentation showcasing the communication activities successfully implemented and the results achieved during this reporting period. The presentation also set out the next steps for period 4, which include not only the regular updates of the project’s social media platforms, but also the dissemination of the third project newsletter and the sharing of practical guidelines for the creation and application of billboards and plaques for the investments to be implemented by the pilot leaders. On the second half-day, PP7 also coordinated the study visit, which began at Palazzo Guerrieri with an introduction by the Head of the Public Works Department of the Municipality of Brindisi. The presentation highlighted the municipality's ongoing efforts to reduce emissions through public infrastructure upgrades and introduced City Green Light Srl, a company engaged in a public-private partnership with the Municipality of Brindisi to implement energy efficiency interventions across the local territory. Following this session, PP7 guided partners to the Franco Fanuzzi Municipal Stadium, one of the recent flagship sites for public lighting interventions in Brindisi, where participants took part in an on-site inspection and technical overview. As communication lead, PP7 monitored the implementation of the Communication Strategy and ensured partners used official communication and promotional materials correctly (templates, posters, and leaflets). PP7 managed updates to the project website and social media channels (Facebook, Instagram and YouTube). Since launching the Instagram page on May 13, 2024, PP7 has published 31 contents (posts, reels, stories), gaining a total of 33 followers, a reach of 1444, 2699 visualizations, and 77 content interactions. Since the launch of the Facebook page (May 13, 2024), PP7 has overseen the publication of 35 posts, reaching a total of 62 followers, a reach of 3566, 9307 visualizations, and 348 content interactions. Additionally, PP7 has implemented the ProLIGHTmed YouTube channel, publishing 2 videos and reaching a total of 3 subscribers. At the same time, the official project website has been regularly updated by PP7, with a total of 18 news articles published, and 2 project newsletters has been disseminated: the first the the end of reporting period 1 and the second at the end of reporting period 2. The third newsletter is currently being finalized and will be released to mark the conclusion of reporting period 3. Finally, on the occasion of the 4th project meeting in Brindisi, PP7 produced a dedicated event video, which was shared on the project's social media accounts and the DiTNE's own channels.

  • EcoEnLight was implemented by the municipality of Almada between 2011 and 2013 and was focused on improving the energy efficiency of Almada’s public lighting system, which accounts for more than 60% of the municipality’s energy consumption. During the EcoEnLight project, a smart tele-management system for Almada’s public lighting system was procured and implemented. This Telemanagement system allows to remotely manage public lighting intensity in Almada by adjusting the luminous flux of all lights throughout the day. This way, a more efficient and tailored energy consumption coming from the public lighting system can be ensured, which fully takes into account the needs of the citizens who use Almada’s roads during day and night.
  • The success of the EcoEnlight approach led to the development of the EcoEnlight II project which is currently under preparation and on the pipeline for financing of 3335 light points (this time adding to the tele-management system the replacement of lamps to LED lighting). An overall investment of roughly €1,500,000 for an annual saving of €170,000 and a payback period of 8 years.
