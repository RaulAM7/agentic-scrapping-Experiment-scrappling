improving WEtland GOvernance through a COmmunity Of Practice

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Mediterranean Wetlands are extremely vulnerable ecosystems, often under environmental protection. The Ramsar Convention defines them as one of the most productive and biodiverse environments in the world, providing the habitat on which countless species depend. They are highly subject to external pressures including i) the state of health of the river basins to which they refer; ii) intensive agriculture causing water consumption and water and soil pollution; iii) heavy urbanization. Wetlands are often challenged by the poor coordination and capacity of institutions to manage complex territorial dynamics needing different levels of planning. Thus, the active protection of wetlands biodiversity requires overcoming the current sectoral approach and addressing the problem systemically. WE GO COOP addresses these issues by promoting the implementation of the Wetland Contract, a multilevel collaborative governance tool tested in several EU-funded projects, fostering coordination between organizations previously involved and new central stakeholders. The project will: 1- Set up a Community of Practice (CoP) amongst actors engaged in Wetland Contracts at the Mediterranean level, capitalizing on the outcomes of former EU-funded projects, sharing challenges and solutions; 2- Exchange experiences, methodologies, and strategies, and transfer the Wetland Contract tool to new contexts in the Euro-Med region (Croatia, Portugal, Albania) and the South and Middle East Med region. Transnational cooperation becomes the tool through which WE GO COOP forms the CoP uniting different perspectives facing similar challenges, enabling the project to meet the needs of wetlands on a large scale, ensuring the transferability of its results. WE GO COOP is expected to: 1- Improve knowledge about wetland collaborative governance processes, 2- Improve collaboration between researchers, policy-makers, experts, environmental associations, key stakeholders, and communities.

Description (FR):
Les zones humides méditerranéennes sont des écosystèmes extrêmement vulnérables, souvent sous protection environnementale. La Convention de Ramsar les définit comme l'un des environnements les plus productifs et les plus riches en biodiversité au monde. Ils sont fortement soumis à des pressions externes dont i)l'état de santé des bassins fluviaux auxquels ils se rapportent; ii)agriculture intensive entraînant consommation d'eau et pollution; iii)forte urbanisation. Les zones humides sont souvent confrontées à une mauvaise coordination et capacité des institutions à gérer d’une facon concertée et integrée les dynamiques territoriales complexes nécessitant de différents niveaux de planification. WE GO COOP répond à ces enjeux en promouvant la mise en œuvre du Contrat Zones Humides, un outil de gouvernance collaborative à plusieurs niveaux testé dans de projets financés par l'UE, favorisant la coordination entre les organisations impliquées et les nouvelles parties prenantes centrales. Le projet vise à: i)Mettre en place une Communauté de Pratique (CoP) entre les acteurs engagés dans les Contrats Zones Humides au niveau méditerranéen, en capitalisant sur les résultats des anciens projets financés par l'UE, en partageant les défis et les solutions; ii)Echanger des expériences, méthodologies et stratégies, et transférer l'outil Contrat Zones Humides à de nouveaux contextes dans la région Euro-Med (Croatie, Portugal, Albanie) et dans la région Sud et Moyen-Orient Med. La coopération transnationale devient l'outil par lequel WE GO COOP forme la CoP réunissant différentes perspectives face à des défis similaires, répondant aux besoins des zones humides à grande échelle, assurant la transférabilité de ses résultats. WE GO COOP est censé: i)Améliorer les connaissances sur les processus de gouvernance collaborative des zones humides; ii)Améliorer la collaboration entre chercheurs, décideurs, associations environnementales, acteurs clés et communautés.

The materials were made available in editable formats so that partners could translate them into local languages. The project templates in .doc and .ppt format were prepared and made available on the project's Basecamp platform. The social media channels Instagram, Facebook and Linkedin have been activated and several posts have already been published. The communication strategy has been drafted and is scheduled to be delivered by September 2024, as planned by the JS. To optimize communication activities and integrate with those of the Mission and other thematic projects, the Communication Officer participated in the meeting organized by the Natural Heritage Mission on the 26th and 27th of June 2024 in Rovinj, Croatia. The exhibition on the collaborative governance of wetlands was set up in 7 locations, each chosen by the partners in their own area. The exhibition consisted of posters on previous EU projects on collaborative governance and pilot cases and practices described by each partner. In the end, 8 posters were created for the case studies and practices and 4 on past European projects. The deliverable was concluded, as planned, by the end of June. Concerning the WP2 and the activities in synergy with the Mission, a team member participated in the Natural Heritage Mission's 1st Summer School organized from the 18th to the 20th of June 2024, in Piran, Slovenia. The 2nd semester put in practice all management and administrative procedures and elaboration of the main project outputs’ deliverables and studies of the next phases of the project. All partners worked on mapping the most relevant stakeholders to be part of in the Community of Practice on Wetland Contracts as well as initiated communications with them (Activity 1.2 & 1.4). -The Communication Manager, UNIROMA3, completed the structure and content of the Virtual Collaborative Platform as well as the creation of a promotion video for Wetland Contracts and their significance (Activity 1.3). Work Package 2 - A two-day capacity-building seminar was organised and implemented in Seville (1-2 of October 2024) which established the foundations of a Community of Practice in the Mediterranean and strengthened the capacities through previous experience in the implementation and management of wetland contracts (Activity 2.1). - ANATOLIKI, UNIROMA3, FAMP have started the collection of information about the wetland contract processes in their region/province and the review of the outputs produced by the processes (including field trips and surveys) as well as the organisation of the 2 days Symposium (one event per contract area) (Activity 2.2). - A Guideline for Transferring Roadshows was produced, by the responsible partner FAMP, for the development of a governance strategy and local protocol for wetland contracts (Activity 2.3). All other partners contributed with comments to it. In addition, the experienced partners (MEDWET, ANATOLIKI, UNIROMA3, FAMP) initiated the procedures of transferring the roadshows to the non-experienced partners (NPVL, PPNEA, RCDI). At the same time, the non-experienced partners initiated their procedures of mapping and involvement key stakeholders as well as participating in related events in order to disseminate the WEGOCOOP Project. Local protocols have started to be produced as well as a strategies for Wetland Contracts setting up (2.3.1). -Furthermore, the LP, ANATOLIKI S.A., assumed all necessary activities to monitor carbon footprint within the project (ie. the submission of the partners’ corresponding semester Reports) as well as to encourage the adoption of adequate procedures to minimize GHG emissions (Activity 2.6). -Representatives of the projecton behalf og the project partners participated onsite or online in the Natural Heritage Mission's Annual Institutional Dialogue, held in Malaga (28-29 November 2024) (Activity 2.7). Activity 2.5 During the 2nd Thematic Seminar in Thessaloniki, partners prepared the Final Conference in France as well as MedWet coordinated the draft agenda and partners provided content.
