Mediterranean Network for the Promotion of Sustainability & Authenticity in Olive Oil Sector

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Despite the fact that olive oil products are the most important agri-food products in the Med Area being the backbone of socio-economic and cultural life, serious difficulties had been encountered in exploiting the huge symbolic capital and integrating environmental friendly methods into SMEs activated in olive oil sector. “OliveOilMedNet” project main objective is to improve the olive oil sector by introducing innovative and effective ways through testing & validating relevant novel and eco-friendly concepts (e.g. instruments, methods, models) on the field of “sustainability” and “authenticity”. To attain its’ objective, the “OliveOilMedNet” project brings together Public Authorities, Producers’ Associations and Research Institutions from 7 Mediterranean countries, adopting, further building and go beyond on the existing practices that have been developed in other projects, by strengthen the capacity of a) the national/regional/local authorities engaged in the policymaking in the olive oil sector, and b) the olive oil companies to produce more competitive, “authentic” and “green” olive oil products, in collaboration with academic / research institutions and social society organization in the logic of 4helix, through the: - identification and certification of the variety/botanical identity, specific organoleptic and quality characteristics, and geographical origin (authenticity) of extra virgin olive oils from different olive cultivars in the Mediterranean Area with the use of a classification model capable for olive cultivar/geographical prediction, - development of an innovative network of stakeholders throughout the olive oil chain for the promotion of sustainable production and consumption practices to mitigate climate change impacts on the environment, - introduction of eco-friendly cultivation techniques, sustainable technology solutions in olive groves and olive oil mills, and the use of socio-economic and environmental evaluation tools and methodologies.

Description (FR):
Malgré le fait que les produits de l'huile d'olive soient les produits agroalimentaires les plus importants dans l'Espace Med, de difficultés ont été rencontrées dans l'exploitation de capital symbolique et l'intégration de méthodes respectueuses de l'environnement dans les PME actives du secteur de l'huile d'olive. L'objectif principal du "OliveOilMedNet" est d'améliorer le secteur de l'huile d'olive par l’introduction de moyens en testant et en validant des concepts nouveaux et respectueux de l'environnement dans le domaine de la "durabilité" et de "l'authenticité". Pour atteindre son objectif, « OliveOilMedNet » rassemble les autorités publiques, les associations de producteurs et les institutions de recherche de 7 pays méd, adoptant, développant et dépassant les pratiques existantes (d'autres projets), par le renforcement de la capacité a) des autorités engagées dans l'élaboration des politiques dans le secteur de l'huile d'olive, et b) des sociétés oléicoles pour produire des produits d'huile d'olive plus compétitifs, «authentiques» et «verts», en collaboration avec des institutions universitaires / de recherche et l'organisation sociale et sociétale dans la logique de 4helix, à travers : - l’identification et la certification de l'identité variétale/botanique, des caractéristiques organoleptiques et qualitatives spécifiques et de l'origine géographique (authenticité) des huiles d'olive extra vierges de différents cultivars d'olive dans la région méd à l'aide d'un modèle de classification, - le développement d'un réseau innovant d'acteurs tout à la chaîne de l'huile d'olive pour la promotion de pratiques de production et de consommation durables afin d'atténuer les impacts du changement climatique, - l’introduction de techniques de culture respectueuses de l'environnement, de solutions technologiques durables dans les oliveraies et les moulins à huile d'olive, et l’utilisation d'outils et de méthodologies d'évaluation socio-économique et environnementale.

Actual Achievements (EN):
WP1 LP prepared the Detailed Plan of the Act. 1.1 accompanied with a "Data Collection Template" and presented it to the partnership during the Kick-off Meeting. Until 06.2024 LP, PP4, PP5, PP8, PP9 and PP10 worked on the filling of the “Data Collection Template” and made all relevant contacts with various stakeholders to ensure that the data collected is representative of the national reality. Moreover, specific information has been collected about relevant instruments, strategies and solutions already developed/ implemented in the framework of previous projects. Furthermore, PP2 and PP10 prepared the Detailed Plan of the Act. 1.2. and 2 specific methods for the fruit and physicochemical characterisation of olive oils. These two methodology guidelines or protocols are presented to all partners in the Kick-off Meeting and will be applied in the next olive campaign, 2024/25. PP9 completed the reception and setup of the NIRS equipment (FOSS, DS2500L, Serial no. 91879788) has been carried out, necessary for the physical-chemical characterization of the olive oils produced. WP3 In the framework of Act. 3.1, LP organised an online Meeting for the establishment the Steering Committee of OliveOilMedNet project on 08/02/2024. All partners participated and the Project Steering Committee (PSC) and the Scientific Advisory Board (SAB) were appointed. Also the tasks of Project Steering Committee was determined and the following persons were appointed: PC, FM, CM, RAC, CFC, DPO and ALP. The Kick-off Meeting of the project was organised (hybrid) by LP on 13 & 14/05/2024 in the held of the Chamber. All partners participated and the work plan was agreed. Activity leaders presented the detail plan of the activities. LP prepared the Risk Management & Quality Assurance Plan which describe specific processes, roles/responsibilities to ensure the successful implementation of the project. Furthermore, PP9 selected 18 farmers and 2 olive mills in Andalusia to participate in the test phases of the project and prepared a seminar on harvesting and production techniques of early olive oils. The project web site is ready and feeded with information photographes and news about the project. Also social media are created. Press releases were published in project website and the institutional web site. Communication guidelines are followed. On 13.05.2024 an info day was organised by LP in the held of the Chamber. In this event LP and PP2 after the project presentation discussed with SMEs highlighting the benefits that they will be emerged from the implementation of OliveOilMedNet project. In this event olive farmers, olive oil producers and a representative from Region of Epirus participated informed about the project and its objectives. Moreover, a WhatsApp network has been established between farmers and olive oil millers selected in Spain by the University of Jaen. Finally, in the framework of Act. 3.3, LP, PP7 and PP9 participated in the event "SHAPING SUSTAINABLE INNOVATION IN THE MEDITERRANEAN" organised by ISE in Marseille on 18 and 19 May 2024, in World Trade Center. In more detail Preveza Chamber participated in the Launch of the Community of Practice of the Innovative Sustainable Economy Mission and in the 1st edition of the MED Innovation Day. Based on the above, the implementation of the project had started smoothly and on time contributing to the promotion of sustainability, authenticity and eco-friendly cultivation and production concepts in the olive oil sector, which is the main aim of WP1. Finally, all the above tasks has been designed and implemented with the active participation of all transnational project’s partners. The project's specific objectives are partly achieved. We are already testing methodologies of authenticity in a great nummber of producers in all participating countries. Moreover, the preliminary framework for the organisation of the Capacity building activities was dicusssed outlining key objectives and planned actions. The development of the e-network platform has started The preliminary study has been finilised, with evaluated data, from the participating countries (Greece, Italy, France, Cyprus, Albania, Portugal and Spain), regarding the folowing subjects: - Olive Oil Description / Current situation - Prospects & Trends of the Olive – Oil Sector - Identified instruments, strategies, tools for the authenticity certification of olive oils - Eco-friendly cultivation techniques and production processes in the olivewas completed with the contribution of partners that completed the Data collection template with information concerning their country/region. The activity 1.2 is under finilisation. Two methodologies are developing concerning a)the identification and certification of geographical origin of extra virgin olive oils,b) the use eco-friendly cultivation techniques and sustainable technological solutions and the use of the socio-economic and envir. evaluation tools. All PPs invited olive oil producers to participate in the pilot activity of the project. The specialized staff of every partner, with knowledge in the field, contacted the interested olive oil producers and confirmed their eligibility. Moreover all PPs started collecting olive oil samples. The collected samples were recorded, stored and sent to the laboratory for analyses.The sample collection of all PPs concerned specific local varieties. The data base with the results of chemical and organoleptic analysis is developed. The preliminary framework for the organisation of the Capacity building activities was dicusssed outlining key objectives and planned actions.The development of the e-network platform has started. The project web site and project’ s social media were feeded with information photographs and news about the project. Press releases were published. All partners disseminate the project in their communication channels. Finally, the project video was developed and the 4 pages leaflet was designed. Also yhe project was disseminated in a national chanel and an info day was organised to informed stakeholders about the project. Partnership participate in ISE CoP gathering, also an interview was given and the the video presenting the Cluster of Agri-food System was developed. Partnership make efforts to reduce the emmission carbon footprint. Regarding the certification of the authenticity of extra virgin olive oil (act 1.3), all PPs selected the appropriate beneficiaries (farmers and mill owners). Olive oil samples from selected local monovarietal extra virgin olive oils, collected during the two sampling periods of the first year (2024–2025) in the framework of the test phase, were recorded, stored, and transferred for laboratory analysis. These samples contribute both to the Olive Oil Composition Data Set and to the development of the authenticity model. LP and P8 sent samples to PP2, PP4 sent samples to P9, and P5 sent samples to P6. PP2, PP6, PP9, and PP10, which have the necessary laboratories, along with P7 through a subcontractor, carried out the chemical and organoleptic analyses. Only at P9, the sensory evaluation of oils from this first campaign is still pending. The results of these analyses were transmitted to P2 for further integration into the project’s digital outputs. This activity supports the identification of authentic extra virgin olive oils. Concerning sustainable practices (act 1.4), PP9 requested that all PPs select 10 beneficiaries and collect data related to farming and processing techniques. Most PPs gathered and submitted detailed information on cultivation methods, production techniques, and marketing practices of their selected beneficiaries. The completed data, structured in an Excel file, was then submitted to PP9 for assessment. The results of the above analyses of monovarietal extra virgin olive oil samples, regarding authenticity, have been compiled into a comprehensive dataset that will serve as the foundation for the online Authentic Olive Data Bank (D.2.1.1). The content of the Sustainable Olive Oil Growing & Production Methodology Handbook (D.2.2.1) was determined by P9 and agreed upon by the PPs, with some PPs contributing to the preparation of specific chapters. PP4 organized the 3rd Steering Committee Meeting, held on 3–4 April 2025 in Alentejo, Portugal. PPs met to review project progress and take decisions. In addition, the 1st Hybrid Peer Learning Event and the 1st Demonstration Event were (act 3.1) held on 3 and 4 April respectively, also in Alentejo. Both events focused on olive oil authenticity. PPs actively engaged in discussions and knowledge exchange during these events. The Demonstration Event attracted a large number of producers and stakeholders from 10 different countries, many of whom were also participating in the 14th International Extra Virgin Olive Oil Competition – Ovibeja Award 2025, one of the most prestigious international olive oil competitions in the world. Regarding the E-Training (act 3.1) component, PP7 provided guidelines for the development of seminar materials. The proposed topics were reviewed and validated by the consortium, laying the foundation for the upcoming digital learning modules. Regarding D3.2.1 e networking Platform, P5 made a presentation in the 3rd Project Meeting and the content was agreed. PP5 prepared the action plan (act 3.2) for the establishment of the OliveOilMedNet. P7 contributed to drafting the Action Plan and took full responsibility for preparing the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU), which will serve as a key framework for future cooperation within the Med Olive Oil Network. Moreover, LP and P8 contributed to the finalisation of both the Action Plan and the MoU to ensure the long-term sustainability of the Network. Furthermore, PPs developed contacts with various entities in the olive oil sector to attract them to join the Network (Activity 3.2). Several expressions of interest were received, supporting the establishment of the Network. In particular, LP, P2, P4, P7, P8, and P10 contributed to an Excel database of stakeholders, which includes declarations of interest from parties wishing to join the project’s MoU. On communication, LP supported all project partners in adhering to Euro-MED visibility rules and compiled the 2024 communication reports and data. As part of dissemination, LP uploaded materials to the project’s website and social media platforms, sharing information, photos, and event updates. Press releases were published, and all partners promoted the project through their respective communication channels. A highlight was PP9’s project presentation at EXPOLIVA 2025. Finally, the project was represented in several events under the Innovative Sustainable Economy (ISE) Mission, including the 3rd ISE Community of Practice (Sarajevo, April 2025) and several online meetings of the ISE Mentoring & Twinning Programme and Communication Network. These engagements strengthened partnerships and increased the project’s visibility. The PPs also participated in the carbon footprint assessment activity, coordinated by Partner 09 (University of Jaén), contributing data and expertise to support the environmental performance analysis of olive oil production.

(protected designation of origin) or P.G.I. (protected geographical indication). The main aim of the project is to efficiently promote the top quality and the competitive advantages of the local olive oils to domestic and international markets, focusing mainly on propping up their entrance to new markets and commercial networks. Furthermore, the project contributes to the establishment of a Network in the Olive Sector in order to develop and offer to the local olive oil companies innovative and global authenticity certification methods, tools, structures, and services.
  • The proposed project has synergies with the implemented activities and goals achieved by the “Authentic-Olive-Net” project, as intends to build on the available knowledge regarding the methodology of authenticity and goes beyond it through the adaptation and testing to a larger number of beneficiaries, users and territories in the Med Area, as the olive oil samples that will be used they will concern not only 2 countries (Greece and Italy) but 7 countries and different types of olive oils. The result of this action is ensured by the P2 partner that had a critical role also in “Authentic-Olive-Net” project.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The same as english
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The Territorial Agenda 2030 underlines the importance of and provides orientation for strategic spatial planning and calls for strengthening the territorial dimension of sector policies at all governance levels. It “requires” for actions to encourage communities, municipalities, counties, regions and Member States to cooperate and strengthening socio-economic prosperity, innovation capacity and global competitiveness across Europe. Promotes stable cross-border and transnational cooperation in macro-regional, national, regional and local development strategies. The “OliveOilMedNet” project addresses all the above mentioned issues, as its actions concern transnational cooperation aiming at strengthening territorial cohesion across borders, strengthening the territorial dimension of olive oil sector policies and promoting an integrated and multi-level approach with the collaboration of academic / research institutions and social society organization in the logic of 4helix approach.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The same as english
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion The project contributes with the EU Strategy for the Alpine Region (EUSALP), as it will contribute to the objective one (1) promoting the innovation through the connection between research centers and enterprises in the olive oil sector. Also it will contribute to objective three (3) by promoting the protection of the environment and avoiding the overexploitation of natural resources.
    • Other "European Enterprise Network - Enterprise Europe Network (EEN)”
    • EEN is the largest European network for innovation and entrepreneurship, supporting SMEs to enhance their competitiveness through innovation and co-funded by the European Union's COSME and Horizon 2020 Programmes. The “OliveOilMedNet” project will contribute to the EEN objectives, as its main goal concerns the improvement of the sustainability of the olive oil sector and the support to the olive oil companies to produce more competitive, “authentic” and “green” products.
    • Other Horizon 2020
    • The general objective of Horizon 2020 is to help to build a society and an economy based on knowledge and innovation, to provide funding for research, development and innovation. The “OliveOilMedNet” project contributes to this objective introducing innovative and sustainable solutions, tools and artificial intelligence models in agro-food science where researchers will process vast amounts of experimental data into computational models and generate accurate predictions for authenticity related issues.
    • Other EU Rural Action Plan
    • The Rural Action Plan aims to foster territorial cohesion and create new opportunities to attract innovative businesses, and leverage the role of sustainable agriculture and diversified economic activities. The The “OliveOilMedNet” project contributes to this objective as encourage territorial cohesion and also support SMES of the olive oil sector to use innovative and sustainable solutions during the cultivation and production processes.
