Description

Description (FR):
Le tourisme est l'un des moteurs de l'économie méditerranéenne et pionnier des technologies de transformation numérique. Covid-19 l'a sévèrement frappé, montrant sa vulnérabilité et sa non-résilience aux risques imprévisibles et la rigidité de la gouvernance pour y réagir et les atténuer, puis exiger plus d'informations au temps opportun pour les politiques et les décideurs. LIBECCIO fait face à ce défi en améliorant la gouvernance touristique, la gestion des destinations puis la résilience face aux risques. L'utilisation des technologies de transformation numérique pour un paradigme touristique plus efficace, durable et résilient vise à fournir aux décideurs politiques une prise de décision éclairée basée sur les données et un impact positif sur la mise en œuvre des politiques. LIBECCIO suit une approche intersectorielle et transdisciplinaire en Euro Med en impliquant la communauté touristique Q Helix dans la Plateforme d'Innovation Touristique Euro Med (ETIP) pour l'expérimentation de solutions adaptées suivant une méthodologie Living Lab. Destination Management Support System (DMSS) est une plate-forme Web sémantiquement enrichie qui intègre des indicateurs de durabilité et des analyses de Big Data pour exploiter la prise de politiques et décision basées sur les données. Le test DMSS dans les thématiques touristiques et les enseignements tirés sont évalués par l'ETIP qui élabore un plan de capitalisation sur le transfert et l'opérationnalisation des plateformes ETIP et DMSS dans les écosystèmes locaux, après la fin du Projet. LIBECCIO bénéficie à ETIP de capacités institutionnelles communautaires, de solutions, d'outils, de méthodologies et de politiques. Il capitalise les résultats des projets précédents en dotant les administrateurs publics régionaux et locaux et les OSA de solutions innovantes, basées sur des indicateurs de durabilité et le Big Data en temps réel, permettant une analyse comparative et une prise de décision au temps opportun.

Description (EN):
Tourism is one of the driver of the global economy and an early adopter of digital transformation technologies in Mediterranean basin. Covid-19 pandemic hit severely, showing vulnerability and non-resilience to unpredictable risks and governance rigidity in reacting and mitigating them then finally demanding more timely information for policy and decision makers. LIBECCIO faces this challenge enhancing tourism governance, destination management and sustainability then resilience against risks too. Through digital transformation, it addresses the need for improved destination management, supporting policy makers with informed data driven decision making, positively impacting the policies implementation for a more sustainable tourism paradigm. It follows an intersectoral and transdisciplinary approach in Euro Med by involving together with partners the Q Helix tourism community, through the Euro Med Tourism Innovation Platform (ETIP), in testing and transfer planning of suitable solutions following a Living Lab methodology. LIBECCIO Destination Management Support System (DMSS) is a semantically enriched web-enabled platform that integrates sustainability indicator datasets and Big Data analytics to exploit data driven policy and decision making. The DMSS test in thematic tourism partner areas and the lessons learned are assessed and evaluated by ETIP that draws up a capitalization plan how the partners will transfer and make operational in local ecosystems the ETIP platform and its main tool DMSS after the end of the Project. LIBECCIO benefits ETIP with community institutional capacities, through solutions, methodologies and policies for a more efficient, sustainable and resilient tourism paradigm. It capitalizes previous projects outcomes equipping regional and local public administrators and BSOs with innovative solutions, integrating tourist sustainability indicators and real time Big Data, enabling timely analysis, advanced benchmarking and decision making.

Actual Achievements (FR):
During the 3rd RP, the LIBECCIO consortium made substantial and well-coordinated progress across all work packages, combining technical development, methodological design, stakeholder engagement, and communication activities. The period was marked by both consolidation of earlier achievements and preparation for the pilot testing phase of the project. A central focus was the advancement of the DMSS. Several partners contributed with expertise, data, and methodological insights to refine the platform’s architecture and functionalities. ATHENA (PP3) concentrated on data acquisition processes and visualization tools for stakeholders such as policy makers and enterprises, while RDA(PP7) and RAIS(PP9) provided inputs on indicators, data availability, and system testing. The RWG (PP10) and ERR (LP) helped coordinate the technical discussions and the integration of institutional perspectives.Together, these contributions ensured that the DMSS moved from conceptual requirements toward a more concrete, test-ready tool. Closely linked to this was the development of the Living Lab methodology, which will guide the testing of the DMSS in real-world contexts.CNA Abruzzo (PP2) took a leading role by drafting and presenting the initial methodology at the Patras consortium meeting. This emphasized a participatory approach, where local authorities, tourism operators, SMEs, and research institutions collaborate to validate and adapt services dynamically, using real data and seasonal variations.Other partners enriched the methodology with contextual feedback, ensuring that it would be adaptable across diverse territories.By the end of the period, the methodology had been collectively refined and finalized, creating a shared operational roadmap for the next phase. Parallel to the technical and methodological work, the consortium devoted significant energy to stakeholder engagement and communication. A series of Info Days were organized in multiple locations (Patras, Terrassa, Nessebar, Kotor, East Sarajevo, Aigio, Agrinio, Rimini, and Cesenatico) reaching audiences that included municipal and regional authorities, tourism businesses, universities, NGOs, cultural institutions, and citizens.These events played a dual role: they raised awareness about the LIBECCIO project and its objectives and promoted the (ETIP), inviting stakeholders to join and contribute data for DMSS testing.The CCIT (PP5) consolidated the reports of all Info Days, while local partners tailored their events to the specific needs and dynamics of their territories. Beyond local outreach, LIBECCIO strengthened its visibility and networking at the Euro-MED level, through the participation of PP in thematic seminars and international events (D4T in Brussels, CircletheMed in Athens, and EuroMed cluster innovation workshops).These activities enabled exchanges with other projects, opening the way for synergies, joint actions, and shared learning within the broader community. Communication activities intensified: social media accounts were launched or strengthened, newsletters were circulated, and a promotional video was produced by the Lead Partner. Local and national media were engaged, ensuring broader dissemination of project results. In summary, the third reporting period consolidated LIBECCIO’s technical, methodological, and communication activities. The consortium advanced on the DMSS design and the Living Lab methodology, but also ensured that the project’s vision was rooted in local territories, supported by engaged stakeholders, and connected to Euro-MED networks. This integrated approach reinforced LIBECCIO’s positioning as a driver of innovation in sustainable and data-driven tourism management across the Mediterranean. During the 3rd RP, two deliverables were successfully finalized, while two others remain under development. The Info Day Report (D.3.1.1), originally scheduled for Period 2, was completed in this reporting period. The report documents the organization of 22 Info Days held across partner regions, fully aligned with LIBECCIO’s communication, dissemination, and stakeholder engagement strategy. These events engaged stakeholders from the Quadruple Helix model – public authorities, academia, businesses, and civil society – with the objective of presenting the LIBECCIO project and its two core instruments, the Euro Med Tourism Innovation Platform (ETIP) and the Destination Management Support System (DMSS). The Methodology (D.2.2.1) was also finalized, albeit shortly after its official deadline. It provides the methodological framework guiding co-creation and testing activities within the project. Meanwhile, the DMSS software and Technical Test Report (D.2.1.1 + D.2.1.2) are currently available in draft form and are expected to be finalized by the end of Sept 2025. The CAST Modules and Guidelines (D.2.3.1) remain in draft status pending feedback from ATHENA, with a formal submission deadline set before Dec 2025.

At the beginning of the project, the pre-contracting phase to complete the process of final definition of the Application Form with the supporting documents was finalised. It involved the entire project partnership with the collection of due documents. All partners signed the official legal agreements, set up the internal work teams and the LIBECCIO Management structure, designed the members of the Steering Committee. Given the delays in the procedures to be able to carry out the first project meeting for which it was scheduled to take place within 3 months of the start of activities, an online pre-kick off meeting was organized on 27th Feb. 2024 to meet all the partnership and start planning activities. The kick-off meeting was organized in Bologna on March 26 and 27, 2024 during which the Steering Committee was installed. Before the kick-off meeting, an Initial High Level International Conference for presenting the project was held on the morning of March 26, attended by several stakeholders with different experiences. PPs participated with some of their representatives either in presence or remotely. The partner PP10 Region of Western Greece participated remotely only to the Conference. Each partner identified the area that will be the subject of the pilot project. Following the Steering Committee's decision to unify two task force group to analyze the user requirements and functional specifications of the up scaled Destination Management Support System (DMSS), all partners provided inputs contributing to the drafting of the report "Preliminary identified DMSS commercial planning, service quality and marketing actions outputs/services” which was released on June 28. A main element of the strategy is to refocus government policy within the EU framework, proposing the establishment of a European Agency for Tourism that will be responsible among others to provide “the EU and its member states with factual overview and data for policymakers, enabling them to devise informed strategies based on collected and analyzed tourism data”, and to support “the tourism ecosystem by sharing good practices to make informed decisions about improved tourism policies”. LIBECCIO proposed DMSS drives towards this direction enabling data driven informed decision making. A second element of the strategy is its targeting the transition to sustainable, responsible and smart tourism, specifically calling the EC to bring ETIS into operation, “to equip it with a permanent governance structure and introduce it to tourism destinations, with static indicators and real-time data for their management and evaluation”. LIBECCIO addresses this element through its sustainability indicator framework based on ETIS and the utilization of both static indicators and real time Big Data analysis for informed decision making.
