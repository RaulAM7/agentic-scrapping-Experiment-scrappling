Adapting IUCN Green List Standard to the Mediterranean Marine Protected Areas

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
The “GreenList4MMPAs” Project aims to adapt, for the first time, the IUCN Green List Standard (GL) to the Mediterranean marine context. The GL is the first global and only universal Standard of best practice for area-based conservation. It aims to provide an international benchmark for quality that motivates improved effectiveness and achievement of conservation objectives. GreenList4MMPAs will develop specific indicators and methods to assess marine protected areas (MPAs), providing an actionable and useful tool for MPA managers and practitioners. The common challenge that GreenList4MMPAs will tackle is the need for Mediterranean MPAs to enhance their capacities in preserving marine biodiversity and ecosystems in order to mitigate the overall degradation of the Mediterranean marine environment (fully in line with the Programme S.O. 2.7). GreenList4MMPAs will support Mediterranean MPAs in achieving their conservation and social objectives through a reliable system for the assessment of the effectiveness, based on a tool that will ensure MPA effective management. The main project’s outputs will be the Mediterranean MPAs-tailored IUCN Green List Set of Indicators and the establishment of the MED MPAs EAGLs (a committee of experts trained by IUCN that examine protected areas’ applications for the Green List Standard). These outputs will largely benefit local public authorities such as MPAs’ management bodies, but also national public authorities (e.g. Ministries and technical agencies) and IUCN, which represents globally the largest international organisation about nature conservation. The “GreenList4MMPAs” Project will adopt an approach based on the use of a large number of case studies in seven EU countries, two IPA countries, and six Third Med countries (thanks to an additional funding by IUCN), in order to represent all the existing seven Ecoregions in the Mediterranean Sea. In this perspective only a true transnational approach can allow project’s goals to be met.

Description (FR):
GreenList4MMPAs vise à adapter, pour la première fois, le Standard de la Liste Verte (GL) de l'UICN au contexte marin méditerranéen. GL est la première norme mondiale et universelle de meilleures pratiques pour la conservation des zones naturelles. Il vise à fournir une référence internationale de qualité qui motive l'amélioration de l'efficacité et la réalisation des objectifs de conservation. Il développe des indicateurs et des méthodes spécifiques pour évaluer les aires marines protégées (AMP), fournissant un outil exploitable et utile pour les gestionnaires et les praticiens. Le défi commun que GreenList4MMPAs va aborder est la nécessité pour les AMP Med de renforcer leurs capacités à préserver la biodiversité et les écosystèmes marins afin d'atténuer la dégradation globale de l'environnement marin méditerranéen (entièrement conforme au S.O. 2.7). Le projet soutiendra les AMP Med dans la réalisation de leurs objectifs de conservation et sociaux grâce à un système fiable d'évaluation de l'efficacité, basé sur un outil qui assurera une gestion efficace. Les principaux résultats du projet seront l'ensemble d'indicateurs de la GL adaptés aux AMP Med et l'établissement de “AMP MED EAGL” (un comité d'experts formés par l'UICN qui examine les candidatures des AMP pour le Standard GL). Ces résultats bénéficieront aux autorités publiques locales telles que les organes de gestion des AMP, mais aussi aux autorités publiques nationales (ministères et agences techniques) et à l'UICN, qui représente la plus grande organisation internationale de conservation de la nature. Le projet « GreenList4MMPAs » adoptera une approche basée sur l'utilisation d'un grand nombre d'études de cas dans 7 pays de l'UE, 2 pays IAP et 6 Pays Tiers (grâce à un financement supplémentaire de l'UICN), afin de représenter tous les 7 éco-régions existantes en mer Méditerranée. Dans cette perspective, seule une véritable approche transnationale peut permettre d'atteindre les objectifs du projet.

Actual Achievements (EN):
GreenList4MMPAs partnership started to meet regularly (mainly online) since the announcement of the Programme approval “under conditions”, to resolve conditions and finalize the contracting procedure. This took quite a long time, also because in February JS communicated the need to have a new PP from an additional eligible country, since the withdrawn of the Albanian PP reduced the total number of participating countries. The new PP (Cote Bleu MPA) was formally taken on board in early March, while the kick-off meeting (with PSC establishment) was held online on January the 26th. Concerning project implementation, first activity, was the drafting of the Project's activities workplan (Del. 1.1.1). The following activity was the “Review of existing national and international standards to assess MPA effectiveness” (Act. 1.2). Thanks to the results of Act. 1.2, Act. 1.3 “Development of a Mediterranean MPAs-tailored IUCN Green List provisional set of Specific Indicators based on reviewed information” has started. To harmonize knowledge of all the PPs about the starting reference of the project, a specific training on IUCN Green List Standard was carried out during the presential 2nd PSC project meeting in Rome (9-10 April 2024). Act. 2.2 “Drafting of Med MPA EAGLs’ Terms of Reference” (ToRs) has been finalized, with the IUCN Headquarter agreement too. Publication of an open Call for experts (based on the approved ToRs) is expected in October. Consequently, Act. 2.3 “Selection of experts and establishment of Med MPA EAGLs committee” needs to be moved to the 2nd semester and will be finalised by November 2024. LP and PP2 attended the event organized by the Dialogue4Nature governance project (June 27th 2024). Internal communication is carried out mainly through e-mails and monthly online meetings. The primary external communications tool implemented is the Project’s website, finalised and published in early June 2024. PP2 has developed a communications strategy for the project which, after inputs and approval from the rest of the partners, identified key communications objectives, target audiences, platforms and tools to guide the project´s external communications. The project is running smoothly. LP assured permanent monitoring activity on the overall project implementation through a stable (monthly) exchange of info among PPs and a regular decision making process (2 Project steering Committe meetings wereheld). CO2 related data have been input by each PP on the Programme Calculator. Concerning WP1, all the steps (analysis of similar existing standards, interviews to MPAs already labelled by Green List, exploitation of results from similar projects - Act. 1.2 and partially 1.3) have been fulfilled and have allowed to prepare a first draft of the Mediterranean MPAs-tailored IUCN Green List provisional set of Specific Indicators (called also "Guidance Notes") that constitue the deliverable 1.3.1 and have been shared with the project partnership in the final days of the 2nd semester. Concerning WP2, ToRs and operational rules for the selection and functioning of the "Marine Mediterranean Expert Group for the Green List (Med-EAGL)" have been drafted and adopted by the whole partnership, thus allowing the publication of the "Call for expressions of interest to join the Med-EAGL" that opened on the 14th of November and closed the 11th of December. Candidatures evaluation process, involving also IUCN Headquarters and the IUCN World Commission on Protected Areas (WCPA), was finalised in February 2025. Concerning the coordination with the "horizontal projects", LP attended the capitalization event organized by the Italian National Contact Point in Florence (November 21st), and the public event organized in Malaga on November 28th and 29 by the mission's governance project. Finally, as far as communication is concerned, project's section in the Programme website was finalized and the first draft of the project introduction video was realized. During the IUCN Regional Conservation Forum held in Bruges, where IUCN MED presented GL4MMPAs project, the Albanian agency of Protection of Environment (NAPA) expressed its interest in joining the project as Associate partner, in order to be informed of the development of the project and contribute to the last phase of the project. During the third semester, the LP maintained permanent monitoring of the overall project implementation through regular (at least monthly) exchanges of information with PPs and a structured decision-making process. Three Project Steering Committee (PSC) meetings were held: two online (3 February and 13 March) and one in-person (26 June, Carry-le-Rouet, Marseille, hosted by PP6 PMCB with support from PP5 IUCN France). In its 3rd semester of implementation, the project entered in its crucial phase, i.e. to assess the applicability of the first version of the Mediterranean MPAs-tailored IUCN Green List provisional set of specific indictors (i.e. guidance and notes for applying the Generic Indicators). Under LP coordination, the partnership revised Deliverable 1.3.1 (issued in Semester 2) to serve as the reference document for Activity 1.4 “Assessment of applicability of the Mediterranean MPAs-tailored IUCN Green List set of indicators.” The assessment was carried out through an online questionnaire to evaluate respondents’ views on the applicability and clarity of the indicators and related guidance. It was distributed to over 100 Mediterranean stakeholders (MPA managers, Natura 2000 authorities, scientists, practitioners, public bodies and NGOs) from seven EU countries (Spain, France, Italy, Slovenia, Croatia, Greece, Cyprus), two IPA countries (Albania, Montenegro) and MPCs (Turkey, Tunisia, Algeria, Egypt, Lebanon, Libya), thanks to additional IUCN Med resources. Each PP translated the 54 questions into its national language to facilitate participation. IUCN Med also produced two explanatory videos, and SZN with IUCN Med organised two webinars. In total 45 completed questionnaires were received from all nine EU and IPA countries and from two of the six MPCs, representing a significant sample of the Mediterranean MPA community. Full details are in D1.4.1 “MPAs’ assessment of applicability of the Med MPAs-tailored IUCN Green List set of indicators.” The LP processed the quantitative data, and in order to manage the big bulk of qualitative feedback (open-end comments) from the questionnaire, 3 internal (i.e. with PPs representatives) working groups were created – during the PSC held in June – for the processing of such inputs and for the drafting of the new version of the “Mediterranean MPAs tailored IUCN Green List provisional set of indicators, with adapted guidance and notes”, to be presented in the forthcoming National Focus Group meetings (Act. 1.5) in order to be furtherly improved. Regarding Activity 1.5 “National Focus Group meetings” with MPA managers on survey results, the six meetings are scheduled as follows: Spain – early October; France – end of October; Italy – early October; Croatia (with Slovenian and Montenegrin participants) – 16 September; Albania – end of October; Greece – early October. Due to lack of additional resources, no meetings can be organised in Turkey and Tunisia. Under WP2, the recruitment of the Med MPA EAGL Committee (Activity 2.3), coordinated by IUCN Med, concluded with the appointment of 11 experts covering marine research, MPA management/governance and community engagement. Formally established on 12 March, the committee includes members from four EU countries (Spain, France, Italy, Croatia), one IPA country (Albania), two MPCs (Algeria, Tunisia) and one expert based in the UK. IUCN Med organised a two-day in-person training workshop in Málaga on 12–13 May (Activity 2.4) attended by all members. The training covered the Green List Standard, experts’ roles and a review of the Mediterranean MPAs-tailored Green List guidance (D1.3.1), thus initiating Activity 2.5 “Contribution of Med MPA EAGLs on IUCN Green List adaptation to Med MPAs.” Members were also invited to complete the Activity 1.4 questionnaire (four responses to date) and will take part in Focus Group meetings in their own countries. During the same days, the committee held its first meeting, defined its working methods and elected a chair and co-chair (see D2.4.1 “Training report”). On communication, coordinated by IUCN Med, the partnership continued to update the project website, share milestones on social media and attend major events such as the “One Ocean Science Congress” (Nice, 4–6 June), the “UN Ocean Conference” (Nice, 9–13 June) and the “High Ambition Coalition on Nature and People” meeting (Nice, 9 June). An introductory video on the main project goals and expected outcomes was produced and subtitled in the project’s six languages (available on YouTube). Finally, thanks mainly to SZN and IUCN Med, the project continues to coordinate with the Natural Heritage Mission Governance projects, participating in communication meetings, peer-review processes and thematic clusters. IUCN Med also drafted content for the Mission’s “Solve the Challenge” campaign on environmental governance.
