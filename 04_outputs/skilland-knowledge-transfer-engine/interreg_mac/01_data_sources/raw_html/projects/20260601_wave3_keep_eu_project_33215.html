Mediterranean Health in Crisis: Harnessing AI for Resilience

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 160. Measures to improve the accessibility, effectiveness and resilience of healthcare systems (excluding infrastructure) ~ SUMMARY: Advancements in Artificial Intelligence (AI) bring about opportunities to not only transform health risks governance but also healthcare access, equity, and system resilience in the face of emergencies and crises. Emergency management, with its five stages (prevention, mitigation, preparedness, response, recovery), has had an integral role in reducing the vulnerability of communities to the impact of predictable and unpredictable disasters. The Med region is fraught with increasing frequency and severity of transnational compounded shocks, ranging from natural disasters (earthquakes, floods, forest fires, etc.), human-made crises such as conflicts and wars to pandemics, causing mass injuries, loss of lives, destruction, and displacement, leaving a heavy toll on the most developed healthcare systems. The experience of the past few years, highlighted the need for multi-stakeholder transnational efforts to develop innovative solutions to enhance resilience in the face of those complex and protracted challenges. The Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction, established four priorities of action, two of which are at the heart of this proposal: “Strengthening disaster risk governance to manage disaster risk” and “Investing in disaster risk reduction for resilience”. AIGENCY will match needs to AI opportunities (WP3), adapt the technologies to the context and pilot the resulting transnational AI-powered solutions with regional and local actors (WP3), enhance the “agency” of stakeholders (WP4), and scale-up the adoption of the solutions to improve health resilience before, during and after “shocks” (WP5). For such a transnational endeavor to succeed it has to engage beneficiaries throughout the life of the project and beyond (WP3, 4, 5) (community-based groups, decision-makers, emergency first responders, medical & health professionals, academic institutions and technology developers & innovators), and must be designed with ethics and privacy front and center (WP5). ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Health and social services, Knowledge and technology transfer, Managing natural and man-made threats, risk management

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other AIGENCY’s AI solutions contribute to the EU’s crisis resilience (New European Agenda for Mediterranean) and response to disasters (EU Adaptation Strategies) efforts. The digital nature of the transnational AI solutions promotes the Pact for Research and Innovation in Europe that fosters the creation of digital solutions that can be utilized across the region. The adaptation of the AI solutions is based on cross-border collaboration promoting a more social Europe (European Territorial Cooperation- Interreg).
    • Active collaboration is a key pillar in this project, including diverse actors and teams presenting a borderless environment for shared experiences and innovation.
