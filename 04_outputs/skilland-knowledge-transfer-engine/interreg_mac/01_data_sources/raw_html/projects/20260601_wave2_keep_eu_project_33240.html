CROSS-BORDER CHAMBERS' NEW SERVICES as driver FOR SUSTAINABLE FAIR TRADE OF TEXTILE INDUSTRY ACROSS MEDITERRANEAN

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 3 - Governance Projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 173. Enhancing institutional capacity of public authorities and stakeholders to implement territorial cooperation projects and initiatives in a cross-border, transnational, maritime and inter-regional context ~ SUMMARY: The MEDinCHAMBERS project aims to enhance the cross-border governance model in the textile value chains in Italy, Spain, Tunisia, Egypt, and Turkey. The partner institutions have different organizations, diverse and not always shared information, independent operating procedures, and non-integrated tools. Therefore, it is necessary to take governance actions to structure a network of knowledge and establish lasting relationships, so that collaboration and support are effectively possible and mutual cooperation between the EU and MPC chambers of commerce, as already provided for by agreements in force at national and regional levels. The goal is to build a shared common knowledge framework (hubs&detector) of the rules, risks, focusing on those linked to textile sector needs and constraints, resources, and to create a cross-border operational team (MEDinCHAMBERS) composed of several bilateral chambers of commerce in the Med area. It is important to note that the textile sector is export-oriented, with the European Union being the main destination market. Therefore, compliance with the new EU Strategy for Sustainable and Circular Textiles and recognizing the importance of the textiles sector, along with the urgent need for new sustainable and environmental approaches, the main goal is to promote a more circular, energy-saving, and sustainable textile sector in Italy, Spain, Egypt, Tunisia, and Turkey, by providing tailored support services and creating a cross-border alliance. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Governance, partnership, Innovation capacity and awareness-raising, New products and services
