Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 026. Support for innovation clusters including between businesses, research organisations and public authorities and business networks primarily benefiting SMEs ~ SUMMARY: CLUSTERATLAS4Med is the Mediterranean meta-cluster for rural tourism. Following a hub-and-Spoke model, ATLAS4MED will boost the competitiveness of tourism SMEs in rural areas through better and bespoke services useful to address the challenges of the digital and green transition, fostering interconnections among the relevant stakeholders of the quadruple-helix in the Mediterranean area. For Ancient Greeks, Atlas was a giant so strong to carry the weight of the world on his shoulders. In Berber culture, Atlas refers to the main mountain range of North Africa. In Latin, the Atlas has come to mean book of maps. Atlas can thus be associated with meanings such as "strength", "support" and "orientation", reflecting the ATLAS4Med meta-cluster’s objective of developing a strategic approach to guide rural tourism actors across the NEXT MED area toward more sustainable growth. The main novelty of our project will be the creation of a strategic trans-Mediterranean meta-cluster that will specifically support rural tourism, instead of tourism in general. Rural tourism can play a leading role for the sustainable growth of rural areas but it needs a different approach. Rural SMEs are, on average, less innovative and digitalised. This can prevent them from grasping the benefits of the global markets. Our trans-regional meta-cluster, closely aligned with the Eurocluster Rural Tourism funded under the Single Market Programme, will create synergies among the different actors and territories to sustain the long term growth of rural tourism while addressing the twin transition. The target groups to be involved are: R&D Organisations, VET providers, public administrations, Chambers of Commerce, Business organisations and rural associations. 8 full partners and 6 associated partners from 7 NextMED countries will work together to capitalise and share their experiences gained through other EU-funded projects and to create a new and durable trans-mediterranean cluster. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Clustering and economic cooperation, SME and entrepreneurship, Tourism

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other We will foster digitalisation and the use of data for more sustainable business opportunities in the rural tourism sector, aligning to the strategic objectives of the New European Agenda for the Mediterranean. Our trans-mediterranean cluster will be the support hub for the Mediterranean rural tourism with several local spokes, acting as multiplier and coordinator. This matches the vision of the Mediterranean Strategy for Sustainable Development, as we will contribute to building a more prosperous Mediterranean region, targeting rural areas and promoting strong involvement of all stakeholders, cooperation, solidarity, equity and participatory governance.
    • The ATLAS4MED Meta-Cluster will provide services to support digitalisation of rural tourism SMEs, offering better services and promoting professional training and capacity-building. This will lead to the uptake of new technologies, innovations and internationalisation opportunities, thus contributing to reducing territorial and societal disparities and to promoting more equitable and sustainable growth, especially in peripheral regions, in synergy with the European Skills Agenda and the UN Sustainable Development Goals 4, 8 and 9.
    • Our project moves on the path of other EU Strategies and programmes, such as the SME Strategy for a Sustainable and Digital Europe, as we will support the empowerment of rural tourism SMEs. Aligned with the COSME Programme objectives, we will contribute to promote entrepreneurship and entrepreneurial culture across the NEXT MED area. Eventually, we took direct inspiration from the Erasmus for Young Entrepreneurs programme, having included as final beneficiaries of our project young entrepreneurs already operating in the sector (rural tourism) with less than 3 years of experience.
    • Moreover, we will create synergies with European projects funded by the EU's flagship research and innovation program, Horizon Europe, as further detailed in the following section.
