Resilience ENhancing to adapt FORests to Climate change Effects and to prevent biotic and abiotic hazard

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
Dans les régions méditerranéennes touchées par les incendies et les tempêtes, RENFORCE fait passer la gestion forestière de la réaction à la prévention. En d'autres termes, le projet développe des outils pratiques pour réduire les risques avant que la catastrophe ne frappe. Il met en place des plans de prévention participatifs dans des zones pilotes, combinant les connaissances locales et la science forestière. Sur le terrain, cela se traduit par la cartographie des zones à haut risque, la gestion de la végétation pour qu'elle serve de coupe-feu naturel et l'application de stratégies de plantation résistantes aux tempêtes. RENFORCE élabore également des protocoles communs au-delà des frontières afin d'anticiper les dégâts. Il fournit des outils d'aide à la décision aux autorités publiques, des programmes de formation aux propriétaires fonciers et une stratégie commune visant à intégrer la réduction des risques dans la planification forestière.
Read more
Description (EN):
In Mediterranean regions hit by wildfires and windstorms, RENFORCE shifts forest management from reaction to prevention. In other words, the project develops practical tools to cut risks before disaster strikes. It sets up participatory prevention plans in pilot zones, combining local knowledge with forestry science. On the ground, this means mapping high-risk areas, managing vegetation to act as natural firebreaks, and applying storm-resistant planting strategies. RENFORCE also builds common protocols across borders to anticipate damage. It delivers decision-making tools for public authorities, training programmes for landowners, and a shared strategy to embed risk reduction into forest planning.
Read more
Expected Achievements (EN):
To develop sustainable strategies for wildfires and windstorm risk reduction by multifunctional planning of vegetation on the ground, with particular regard to climate change, in collaboration with bodies in charge with the management and monitoring of forest ecosystem. To test solutions increasing the resilience of forests to sudden disturbances and climate change by testing integrated forest operations and strengthening the transnational research and innovation synergy to manage forests affected by potential diseases boosted by global warming, integrating transnational research and innovation in forest management strategies. To increase the institutional capacities of local bodies and to inform policy dialogue and strategic engagement of local actors on forest management to safeguard forest areas from abiotic disturbances.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The EU Green Deal acts as a container for many environmental and climate change mitigation strategies, such as the Forest Strategy which can be implemented in synergy with wildfire and windstorm risk prevention. RENFORCE project can positively contribute in strengthening knowledge about ecosystem based and risk prevention strategies supporting the goals of sustainability, enhancing sustainable forest management for the prevention of natural hazards, and improving the monitoring and planning on forests in the EU with a view to ensuring resilient forest ecosystems. It will promote the transition towards reduction of the effects of climate change and halting loss of habitats and species, supporting public authorities and stakeholders in (n.3) “protecting, restoring and enlarging EU’s forests to combat climate change, reverse biodiversity loss and ensure resilient and multifunctional forest ecosystems” and building (n. 6) “inclusive and coherent EU forest governance framework”.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 Linking to the Territorial Agenda 2030, the core actions of RENFORCE project address the importance of well-functioning and resilient ecosystems for the adaptation of the impact of climate change, and the essential role of integrated management and cooperation beyond administrative boundaries. The WP activities seek to strengthen the interaction between multi- stakeholders’ governance to ensure sustainable prevention of abiotic disturbances. In terms of HEALTHY ENVIRONMENT -Better ecological livelihoods, climate-
    • neutral and resilient towns, cities and regions (par.54 of the TA2030) RENFORCE will contribute building up integrated forest and protected areas management taking into consideration the different geographical specificities of project partners, supporting the development of nature-based solutions.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion Four out of eight partners of RENFORCE project belong to the Alpine region and will bring their great variety of governance systems, experiences and traditions to enrich the results of the project. Thanks to the comparative methodology, RENFORCE project will create specific guidelines for the proper implementation of co-management approaches and nature-based solutions for preventing and managing natural risks, thus it will directly contribute to the objective 3, spearheading Alpine risk management and confronting climate change-induced threats through collaborative dialogue, and to Action Group 8 " Risk Governance, to improve risk management and to better manage climate change, including major natural risks prevention". The project will also establish new instruments providing valuable contributions allowing sustainable and balanced models, in particular in relation to EUSALP Action Group 6 that aims at preserving and sustainably valorizing the Alpine natural and cultural heritage.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion RENFORCE project will contribute to the EU Strategy for the Adriatic and Ionian Region (EUSAIR) in particular in reference to pillar 3 “Environment Quality” ensuring, through innovative co management strategies, the well-being of forestry environments. The main contribution will concern the outputs of WP3 which regards the production of a methodological guidance and technical standards in support of policy makers and relevant local public/private entities to safeguard forest areas. By actively participating in these frameworks and implementing sustainable practices, the project is helping to preserve the forestry environment for present and future generations also in line with the broader objectives of the EU Biodiversity Strategy mentioned above.
