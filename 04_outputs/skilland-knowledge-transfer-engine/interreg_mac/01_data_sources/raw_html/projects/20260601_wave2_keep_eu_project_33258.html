Fostering Transnational Knowledge for More Inclusive and Participatory Mediterranean

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 3 - Governance Projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 173. Enhancing institutional capacity of public authorities and stakeholders to implement territorial cooperation projects and initiatives in a cross-border, transnational, maritime and inter-regional context ~ SUMMARY: The lack of participatory planning for social inclusion programs on the part of local authorities has led to a misguided conception in identifying real needs and inclusive understanding of the pressing social challenges across the Mediterranean. This has hindered the development of society driven solutions that effectively respond to these challenges. TRANSFORMED will foster cross-sector synergies through a transnational cooperation to jointly develop solutions to facilitate the establishment of efficient governance models for improving social inclusion at local level. The project proposal comprises analysing, complementing and disseminating the transnational knowledge demonstrated in implementing 4 regionally-acclaimed governance models that have adopted bottom-up and participatory approaches to deliver inclusive community-serving programs; the objective being to exploit and upscale this knowledge. The outputs are complementary, using a building blocks approach. They are: -Transnational Handbook, that is a fruit of 5 transnational Community-engagement symposia, Online data analysis clusters and a practitioners’ lab; -Enhanced institutional capacities of 5 local authorities and 5 social inclusion civic actors, thanks to the project’s call for inclusive Local Planning concept notes, the Peer-to-peer knowledge capsules and the procedure demonstration pilots; - 5 Blueprints with jointly developed social inclusion community-serving programs operated by tandems between local authorities and civil society actors responding to inclusion challenges identified on the national level. Inclusive Local Planning Blueprints resulting from Peer-to-peer mentoring, enhanced by national round table debates and will be introduced in a regional knowledge sharing exhibition. Final beneficiaries are local authorities and civic actors from partner countries benefiting from best practices of participatory governance, and local communities benefiting from the improved local governance. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Community integration and common identity, Governance, partnership, Knowledge and technology transfer

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other TRANSFORMED contributes to the achievement of the UN Sustainable Development Goal 17 (Partnerships for the goals) through supporting a cross-sector cooperation between Local authorities and Civil society organisations. The multilevel knowledge dissemination synergies that are going to take place will result in a resilient civil society/local authority tandem with empowered partners that should respond to SDGs 10 and 16 specifically the following associated targets:
    • 10.2: empower and promote the social, economic and political inclusion of all, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion or economic or other status;
    • 10.7: Facilitate orderly, safe, regular and responsible migration and mobility of people, including through the implementation of planned and well-managed migration policies;
    • 16.7: Ensure responsive, inclusive, participatory and representative decision- making at all levels;
    • 16.10: Ensure public access to information and protect fundamental freedoms, in accordance with national legislation and international agreements;
    • 16.a: Strengthen relevant national institutions, including through international cooperation, for building capacity at all levels, in particular in developing countries, to prevent violence and combat terrorism and crime.
    • The project is committed to replicate best practices and facilitate the introduction of participatory governance models for community serving programs that respond to social inclusion challenges in the Mediterranean and the southern neighborhood region in line with preliminary inputs on the development macro-regional strategy in the Mediterranean. By implementing activities anticipated under TRANSFORMED the project will make available a transnational community-based Handbook that should feed the Macro-regional strategies with the most pressing social and territorial cohesion challenges in the Mediterranean and southern neighborhood region, in complementarity with NEXT MED program and SDG 17.
