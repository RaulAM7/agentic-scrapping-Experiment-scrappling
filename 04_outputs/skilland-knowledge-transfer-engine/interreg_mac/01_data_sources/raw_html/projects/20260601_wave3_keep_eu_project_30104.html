INnovative FInancing solutions for climate planning of REsilient and carbon neutral living areas

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
La Méditerranée est à l'avant-garde de la guerre contre le changement climatique, ce qui exige des territoires qu'ils agissent rapidement et coopèrent pour prévenir d'autres implications environnementales, sociales et économiques négatives. INFIRE aborde deux aspects clés de la lenteur de la transition vers la neutralité climatique et l'adaptation au climat dans la région Euro-MED: les faibles capacités des autorités publiques pour le développement et l'intégration des documents de planification stratégique et l'incapacité à mobiliser les capitaux publics et privés pour les projets d'investissement nécessaires pour atteindre les objectifs UE pour 2030. INFIRE rassemble des partenaires très expérimentés qui coopèrent pour établir une structure de soutien durable afin de renforcer les capacités des autorités publiques pour développer et mettre en œuvre des solutions et des instruments politiques holistiques d'adaptation au changement climatique et de neutralité carbone (CACN). INFIRE fournit des solutions pratiques et développées conjointement: - Boîte à outils d'évaluation du financement vert pour déclencher le financement de projets CACN et de stratégies à long terme. - Documents de planification stratégique améliorés et alignés et programmes de financement public pour soutenir la transition. - Programme de formation intensif et sur mesure pour les autorités publiques de la région Euro-MED. - Engagement des citoyens par le biais d'un processus de co-création et de modèles de crowdfunding civique. - Le pilotage de 8 solutions CACN concrètes et innovantes pour réplication et une mise à l'échelle.. L'originalité du projet INFIRE réside dans une approche holistique ascendante qui s'adresse à la fois à l'offre (les autorités publiques en tant que développeurs de projets) et à la demande (les institutions financières et les citoyens en tant qu'investisseurs) en comprenant leurs moteurs et leurs lacunes et en comblant le fossé par une coopération intensive.

Description (EN):
The Mediterranean is at the forefront of the fight against climate change, requiring cities and regions to act quickly and cooperate to prevent further negative environmental, social, and economic implications. INFIRE addresses two key aspects of the slow transition towards climate neutrality and climate adaptation in the Euro-MED region: low capacities of public authorities for the development and integration of strategic planning documents and the inability to properly evaluate and mobilize public and private capital for large-scale investment projects needed for reaching EU’s 2030 targets. INFIRE gathers experienced partners with highly complementary skills that cooperate to establish a long-lasting support structure to build capacities of public authorities in the involved regions for developing, implementing, and monitoring holistic climate adaptation and carbon neutrality (CACN) solutions and policy instruments. INFIRE provides jointly developed and practical solutions in form of: - Green Finance Evaluation Toolkit for triggering financing of CACN projects and long-term strategies with innovative financing schemes - Enhanced and aligned strategic planning documents and public financing programmes to support the transition to resilience and carbon neutrality - Tailor-made curriculum and intensive capacity-building programme for public authorities of the Euro-MED region - Citizens’ engagement through co-creation process and civic-crowdfunding models, promoting behavioral change - Piloting 8 concrete and innovative CACN solutions for future replication and upscaling. The originality of INFIRE project arises from a holistic bottom-up approach that addresses both the supply (public authorities as project developers) and demand side (financial institutions and citizens as investors) by understanding their drivers and shortcomings and bridging the gap through intensive cooperation with both sides and promotion of best available practices.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the first reporting period, the INFIRE project made substantial progress in establishing its administrative framework and initiating key activities, despite facing some delays. This phase focused on building a solid foundation for future work, emphasizing partnerships, project management, and initial research efforts. The administrative setup included the finalization of the Partnership Agreement and Subsidy Contract, and formalizing the project's structure. Active participation in Steering Committee meetings ensured coordinated action across the work packages. Essential tools and templates for financial framework assessments and gap analyses have been under preparion setting the stage for more detailed assessments in the next period. Regarding activity progress, the work on innovative green financing focused on the preparation of critical templates for the assessment and the initiation of a gap analysis of financing models, providing a foundation for exploring financing mechanisms across regions. Methodologies and tools, such as questionnaires, were developed to guide the integration and improvement of policy instruments, ensuring alignment between different work packages. Work also began on reviewing regional frameworks for climate change adaptation and financing opportunities, adapting the project to evolving local needs. Communication and engagement efforts such as the project’s website, branding materials, and social media presence were created to raise awareness, and the drafting of a Communication Strategy was initiated. Efforts were also made to engage associated partners, forming working groups with local municipalities to ensure the widespread dissemination of project objectives through various channels. Challenges during this period included delays in finalizing the Subsidy Contract, which affected the start of activities across work packages, leading to the postponement of certain deliverables. Additionally, there were difficulties in appointing external consultants for pilot activities, but despite these obstacles, core activities were launched with minimal delay.Transnational cooperation has been a valuable asset to the project, allowing partners to leverage expertise from different regions, particularly in green financing, climate resilience, and policy integration. In summary, the first reporting period primarily focused on administrative setup and managing initial delays. Nevertheless, the project managed to prepare assessments, forming regional partnerships, and launching communication efforts, setting the stage for more impactful outputs in the next phase. Second period marked substantial progress despite an important transition in leadership, with the Region of Peloponnese (REGPEL) transferring the Lead Partner role to the North-West Croatia Regional Energy and Climate Agency (REGEA). This transition was discussed, voted on, and officially adopted through multiple SC meetings, ensuring a smooth and structured shift. REGEA established structured coordination mechanisms, including monthly online and biannual in-person meetings, to strengthen leadership, optimize resource allocation, and prevent project delays. Importantly, these changes did not affect non-management project activities, with REGPEL retaining its responsibilities apart from coordination. A key milestone was the in-person SC meeting in Zagreb in October 2024, where the modification of the Lead Partner role was confirmed within the Partnership. The subsequent online SC meeting in December 2024 further solidified coordination mechanisms and partner engagement. In WP1, INFIRE partners completed an assessment report analysing financing frameworks across the Euro-MED regions and a gap analysis on innovative green finance mechanisms and investment procedures. Further advancements were made with developing key financial tools, such as the socio-economic evaluation methodology for CACN projects, the INFIRE Financial and Economic Evaluation Tool, and a Decision-Making Cost Comparator. These tools equip public authorities with structured guidance for assessing investment options and optimising strategies. Stakeholder engagement has also been a major success. A roadmap for engaging financial institutions, policymakers, and project ambassadors was completed. Related to stakeholder engagement, REGEA organised the first National Roundtable session, which was held in December 2024 in Karlovac, Croatia. Other partners have begun planning similar sessions in their regions. Under WP2, partners defined the methodology for integrating and improving policy instruments for resilient and carbon-neutral living areas. Partners participated in a joint analysis of selected policy instruments, identifying integration opportunities and evaluating local planning capacities. This process included data collection, SWOT and PESTLE analyses, analyses of the city CACN-related policy instruments and assessment of the city capacity and knowledge gaps. WP3 focused on preparing for the testing and demonstration of CACN solutions. Achievements in this WP include developing a methodology for assessing pilot project performance, defining a structured approach for pilot monitoring, and establishing key performance indicators (KPIs) relevant to CACN solutions. A significant component of WP3 has been the launch of territorial working groups to engage citizens and stakeholders. DVISION delivered the INFIRE Communication Strategy along with promotional materials such as the INFIRE leaflet, pilot flyers, a promotional video, newsletters, brochures, posters, and a roll-up banner. These materials aim to enhance public awareness and participation in the project’s climate adaptation initiatives. In WP4, INFIRE strengthened its role within the broader Euro-MED mission for Green Living Areas. PP’s participated in an online session of the C4LA Thematic Working Group on Financing the Green Transition, held on December 12, 2024. During this session, project representatives exchanged insights with other initiatives, identified opportunities for collaboration, and outlined joint activities for 2025. Additionally, all PP’s collected carbon footprint data for the second period, contributing to a more comprehensive evaluation of the project’s environmental impact. Throughout the second implementation period, INFIRE has demonstrated the significant added value of transnational cooperation in addressing climate adaptation and carbon neutrality challenges. By uniting diverse partners with complementary expertise, the project has successfully developed practical financial tools, started working on strengthening policy frameworks, and initiated meaningful stakeholder engagement processes. As the project progresses, the focus will be on scaling up pilot initiatives, refining methodologies based on real-world testing, and ensuring the long-term sustainability of CACN solutions through effective policy integration and financing mechanisms.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The EU Green Deal comprises a set of policies for transforming the EU into a climate-neutral and resource-efficient economy by 2050. The main policy-areas of intervention that INFIRE contributes to are the following:
    • 1. A higher level of EU climate ambition for 2030 and 2050.
    • 2. Achieve zero pollution in an environment without toxic substances.
    • 3. Clean, affordable, and safe energy supply.
    • 4. Efficient use of energy and resources in construction and renovation.
    • 5. Acceleration of the transition to sustainable and smart mobility.
    • This will be achieved through the planning and testing of pilot solutions for carbon neutrality and climate adaptation (WP3), triggering their transferring and uptake (WP4) while building capacities to enable and accelerate the transition (WP2) and its financing (WP1).
    • Moreover, through WP4, INFIRE contributes to the EU Green Deal Investment Plan and Just Transition Mechanism (EGDIP), supporting the Mediterranean Living Areas to decrease the transition divide among South and North Europe.
    • Lastly, the replicable pilot testing approach of INFIRE contributes to the concept of “Local Green Deals” promoted by the EC Intelligent Cities Challenge, by testing good practice examples, but also by building local and international networks of stakeholders to act as enablers and supporters of the transition, namely the: Territorial Working Groups (WP 3) and the Advisory Hubs (WP1). Lastly, the international activities of INFIRE are expected to be useful for the “National Energy and Climate Plans” elaborated in several Euro-MED countries under the Green Deal.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 Territorial Agenda 2030 stresses the need to be supported by actions from committed players in order to address priorities and concerns over spatial inequalities and the transition towards a carbon/climate-neutral economy, which is the core of the INFIRE project.
    • INFIRE fosters cooperation and coordination between places, levels of governments, policy sectors and societal groups to address complex issues and utilise diverse potential, as well as territorial development policies and cooperation on joint objectives that are essential to increase the resilience of municipalities, regions and countries, by strengthening their recovery processes.
    • Specifically, INFIRE contributes to the strengthening of:
    • - place-based approaches; (WP2 and WP3)
    • - coordinated sector policy territorial impacts and coherence (WP1 and WP2)
    • - cooperation between territories;
    • - territorial cohesion at European level
    • - territorial cohesion at cross-border, transnational, inter- and intra-regional level
    • - Member State and neighbouring country contributions to territorial cohesion through the WP1 and WP2 interregional approaches for financing and planning climate adaptation and carbon neutrality, WP3 cooperation among pilot territories and WP4 on triggering the transferring. Lastly, through Associated Partners of INFIRE, the project contributes to synergies with the Neighbourhood bilateral and multilateral relations.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion Several INFIRE Partners and Associated Partners are located in the EUSAIR macro-region, thus ensuring the creation of direct links with the INFIRE (and consequently with the Interreg Euro-MED Programme), at least in the following countries: Greece, Italy, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina and Montenegro. The integrated and holistic approach of INFIRE is expected to increase the potential synergies at the local level. Increased contribution can be achieved through the Mission 3 TC and IDP projects with which INFIRE will be in close collaboration and the Coordinator of the Result Amplification Strategy. Specific common interests among INFIRE and EUSAIR include the creation of further opportunities for smart, sustainable and inclusive growth right across the Adriatic and Ionian Region, and investment in energy improvements, such as to increase energy efficiency and the increase production of renewable energy.

    • Thematic links derive through topics of common interest with INFIRE, and specifically the EUSALP Action Groups on Mobility, Green Infrastructure and Energy. The INFIRE approach contributes to all three Action Groups from a thematic point of view, while the INFIRE pilots of Italy and France are also directly relevant. Especially regarding the Action Group 9 on Energy, EUSALP aspires to set-up several clusters for energy efficiency, greening the Alpine infrastructure, renewable energy and energy management systems. INFIRE Advisory Hubs and IT, FR pilots could be considered for integration in the said clusters.
    • In addition, synergies and exchanges of practices will be achieved through the INFIRE cooperation with the Mission 3 Thematic Community and Institutional Dialogue projects via the Coordinator for Result Amplification Strategy.
