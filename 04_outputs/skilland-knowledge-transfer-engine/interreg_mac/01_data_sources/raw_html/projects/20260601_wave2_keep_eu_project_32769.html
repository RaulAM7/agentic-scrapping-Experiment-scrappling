Biodegradable aquaculture nets

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
AquaBioNets est un projet qui soutient l’aquaculture durable dans six pays méditerranéens. Il réunit des groupes clés tels que les gouvernements, les entreprises, les chercheurs et les communautés afin de promouvoir des pratiques respectueuses de l’environnement, notamment l’utilisation de filets biodégradables. Le projet renforce les compétences, soutient l’innovation et contribue à l’élaboration de politiques locales grâce à une approche collaborative appelée « Living Lab ». Il aborde des problématiques telles que la pollution, la forte concurrence mondiale et la réglementation floue. Tous les résultats, y compris les supports de formation et les guides politiques, seront partagés publiquement afin de favoriser la diffusion de l’aquaculture durable dans toute la région.

Description (EN):
AquaBioNets is a project supporting sustainable aquaculture in six Mediterranean countries. It brings together key groups like governments, businesses, researchers, and communities to promote eco-friendly practices, especially the use of biodegradable nets. The project strengthens skills, supports innovation, and helps create local policies through a collaborative approach called the Living Lab. It addresses issues like pollution, tough global competition, and unclear regulations. All results, including training materials and policy guides, will be shared publicly to help spread sustainable aquaculture across the region.

Expected Achievements (FR):
The overall objective of WP1 is to gain upgraded and up-to-date knowledge in the field of Sustainable Aquaculture, to be transferred to 5helix stakeholders through WP2 and WP3, by performing the Living Lab approach. The objective will be achieved through three different types of activities, namely (1) capitalisation of results from previous EU funded projects (Horizon 2020, Interreg, EMFF), (2) research and testing activities with the aim of optimisation of findings and lastly (3) monitoring their environmental impact. The output O1.1 establishes a transnational partnership of 12 organisations from six Mediterranean countries, cooperating through the Living Lab approach to advance sustainable aquaculture. By jointly capitalising on previous project results, conducting targeted research, and sharing expertise, the partnership ensures effective cross-border knowledge transfer and stakeholder engagement. The overall objective of WP2 is to educate and train representatives from organisations covering 5helix actors, including HEIs and ROs, industry (SMEs), public administration, and civil society, by performing the Living Lab approach. WP2 activities will be implemented in Croatia, Spain, Greece, Cyprus, Italy & Albania. PP MUSOL is WP2 lead and objective will be achieved through 4 types of capacity building sessions, each focused on 1 target group (civil society and environment actors are put together because of their similar background and learning objectives). Capacity building sessions will be carried out in all six countries, based on inputs from WP1, additionally enriched by country specific data and target group’s interests, determined in Working Groups. The main output of WP2 is a jointly developed methodology titled, which applies the LL approach to the field of sustainable aquaculture. Having implemented the Education, Training and Connecting activities in the field of Sustainable Aquaculture for 5helix stakeholders, in WP3 PPs continue with Connecting and Co-designing activities, focused on shifting policies towards Sustainable Aquaculture, following the Living Lab approach, covering Implementing, Improving, Evaluating and Dissemination components. WP is led by PP ANETEL and aims to support the wider implementation of solutions, tools and methodologies for policy regulations. Essential key of this WP is the active involvement of stakeholders in improving process of solutions and specially to encourage them to provide feedback for adoption of solutions to their actual needs. Stakeholders will be encouraged to implement the improved solutions (methods, tools, etc.) in their real environment, as well as to evaluate the solutions, communicate and deploy results, to share gained knowledge and to expand the network of stakeholders in participating region and beyond.

    • EuropeanGreenDeal In the context of the European Green Deal, sustainable aquaculture growth makes great contribution to achieving Green Deal’s key objectives: reducing carbon emissions, transitioning to sustainable food systems, reversing biodiversity loss, reducing pollution, and creating jobs in coastal and rural communities.
    • Sustainable aquaculture offers several benefits:
    • - Provides nutritious and diverse food for the growing global population.
    • - Reduces the need for wild fish catches, helping to preserve fish stocks.
    • - Supplies fresh and local food.
    • - Has a lower climate and environmental impact compared to other farming methods.
    • - Creates jobs in coastal and rural communities, including remote areas.
    • - Helps preserve ecosystems and biodiversity in certain forms
    • AquaBioNets results will contribute to 1. reduction of need for wild fish catches; 2. supply of fresh and local food; 3. the lower environmental impact of farming; 4. creation of jobs; 5. ecosystems preservation.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion EUSAIR aims to tackle challenges by promoting economic growth and prosperity in the Adriatic-Ionian region through improving its attractiveness, competitiveness and connectivity.
    • AquaBioNets project contributes to Pillar 1 (Blue Growth), in 3 themes: 1. Blue technologies, 2. Fisheries and aquaculture, 3. Maritime and marine governance and services; and Pillar 3 (Environmental Quality), in 2 themes: 1. The marine environment and 2. Pollution of the sea.
    • The contribution is made through 1. the development of innovative biodegradable aquaculture nets, that support sustainable aquaculture; 2. application of the participatory Living Lab approach while co-creating relevant policy recommendations, connecting all the marine key actors, with the aim of better governance; 3. undertaking actions to preserve marine environment by carrying out quality controls, and 4. facilitating the reduction of sea pollution with non-degradable aquaculture nets, by developing high-quality biodegradable ones.
    • MediterraneanSeaBasin The WestMED Initiative supports project development by providing technical advice related to funding opportunities and connecting potential PPs across the sea basin.
    • By carrying out its activities and achieving results and outcomes, AquaBioNets is aligned with all three goals: 1. A safer and more secure maritime space (developing biodegradable nets); 2. A smart and resilient blue economy (applying advanced and innovative technology); Improved maritime governance (co-creation of local policy recommendation in the sustainable aquaculture sector).
    • Regarding its priorities, AquaBioNets is aligned in following: Priority 1 Maritime safety and the fight against marine pollution (developing biodegradable nets that have less negative impact on environment and are degradable); Priority 3. Skills Development and Circulation (building skills and capacities of 5helix actors); Priority 6. Development of Coastal Communities, Sustainable Fisheries and Aquaculture (creating communities of interest).
