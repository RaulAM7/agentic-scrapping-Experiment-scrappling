Testing novel environmental quality measures in and around Euro-MED ports

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
TREASURE’s overall objective is to improve environmental quality in and around Mediterranean port areas, by mitigating and reducing sediment and water pollution. Human activities cause unprecedented environmental changes in coastal and marine ecosystems. Port areas become both the final accumulation site for chemicals from anthropic activities and a cause of pollution that is difficult to contain. This represents a significant environmental risk for marine biodiversity and surrounding areas and a risk to human health. Within this overall challenge, TREASURE identifies 3 sub-challenges: CHALLENGE 1: GOVERNANCE – need for cross-sector and cross-cutting governance frameworks to improve environmental quality in port areas. CHALLENGE 2: MONITORING ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY – need for a common and advanced monitoring framework, able to assess impact and pressures on port environments. CHALLENGE 3: NOVEL TECHNIQUES –need to support commercialisation and roll-out of new techniques to remediate polluted sediments and water. Our approach, capitalising on previous projects, merges 3 multi-disciplinary Action Lines to address the core challenges and produce the integrated TREASURE solution (key output): ACTION LINE 1: Multi-stakeholder Supporting Ecosystem for the creation and management of territorial labs. ACTION LINE 2: New approaches to environmental assessment, through application of an integrated matrix of criteria and using the Lines of Evidence approach for qualitative evaluation. ACTION LINE 3: coordinated sourcing and experimentation of novel techniques for pollution reduction in sediments and water, allowing for validation in industrially relevant conditions. This integrated approach is novel, as is our proposal for multi-stakeholder labs, our Lines of Evidence approach and our focus on novel techniques. Transnational cooperation is necessary to achieve our objectives, both due to the common challenges that we address and to the innovative approach that we propose.

Description (FR):
L'objectif global de TREASURE est d'améliorer la qualité de l'environnement dans et autour des zones portuaires méditerranéennes, en atténuant et en réduisant la pollution des sédiments et de l'eau. Les activités humaines provoquent des changements environnementaux sans précédent. Les zones portuaires deviennent à la fois le site d'accumulation finale des produits chimiques et une cause de pollution difficile à contenir. Cela représente un risque environnemental pour la biodiversité marine et les zones environnantes, ainsi qu'un risque pour la santé humaine. Dans ce cadre, TREASURE identifie 3 sous-défis : 1 : GOUVERNANCE - nécessité de cadres de gouvernance intersectoriels et transversaux pour améliorer la qualité environnementale dans les zones portuaires. 2 : SURVEILLANCE - nécessité d'un cadre de monitorage commun et avancé, capable d'évaluer l'impact et les pressions sur les environnements portuaires. 3 : TECHNIQUES NOUVELLES - nécessité de soutenir la commercialisation et le déploiement de nouvelles techniques pour assainir les sédiments et les eaux pollués. Notre approche, qui s'appuie sur des projets antérieurs, fusionne 3 lignes d'action pluridisciplinaires pour produire la solution intégrée TREASURE (résultat clé) : 1 : Ecosystème de soutien multi-acteurs pour la création et la gestion des laboratoires territoriaux. 2 : Nouvelles approches d'évaluation environnementale, par l'application d'une matrice intégrée de critères et l'utilisation de l'approche « Lines of Evidence » (LOE). 3 : Approvisionnement coordonné et expérimentation de nouvelles techniques de réduction de la pollution dans les sédiments et l'eau. Cette approche intégrée est nouvelle, tout comme notre proposition de laboratoires multipartites, notre approche fondée sur les LOE et notre attention aux nouvelles techniques. La coopération transnationale est nécessaire pour atteindre nos objectifs, tant en raison des défis communs que nous relevons, que de l'approche innovante que nous proposons.

Actual Achievements (EN):
In Period1 (P1), all WPs were launched with the following achievements: On 05/03/24 a virtual meeting was held. WPs’ work plans for P1/2 were presented by WP leaders. To create multi-stakeholder governance ecosystems (WP1) a tool to gather preliminary input on territorial background on environmental challenges and stakeholders was shared. To introduce the new approach to environmental assessment (WP2), project GEREMIA was presented, together with a form to verify partners’ capability in environmental data sampling. The schedule for sourcing novel techniques for environmental remediation (WP3) was shared. WP 1 - At Kick-off meeting (KO) (Livorno 03-04/04/24), TANIA project was presented to capitalise its knowledge on novel solutions for remediation. With an eye to the signature of territorial agreements for instating mini-labs in TREASURE, all partners presented territorial analyses. In June, a template was shared to prepare the signature of territorial cooperation protocols. All partners set up local stakeholder groups by end of P1. WP 2 – At KO, the Lines of Evidence approach was deepened. All partners reported on stakeholders’ scientific knowledge, data available and data collection capability. WP 3 – at KO, the questionnaire designed by PP6 to select environmental challenges was presented. Partners highlighted missing subjects to expand the proposed topics. In P2 environmental challenges will be addressed by a call for interest to catalogue novel remediation solutions. In May the questionnaire was circulated. All partners, associates and a number of stakeholders answered to it. On 25/06/24, a virtual meeting was held to validate the results. WP4 - PP9 elaborated the communication strategy and its tools, including: project poster, templates, social media, internal monitoring. Tools and strategy were presented at KO. LP and PP6 attended all virtual meetings of Natural Heritage Mission, shared infos on TREASURE and joined the mission launch event (Rovinj 26-27/06/24). In Period 1 and 2 (P1/2), TREASURE project launched its activities and progressed effectively in accordance to its work plan. Activities and exchange gradually increased, with the first trimester of Period 1 mainly dedicated to set up internal monitoring and coordination procedures, while technical implementation actually started since April 2024. Throughout P1/2, all partners provided sound contribution in addressing the 3 project challenges to environmental quality in and around Mediterranean port areas. The interregional cooperation demonstrated extremely valuable since the project start, with partners directly involved by the WP leaders in building a multi-stakeholder supporting environment for mini-labs (Action Line 1), deepening and testing new approaches to environmental assessment through the Lines of Evidence (LoEs) approach (Action Line 2), and sourcing and experimenting novel techniques for environmental remediation (Action Line 3). The interregional cooperation provided from start a significant added value, providing for the opportunity to compare different but coherent environmental challenges and to reach out a wide range of stakeholders gravitating around the project port areas. WP1 Within the first work package, partners acknowledged a multi-stakeholder approach to lay the foundations to transnational environmental testing, capitalising on the experience of the Interreg Europe project TANIA, funded under MFF 2014-2020. Within each TREASURE port area, partners begun to map and build a local stakeholder ecosystem, with the aim to formalise a group of regional actors involved in the management of mini-territorial labs, around which testing activities will be implemented. By the end of P2, most TREASURE partners signed local agreements for running mini-labs within TREASURE scope, with a selected group of stakeholders. All partners are expected to finalise their agreements by P3. WP2 Within the second work package, partners began to create a common understanding of a new approach to environmental assessment, capitalising on the results of GEREMIA project, funded under the Programme Italy-France Maritime 2014-2020. The LoEs approach, aims to integrate vast amounts of environmental data into complex monitoring scenarios. Such approach allows for the creation of an integrated environmental risk index, which will be tested by TREASURE partners. By the end of P2, all partners with relevant competence on their port areas selected a number of LoEs and related thresholds to monitor, feeding into an Impact Evaluation and Hazard Management matrix that is tested at transnational level in TREASURE. WP3 Within the third work package, partners cooperated in elaborating, launching, and disseminating a call for interest aimed to source and select a number of innovative solutions for environmental remediation. In P1, partners identified a set of environmental challenges characterising their port areas, addressing which the call for interest was released in P2. A number of solutions submitted to the call are awarded building on their relevance for TREASURE port areas, and gathered into a catalogue for further testing within the mini-lab framework. WP4 Within the fourth work package, partners defined a project Monitoring and Evaluation methodology to set the ground to project implementation and later transferability of results. Coordination among key project roles and partners was set up in P1, fostering exchange at project level. Exchange with regional and international stakeholders was facilitated also thanks to the coordination with the Natural Heritage Mission governance project. The above mentioned cooperation developed revolving around several virtual and two in-person meetings, in particular the Kick-off meeting in Livorno (03-04/04/2024) and the second meeting in Valencia (15-16/10/204), during which the partnership validated the exchange carried out through the internal documents and organised valuable study visits to the hosting porta areas. In terms of concrete outputs, two deliverables were produced by the end of P2. WP2 generated D2.2.1TREASURE Environmental Impact evaluation and Hazard Management matrix, to provide the overall environmental quality assessment matrix developed at transnational level, using the LoEs approach. It gathers the initial indicators for a qualitative evaluation of different compartments (sediments / water / biota) of the port environments subject to environmental testing within WP2. WP3 produced D3.1.1Open call for interest methodology, to present the methodology used to design and implement the call for interest, along which all the supporting documents i.e., call text, application form, evaluation criteria, selection process were shared to allow a fair and transparent selection of novel techniques for remediation. The output indicator 4.4 was obtained at project start, counting 15 organisations, including partners and associates, cooperating across the border. In Period 3 (P3), TREASURE partners cooperated to obtain the following achievements: On 23/01/2025, during a final selection virtual meeting, all project partners validated the innovative solutions submitted to the TREASURE call for interest to improve environmental quality of port areas. On 18-19/02/2025, partners and stakeholders convened at meeting 03 in Durres (AL), during which data collection methods were discusses to apply environmental management matrixes and to plan data sampling in each territory (WP2), progress in solution testing activities was presented (WP3), an innovative solution workshop was organised on Water Management/Quality and Biodiversity preservation (WP3), synergies with ASAP and TRUEBLUE projects were deepened (WP4). On 13/03/2025, a virtual meeting was held. WPs’ work plans for P3/4 were presented by all WP leaders, in order to coordinate the interregional exchange in the upcoming year. On 26-27/06/2025, partners participated in meeting 04 in Bar (ME), during which an innovative solution workshop was organised on Air quality and Energy management in ports (WP3), an update on state of testing activities was presented (WP2), progress in solution testing activities was presented (WP3), the Governance Partner Report (WP1) and Mainstreaming Action Plan and Transferability Strategy (WP4) were discussed in depth and validated. WP1 – Cooperation with local governance ecosystems continued to support implementation of testing activities in WP2/3, leading to tailor-made governance models and territorial mini-labs. In May, the LP shared a Governance Partner Report template to summarise local context analyses, including environmental challenges and port area stakeholders, to report on agreements signed for the implementation of TREASURE approach, and to present the local customization of the mini-lab concept. All partners continued work to finalise the signature of the local agreements with the stakeholders involved in the TREASURE mini-labs. WP2 – A first phase was dedicated to the planning of environmental data sampling within each port, and the development of the dedicated web-based tool for data processing. All partners planned sampling activities and related tender procedures. The demo web-based tool to integrate data was tested by partners to familiarise with it and gather feedback. 3 PPs (ISPRA, DPA, FV) performed the first samplings of environmental data to feed the common Impact Evaluation and Hazard Management Matrix. WP 3 – 52 innovative solutions for improvement of environmental quality, out of 59 submitted to the TREASURE call for interest, were approved by the partnership. The awarded solutions were noticed and promoted through the TREASURE catalogue, published in June 2025. 2 workshops with solution providers were held during project meetings to present solutions and to network with project partners and port authorities, representing also an opportunity to better understand the challenges ports are facing and how to better adapt technologies to those. Study visits were organised after each workshop. Partners mapped the scope of the pilot testing activities and began related public procurements. WP4 - LP and CEI joined all NHM physical/online initiatives, participated in Working Groups, and contributed to the Euro-MED Academy preparation. Partners monitored and evaluated TREASURE activities, assigning roles and responsibilities at project and port level. Partners agreed to launch online surveys for partners/stakeholders to receive feedback on the quality and accessibility of project activities. LP drafted an Action Plan template, to be filled in by partners, outlining how to integrate and follow up on project results in local practice, including an overview of local policies/strategies for the port area, a summary of key achievements, and a plan for exploiting results. CEI presented a Strategy for Transferability of Results, to promote project solutions and outcomes outside the consortium. A roadmap of initiatives for result transfer was defined, to be implemented until June 2026, including online (3) and in persons (2) workshops.

Relevant linked projects:

  • TANIA - TreAting contamination through NanoremedIAtion (Interreg Europe 2014-2020) (EN), TANIA - TreAting contamination through NanoremedIAtion (Interreg Europe 2014-2020) (FR) | TANIA supported interregional cooperation among 5 European regions in order to develop regional action plans to support wide and effective application of nanoremediation for contaminated soil and water.
  • The project was coordinated by TREASURE Lead Partner ASEV and it piloted a mini- (nano)remediation laboratory in Tuscany (geographically diffused laboratory combining multidisciplinary expertise of different laboratories).     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal TREASURE contributes to the Green Deal and in particular its key deliverable “EU Action Plan: “Towards Zero Pollution for Air, Water and Soil”, which ties together all relevant EU policies to tackle and prevent pollution. The plan has the following overall vision "Air, water and soil pollution is reduced to levels no longer considered harmful to health and natural ecosystems and that respect the boundaries our planet can cope with, thus creating a
    • toxic-free environment."
    • In general, TREASURE hopes to contribute to the creation of toxic-free environment, focused in and around port areas, seen as crucial to protect our biodiversity and ecosystems and to reduce negative impact on human health.
    • Among its key targets for 2030, those to which TREASURE hope to contribute are particularly those related to improving water quality (reducing waste, plastic litter at sea (by 50%) and micro plastics released into the environment (by 30%). TREASURE also considers pollution in sediments and the areas surrounding the port. As such, it can also contribute to the objectives to improve soil quality.
    • Within its section on "Living within our planetary boundaries", the Action Plan dedicates signficant space to the quality of marine water and marine ecosystems.
    • The Action Plan includes a number of flagship initiatives and actions, with the most pertinent for TREASURE being those related to reviewing the standards for the quality of water, including in EU rivers and seas and reducing soil pollution and enhancing restoration. TREASURE is also relevant to initiatives promoting multi-stakeholder input to zero pollution.
    • TREASURE also responds to the need explicitly cited in the Green Deal to support research and innovation that will accelerate and navigate green transition and deploy, demonstrate and de-risk novel techniques that can contribute to restoring degraded and polluted port ecosystems.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 TREASURE contributes to the Territorial Agenda 2030 and its priority “A Green Europe that protects common livelihoods and shapes societal transition”.
    • The Agenda specifically cites the importance of coordinated actions to improve air, soil and water quality and to halt the rapid, man-made loss of biodiversity.
    • In particular, TREASURE is relevant to the HEALTHY ENVIRONMENT priority, which should encourage better ecological livelihoods, climate-neutral and resilient towns, cities and regions. This priority cites the marine ecosystem as one of those being “fundamental to human existence and important for long-term sustainable development.” In the case of TREASURE, we propose practical solutions to encourage the recovery of such healthy environments in and around port areas.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion TREASURE contributes to the EUSAIR strategy, within its priority on Blue Growth. This priority cites the importance of “Blue technologies”. The novel remediation techniques being tested in TREASURE fit into this category and their testing in industrially relevant conditions is in line with the Strategy’s aim not only to promote R&I, but also business opportunities.
    • Moreover, the priority has a focus on governance and quadruple helix engagement. TREASURE’s governance framework is in line with this, encouraging joint commitment to reducing pollution in port areas through the creation of territorial labs.
    • TREASURE involves partners from the following EUSAIR member states: Greece, Italy, Albania, Montenegro. Partner CEI is directly involved in EUSAIR working groups.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion N/A. Focusing on port areas, TREASURE does not contribute directly to the EU Strategy for the Alpine Region.
    • Other WESTMED initiative.
    • The WESTMED initiative focuses on the concept of the blue economy and the related traditional and emerging economic maritime sectors in the Mediterranean region. Naturally, this include ports and the services that ports provide.
    • TREASURE is particularly relevant to Action 2. Biodiversity and marine habitat conservation and restoration. It can also contribute to Action 6. Maritime safety and the fight against marine pollution.
    • TREASURE involves partners from WEST MED member states: Italy, Spain, France and Malta, 1 Associate partner from Tunisia and 1 Associate partner representing a network able to involve other countries (CRPM).
    • Other Marine Strategy Framework Directive (MSFD)
    • The Marine Directive (MSFD – adopted in 2008, amendments in 2017, revision in 2023) aims to protect more effectively the marine environment across Europe. It set up initiatives to improve the qualitative and quantitative status of all water bodies, safeguard public health and clean bathing water, and halt the loss of biodiversity.
    • The report on the first implementation cycle (2020) showed that the framework requires work to tackle a number of predominant pressures, which includes excess nutrients and others types of pollution.
    • TREASURE seeks to become part of the move towards reducing sediment and water pollution, from various sources, in port areas.
