Testing energy-community & climate-resilient integrated paradigm for carbon neutrality and energy poverty shielding in MED city-port hinterlands

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
EnerCmed propose un nouveau paradigme pour l’arrière-pays énergétique positif et résilient au climat qui est centré sur le concept de communautés d’énergie renouvelable (CER) pour permettre la régénération énergétique des quartiers marginalisés de l’arrière-pays portuaire, normalement habités par des personnes exposées à des risques de pauvreté énergétique.Le paradigme est testé par l’activation de 6 CER dans l’arrière-pays marginalisé de Gênes (1), Valence (1), Patras (2), Pula (1) et Novigrad (1) pour rassembler 345 familles / utilisateurs exposés à la précarité énergétique, générer 278 MWh / an à partir de sources renouvelables (-160 tonnes de CO2 / an) et réduire de 15 à 20% les factures d’énergie.Pour chaque CER , un investissement dans la solution basée sur la nature (NBS) est effectué dans les quartiers cibles en tant que dissipateur thermique naturel pour équilibrer la consommation d’énergie de refroidissement résidentiel et contracter les phénomènes d’îlot de chaleur urbain.L’idée innovante est d’interpoler –de la conceptualisation du paradigme à la démonstration– 3 principes techniques tels que #CER #urban phénomènes d’îlots de chaleur # protection contre la pauvreté. Dans cette perspective,EnerCmed intègre le principe de planification énergétique au niveau de la dimension climatique et sociale des quartiers marginalisés.Dans EnerCmed, la pierre angulaire de la coopération transnationale est représentée par l’instrument de facilité de connaissances (KFI), un mécanisme de soutien complet dans le cadre duquel un pool de partenaires hautement spécialisés aide le partenaire pilote à transposer le plan d’action dans des projets pilotes applicatifs de CER-NBS.EnerCmed répond à l’objectif d’INTERREG MED de faciliter les plans de transition énergétique/résilience dans les villes,renforcer l’engagement des citoyens pour un espace de vie plus durable et fournit un programme de réplication multi-villes pour permettre à d’autres villes MED d’adopter les solutions

Description (EN):
EnerCmed proposes of a new paradigm for energy-positive & climate resilient hinterlands that is centred on the concept of Renewable Energy Communities (REC) and that has the ambition to enable the energy regeneration of marginalized neighborhood in port hinterlands, normally inhabited by people exposed to risks of energy poverty. The paradigm is tested by the activation of 7 RECs in marginalized hinterlands of Genova (1), Valencia (2), Patras (2), Pula (1) & Novigrad (1) to gather 345 families/users exposed to energy poverty, generate 278 MWh/y from renewable sources corresponding to 160 tons CO2/year reduction and reduce by 15-20% the energy bills. For each REC created one Nature Based Solution (NBS) investment is carried on in the target neighborhoods as natural heat sink to balance the residential cooling energy-use & contract the Urban Heat Island phenomena. The innovative idea is to interpolate – from paradigm conceptualization to test demonstration - 3 technical-driven principles such as #energy communities #urban heat islands phenomena and #energy poverty shielding. In this perspective, EnerCmed integrates traditional REC trilemma (engaging people, ideal business model and governance structure, system engineering/interoperability/digitalization) with energy planning principle at micro-site climate & social dimension (marginalized neighborhoods) level. In EnerCmed, the cornerstone of the transnational cooperation is represented by the Knowledge Facility Instrument (KFI), a comprehensive support-mechanisms where a pool of highly specialized partners helps the pilot partner to transpose the transnational Hinterland Renewables Communities Action Plan in applicative REC-NBS pilots. EnerCmed responds to INTERREG MED goal of facilitate plans&strategies for energy transition and resilience in cities and reinforce citizens engagement for more sustainable living area and delivers a multi-city replication programme to enable further MED cities to adopt the solutions

Actual Achievements (FR):
Au cours de sa première année, EnerCmed a réalisé des avancées significatives en posant les bases communes pour le déploiement des Communautés d’Énergie Renouvelable (REC) associées à des Solutions Basées sur la Nature (NBS) dans cinq villes portuaires méditerranéennes. Le projet s’est concentré sur l’identification des conditions techniques, réglementaires, environnementales et sociales influençant la faisabilité des modèles REC-NBS dans les quartiers urbains vulnérables. À travers des évaluations conjointes, l’engagement des acteurs locaux et la co-conception d’outils stratégiques, les partenaires ont préparé le terrain pour une mise en œuvre concrète visant à lutter contre la précarité énergétique et à renforcer la résilience climatique dans les quartiers marginalisés. Deux études clés ont soutenu ce travail : l’Analyse de Contexte (D1.1.1), qui a cartographié les conditions juridiques, infrastructurelles et sociales ; et la Cartographie des REC activables (D1.2.1), qui a identifié les sites propices à l’implantation des REC et a favorisé l’engagement local à travers des Journées Portes Ouvertes dans toutes les villes pilotes. Les progrès se sont poursuivis avec la rédaction du Plan d’Action pour les Communautés d’Énergie Renouvelable de l’Hinterland, qui guidera la mise en œuvre des pilotes, ainsi que la définition d’un Cahier des Charges commun pour la création des REC. Parallèlement, le projet a élaboré le Portefeuille de Solutions Basées sur la Nature à Petite Échelle (D2.4.1), offrant aux villes pilotes un catalogue d’interventions vertes reproductibles — telles que des toitures végétalisées, des surfaces perméables et des structures d’ombrage — afin d’atténuer les effets des vagues de chaleur et de réduire la demande en climatisation. L’Instrument de Soutien Technique (KFI) a été lancé pour fournir un appui technique et des outils partagés à l’ensemble du partenariat. Le projet a également initié le suivi de son propre bilan carbone via la plateforme ZeroCO2. La présence numérique d’EnerCmed a été établie à travers un site web, une page LinkedIn et une vidéo institutionnelle, favorisant la communication à la fois avec les communautés locales et les parties prenantes du secteur. Cette première année a mis en évidence la valeur de la coopération transnationale — combinant des méthodologies communes avec des adaptations locales — pour concevoir des solutions inclusives et reproductibles.

Actual Achievements (EN):
During the first reporting period, significant progress was made in various project activities, primarily within Work Package 1 (WP1): Transnational Paradigm of Energy-Positive & Climate-Resilient Mediterranean Hinterlands Centered on Renewable Energy Communities (REC) & Nature-Based Solutions (NBS). The work packages were subdivided into activities led by different partners, fostering a truly collaborative approach. The key achievements include: -Strategic Assessment for REC Development: A comprehensive Background Analysis for pilot development was carried out. This included legal and normative dashboards, spatial planning, critical infrastructure audits, micro-climate analysis of Urban Heat Islands (UHI) at neighborhood levels, and social composition and energy poverty reviews. These analyses were tailored for each of the partner cities, with contributions from UNIGE, SIN, UCY, VCE, IRENA, PAT, MGE, ETRA, and LAR. -Mapping of REC Potential: REC activation potential was mapped for pilot projects. Local stakeholder groups were engaged to initiate a co-design process. Open Days were organized in the five pilot cities to explain the benefits and potential of RECs, aiming to garner citizen support. While some Open Days have already been held, others are planned. -Establishment of the Transnational Knowledge Facility Instrument (KFI): This tool has been successfully created to mentor pilot city investments during the test phase (WP2) and facilitate knowledge transfer during the replication phase (WP3). The KFI meets regularly, and partners such as UNIGE, SIN, UCY, PAT, and ETRA are actively involved. -Carbon Footprint Monitoring: All project partners have put in place mechanisms to regulate and monitor the project’s carbon footprint in alignment with the overall environmental goals. -NBS portfolio: Draft results for a portfolio of small-scale nature-based solutions (NBS) have been developed, led by UCY. Key meetings and events included a kick-off meeting and technical workshop in Genoa (organized by UNIGE), monthly KFI meetings, Open Days with local communities and stakeholders (e.g., in Genoa), and participation in national conferences: 2.Project performance monitoring To ensure smooth project implementation, internal deadlines were established for submitting drafts and preliminary results of activities. So far, a few delays have occurred but have not affected the overall delivery timeline of key activities. No significant issues with performance or spending deviations have been encountered. 3. Progress During this reporting period, significant progress was made across multiple work packages, with each partner playing a vital role in advancing their respective tasks. The collaborative efforts and specialized contributions ensured a well-coordinated approach, fostering the achievement of key milestones. Specific examples of partner involvement include: WP1 Transnational paradigm of energy-positive & climate resilient MED hinterlands centred on REC & NBS: - A 1.1. UNIGE took a leadership role by organizing the kick-off meeting and technical workshop in Genoa, with participation from all project partners. This provided a platform for aligning strategies and work plans. - A 1.1. Strategic Assessments and Background Analysis: UNIGE led the strategic assessments, while background analysis activities were carried out by project partners according to their respective pilot cities: MSE for Genoa, VCE for Valencia, IRENA for Pula and Novigrad, and PAT for Patras. This collaboration ensured tailored analyses that reflect each city's unique characteristics and needs. - A 1.2 Open Days and Local Stakeholder Engagement: The organization of open days and the activation of local stakeholders for the co-design process were critical to the project’s success. This activity, led by MSE, saw contributions from UNIGE, MSE for Genoa, VCE for Valencia, IRENA for Pula and Novigrad, and PAT for Patras, emphasizing the widespread engagement of local communities. - A 1.4 Transnational Knowledge Facility Instrument (KFI): UNIGE, SIN, UCY, PAT, and ETRA actively participated in the formation and ongoing operations of the KFI. This activity, led by SIN, ensures the transnational exchange of knowledge and experience, reinforcing cooperation among project partners. - A 1.5 Carbon Footprint Monitoring: Partners including UNIGE, SIN, UCY, VCE, IRENA, PAT, MGE, ETRA, and LAR were deeply involved in the carbon footprint monitoring processes. This activity, jointly led by SIN and UNIGE, is a critical aspect of the project, ensuring that the environmental impacts are continuously monitored and regulated. WP2 Testing applicative models for emergence energy & climate positive neighborhoods combining REC & NBS - A 2.4 Nature-Based Solutions Portfolio: UCY took the lead in developing the portfolio of small-scale nature-based solutions, contributing to the overall goal of enhancing climate resilience in the Mediterranean hinterlands. In its first year, EnerCmed made solid progress in building a shared foundation for deploying Renewable Energy Communities (RECs) coupled with Nature-Based Solutions (NBS) in five Mediterranean port cities. The project focused on identifying the technical, regulatory, environmental, and social conditions that influence the feasibility of REC-NBS models in vulnerable urban areas. Through joint assessments, local engagement, and the co-design of strategic tools, partners prepared the ground for real-world implementation aimed at tackling energy poverty and enhancing climate resilience in marginalized districts. Two key studies supported this work: the Background Analysis (D1.1.1), which mapped legal, infrastructural, and social conditions; and the Mapping of Activable RECs (D1.2.1), which identified viable locations for REC deployment and supported local engagement through Open Days in all pilot cities. Progress continued with the drafting of the Hinterland Renewables Communities Action Plan, which will guide pilot implementation, and the definition of a common Terms of Reference for REC setup. In parallel, the project delivered the Portfolio of Small-Scale Nature-Based Solutions (D2.4.1), offering pilot cities a catalogue of replicable green interventions—such as green roofs, permeable surfaces, and shading structures—to help mitigate heat stress and reduce cooling demand. The Knowledge Facility Instrument (KFI) was launched to provide technical support and shared tools across the partnership. The project also started monitoring its own carbon footprint through the ZeroCO2 platform. EnerCmed’s digital presence was established through a website, LinkedIn page, and institutional video, supporting communication with both local communities and sector stakeholders. The year highlighted the value of transnational cooperation—combining shared methodologies with local adaptation to shape inclusive, scalable solutions. During its first 18 months, EnerCmed has completed the conceptual phase and launched the implementation of pilot actions, while also setting the basis for replication in new Mediterranean cities. The project has already delivered concrete results and demonstrated the added value of working transnationally. Conceptual framework (WP1): The consortium finalised the Terms of Reference (D1.3.1), a common methodological framework for Renewable Energy Communities (RECs) in marginalised port districts. The document integrates legal, financial, technical, and social aspects, and was refined step by step with partner contributions. The Knowledge Facility Instrument (KFI) was established with its guidelines and first training materials (videos, infographics, technical checklists). It now acts as a mentoring and knowledge-sharing tool for pilot cities and follower municipalities. Partners contributed to the carbon footprint monitoring, ensuring the project aligns with Euro-MED environmental standards. Pilot preparation and activation (WP2): All pilot cities (Genoa, Valencia, Patras, Pula, Novigrad, and Larnaka) advanced in preparing or activating RECs and Nature-Based Solutions (NBS). In Genoa, one photovoltaic (PV) plant is already operational at Volta school, while a second is under development at Ferrero school, combined with an educational NBS garden. In Valencia, two schools were chosen: Ballester Fandos (PV + NBS) and Ausiàs March (community engagement and NBS). Families, teachers, and local NGOs are directly involved. In Patras, feasibility assessments and stakeholder engagement were completed to support pilot REC activation. In Pula and Novigrad, technical designs for PV systems and NBS measures were finalised, with local approvals secured. In Larnaka, the municipality prepared its role as lighthouse meta-replicator and successfully hosted the 3rd Steering Committee and a scientific symposium. Deliverable D2.2.1 Preliminary assessments consolidated technical and economic sustainability analyses across all pilots. UCY produced the first Portfolio of small-scale NBS (D2.4.1), offering practical design solutions adapted to Mediterranean microclimates. ETRA advanced the Energy Tracking Platform, defining key indicators, data flows, and digital functionalities for common monitoring. Replication (WP3): The MED Multi-City Replication Programme was launched, including a call for new follower cities and the preparation of an info webinar. Work has started on the MED Joint Strategy and the Atlas of coupled REC/NBS, which will transfer project knowledge to a wider network of Mediterranean municipalities. Communication and engagement: Over a dozen Open Days, workshops, and conferences were organised in partner countries, reaching local citizens, schools, NGOs, and public authorities. EnerCmed launched its newsletter, while partners produced infographics, videos, and media articles to improve visibility and awareness. The Larnaka Symposium (May 2025) brought together academics, municipalities, and NGOs, reinforcing the scientific and practical dimension of EnerCmed.

(EN) | The project exploits, upgrades and customizes the system architecture developed in the research project HESTIA project, which is currently used for energy tracking and energy balancing in the 3 pilots (Berchidda, Italy ; Palaiseau, France ; Voorhout, The Netherland). Hence, the energy tracking platform implemented in the action 2.5 will consider the business logic of HESTIA platform and will represent a necessary and crosscutting requirement for the full functioning of RECs within the MED project. Furthermore, the platform will test -as simulation - a preliminary form of demand response (platform advanced function), to enable potential inter-consumers and/or inter-communities trading/sharing of sustainable energy virtual-net-metering. Such functionalities will be inspired to HESTIA User-personalized services (consumer digital twin and non-intrusive data analytics) to process aggregated energy resource flexibility at the demand-side, in terms of cumulative energy consumption, distributed energy generation and storage, to better manage the disparity between energy demand, RES availability and grid requirement. The synergies are assured by the presence of SINLOC, that will put the system design & logic available to the developer of the project’s platform.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal As the overall goal of EU Green Deal is to decarbonize Europe, the 6 RECs activated in EnerCmed generate 278 MWh/y energy from renewable sources corresponding to 160 tons CO2/year reduction and offer a sounding example of REC feasibility and deployment that shall be transferred to the multitude of similar cities in MED cooperation area. Hence, EnerCmed contributes directly to the EU Green Deal, which is characterized by 1 overall framework policy that aim to deliver the decarbonization of the building sector through the Renovation Wave initiative. EnerCmed also contributes to the Clean Energy for All Europeans Package, as part of EU Green Deal, which puts emphasis on the need to empower, mobilise and engage citizens & companies for reaching our climate energy objective. At this regard, the Energy Communities have been considered as a priority topic for local development and for the Cohesion Policy to support the transition to cleaner and more efficient energy system both in urban and internal areas.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 EnerCmed contributes to the EU Territorial Agenda 2030 - Priority “green Europe”, as is meets the goals of strengthening energy resilience and having energy supplies at affordable prices (which is mandatory to support regional development, and preserve natural environment also against energy. Indeed, as underlined in the Preamble, (38) Secure, affordable and sustainable energy and (41) Nature, landscape and cultural heritage, strengthening energy resilience and having energy supplies at affordable prices is mandatory to support regional development, and, at the same time, there is the need to preserve natural environment also against energy production (fossil fuels). In the Priority “green Europe” is stressed how the transition of Europe’s economies towards carbon/climate-neutral economy is based on assets of local/regional players. EnerCmed aims at supporting
    • large-scale uptake of 6 Renewable Energy Communities in marginalized neighborhoods of 5 MED cities (Genova, Valencia, Patras, Pula & Novigrad), with the final aim of enabling vulnerable groups of people to take an active role in energy transition, contrast energy poverty and globally reducing the thermal budget at micro-climate urban scaleFrom one side, it helps to enhance energy resilience and low energy cost, as it leads to a nearly–zero use of fossil fuels. From the other, it benefits the environment, keeping urban heating (and cooling) pollution at a very low level.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The project contributes to EUSAIR Pillar 2 “Connecting the regions”, in
    • particular to Topic 3 “Energy networks” aiming at achieving a well interconnected and well-functioning internal energy market supporting the three energy policy objectives of the EU – competitiveness, security of supply and sustainability. The REC’s way to clean, inclusive and independent energy enhance the security of supply of energy, especially looking at this historical moment of continental energy supply crises, as it diminishes the necessity of fossils fuels for buildings.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion With the innovative pilot approach, the EnerCmed project contributes to EUSALP’s policy areas (1) ECONOMIC GROWTH AND INNOVATION, as the innovative approach shall be replicated and implemented in alpine space cities, which are still predominantly carbon-based and will thus also trigger the sustainable development and new job opportunities (2) ENVIRONMENT AND ENERGY; The aim of the project is to directly implement an innovative RES solution that will, as multiplied, encourage the sustainable approach in other alpine regions, to make the area more environmental and and carbon-free.
    • Other As mentioned, REC have a value-driven concepts that have as primary goal to provide environmental, social and economic benefits to the community members or to the local areas where they operate. One of the key drivers of EnerCmed is indeed the social dimension of the pilot RECs, as the 6 RECs gather 345 families/users exposed to energy poverty in the 5 targeted neighbourhoods in Genova (1) Valencia (1) Patras (2) Pula (1) and Novigrad (1). In this sense, EnerCmed provides a significant contribution to contrast energy poverty as RECs represent a direct response of the Local Authorities to alleviate energy poverty risks of the most vulnerable sections of populations and has directly connected to social inclusion and integration of marginalized group of people. This is lined up with the Electricity Directive that addresses appropriate frameworks to tackle energy poverty', define 'vulnerable customers', and protect them, for instance through social security systems, prohibition of disconnection of gas and electricity in critical times (ie. energy supply crisis linked to Russia's war on Ukraine), and provision of energy efficiency improvements. Furthermore, EnerCmed directly contributes to the provisions set into the fourth energy package ('Clean energy for all Europeans', that included several legislative acts with energy poverty clauses.
