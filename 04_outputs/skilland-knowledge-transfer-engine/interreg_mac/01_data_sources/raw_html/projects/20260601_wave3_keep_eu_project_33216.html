Non Conventional Water Resources for Resilient Urban Water Management

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 064. Water management and water resource conservation (including river basin management, specific climate change adaptation measures, reuse, leakage reduction) ~ SUMMARY: The cumulative impacts of climate change and demographic shifts in the Mediterranean will increase both the stress on freshwater resources and the difficulties in all countries to sustain water supply security (WSS). This joint challenge will be particularly felt in urban coastal catchments (UCC) who suffer water deficits due to high population densities and limited inland natural freshwater resources, which resources are further threatened by anthropogenic pollution and sea-water intrusion. In UCCs municipal water demand becomes the predominant demand factor in the catchment water balance, and hence a water management priority. NCWR RUWM focuses on urban water demand hotspots and will identify trends in water resources availability and water demand development to assess inherent future risks to WSS. The assessment will identify future alternative opportunities and develop resilient Urban Water Management Plans (UWMPs) for sustaining WSS which complement current solutions, namely centralized supply augmentation measures such as (basin-scale) sea-water desalination, the construction of dams for surface water reservoirs and/or end-of pipe wastewater treatment. NCWR RUWM will therefore explore the potential application of alternative water demand management solutions based on the application of small scale decentralized NCWR technologies, providing opportunities for addressing urban water demand at the point of use without increasing the dependence on freshwater resources. Decentralized NCWR technologies, if integrated in UWMPs can ensure a sustainable and reliable water management framework for UCCs. Outcomes are beneficial for national and municipal authorities in enabling future climate-proof UWMPs (adaptation), and to all water consumers in ensuring long-term WSS as well as lower consumption and associated water bills. NCWR RUWM solutions will also lead to lower GHG emissions entailing a broad climate change mitigation benefit to the region. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Knowledge and technology transfer, Urban development, Water management

Relevant linked projects:

  • Nature Based Solutions for Domestic Water Reuse in Mediterranean Countries - NAWAMED/Eni CBCMed Programme (EN) | NCWR-RUWM will keep building on the outcomes of the NAWAMED Project which was concluded in December 2023. The Energy and Water Agency and CERTE were partners to the NAWAMED Project.
  • The main outcomes of NAWAMED which will be used by NCWR-RUWM include:
  • - Outcomes of surveys on the potential separation of greywater and reuse in Italy, Malta, Jordan, Lebanon and Tunisia
  • - Strategy documents for the application of greywater reuse technologies in Italy, Malta, Jordan, Lebanon and Tunisia
  • - Policy Document on the application of greywater reuse in the Mediterranean Region
  • - Results of the application of pilot projects (greenwalls and wetlands) in Tunisia, as well as by the Associated Partners in Lebanon and Jordan.
  • NCWR-RUWM will seek to capitalize on the outcomes of the NAWAMED project upscaling the outcomes of this project from the demonstration case level to the urban catchment level through the integration in Urban Water Management Plans.
  • The outcomes of the NAWAMED Project will therefore provide support to WP3 (development of catalogue of NCWR solutions) and WP6 (governance and policy assessments for the development of Urban Water Management Plans).
  • EWA (applicant) and CERTE(PP4) were partners to the NAWAMED Project. (EN), WEFE4MED - Mediterrranean Water Energy Food Ecosystems Nexus Community of Practice - PRIMA (EN) | The following WEFE4MED outputs will be exploited:
  • (i) Methodology for the development of a Community of Practice on the application of the WEFE Nexus in the Mediterranean Region, and
  • (ii) IWRM Action Hub Platform - a structured and inclusive platform, providing a neutral space for enhanced interactions among sectors and stakeholders to demonstrate and unleash the power of a cooperative Nexus approach, and lead a green transformation of Mediterranean societies and economies.
  • The outcomes of WEFE4MED will primarily support WP4 of the NCWR_RUWM project providing key experience on the challenges related to the setting up and management of the Community of Practice, and the online tools such as the Action Hub required to maintain information flow within the Community of Practice. Moreover NCWR-RUWM aims to develop synergies with the WEFE4MED Community of Practice and ensure a high level of communication and support between both initiatives.

  • - Training Programmes for technicians on the application of modern NCWE solutions, intended to build capacity in installing and repairing these system at the local level, and
