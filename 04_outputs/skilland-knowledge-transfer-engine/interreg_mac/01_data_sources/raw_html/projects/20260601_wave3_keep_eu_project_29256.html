Testing New European Bauhaus Solutions for Deep Green Transformation of Cities and Regions in Euro-MED area.

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (EN):
Average citizen usually considers ecology and green transformation as something that “must be” done, instead of something they would “love to” do. To change this perception, the New European Bauhaus (NEB) initiative introduced aesthetic (beautiful) aspects along with citizens participation to support urban green transformation. Project Bauhaus4MED will test innovative NEB solutions integrating sustainability, inclusion and aesthetic aspects into decision making process, for improvement of green living areas. Results from Interreg MED BLUE CROWDFUNDING project will be upgraded into a new “CROWDVOCACY” tool combining crowdfunding, crowdsourcing and participative democracy principles, with aesthetic green architecture design resulting in citizens tailor-made solutions for better living. Each of the 5 testing regions/cities will identify participative green community challenges, use crowdsourcing to co-create solution ideas, run civic crowdfunding campaigns to co-finance solutions and use participative budgets to implement pilot solutions. Pilot solutions will be focused on circular building materials, regenerated green urban spaces, green products, or green lifestyle. Transferrable toolbox will be offered as a solution including best example catalogue, lessons learned and CROWDVOCACY digital tool. To mainstream NEB principles into the decision making process, and ensure long term sustainability, the regions/cities will develop green transformation action plans, using green, participative, and aesthetic principles. Project will improve policy impact, transferability and durability of outputs and deliverables by organizing significant number of regional and EU level events taking advantage of PP10 IFLA Europe unique position as member of NEB COLLECTIVE. Actions will include high-policy NEB annual conferences in Brussels, regions joining NEB initiative, Transnational Green Transformation Challenge event and preparation of Applications to NEB Rising Stars 2025.

Description (FR):
En général l'écologie et la transformation verte sont considérées comme une obligation. Pour changer cette perception, l'initiative New European Bauhaus (NEB) a introduit des aspects esthétiques et la participation des citoyens pour soutenir la transformation verte urbaine. Euro-MED BAUHAUS testera des solutions innovantes du NEB en intégrant la durabilité, l'inclusion et les aspects esthétiques dans la prise de décision pour améliorer les espaces verts. Les résultats du projet Interreg MED BLUE CROWDFUNDING seront mis à niveau dans un outil "CROWDVOCACY" avec l’aide du financement participatif, du crowdsourcing et de la démocratie participative, et d’une conception d'architecture verte esthétique apportant des solutions sur mesure pour améliorer le niveau de vie. Chacune des 5 régions/villes à essai identifiera les défis communautaires verts participatifs, utilisera le crowdsourcing pour cocréer des solutions et lancera des campagnes de financement participatif civil pour les cofinancer, utilisera des budgets participatifs pour mettre en œuvre des solutions pilotes qui seront axées sur la construction circulaire, les espaces urbains verts régénérés, les produits verts et le style de vie vert. Une boîte à outils transférable comprendra un catalogue de meilleurs exemples, des leçons apprises et l'outil numérique CROWDVOCACY. Pour intégrer les principes du NEB et assurer la durabilité à long terme, les régions/villes élaboreront des plans d'action de transformation verte, en utilisant des principes verts, participatifs et esthétiques. Le projet améliorera l'impact politique, la transférabilité et la durabilité des résultats et des livrables en organisant des événements au niveau régional et européen grâce à la position unique de PP10 IFLA Europe - membre du NEB COLLECTIVE :des conférences annuelles du NEB à Bruxelles, des régions rejoignant le NEB, un événement Transnational Green Transformation Challenge et la préparation des candidatures au NEB Rising Stars 2025.

Actual Achievements (EN):
The following tasks were implemented in PR1: WP1: TESTING ADVANCED BAUHAUS PARTICIPATORY METHODS FOR THE GREEN TRANSFORMATION OF LIVING AREAS The specific objective of WP1 is to test and develop sustainable, inclusive and aesthetic green solutions in partner-testing regions' living areas. Partnership work was focused on Activity 1, creating the internal deliverable "D1.1.0 Tailored Roadmap of participatory methods for enhancing development and testing NEB solutions". The Roadmap will be a tool to support our partner-testing regions and cities (PP2 RCM, PP3 SVEM, PP6 Regione Toscana, PP8 Sarajevo, PP9 Burgas) with digital and offline approaches, explaining how to engage local stakeholders and citizens in co-creating and co-developing green solutions. An essential aspect of our project is setting up the community dialogue and co-creation process with consideration of three NEB values: sustainable (in harmony with nature), together (connecting disciplines), and beautiful (inspired by local art and culture). To learn how to plan and implement participatory methods and how to use NEB values, task leader PP4 Platoniq, with the support of expert partners LP, PP5, PP7 and PP10, prepared eight online training courses for all partners (on the 26th of March, 16th and 23rd of April, 9th and 21st of May, 4th, 11th and 18th of June 2024). The final two courses will be implemented in person as a workshop at the 2nd Project Group meeting in Burgas, Bulgaria (2nd and 3rd July 2024). The materials prepared for online courses present the content base for "D1.1.0 Roadmap" which was finalised at the end of PR1. WP2: ENSURING SUSTAINABILITY OF GREEN TRANSFORMATION The specific objective of WP2 is to create a long-term support framework for the development of green transformation in five partner-testing regions (PP2 RCM, PP3 SVEM, PP6 Regione Toscana, PP8 Sarajevo, PP9 Burgas) by setting up operational local nodes (networks) consisting of quintuple helix stakeholders. Nodes will include representatives of regional policy, academia, business, civil society and culture & creative sector representing the 5th "aesthetic" helix. The stakeholders of five local nodes will co-create regional strategies and action plans focused on green transformation, encompassing NEB principles and values. In PR1, project partners (PP2 RCM, PP3 SVEM, PP6 Regione Toscana, PP8 Sarajevo, PP9 Burgas), who are responsible for setting up the local nodes, as part of "Activity 2.1 Creating and operation of local nodes to evolve a Green Planetary Mindset" started to identify their key regional stakeholders. PP3 SVEM prepared a joint template report in which each partner will present the team of stakeholders and their competencies and roles. As part of "Activity 2.4 Contribution to the Results Amplification Strategy", Lead partner E-zavod, PP5 Crowdpolicy and PP6 Tuscany region participated in the Green Living Areas Thematic Community Kick-off event on the 10th and 11th of April 2024 in Turin, Italy, presenting the project and identifying synergies with other community projects. WP3: BAUHAUS4MED GOES BEYOND BORDERS The specific objective of WP3 is improving the policy impact and visibility of Euro-MED green transformation towards the New European Bauhaus initiative and the visibility of the Interreg Euro-MED programme in the EU. Our expert partner, PP10 IFLA (European Region of the International Federation of Landscape Architects) started to implement tasks of "Activity 3.5 High-level BAUHAUS4MED visibility and dialogue with EU decision makers". Firstly, PP10 IFLA participated and presented our project at the "New European Bauhaus Festival" on 9-13 April 2024 in Brussels, Belgium. Secondly, IFLA Europe Med Working Group and our project partners PP10 IFLA and PP2 RCM organised a conference and meeting, "Vulnerable landscapes - Towards green living landscapes", on 24-25 May 2024 in Athens, Greece. MANAGEMENT Project group meetings in person: the lead partner organised a kick-off project meeting in Bled, Slovenia, on 23.01.2024. The Steering Committee was set up at the kick-off meeting, and Steering Committee rules were presented. The second project group meeting is planned in Burgas, Bulgaria, on the 2nd and 3rd of July 2024. Project task online meetings organised by lead partner and task leaders: 27th of February, 4th of March, 4th of April 2024 and 28th of May 2024. COMMUNICATION Communication coordinator PP5 Crowdpolicy developed and presented the project Communication Plan. The plan outlines future communication strategies, guidelines for publishing relevant material and information on social media, and the project branding manual. PP5 prepared the content for the project website and set up the social media channels (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and X). In the first year of project implementation, the first steps were focused on preparation, how each city or region can adapt the values of the New European Bauhaus to local needs and address green urban challenges with citizens and stakeholders. To answer this, teams in Italy (Tuscany and Marche region), Bulgaria (Burgas), Bosnia and Herzegovina (Sarajevo), and Greece (Central Macedonia region) worked closely with local stakeholders to shape a common approach. To support the dialogue and engagement at local levels, we developed a “CROWDVOCACY”, an online platform that allows citizens to share ideas, give feedback, and participate in designing greener and more liveable neighbourhoods. With this platform, each region has begun identifying the environmental challenges that matter most to local people, from heat islands and lack of shade to abandoned parks and underused public areas. These findings are now feeding into a public call for citizen-designed solutions. Later this year, each region will run a competition inviting people to submit creative ideas for improving their green spaces—ideas that are sustainable, inclusive, and beautiful. The best ones will be tested and implemented on-site. To ensure long-term change in approaching climate and green challenges, each region has also formed a local green transformation group called “LOCAL NODES”. These are diverse networks comprising representatives from government, education, business, civil society, and the creative sectors—all committed to keeping the momentum going. Local nodes are playing an active role in our project. For example, representatives recently joined a Europe-wide session to share what they have learned and contribute to shaping future actions for greener living areas. Although focused on local communities, this project also aims to spark change at the European level. One of the project partners, the European Federation of Landscape Architects, has already begun presenting the initiative at major events across Europe, bringing the voices of our five regions and cities into conversations with national and European decision-makers. With people at the centre and nature as a guiding principle, these regions and cities are transforming public spaces and shaping a new way of thinking about how we live together, care for our environment, and create a shared sense of beauty and belonging. WP1: TESTING ADVANCED BAUHAUS PARTICIPATORY METHODS FOR THE GREEN TRANSFORMATION OF LIVING AREAS The specific objective of WP1 is to test and develop sustainable, inclusive, and aesthetically pleasing green solutions in the living areas of partner-testing regions. Partnership work focused on finalising Activity 1.2, Identifying Green Transformation Community Challenges. A coordinator of the task, PP6 Regione Toscana, prepared an internal deliverable, D1.2.1, titled "Internal Deliverable Report on Green Transformation Challenges." The deliverable includes the presentation of identified turbulences, NEB categories related to challenges, a summary of activities realised and ongoing, NEB principles targeted, a presentation of local stakeholders, participatory tools used, and a time framework for the pilot site of the testing partners (PP2, PP3, PP6, PP8, PP9). Additionally, Activity 1.3, the Citizens-driven BAUHAUS solutions competition, was implemented. All partner regions/cities, except PP2 RCM, published an open call on the project’s digital Crowdvocacy tool and invited citizens to provide solutions for the identified challenges. To ensure a joint project approach related to local open calls, task coordinators PP4 Platoniq, PP5 Crowdpolicy and PP7 AF ZG prepared an Open Call Handbook. This guide supports institutional partners in organising local Open Calls to gather citizen-driven, innovative solutions that address green transformation challenges in alignment with the New European Bauhaus (NEB) values: Sustainability, Aesthetics, and Inclusion. An internal deliverable, D1.3.1 Factsheet list of selected pilot projects, will be prepared in September 2025, once the PP2 RCM open call is finalised. The winning solutions of the local open calls will be implemented in Activity A1.4 Pilot implementation. WP2: ENSURING SUSTAINABILITY OF GREEN TRANSFORMATION The specific objective of WP2 is to create a long-term support framework for the development of green transformation in five partner-testing regions (PP2 RCM, PP3 SVEM, PP6 Regione Toscana, PP8 Sarajevo, PP9 Burgas). In PR3, project partners (PP2 RCM, PP3 SVEM, PP6 Regione Toscana, PP8 Sarajevo, and PP9 Burgas) and the stakeholders of five local nodes, under the coordination of PP3 SVEM, started the process of co-creating regional strategies and action plans focused on green transformation, encompassing NEB principles and values. The first task of Activity 2.2 was to investigate existing policies and plans in regions and cities that support green transformation. We also started with Activity A2.3 Local Bauhaus Festivals. The task leader, PP8 Sarajevo, organised an international Local Bauhaus Festival, titled “Green Transformation of Cities through New European Bauhaus,” on April 24, 2025. All PP members and stakeholders participated. Project partner PP3 SVEM organised a local Bauhaus festival on June 20, 2025. PP6 Regione Toscana organised a local Bauhaus Festival on June 14, 2025. As part of "Activity 2.4 Contribution to the Results Amplification Strategy", the communication coordinator, PP5 Crowdpolicy, presented our project and participated in the networking event, the 2nd Gathering of the Green Living Areas Thematic Community, held in Barcelona on May 28-29. 2025. Lead partner E-zavod participated in the Green Living Areas territorial event in Ljubljana, Slovenia, on April 16, 2025. WP3: BAUHAUS4MED goes beyond borders The specific objective of WP3 is to improve the policy impact and visibility of the Euro-MED green transformation in relation to the New European Bauhaus initiative and enhance the visibility of the Interreg Euro-MED programme within the EU. Our expert partner, PP10 IFLA, organised the event “Med_net Working Group”, organised by IFLA in Rome, Italy, from 8th to 10th of May 2025. On the event-specific project workshop was organised, “Bauhaus4Med Med-net Workshop I and II - Green transition and Nature-Based Solutions implementation” on May 8 2025. MANAGEMENT Project group meetings in person: PP8 Sarajevo, together with the lead partner, organised a 4th project group meeting in Sarajevo on April 23, 2025. Project task online meetings organised by the lead partner and task leaders: January 20, 4th and 31st of March 2025. COMMUNICATION The Communication Coordinator, PP5 Crowdpolicy, is leading the communication activities, including the preparation of articles, videos, and content for thematic areas. The website and social media are regularly updated.

Relevant linked projects:

  • N/A (FR), BLUE CROWDFUNDING-Capacity Building of BLUE Economy Stakeholders to Effectively use Crowdfunding (EN) | N/A (FR), Interreg MED (2019-2022), coordinated by LP1 E-zavod, focused on improving innovation capacities in blue growth sector by mainstreaming use of crowdfunding. Project was focused on triggering change from using public funds for innovation to use crowdfunding and crowdsourcing to fund, test and validate innovative blue economy products and services.
  • SYNERGY:
  • The training material for crowdfunding will be directly transferred to support new cities and regions (PP6, PP8, PP9) when implementing crowdfunding campaigns. In the final conference of BLUE-CROWDFUNDING, Platoniq presented the possibility of adoption and transferring the Crowdvocacy Tool - this tool will be transferred to the BAUHAUS4MED project for testing crowdsourcing and civic participation for improving green living areas.
  • (EN), N/A (FR), UPSURGE-City-centered approach to catalyze nature-based solutions through the EU Regenerative Urban Lighthouse for pollution alleviation and regenerative development (EN) | N/A (FR), Horizon 2020 (2021-2024), coordinated by LP1 E-zavod, project bridges the gap between the existing knowledge base on Nature Based Solutions and their step-by-step practical implementation for regenerative development of cities focusing on air pollution alleviation and climate neutrality.
  • Project is the response to the challenges faced by cities in their efforts to decrease the impacts of urban existence on climate change, mitigate air pollution and its health effects and reduce climate change stressors.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Using the knowledge and experience from the project, how to response to the challenges faced by cities in their efforts to decrease the impacts of urban existence on climate change, mitigate air pollution and its health effects and reduce climate change stressors. (EN), N/A (FR), NEB goes south - This community-led NEB Lab project connects six south European countries which join forces to reflect about and improve education through architecture (EN) | N/A (FR), EU Commission (2021-2023).PP7 AF ZG participates as project partner.
  • NEB goes South hopes to introduce a step forward by combining the efforts of students, architects, designers, engineers, geographers, sociologists, and other scientists and artists, with the ideas of everybody interested, to create a Southern perspective for New European Bauhaus goals to contribute to a climate neutral Europe by 2050. The NEB goes South initiative emerged as a direct response to the New European Bauhaus challenge. The project is organized by 6 schools of architecture from Portugal, Spain, France, Italy, Croatia and Greece. Its goal is to set up an open and collaborative platform for learning, debating, designing and inspiring actions combining sustainability, aesthetics and inclusion.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Using the knowledge and experience from community-led NEB Lab project that connects six south European countries which join forces to reflect about and improve education through architecture. Learning about an open and collaborative platform for learning, debating, designing and inspiring actions combining sustainability, aesthetics and inclusion. (EN), N/A (FR), URBAN INNO-Utilizing innovation potential of urban ecosystems (EN) | N/A (FR), Interreg CE (2016-2019). LP1 E-zavod participated as project partner. URBAN INNO focused on maximising innovation potentials of urban ecosystems through:
  • -Better linking actors in innovation systems by establishing and interlinking quadruple helix clusters and networks in the partner regions and
  • -on developing and implementing new participatory methods and tools to engage end-users in innovation processes with the objective to have educated and motivated users.
  • SYNERGY
  • Using one of the project's outputs, the toolbox of Smart urban innovation participatory methods & tools that helps to engage end-users (citizens, consumers) into urban innovation process (developing urban development strategies, smart urban solutions, services or technologies).
  • (EN), N/A (FR), CE RESPONSIBLE -ALTRUISTIC ENTREPRENEUR,
  • CE Sustainable model to support social entrepreneurship (EN) | N/A (FR), Interreg CE project (2019-2022), coordinated by LP1 E-zavod, focused on setting-up and improving transnational cooperation between social and altruistic entrepreneurs, on upscaling knowledge and innovation capacities of social entrepreneurs, development of model of cooperation between private sector andsocial entrepreneurs.
  • Projects result and mutual learning about already existing innovation capacities and cooperation between non-profit and for-profit sector in
  • the field of tackling different local social and envrionmental challenges. Project supported 90 pilot collaborations between two mentioned target groups.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Using the projects results (from 90 innovative pilot actions) about cooperation between non-profit and for-profit sector in the field of tackling different local social and environmental challenges.
  • (EN), N/A (FR), PROMETHEUS- Innovation and Entrepreneurship in the domains of Digital
  • Transformation, Circular Economy and Sustainable development (EN) | N/A (FR), EIT HEI Initiative (2021-2023). PP5 Crowdpolicy participates as project partner. PROMETHEUS is an initiative of eight partners from the R&I ecosystem (all participating in the PROMETHEUS HEI Accelerator proposal), with a vision to foster institutional engagement and change, through developing innovative and sustainable systems and services promoting Innovation and Entrepreneurship in the area of Digital Transformation, related to Circular Economy, Climate Change and Sustainable Development. PP5 Crowdpolicy is active in digital tools development and overall dissemination.
  • SYNERGY
  • Using the project results about fostering institutional engagement and change, through developing innovative and sustainable systems and services promoting Innovation and Entrepreneurship in the area of Digital Transformation, related to Circular Economy, Climate Change and Sustainable Development.
  • (EN), N/A (FR), TeRRItoria- Territorial Responsible Research and Innovation Through the involvement of local R&I Actors (EN) | N/A (FR), HORIZON 2020 (2019-2022), PP2 RCM particpated as a project partner.The aim of Territoria project was to create a "Gender Equality Plan for the Research and Innovation Field in the Region of Central Macedonia". The first step for the creation of GEP was the mapping of the RCM Research and Innovation ecosystem, the analysis of the local environment and stakeholders and the examination of the conditions for gender equality in Research and Innovation in Greece. Following that, Territoria relied heavily on co-planning with the quadruple helix for the shaping of the Plan with the significant contribution of the representatives of the academic and research community, the industry, and the political and social scene of the place.
  • During this co-creation phase, the strategic areas of intervention and the actions of the Plan were thoroughly discussed among the triple helix representatives with special workshops and “writeshops”. In addition, a publicly open event was organized with the participation of citizens during which the gaps and the challenges in gender equality issues in various social levels and environments were discussed.
  • (EN), N/A (FR), TRAM - Towards new Regional Action plans for sustainable urban Mobility (EN) | N/A (FR), Interreg Europe (2016-2021), PP3 Marche region was a project coordinator. Project focused on interregional learning process of Public Authorities for achieving sustainable Urban Mobility solutions resulting in Action Plans for the integration of lessons learnt into Investment for J&G OPs and Regional Low-Carbon Strategies.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Using experiences and lessons learned from the project for development of strategy and action plan. (EN), N/A (FR), Connecting Nature - "COproductioN with NaturE for City Transitioning, INnovation and Governance" (EN) | N/A (FR), Horizon 2020 (2017-2022). PP8 Grad Sarajevo and PP9 Municipality of Burgas participated as a project partners. Project aimed to position Europe as a global leader in the innovation and implementation of nature –based solutions.
  • The community of cities fostered peer to peer learning and capacity building among front runner cities who are experienced in delivering large scale nature-based solutions, and our fast follower cities who have the desire to implement large scale nature-based solutions but lack the expertise. As knowledge and expertise increases, new members (multiplier cities) were included.
  • At the same time, project partners developed policy and practices necessary to scale up urban resilience, innovation and governance using nature-based solutions. Approach included open and innovative, fostering the development of co-operation between local governments, SME’s, academic research and community partners to produce a tool kit and guidebook for cities seeking to deliver nature-based solutions in their locales.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Using the knowledge related to community of cities fostering peer to peer learning and capacity building in the field of nature-based solutions.
  • (EN), N/A (FR), ProGIreg-Productive Green Infrastructure for post-industrial urban regeneration (EN) | N/A (FR), Horizon 2020 (2018-2023). PP7 AF ZG participates as project partner.
  • For proGIreg four front-runner cities (Dortmund (DE); Turin (IT); Zagreb (HR); Ningbo (CN)) created Living Labs in urban areas which face the challenge of post-industrial regeneration. These areas suffer from social and economic disadvantages, inequality and related crime and security problems. They lack quality greenspaces, have a negative impact on human health and wellbeing and are more vulnerable to the effects of climate change. Going beyond the current state-of-the-art with Green Infrastructure as a one-off state intervention, the proGIreg Living Labs will develop NBS which are citizen owned and codeveloped by state, market and civil society stakeholders.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Building on living lab methodology, civil participation and co-development of solutions as well as knowledge on Nature based solutions for building green and aesthetic architectural solutions.
  • (EN), N/A (FR), CLL Innovative Green Building Research in the Campus Living Lab (EN) | N/A (FR), Croatian Science Foundation (2014-2017).PP7 AF ZG participated as project partner. The goal of this project is to research and develop an innovative model of green construction on campus (on the example of student housing) with A+ energy efficiency, using new materials and technologies, a design approach sensitive to climate changes and testing possible alternatives with the help of future users, all in accordance with the Living laboratory principles.
  • SYNERGY:
  • Lessons learned from green constructions, A+ energy efficiency and new materials for advising partner regions and cities on most appropriate New Bauhaus solutions. (EN)
