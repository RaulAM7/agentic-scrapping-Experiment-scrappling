Competitiveness and Internationalisation of Rural Tourism SMEs in the Mediterranean

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 021. SME business development and internationalisation, including productive investments ~ SUMMARY: CReSInMed focuses on strengthening and improving the internationalisation capacities of tourism (M)SMEs in the Mediterranean rural areas which face unique challenges: they are small, uncompetitive and their remoteness from large urban centres reduces access to markets, limiting opportunities for growth. Human resources have limited access to training opportunities. CReSInMed actions contribute to increase the potential of these (M)SMEs and focuses on improving skills and entrepreneurship, while also contributing to networking and strengthening transnational cooperation in the Mediterranean. The changes expected from the project compared to the existing situation is to increase competitiveness in regional, national and international markets of (M)SMEs, improve the related skills, and strengthen networking capacities of operators. 50 (M)SMEs enhance their knowledge with specific training programmes supported by a task force of experts. Topics related to productivity, innovation, considering the importance of digital transition and sustainability are addressed. Networking among operators is the basis for exchanging experiences, developing joint activities to make internationalisation sustainable. (M)SMEs can count on technological support to facilitate their work through the use of a digital toolbox that complements capacity building initiatives by providing tools to support (M)SMEs' growth and internationalisation efforts. The actions taken generate multiplier effects that extend to the sector concerned and the community. The solutions implemented by CReSInMed, in particular cluster, MoU and the platform, can continue to produce benefits after the end of the project. (M)SMEs, local communities, public authorities, stakeholders receive long-term benefits from project implementation, with improved socio-economic conditions. CReSInMed positively contributes to the transformation of the tourism and economic landscape of the rural regions involved. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Rural and peripheral development, SME and entrepreneurship, Tourism

  • - Development of business and internationalisation adaptation plans for the formation of local clusters: We will follow the INCLUST approach in developing detailed business and internationalisation adaptation plans that will guide the sustainable growth and competitiveness of local clusters, adapting them to the specific needs of rural (M)SMEs.
  • - Participation in a touristic fair: By participating in international exhibition, we will follow INCLUST example to promote the products and services of rural (M)SMEs globally.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other CReSInMed fits perfectly with the objectives of INTERREG NEXT MED and others policies of the EU as well as major international organizations such as the UN and the UfM, promoting economic and social development through cross-border cooperation.
    • Project actions address specific challenges in the Mediterranean region, such as promoting environmental sustainability, rural and sustainable tourism, and agribusiness, in line with the UN Agenda 2030 (SDGs 8, 9, and 16) and the EURO-MED programme objectives. We facilitate cultural exchange, promote intercultural understanding, and encourage collaboration among international (M)SMEs and stakeholder by improving skills and networking, thereby strengthening transnational cooperation in the Mediterranean.
    • CReSInMed aligns with UfM agenda to promote regional cooperation and tackle common challenges like economic development, inclusive growth, and environmental sustainability. It fosters dialogue and cooperation among Mediterranean by enhancing entrepreneurial skills in the tourism sector and promoting joint initiatives and partnerships. The project fully embraces the ESF by supporting rural tourism (M)SMEs to enhance capabilities and skills at Mediterranean level and aligning its objective with ERDF goals of regional development and economic competitiveness.
    • The project directly contributes to the NRRP objectives of EU Member States by promoting economic competitiveness and improving social and territorial cohesion. It also aligns with national programmes such as Tunisia's National Development Plan, Turkey 11th Development Plan, Jordan Economic Growth Plan, and Palestine's National Policy Agenda. CReSInMed share values of sustainable development and economic growth, enhancing regional cooperation and promoting social inclusion. It integrates in a complementary way with the COSME programme, contributing to strengthening initiatives aimed at supporting (M)SMEs in the tourism sector and promoting economic competitiveness.
