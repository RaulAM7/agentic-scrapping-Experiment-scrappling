Inclusive Coastal Resilience in the Euro-Med

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
INCORE-MED replace les citoyens au cœur de la planification côtière. En Italie, en Espagne, au Portugal et en Croatie, le projet teste deux outils de participation avancés visant à améliorer les politiques côtières et maritimes : SWAP et Democracy Labs. L'outil SWAP (Ateliers de scénarios + Pistes d'adaptation) aide les acteurs locaux à co-concevoir des plans d'adaptation au changement climatique réalistes en combinant les visions de la communauté avec les projections climatiques des experts, à l'aide de la cartographie de scénarios et de l'analyse multicritères. Quant aux laboratoires de démocratie, ils créent des espaces de discussion où les citoyens et les autorités élaborent conjointement de nouvelles règles et de nouveaux formats pour l'engagement public dans la planification marine. Ces outils sont déployés pour : 1) créer et mettre à jour des cartographies participatives et des cartes de perception des risques climatiques ; 2) intégrer des solutions basées sur la nature dans les plans d'action locaux ; 3) diffuser la méthodologie à de nouveaux territoires grâce à des réflexions méthodologiques et des cours en ligne.

Description (EN):
INCORE-MED brings citizens back into the heart of coastal planning. In Italy, Spain, Portugal, and Croatia, the project tests two advanced participation tools to improve coastal and marine policies: SWAP and Democracy Labs. The SWAP tool (Scenario Workshops + Adaptation Pathways) helps local actors co-design realistic climate adaptation plans by blending community visions with expert climate projections, using scenario mapping and multicriteria analysis. As for Democracy Labs, it creates discussion spaces where citizens and authorities co-develop new rules and formats for public engagement in marine planning. These tools are deployed to: 1) create and update participatory cartographies and climate risk perception maps; 2) integrate nature-based solutions in local action plans; 3) disseminate the methodology to new territories through methodological insights and online courses.

The framework provides a cross-cutting approach to the EGD key topics relevant for marine environment and sustainable transition of blue economy: climate change adaptation and mitigation, circular blue economy, marine biodiversity, marine renewable energies, sustainable food provision. (EN), MSP4Biodiversity Project, funded under the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (NRRP) 2023-2026, focuses on integrating biodiversity into Maritime Spatial Planning (MSP). It is part of the National Biodiversity Future Center (NBFC), specifically Activity 4 of Node 2, which aims to mitigate human impact on marine biodiversity. The project develops and tests solutions to combat biodiversity loss from activities such as fishing, navigation, and tourism. The goal of Activity 4 is to promote the sustainable development of the maritime economy while protecting biodiversity and marine ecosystems, in line with international frameworks on sustainable marine use and biodiversity protection. The project's primary spatial focus is on Italian marine waters, divided into three maritime areas covered by national plans nearing completion. It also considers the Mediterranean Sea as a whole, addressing the ecological, socioeconomic, and geopolitical complexities inherent in the MSP process.     Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • EuropeanGreenDeal The European Green Deal is a response to the challenges of climate change, loss of biodiversity and the destruction of nature, and establishes that a sustainable ‘blue economy’ will have to play a central role in alleviating the multiple pressures on the EU's land and sea resources and tackling climate change, while promoting greater uptake of nature-based solutions. This project contributes to addressing these challenges by adopting an ecosystem-based approach and reinforcing public participation in order to increase awareness, create social acceptance and reduce the level of conflict around restoration and conservation measures in coastal and marine spaces. This is coherent with the Green Deal’s approach, which indicates that “game changing policies only work if citizens are fully involved in designing them” and that “citizens are and should remain a driving force of the transition”.
    • TerritorialAgenda2030 The proposal complements the Territorial Agenda 2030’s strategic spatial planning objectives in terms of ICZM and MSP , thanks to the reinforcement of public participation and deliberation. It also contributes to reach the Agenda’s priority of building a healthy environment (better ecological livelihoods, climate-neutral and resilient towns, cities and regions), since the project will encourage the development of nature-based solutions, while increasing awareness and empowering local and regional communities to protect, rehabilitate, utilise and reutilise their (built) environments, as the Agenda specifies. The project also complements the Agenda 2030 by relying on a place-based approach, which seeks to ensure cooperation and coordination involving citizens, civil society, businesses, research and scientific institutions and knowledge centres.
    • EUStrategyAdriaticIonianRegion The EU Strategy for the Adriatic and Ionian Region (EUSAIR) is a tool adopted by the European Council in 2014 to foster social, economic & territorial cohesion to reduce disparities in the Adriatic-Ionian macro-region through cooperation. Pillar 1 of the Strategy is Blue growth, which includes the objective to “improve sea basin governance, by enhancing administrative & institutional capacities in the area of maritime governance & services”. The present proposal, with 2 Croatian partners & a pilot case in Croatia, supports the achievement of this goal by reinforcing the administrative & institutional capacities of authorities to manage and incorporate deliberative processes related to maritime governance, and establishing accountability & feedback mechanisms to ensure the integration of such participatory processes in the ICZM and MSP governance frameworks. Through the EUSAIR framework, the results of the project could be replicated to other Adriatic and Ionian areas beyond Croatia.
    • EUStrategyAlpineRegion The scope of this proposal is centred on marine and coastal areas, so there is no relevant contribution to the EU Strategy for the Alpine Region, which has a clear focus on improving policies related to mountainous areas.
    • Other The Westmed Initiative invites the participating countries to explore the added value and feasibility of appropriate maritime strategies at the sub-regional level, creating a smarter and more resilient blue economy and improving maritime governance for the Western Mediterranean. This project will contribute to the initiative by increasing the robustness of coastal and maritime policies and solutions thanks to reinforcing public participation and inclusive democracy in Spain, Italy, Portugal and France. The results of the project will be replicated to other Euro Med areas beyond the mentioned countries. This is coherent with the Communication of the Initiative, which establishes that for the initiative to work, a key condition is that the key stakeholders should be involved: national, regional and local authorities, including managing authorities, economic and social actors, citizens, academia and non-governmental organisations.
    • Other The EU Adaptation Strategy seeks to actively mainstream climate resilience considerations in all relevant policy fields and support the further development and implementation of adaptation strategies and plans at all levels of governance. This project will contribute to these goals by mainstreaming climate resilience considerations in ICZM and MSP policies through 2 of the 3 cross EU Adaptation Strategy cross-cutting priorities: nature-based solutions for adaptation and local adaptation action. It will contribute with concrete adaptation and resilience actions plans and nature-based solutions based on SWAP and DLABs participatory approaches, contributing to make the adaptation challenge a whole-society endeavour as the EU Adaptation Strategy establishes.
    • Other The EU’s Biodiversity Strategy for 2030 is a comprehensive, ambitious and long-term plan to protect nature and reverse the degradation of ecosystems, including coastal and marine areas. The project will support the development of action plans and nature based solutions which will support the EU Nature Restoration Plan that the strategy foresees, which, among others, aims to restore the good environmental status of marine ecosystems . This will be done by reinforcing participatory approaches and thus respecting the principle of equality that the strategy sets, which means adopting an inclusive approach to involve all stakeholders, including women, youth, civil society, local authorities, the private sector, academia and scientific institutions.
    • Other Maritime Spatial Planning (MSP) is a strategic process aimed at organizing the use of marine spaces to balance ecological, economic, and social objectives. It promotes sustainable development of maritime areas by coordinating activities and conserving marine ecosystems. MSP involves comprehensive mapping and planning to manage conflicts and synergies among various marine uses. The EU's MSP Directive (2014/89/EU) guides member states in implementing MSP, ensuring coherent and integrated approaches to marine resource management. This directive supports ecosystem-based management, stakeholder engagement, and cross-border cooperation, fostering sustainable use and protection of marine environments. The project results will be shared with the Mediterranean MSP CoP to present valuable engagement systems.
    • Other Integrated Coastal Zone Management (ICZM) is a framework promoting sustainable coastal development by integrating environmental, socio-economic, and cultural factors. Its objective is to balance ecological health with economic and social well-being. ICZM emphasises holistic, adaptive management and active public participation. It coordinates multiple sectors and stakeholders, supported by robust policy and legal frameworks like the EU's ICZM Recommendation (2002/413/EC) and the Marine Strategy Framework Directive (2008/56/EC). This directive guides the sustainable use and protection of coastal resources, ensuring that development meets present needs without compromising future generations' ability to meet theirs. Within the Barcelona Convention it is understood that ICZM covers the coastal zone up to 12 miles while MSP complement and further integrate waters that extend beyond the 12 miles mark (EEZ and transnational waters).
    • Other The EU adopted the Marine Strategy Framework Directive (MSFD 2008/56/ec), for protecting the health of EU oceans and seas. Its goal is to ensure the conservation of marine biodiversity and the sustainable use resources through an integrated, ecosystem-based approach. The MSFD aims to achieve or maintain a "good ecological status" for all EU marine waters by 2020, ensuring the long-term sustainability of marine ecosystems. To reach this goal, the directive sets a framework for action involving all EU member states and encourages collaboration between countries to address common environmental challenges. The directive requires member states to develop and implement marine monitoring programs to regularly assess the ecological status of marine waters and the effectiveness of management measures. These common monitoring programs, known as "descriptors," provide essential data to evaluate progress towards the directive's objectives and identify areas where further action is needed.
