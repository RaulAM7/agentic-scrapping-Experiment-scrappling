Building Smart Green SME Ecosystem: Transforming MED SMEs into active participants of a smart and green ecosystem and enhancing the economic activity of the food industry

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B NEXT Mediterranean Sea Basin (NEXT MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-02-27

Description

Description (EN):
PROJECT TYPE: 1 - Thematic projects ~ MAIN INTERVENTION FIELD: 026. Support for innovation clusters including between businesses, research organisations and public authorities and business networks primarily benefiting SMEs ~ SUMMARY: PROJECT IDEA: This project aims to create a MED food industry ecosystem comprising 100 SMEs to foster collaboration and facilitate digital and green transitions, aligning with three main EU policies: industrial strategy and ecosystems development, digital transition, and green transition. It includes four implementation WPs: WP3 focusing on setting up the SME ecosystem, WP4 dedicated to developing services and collaborative platforms for digital and green practices, WP5 on using these services by 100 SMEs, and WP6 ensuring the ecosystem's sustainability and its scale-up to all MED countries. CHALLENGES: The project addresses growth challenges in the MED food industry. It promotes ecosystem development, collaboration, and supply chains among SMEs in MED countries. Additionally, it advances the digital and green transition of SMEs through data awareness, benchmarking, learning, capacity building, and collaborative new product development. EXPECTED CHANGE to EXISTING SITUATION: The project will lead to the development of an ecosystem, 100 procurement agreements, smart green capacity building in 10 thematic areas, and NPD collaboration and creation of 10 new product prototypes. The services sustaining these outputs will be offered to all MED food SMEs. MAIN OUTPUTS: The project will establish an ecosystem of 100 SMEs from four MED countries. Beyond the project, it will open the ecosystem for participation to all MED SMEs. It will create an Association of SMEs to promote the MED food industry and develop four platform-based services for AI-based benchmarking, capacity building, digital procurement/trade, and new product development. The project will ensure the utilization of all services by 100 SMEs in the food industry, sustaining joint supply chains, and digital and green practices. Ultimately, the project aims to provide a model for ecosystem development and smart green transition in the MED food industry, enabling future replication in other industry groups. ~ KEEP KEYWORDS: Clustering and economic cooperation, ICT and digital society, Innovation capacity and awareness-raising

  • New product development (NPD) is about the renewal of products which because of technological and socio-economic conditions became obsolete and the gain of new markets for existing products. While in many regions technological innovation may reduce employment, mainly because of the introduction of ICT and automation, in the case of product-based innovation, regional wealth increases in parallel with employment. However, it is quite difficult for many EU regions to develop product innovation skills because NPD covers a wide range of expertise from consulting (product conception, market analysis, competition analysis, marketing etc.) to product design and prototyping.

    Contribution to wider strategies and policies:

    • Other Synergies of SmartGreenEcos with major strategies, particularly those outlined in the “Overview of main policies, strategies, and initiatives having synergies and complementarities with the Interreg NEXT MED Programme,” include:
    • Synergies with the EU Digital Strategy: The EU is pursuing a human-centric, sustainable vision for a digital society to empower citizens and businesses. Even small businesses can use digital technology to make better business decisions, interact with customers, or improve business operations. The synergies of our proposal with the EU digital strategy exist on three levels: (a) targets, as the proposal promotes digital skills, digital business, and digital services; (b) multi-country projects that allow the EU MED States and MED countries to pool efforts and launch cross-border initiatives; and (c) the digital transformation of businesses, which is strongly promoted by the services created in our project.
    • The Smart Specialisation Strategy for EU Regions: In particular, the Thematic Smart Specialisation Platforms and the agri-food platform include partnerships on high-tech farming, traceability, energy, and smart systems. Our project will create four platforms relevant to the food industry that have mutual synergies with the RIS3 thematic platforms.
    • Partnership for Research and Innovation in the Mediterranean Area: Synergies include building research and innovation capacities and developing knowledge and common innovative solutions for agro-food systems to make them more sustainable and improve their management in the Mediterranean area.
    • Transition Pathway for the EU Agri-food Ecosystem (EC 2024): This informs digital transformation, sustainability, competitiveness, and international cooperation, and supports the objectives of the EU Industrial Strategy. The Transition Pathway described the expected contributions of SMEs to the overall green and digital transformation of the industrial ecosystem in ways similar to our proposal.
