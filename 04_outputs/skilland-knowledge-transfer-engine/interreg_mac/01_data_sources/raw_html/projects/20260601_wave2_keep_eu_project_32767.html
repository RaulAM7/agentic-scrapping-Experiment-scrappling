Transferring Tourism Carrying Capacity Tool TO CARE about Sustainability of MED ecotourism strategies, plans, products and services.

Programme 2021 - 2027 Interreg VI-B EURO Mediterranean (EURO MED)
Date of latest update: 2026-01-27

Description

Description (FR):
L'outil de limitation de la capacité d'accueil touristique (TCCL) développé dans le cadre du projet ALTERECO aborde le risque de surtourisme et son impact sur le patrimoine naturel et culturel des destinations méditerranéennes. Le projet TO CARE MED étendra l'outil TCCL à d'autres destinations touristiques côtières, insulaires, rurales et naturelles protégées, en se concentrant sur deux aspects. Tout d'abord, l'outil TCCL sera adapté à chaque destination. Ensuite, il sera transféré à l'aide d'un « modèle de formation en cascade ». Cela permettra de transférer les méthodologies du niveau transnational à l'apprentissage et à la mise en œuvre au niveau local.
Read more
Description (EN):
The Tourism Carrying Capacity Limit (TCCL) tool developed in the ALTERECO project addresses the risk of overtourism and its impact on the natural and cultural heritage of Mediterranean destinations. The TO CARE MED project will extend the TCCL tool to other coastal, island, rural and protected natural tourist destinations, focusing on two aspects. First, the TCCL tool will be adapted to each destination. Then, it will be transferred using a 'cascade training model'. This will bring methodologies from the transnational level to local learning and implementation.

Expected Achievements (EN):
Promote tourism stakeholders' understanding of what sustainability means in ecotourism and how the exploitation of ecosystem services must be balanced with the conservation of nature and biodiversity while ensuring the well-being of the community and tourists. It is not certain that ecotourism is sustainable, there is an environmental carrying capacity that must be understood, analyzed, and constantly monitored. This requires a collaborative effort between the different subjects who together build the ecotourism offer, and this also represents the ambition of WP1 which intends to establish a working group on the destination that cooperates at both local and transnational levels. The successful implementation of the ecotourism sustainability tourism activities as well as the alignment of stakeholders' understanding with the carrying capacity principles (approached and developed in WP1) pave the way to focusing on the optimization of the Tourism Carrying Capacity Limit (TCCL) tool (developed in previous ALTERECO projects) to transfer it to MED ecotourism destinations, while ensuring a personalized application to the pilot project areas. This implies that WP 2 aims to transfer the TCCL tool/model and to orient the organization of tourism subsystems (catering, accommodation, attractions, mobility) and therefore allow the dynamic and strategic alignment of the territory's offers with the results of the analysis of tourist flows carrying capacity, in ecotourism areas characterized by high levels of natural, cultural and biodiversity heritage. To steer the uptake of solutions by jointly defining sustainable tourism strategies and action plans. The goal is to align ecotourism products and services with carrying capacity limits, ensuring environmental protection, sustainable development, and citizens' health while maximizing the exploitation of project results.
