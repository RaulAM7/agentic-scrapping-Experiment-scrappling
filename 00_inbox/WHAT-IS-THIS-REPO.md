# Experiment Context — Smart Scraping Stack Evaluation for Agentic ICP Research

## 1. Purpose of this repository

This repository is **not** a production implementation of a final business system.

It is an **experiment repository** created to evaluate a specific smart scraping stack inside a broader agentic business architecture.

The purpose of the experiment is to understand how this stack behaves when used as part of a real workflow that starts from a strategically grounded ICP and moves toward intelligent data extraction, research, and downstream commercial activation.

This experiment should help us extract conclusions about:

- installation and setup difficulty
- usability inside an agentic repo workflow
- fit for AI-assisted research and scraping
- ability to support structured ICP research
- potential role inside a larger end-to-end business system

---

## 2. Broader strategic context

This experiment sits inside a larger business and agentic systems vision.

The broader chain we care about is:

**ICP → smart scraping → offer design → funnel building → outreach/emailing → CRM**

The scraping layer is not an isolated technical curiosity. It is meant to become one of the operational bridges between strategic market understanding and commercial execution.

We are therefore not evaluating this tool only as a scraper.

We are evaluating it as a possible building block for an **agentic business operating system**.

---

## 3. Nature of the experiment

We are running a **comparative experiment** across two separate repositories.

Each repository tests a different stack for smart scraping and web data extraction.

This repo corresponds to:

https://github.com/D4Vinci/Scrapling


The experiment should be approached as a structured evaluation, not as a random sandbox.

The goal is to understand:

- how this stack is installed
- how it is configured
- how it is used in practice
- what kinds of workflows it enables
- what limitations it has
- what it would look like to use it for a real ICP-driven research workflow

---

## 4. Comparative mindset

This repo is one side of a benchmark.

The benchmark is **not** only about raw scraping power.

It is about which stack is better suited for an AI-native, repo-based, agentic workflow where an LLM-driven operator needs to:

- understand the stack quickly
- install and operate it inside a repository
- use it for a realistic business research task
- generate reusable findings
- document conclusions clearly

The comparison should include both technical and workflow dimensions.

We care about:

- friction of adoption
- clarity of mental model
- flexibility
- ease of orchestration by Claude inside a repo
- fit for structured research
- future scalability into a more serious lead research / smart scraping system

---

## 5. ICP and business use case that grounds this experiment

This experiment is grounded in a realistic commercial and strategic use case.

We want to explore an **AI-native future product/service** aimed at professional firms in the Canary Islands, especially:

- law firms
- accounting firms
- tax advisory firms
- mixed legal-fiscal-professional offices

The product vision is not generic “AI for business”.

The angle is much sharper:

an AI-native system for document-heavy professional firms that suffer from operational overload caused by:

- too many files and folders
- disorganized documentation
- manual classification work
- repeated handling of invoices, records, and case files
- administrative backlog
- compliance-sensitive review processes
- operational bottlenecks caused by human sorting and verification

The core idea is that many of these firms are still trapped in low-leverage operational work and document chaos, while the real opportunity is to help them become more efficient, more structured, and more scalable through AI-assisted workflows.

---

## 6. The kind of pain we are anchoring on

The business pain behind this experiment is not abstract productivity language.

It is a much more concrete pattern:

**highly skilled professionals drowning in manual backoffice work, document handling, administrative review, exception checking, and fragmented information flows**

Typical pain signals include:

- documents arriving through multiple channels
- manual filing and re-filing
- large backlogs of pending administrative work
- too much staff time spent on low-value processing
- review work caused by unreliable OCR or partial automation
- fear of mistakes in accounting, tax, or legal contexts
- difficulty scaling service quality without growing headcount
- professionals wanting to spend more time on high-value advisory work, but being trapped in operational gravity

This pain pattern should guide how the experiment is framed.

---

## 7. Why ICP matters in this experiment

The ICP is not an ornamental deliverable.

It is meant to shape what we try to scrape, what signals we care about, and how we interpret the usefulness of the stack.

The ideal future workflow is:

1. generate a strong ICP
2. translate ICP into observable market signals
3. use scraping/research stack to collect relevant external data
4. structure those findings into usable insight for downstream business operations

So this experiment should be thought of as an early validation of whether this stack can support that kind of flow.

## 8. Practical experimental scenario

For the purpose of this experiment, we want to simulate a realistic workflow.

The simulated scenario is:

- we have defined an ICP for professional firms in the Canary Islands
- we want to explore how to research and scrape relevant public data/signals for that ICP
- we want to understand what this stack enables in terms of:
  - website extraction
  - structured data extraction
  - crawling
  - discovery
  - categorization
  - signal collection
  - documentation of findings

This is still an experiment, so it does not need to become a huge production system.

But it should go far enough that we can genuinely evaluate the stack.

## 9. What a good outcome looks like

A good outcome from work in this repo would include:

- a clear understanding of what this stack is and how it works

## 10. Working principles for this experiment
